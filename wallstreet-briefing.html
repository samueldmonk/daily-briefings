The Lead

Stocks extend the chip-led rebound as oil eases — S&P up ~0.6% as of ~11 a.m. ET, with May CPI on deck

Wall Street built on Monday's bounce on Tuesday, with semiconductors again leading a broad advance. As of roughly 11 a.m. ET, the S&P 500 was up about 0.63%, the Dow added about 0.67%, and the Nasdaq Composite rose about 0.69%, with the small-cap Russell 2000 up around 0.77% — a sign the recovery from last week's AI-trade slide is broadening. Nvidia gained about 2.3% after announcing a next-generation memory partnership with South Korea's SK Hynix, and chip-equipment names rallied. Oil retreated as investors bet on de-escalation in the Middle East. (Figures are intraday moves as of ~11 a.m. ET and will change through the session; Tuesday's official close posts after 4 p.m. ET.)

The advance isn't without cautionary notes — Bank of America strategists flagged what they called "bear market signposts," and traders are positioning for Wednesday's May CPI (due 8:30 a.m. ET, June 10), with FactSet's consensus looking for headline inflation around 4.2% year over year (up from 3.8% in April) and core near 2.9%. The backdrop: Monday's rebound recouped part of last week's rout, with the Nasdaq Composite closing up 0.86% at 25,929.66 and the S&P 500 up 0.30% at 7,405.73, while the Dow slipped 0.16% to 50,786.01. That followed Friday's plunge, when the Nasdaq Composite fell 4.18% — its worst day since April 2025 — as a chip selloff erased roughly $1 trillion after a hot May jobs report (+172k vs ~80k expected) fueled Fed-hike bets. The 10-year Treasury yield is holding around 4.57%.
