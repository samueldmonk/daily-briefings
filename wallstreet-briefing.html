The Lead
Weekend update · Markets closed — last close Friday June 12

Stocks closed out a winning week as SpaceX jumped 19% in the biggest debut ever — Dow added 353 points to finish at 51,202

Markets are dark for the weekend, so the tape stands at Friday's close — the end of a whiplash week that finished on a high note. At Friday's close (per CNBC): the Dow rose 353.51 points (+0.7%) to 51,202.26, the S&P 500 gained 0.5% to 7,431.46, and the Nasdaq Composite added 0.31% to 25,888.84 — the Nasdaq capped by a mega-cap fade as investors made room for the week's main event. That sealed a winning week across the board: versus the prior Friday's closes, the S&P 500 added about 0.6%, the Nasdaq about 0.7% and the Dow about 0.7%, a full round trip from Wednesday's 953-point Dow rout to fresh gains by the bell.

The main event delivered. SpaceX closed its first session at $161.11, up 19% from the $135 IPO price — after pricing the largest IPO in history Thursday night ($75 billion raised on 555.6 million shares), opening at $150 and touching $176.52 intraday. CNBC and NPR put the day-end valuation around $2.1 trillion, and the debut makes Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire. Volume was historic too: more than 500 million shares changed hands on the day, approaching Facebook's 580 million-share first-day record from 2012.

The other engine that powered the week — peace hopes — is the swing factor heading into Monday's open, and Sunday brought a big step. President Trump declared the U.S.–Iran deal "is now complete" and authorized the immediate removal of the U.S. naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz (CBS News, NBC News). Pakistan's prime minister, Shehbaz Sharif — whose government has been mediating alongside Qatar — said the two sides reached a deal declaring "the immediate and permanent termination of military operations on all fronts," including an end to military operations in Lebanon, with an official signing ceremony expected Friday, June 19, in Switzerland. The terms taking shape: Iran reopens the Strait of Hormuz immediately while the U.S. lifts its naval blockade, Washington releases about $25 billion in frozen Iranian assets, holds off on new sanctions and waives existing oil sanctions. One catch looms large for markets: Tehran did not immediately confirm the agreement — Iranian officials stayed silent and Bloomberg reported Tehran was "still reviewing" the text. The bigger threat is on the ground: fresh Israeli strikes on Beirut's southern suburbs killed at least three people, and Iran's security establishment has called Lebanon a "red line," prompting Trump to publicly warn both Israel and Iran not to "blow it" (PBS, CNBC, Al Jazeera). For markets the read-through is clear: a reopened Strait and oil-sanctions relief would point oil lower, and Friday's tape already leaned that way — WTI crude slid to an eight-week low near $85 (intraday under $84, down about 6% on the week). June consumer sentiment, reported Friday, improved to 48.9 — well above the 46.0 expected and May's record-low 44.8 — on easing gas prices.

And after weeks of regulatory limbo, the Justice Department approved Paramount's $110 billion takeover of Warner Bros. Discovery on Friday, per CNBC and Variety — no divestitures, behavioral remedies or concessions required. The fight isn't over: state attorneys general are taking a harder line, with California's Rob Bonta reportedly planning an investigation and New York likely to join, on a deal still on track to complete by September. Brussels gets a say too: the European Commission is reviewing the tie-up under the EU's Foreign Subsidies Regulation, with provisional deadlines in the first half of July.

Movers & Drivers
IPO Debut

SpaceX (SPCX) +19% — closed $161.11

Priced at $135, opened at $150, ran to $176.52, settled at $161.11 for a ~$2.1T day-one valuation. Record $75B raised; retail orders reportedly topped $100B. A dozen leveraged SPCX ETFs are queued, and the ~83M-share greenshoe (~$11.2B) is the supply overhang to watch.

Rotation Watch

Mega-caps make room: AMZN −2.2% · AAPL −2.0%

Amazon (−2.17%) and Apple (−1.95%) were the S&P's notable heavyweight decliners into the final half hour even as most of the index advanced — The Motley Fool's read: investors selling existing tech to fund SPCX buys. Alphabet and Nvidia held up through the afternoon.

Post-Earnings

Adobe (ADBE) ~−7%

A record quarter ($6.62B revenue, $5.96 EPS beat, FY guidance raised) sunk by CFO Dan Durn's exit to Marvell effective June 15 — Stifel, Wolfe and Evercore ISI downgraded. Beat-and-raise, but the Street followed the CFO out the door.

Space Shakeout

Space stocks sell the news

Virgin Galactic −24% after surging into the debut, AST SpaceMobile −10%, Rocket Lab −8% during the session — despite RKLB's coming Nasdaq-100 inclusion (effective before the June 22 open), which had it up 4.5% earlier in the day.

Deal Cleared

Paramount–Warner Bros. Discovery: DOJ approves

The Antitrust Division signed off on the $110B tie-up Friday with no divestitures, remedies or concessions, finding no threat to competition (CNBC/Variety); WBD shares gained on the clearance. Next hurdles: state AGs (California reportedly preparing, New York likely) and EU/UK reviews. Targeted completion: September, after which a "ticking fee" makes the deal pricier.

Homebuilder Earnings

Lennar (LEN) lower — guidance trails

Fiscal Q2: 20,519 deliveries (+2% y/y), new orders 21,749 (−4%), EPS $1.24 ($1.31 ex-mark-to-market), revenue $7.9B, 15.6% home-sale gross margin. Full-year delivery guidance trimmed to 82,000–83,000 on "current pressures on interest rates and continued macro uncertainty"; shares fell as order and delivery forecasts trailed expectations (TheStreet — no settled % in sources).
