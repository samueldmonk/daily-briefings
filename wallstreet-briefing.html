The Lead

Stocks lower as inflation tops 4% and U.S.–Iran strikes escalate — as of ~3 PM ET

Wall Street spent Wednesday in the red after a double dose of bad news: May CPI came in at +0.5% month-over-month and +4.2% year-over-year — the first reading above 4% in three years and the highest since April 2023 — while the U.S. and Iran traded fresh military strikes overnight after Iran downed an American Apache helicopter patrolling the Strait of Hormuz. President Trump said Tehran has "taken too long" to negotiate and "will have to pay the price."

The inflation print had a silver lining: core CPI rose just 0.2% on the month — below the 0.3% estimate — and 2.9% annually, with energy driving more than 60% of the monthly increase. Jim Cramer called the spike "artificial inflation" tied to war-driven oil prices. Still, stocks opened sharply lower (the Nasdaq was off ~1.6% early, per TheStreet) and stayed down all session. As of mid-afternoon, 24/7 Wall St's live data showed the S&P 500 down ~0.9%, the Dow down ~0.7%, and the Nasdaq 100 down ~1.6%; Yahoo Finance pegged the Nasdaq Composite around -1.2% in afternoon trade. Notably, breadth was positive — TheStreet reported ~63% of U.S. issues advancing as the selling stayed concentrated in heavyweight tech.

Movers & Drivers

Super Micro ▼ as much as -12%

SMCI slid after announcing $7 billion in equity financing-related deals — the latest AI infrastructure name raising capital, after Alphabet's $85B stock sale this month and reports Meta may follow. Shares were down ~12% early and around -7.6% by mid-afternoon per 24/7 Wall St.

ServiceNow ▼ ~-6.3%

NOW fell after the company disclosed a security incident: attackers exploited an unauthenticated API endpoint to query customer instance data (full story on The Cyber Wire tab).

J.M. Smucker ▲ ~+10.4%

SJM topped the gainers board mid-afternoon per 24/7 Wall St, extending the rally that began with this week's earnings beat. Defensive staples continued to attract rotation flows.

Early risers: Robinhood, chip-equipment ▲

In early trading, TheStreet flagged Robinhood +8.1%, KLA +6.7%, and Applied Materials +5.5%. Energy names outperformed with crude higher; Coca-Cola, Verizon, and Chevron led Dow gainers.
