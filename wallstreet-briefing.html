The Lead

Chip-led rebound holds to the close: Nasdaq leads Monday's bounce as semiconductors snap back and a cooling Middle East lets oil retreat from its morning spike (June 8 close)

Wall Street closed higher Monday, clawing back part of last week's brutal slide as the semiconductor selloff reversed. The tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite led, finishing up roughly 1.1% (about +273 points to ~25,983), the S&P 500 added around 0.6% (to about 7,427), and the Dow ended modestly higher (roughly +0.5%). Stocks firmed on signs of a cautious de-escalation in the Middle East and a halt to the chip rout: Iran said it had ended its military operation against Israel after weekend strikes, and as tensions cooled abruptly through the session, crude oil gave back most of its early surge — WTI, which had spiked above $94 a barrel overnight on the strikes, faded back toward the $90–91 level (last around $91.3).

Semiconductors did the heavy lifting. Marvell Technology jumped about 14.7% in its first trade after S&P Dow Jones Indices said the stock had grown enough to join the S&P 500 (effective June 22); Intel surged about 12% after The Information reported Alphabet's Google ordered more than three million tensor processing units for 2028; Micron rebounded about 10.5% after sliding 13.3% Friday; and Corning rose about 6.5% on a multibillion-dollar Amazon optical-fiber deal for data centers. Nvidia added roughly 2%. The bounce follows Friday's rout, when the Nasdaq Composite plunged 4.18% — its worst day since April 2025 — and a chip selloff erased roughly $1 trillion in value after a hot May jobs report (+172k vs ~80k expected) fueled Fed-hike bets. The 10-year Treasury yield held near a two-week high around 4.55%, and traders now look ahead to this week's CPI print. Apple opened its Worldwide Developers Conference today.
