The Lead

Hot jobs report slams tech: Nasdaq on pace for worst day in nearly eight months as rate-hike odds climb — as of ~1 PM ET

A stronger-than-expected May jobs report — nonfarm payrolls up 172,000 with unemployment steady at 4.3% — sent Treasury yields higher Friday and accelerated the week's rout in chip stocks. As of roughly 1 PM ET, the Nasdaq Composite was down nearly 3%, on pace for its biggest single-day drop since October 10, 2025, while the S&P 500 fell about 1.1% and the Dow was modestly lower with all four major benchmarks sagging, per TheStreet's midday update. Bloomberg reported information technology shares plunged 2.9% while defensive sectors — consumer staples, healthcare and utilities — led the index.

The selloff puts the S&P 500's nine-week winning streak in serious jeopardy: a tenth straight weekly gain would have been its longest run since 1985, per Bloomberg. Traders now price in an 85% chance of a quarter-point Fed rate hike by year-end, up from 60% a week ago, as elevated oil prices and resilient labor data stoke inflation concerns. Friday's weakness extends Thursday's chip-led rotation sparked by Broadcom, which posted record quarterly revenue but narrowly missed analyst estimates ($22.19B vs. $22.27B expected), with a software miss and unchanged AI outlook.
