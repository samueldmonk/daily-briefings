The Lead

Stocks idle near record highs pre-bell as the Fed convenes — markets coast off Monday's chip-led surge

U.S. equity futures were little changed early Tuesday, holding steady as Wall Street paused after a three-day rally and turned its attention to the start of the two-day Federal Open Market Committee meeting. Investors continued to digest the U.S.–Iran ceasefire framework that has driven oil sharply lower and eased the inflation worries hanging over the rate path.

The calm follows a roaring start to the holiday-shortened week. On Monday, the S&P 500 jumped 1.65%, the Nasdaq Composite surged 3.07% and the Dow climbed 0.92% (+468.77 points), as SpaceX extended its post-IPO rally and President Trump announced an agreement to end the U.S.–Iran conflict and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Seven of the 11 S&P sectors finished higher, led by technology, communication services and consumer discretionary.

The Iran framework remains unsigned — a reported signing ceremony is slated for Friday in Switzerland, Iran has not confirmed terms, and Israel is not a party — but the prospect of freer oil flows has done the heavy lifting for risk appetite. With a Fed decision and new Chair Kevin Warsh's first press conference landing Wednesday, traders are largely sitting on their hands.

Movers & Drivers

SpaceX (SPCX) ▲ ~8.9% pre-mkt

The newly public rocket maker was up roughly 8.9% in premarket trading on an acquisition announcement, after closing up about 19.6% in its first full day of trading. The post-IPO momentum has been a key prop under the tape.

Oil & the Iran deal ▼ WTI ~$80

Crude extended losses toward a two-month low after the U.S. and Iran reached a framework to end their conflict and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. WTI tumbled more than 5% Monday to around $80 a barrel, cooling inflation fears across the curve.

The Fed in focus

Futures markets price a ~99.6% chance the FOMC holds the target at 3.50–3.75% Wednesday, with traders pricing no cuts this year. The market's attention is squarely on Warsh's debut press conference and any shift in tone.
