The Lead
Largest IPO in history · Peace-deal hopes

SpaceX soars in the biggest IPO ever as a U.S.–Iran deal nears — tape modestly green as of ~2 PM ET

Elon Musk's SpaceX debuted on the Nasdaq under ticker SPCX after pricing the largest IPO in history Thursday night: $135 a share, 555.6 million shares, a record $75 billion raised at an initial valuation of roughly $1.77–1.78 trillion. The stock opened at $150, traded as high as $176.52, and was last up about 26–27% in the low $170s early this afternoon — a fully diluted value in the $2.0–2.1 trillion range. Dollar volume (~$33B by midday) outran QQQ and SPY. Yahoo Finance notes the debut makes Musk the world's first trillionaire, with a fortune above $1.05 trillion.

The other engine under the tape: peace. A draft 14-point U.S.–Iran memorandum would lift oil sanctions and reopen the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days, with a signing possible as soon as Sunday in Switzerland. Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif said Friday a "final, agreed upon text" has been reached — though Tehran has pushed back on parts, so it isn't done. WTI crude sank more than 4% to an eight-week low below $84 before bouncing back toward $85.

As of ~2 PM ET the S&P 500 was up ~0.4%, the Dow up ~0.5–0.7% (roughly +350 points), and the Nasdaq Composite up ~0.2% after a choppy, gyrating morning; the Russell 2000 was up ~0.4% and more than 60% of issues were advancing. June consumer sentiment (UMich) improved to 48.9 — well above the 46.0 expected and May's record-low 44.8 — on easing gas prices.
