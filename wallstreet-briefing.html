The Lead

Tech rotation resumes: stocks fade the morning bounce — S&P off ~1% as of midday, with Apple and chips leading the slide ahead of May CPI

Wall Street's chip-led rebound ran out of steam by midday Tuesday as the rotation out of heavyweight technology resumed. After opening higher, the major averages turned lower: as of around noon ET, the S&P 500 was off roughly 1% and the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite was down about 2%, while the more defensive Dow held up better, slipping about 0.5%. Apple fell about 3% after its Worldwide Developers Conference — Tim Cook's last as CEO — where it unveiled a revamped "Siri AI," with reports that the new assistant won't launch in the EU amid Brussels antitrust rules. Nvidia, Oracle and AMD each gave back ground, sliding between roughly 1% and 3% as skepticism over stretched AI valuations reasserted itself. (Figures are intraday moves as of ~12 p.m. ET and will change through the session; Tuesday's official close posts after 4 p.m. ET.)

The pullback was a partial reversal of the morning, when stocks had opened higher on easing oil and a second day of chip gains. Defensive corners caught a bid as money rotated out of AI names — asset managers Blackstone and KKR rose about 2% — and lower energy prices gave Treasuries some respite. Traders are positioning for Wednesday's May CPI (due 8:30 a.m. ET, June 10), with FactSet's consensus looking for headline inflation around 4.2% year over year and core near 2.9%; a hot print would reinforce the Fed-hike worry that has dogged markets since Friday. The backdrop: Monday's rebound recouped part of last week's rout, with the Nasdaq Composite closing up 0.86% at 25,929.66 and the S&P 500 up 0.30% at 7,405.73, while the Dow slipped 0.16% to 50,786.01. That followed Friday's plunge, when the Nasdaq Composite fell 4.18% — its worst day since April 2025 — as a chip selloff erased roughly $1 trillion after a hot May jobs report (+172k vs ~80k expected) fueled Fed-hike bets.
