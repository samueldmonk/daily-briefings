The Lead

Wall Street eyes a tentative rebound before the open as chip names firm up — but resurgent oil and Fed-hike bets keep the bounce in check

Stocks pointed to a modest recovery ahead of Monday's bell, with S&P 500 futures up about 0.6% as of ~7:49 a.m. ET, attempting to claw back some of Friday's brutal slide. The rebound was led by semiconductors after Marvell Technology jumped roughly 9% in premarket trade on news it will join the S&P 500 (effective June 22), pulling other chip names higher. Apple kicks off its Worldwide Developers Conference in Cupertino today, with investors watching for AI announcements including a reported Google Gemini–powered Siri.

The bounce comes off a punishing end to last week: Friday saw the Nasdaq Composite plunge 4.2% — its worst day since April 2025 — as a semiconductor rout erased roughly $1 trillion in market value, after a stronger-than-expected May jobs report (+172k vs ~80k expected) fueled bets the Fed could hike later this year. Capping any rally: WTI crude rebounded about 3.4% toward $93.60 on renewed Middle East tensions, with Iran's military halting strikes on Israel but warning it would resume if operations in Lebanon continue. The 10-year Treasury yield pushed near 4.57%, a two-week high.
