The Lead

Dow plunges 953 points to a session low at the close as Trump vows to hit Iran "very hard" — June 10 close

Wall Street sold off hard into Wednesday's closing bell. Per CNBC, the Dow Jones Industrial Average tumbled 953 points (-1.87%), finishing at its low of the session, while the S&P 500 fell about 1.6% and the Nasdaq Composite dropped roughly 2%. The slide accelerated sharply in the final stretch — delayed quotes still showed the indices down only ~1–1.3% around 3:45 PM ET — after President Trump pledged further strikes on Iran, saying "we're going to be attacking them very hard." TheStreet reported the Cboe Volatility Index surged about 12% on the day.

The day began with a double dose of bad news. May CPI rose +0.5% month-over-month and +4.2% year-over-year — the fastest annual pace since May 2023 and the first reading above 4% in three years, though in line with expectations. The silver lining: core CPI rose just 0.2% on the month, below the 0.3% estimate, and 2.9% annually. Energy did the damage — energy prices jumped 23.5% year-over-year, with gasoline up 40.9% and fuel oil up 58.9%, per the BLS data — as the Iran war and the near-closure of the Strait of Hormuz kept crude elevated. Overnight, the U.S. and Iran traded fresh strikes after the downing of an American Apache helicopter, and Yahoo Finance noted the hot headline reading may boost bets that the Fed hikes rates this year.

Movers & Drivers

Super Micro ▼ ~-14% to -18% intraday

SMCI plunged after announcing a $7 billion equity raise — the latest AI infrastructure name tapping markets for capital. The Motley Fool had shares down more than 17% at midday; Yahoo Finance's losers board showed ~-13.5% to -14% later in the session.

Chips: Nvidia, Micron ▼

Nvidia fell ~2.4% and Micron ~3.6% at midday per The Motley Fool as the AI rotation deepened. Vanda Research said retail selling in single stocks hit its heaviest level since November 2023, concentrated in semis like Micron and SanDisk — while retail buying of space stocks hit its highest since December 2024 ahead of SpaceX's IPO.

Robinhood ▲ +6% to +8%

HOOD climbed 8.1% in early trading per TheStreet and was still up ~6% at midday on a strategic update per The Motley Fool — one of the few bright spots in a risk-off tape. KLA (+6.7%) and Applied Materials (+5.5%) also rose early before the broad slide.

Oil up, gold down ◆

WTI crude climbed ~3% to top $91 after Trump's threats, per CNBC; Brent traded near $93. Gold went the other way — Bloomberg reported bullion fell as much as 2.1% to ~$4,173/oz, now about a fifth below its pre-war level after breaking its 200-day moving average.
