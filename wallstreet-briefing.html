The Lead
Markets open · As of ~12 PM ET, Monday June 15

"Ships of the World, start your engines": stocks rip higher and oil dives under $80 as Trump declares the U.S.–Iran deal "complete" and orders the Strait of Hormuz reopened

Wall Street came back from the weekend in full risk-on mode and held the gains into the lunch hour. As of about noon ET, the S&P 500 was up roughly 1.5%, the Nasdaq Composite up about 2.4%, and the Dow up about 1.2%, with beaten-down technology and semiconductor names out front; the small-cap Russell 2000 added about 0.8%, paring a larger morning gain as the rally narrowed toward big-cap growth. The trigger was a weekend leap toward ending the U.S.–Iran war: late Sunday President Trump declared on Truth Social that "the Deal with the Islamic Republic of Iran is now complete," authorized the "toll-free opening of the Strait of Hormuz" and the "immediate removal of the United States Naval blockade," and added: "Ships of the World, start your engines. Let the oil flow!" The move rippled worldwide — Japan's Nikkei surged about 5.5% and South Korea's Kospi jumped as much as 5.7% overnight.

The clearest market tell was in crude. With traders pricing in the return of Hormuz shipping after months of disruption, WTI crude tumbled about 5.5% to roughly $80 a barrel — dipping below $80 for the first time since March — while Brent fell around 5% to about $82–83. That fed the equity rally on two fronts: relief that a historic supply shock is unwinding, and hope that cooler energy prices feed through to inflation. The flip side hit energy producers hard: oil-and-gas names led the decliners, with Coterra Energy down about 8.6% at mid-morning. Gold climbed about 2.8% to roughly $4,358 and silver jumped over 4% as the dollar and yields eased.

Keep the caveats in view: this is an announcement, not a signed treaty. Trump and Pakistan's prime minister, Shehbaz Sharif — whose government has mediated alongside Qatar — say a signing ceremony is reportedly set for Friday, June 19, in Switzerland, but the two sides still describe the terms differently. Iranian officials have not confirmed the deal is "complete"; Iranian state media frames the Strait as toll-free for only 60 days, after which Iran and Oman would administer it, and Israel is not a party to the agreement. Fresh Israeli strikes on Beirut's southern suburbs over the weekend rattled the talks. The market is trading the relief; the paperwork and the ground situation are the risks.

The backdrop is otherwise constructive. Friday sealed a winning week: the Dow closed at 51,202.26 (+0.70%), the S&P 500 at 7,431.46 (+0.50%) and the Nasdaq Composite at 25,888.84 (+0.31%), each up about 0.65–0.70% on the week. SpaceX, fresh off the biggest IPO in history, kept running — shares rose roughly 8% in early trading to near $174 (last around $171–172, a market cap above $2.1 trillion) after the Wall Street Journal reported Australian mining billionaire Gina Rinehart took a stake of more than $1 billion in the company, and Cathie Wood's ARK disclosed buying $529.7 million of SPCX. All eyes now turn to Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting as Fed chair, June 16–17, where a hold at 3.50–3.75% is widely expected but the new bias — after hot May CPI and PPI — is the real watch item.

Movers & Drivers
Chip Rally

Chips and memory lead: NVDA +1.8% · INTC ~+6.5% · STX ~+7.3%

Semiconductors and storage paced the advance as the peace deal revived appetite for growth. Nvidia traded up about 1.77% to ~$225.00 after Goldman Sachs reiterated its Buy (CY27 estimates "over 30% above the Street"). At ~10:11 a.m. ET (24/7 Wall St): Intel ~$124.57, Western Digital ~$562.93, Seagate ~$931.04; Micron, AMD and Intel all higher (GuruFocus). Intel is up roughly 190% year-to-date.

Oil Tumble

Energy sells off: Coterra (CTRA) −8.6%

With WTI sliding about 5.5% under $80 on the prospect of Hormuz reopening, oil-and-gas producers led the decliners — Coterra around $32.56 at mid-morning (24/7 Wall St). The same supply-relief move that lifts the broad tape is a direct hit to E&P names.

IPO Aftermath

SpaceX (SPCX) ~+8%

Shares climbed about 8% in early trading to ~$173.95, last around $171.72 — a market cap near $2.1 trillion, inside the $150.00–$176.52 debut range — after the Wall Street Journal reported mining billionaire Gina Rinehart took a stake of more than $1 billion; Cathie Wood's ARK separately bought $529.7M of SPCX. SpaceX closed its Friday debut at $161.11 (+19% vs the $135 IPO price).

Materials & Industrials

3M +3.7% · Mosaic +7.6% · Albemarle +7.1%

Cyclicals jumped on the risk-on, lower-energy backdrop. 3M led the Dow at ~$148.62 (+3.70%); fertilizer and lithium names surged (24/7 Wall St, ~10:11 a.m. ET): Mosaic ~$22.69, Albemarle ~$170.42. Easing geopolitical risk gave materials and industrials a lift.

Post-Earnings

Adobe (ADBE) ~−6.8%

Adobe extended Friday's slide, down to ~$204.02 (24/7 Wall St). Last week's record quarter ($6.62B revenue, $5.96 EPS beat, FY guidance raised) was overshadowed by CFO Dan Durn's exit to Marvell — which takes effect today, June 15 — plus Stifel, Wolfe and Evercore ISI downgrades. A beat-and-raise the Street keeps selling.

Laggards

IBM −2.4% · Home Depot −2.1%

Not everything joined the party. IBM was the biggest Dow laggard at ~$213.40 (−2.42%) on a cautious enterprise-software note, and Home Depot slipped to ~$303.85 (−2.14%) on mortgage-rate worries (24/7 Wall St). Defensive and rate-sensitive corners lagged the chip-led charge.
