The Lead

Stocks rebound in morning trade as chip names lead the bounce — but surging oil and Fed-hike bets cap the recovery (as of ~10 a.m. ET)

Wall Street opened Monday in recovery mode, clawing back part of Friday's brutal slide. As of mid-morning (~10 a.m. ET), the S&P 500 was up about 0.9%, the Nasdaq Composite rose roughly 1.4%, and the Dow added about 0.6%. The rebound was led by semiconductors after Marvell Technology jumped roughly 9% on news it will join the S&P 500 (effective June 22), while Corning surged more than 9% on a multibillion-dollar Amazon optical-fiber deal. Apple opened its Worldwide Developers Conference in Cupertino today, with investors watching for AI announcements.

The bounce follows a punishing end to last week: Friday saw the Nasdaq Composite plunge 4.18% — its worst day since April 2025 — as a semiconductor rout erased roughly $1 trillion in market value, after a stronger-than-expected May jobs report (+172k vs ~80k expected) fueled bets the Fed could hike later this year. Capping the rally: WTI crude jumped about 4.5% toward $94.58 as Israel and Iran traded strikes and Israel hit villages in southern Lebanon. The 10-year Treasury yield held near 4.57%, with the 30-year above 5%. Citigroup raised its year-end S&P 500 target to 8,100, citing AI earnings tailwinds.
