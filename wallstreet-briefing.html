The Lead
Midday report — Friday, June 12, as of ~1:15 PM ET

SpaceX soars in the biggest IPO in history as a U.S.–Iran deal nears; stocks gyrate, then push higher

SpaceX (SPCX) began trading on the Nasdaq today after pricing the largest initial public offering ever — a fixed $135 per share for 555.6 million shares, raising roughly $75 billion at a valuation near $1.77 trillion. The stock opened at $150 (+11%) and kept climbing: Motley Fool/Xignite quotes showed it around $172, up ~28%, as of ~1:15 PM ET, with CNBC's live coverage flagging a pop of as much as ~30%. Yahoo Finance reported the debut makes Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire, with his SpaceX stake alone worth nearly $870 billion at the IPO price.

The broad tape spent the morning gyrating (TheStreet's word) as the mega-listing tested the rest of tech — at ~1 PM ET the Nasdaq was slightly red — before turning higher: as of ~1:15 PM ET, delayed quotes showed the Dow up ~0.8% (+~430 points), the S&P 500 up ~0.6% and the Nasdaq Composite up ~0.5%. Helping the mood: CNBC reports a U.S.–Iran deal is nearing after Trump called off his threat to bomb Iran late Wednesday — the trigger for Thursday's monster rally, the market's best day in two months (Dow +930 points). WTI crude fell below $85 for the first time since mid-April on hopes a deal reopens the Strait of Hormuz, and June University of Michigan data showed consumer sentiment improving as gas prices ease.

The fly in the ointment: May PPI, released Thursday, was hot — +1.1% on the month and 6.5% year over year, the highest since November 2022, with core PPI ex-food/energy/trade up 0.8%, the biggest monthly jump since March 2022 (CNBC). Wholesale energy surged 10.7%. That lands on Chair Kevin Warsh's desk at next week's FOMC meeting alongside a 4.2% headline CPI — and a soft 0.2% core.
