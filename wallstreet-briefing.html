The Lead
Record IPO · Iran Deal Hopes · As of ~2 PM ET

SpaceX pops nearly 30% in the biggest IPO ever as stocks drift higher on hopes a US–Iran deal is days away — as of ~2 PM ET

History landed on the Nasdaq Friday: SpaceX priced the largest IPO of all time at $135 a share Thursday night — raising roughly $75 billion at an initial valuation near $1.78 trillion — then opened at $150 and ran as high as $176.52, trading around $172 (up ~27% from the IPO price) in the early afternoon under ticker SPCX. The debut briefly pushed its market value above $2 trillion on a fully diluted basis (NBC News pegged the peak at more than $2.25 trillion) and made Elon Musk, on paper, the world's first trillionaire.

The broader tape gyrated around the spectacle and tilted modestly higher: as of roughly 2 PM ET the Dow was up about 0.5%, the S&P 500 up about 0.3%, and the Nasdaq Composite roughly flat to slightly higher after dipping around midday, with small caps outperforming and more than 60% of issues advancing. The other engine: reports that the US and Iran are nearing an interim peace deal — Iranian media described a 14-point draft (sanctions relief, a Hormuz reopening within 30 days), and Pakistan's prime minister said a "final, agreed upon text" had been reached — though Tehran has pushed back on parts, and no deal is signed. WTI crude fell more than 4% to below $84 intraday, an eight-week low. June consumer sentiment (UMich) improved to 48.9, well above the 46.0 expected.
