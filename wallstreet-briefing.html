The Lead
Midday report — Friday, June 12

SpaceX leaps 25% in the biggest debut in market history as peace hopes lift stocks — possible US–Iran signing Sunday

SpaceX (SPCX) opened at $150 on a first trade of 58 million shares — the IPO itself priced at $135 — and traded around $171.56 as of ~1:30 PM ET, up ~27%, after stretching as high as $176.52 (TradingView data; CNBC's banner flagged a 30% pop). That puts its fully diluted market value in the $2.0–2.1 trillion range — a $2 trillion company on day one. The offering raised a record $75 billion (555.6 million shares, ~$1.78 trillion initial valuation, with a greenshoe of ~83 million more), and the tape noticed: SPCX dollar volume of roughly $33 billion outran QQQ and SPY on the day. At these prices, Yahoo Finance reports Elon Musk becomes the world's first trillionaire.

The broad market is higher in a choppy, SpaceX-distracted tape that cooled into early afternoon: as of ~1:30 PM ET the Dow held ~+0.6% (+~312 points), the S&P 500 ~+0.3–0.5%, and the Nasdaq Composite was roughly flat after briefly dipping red around 1 PM — still consolidating Thursday's surge (the best session in two months) after President Trump called off threatened strikes on Iran. A 14-point draft US–Iran memorandum — lifting oil sanctions, releasing frozen Iranian funds, withdrawing US forces from the region and reopening the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days — could be signed in Europe as soon as this weekend (Bloomberg: Geneva floated for Sunday), though CNBC notes Tehran is pushing back on parts of the text. WTI crude fell more than 4% below $84, an eight-week low; Brent fell as much as 5% to its lowest since March before paring.

The inflation picture stays two-faced: Thursday's May PPI jumped 1.1% on the month (vs. +0.7% expected — the largest rise since March 2022, 6.5% y/y) on a 10.7% energy surge, while core PPI (ex-food and energy) rose a cooler 0.4% — the broader ex-trade core gauge ran hotter at +0.8% — with the annual core rate at 4.9% vs. 5.4% forecast. June's University of Michigan sentiment rebounded to 48.9 from May's all-time-low 44.8, with 1-year inflation expectations of 4.6% below estimates.
