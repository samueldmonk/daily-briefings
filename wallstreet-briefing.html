The Lead

Hot jobs report jolts yields; chips extend their slide as stocks trade mixed — as of ~11 AM ET

May nonfarm payrolls rose by 172,000 — roughly double expectations — with unemployment unchanged at 4.3%, and the government revised March and April job gains upward. The strong print sent Treasury yields sharply higher, with the 10-year jumping to about 4.54%, on concern the Fed may have to tighten against a hot economy.

By late morning, per TheStreet's liveblog, the S&P 500 was down about 0.6% and the Nasdaq off about 1.1% as the post-Broadcom chip selloff extended, while the Dow was roughly flat (+0.1%) and the small-cap Russell 2000 gained about 1.5% — a continuation of this week's rotation out of AI-linked tech and into the broader market.

Sources: TheStreet liveblog (Jun 5), CNBC, Trading Economics
