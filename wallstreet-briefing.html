The Lead

Futures point higher into Tuesday's open as the chip rebound holds — all eyes on Wednesday's CPI

Wall Street is set to build on Monday's chip-led bounce as Tuesday's session approaches. Ahead of the open, S&P 500 futures were up roughly 0.8%, Nasdaq 100 futures led with a gain near 1.5%, and Dow futures added about 0.4%, while oil eased. Speculative AI-economy bets that Broadcom's earnings had knocked back last week returned: Nvidia rose about 2% and Micron and Marvell each climbed more than 4% pre-market — Marvell is set to join the S&P 500 later this month. (Figures are pre-open futures and pre-market quotes; the regular session opens at 9:30 a.m. ET.)

The backdrop: Monday's rebound recouped part of last week's brutal slide, with the Nasdaq Composite closing up 0.86% at 25,929.66 and the S&P 500 up 0.30% at 7,405.73, while the Dow slipped 0.16% to 50,786.01. That followed Friday's rout, when the Nasdaq Composite plunged 4.18% — its worst day since April 2025 — as a chip selloff erased roughly $1 trillion after a hot May jobs report (+172k vs ~80k expected) fueled Fed-hike bets. The 10-year Treasury yield is holding near a two-week high around 4.54%, and the whole market is leaning toward Wednesday's May CPI (due 8:30 a.m. ET, June 10), with economists looking for headline inflation near 4.2% year over year.
