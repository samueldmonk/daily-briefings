The Lead

Stocks pare morning losses as small caps turn green and breadth broadens, even as US–Iran strikes lift oil and a 4%+ headline CPI keeps the Fed in focus — as of ~12 PM ET Wednesday, June 10

U.S. stocks trimmed a sharper morning slide by midday, with war fears and a mixed inflation report cushioned by surprisingly broad buying beneath the surface. As of roughly noon ET, the S&P 500 was down about 0.5% (off from a roughly 1% morning loss), the Dow Jones Industrial Average lower by about 0.6%, and the Nasdaq Composite off roughly 0.6% — well above its morning trough as another wave of selling in crowded AI and semiconductor names eased. The Russell 2000 bucked the majors, up about 0.4%, and breadth was strikingly positive: about 62.7% of U.S. issues were advancing, a sign the rotation out of megacap tech is broadening the rally rather than sinking the whole market. The earlier selling had widened after the United States launched fresh "self-defense" strikes on Iran overnight — Washington said Tehran had shot down a U.S. Army Apache helicopter over the Strait of Hormuz, and Iran retaliated against U.S.-linked sites in Jordan, Bahrain and Kuwait. President Trump warned that "Iran will have to pay the price," reviving fears of a wider Middle East conflict and pushing crude higher.

The session followed a May Consumer Price Index report that ran hot on the headline but offered relief at the core. Headline CPI rose 0.5% on the month and 4.2% from a year earlier — above 4% for the first time in three years and the highest annual rate since 2023 — while core CPI (ex-food and energy) rose just 0.2% month over month, below the 0.3% estimate, for a 2.9% annual rate. Energy did the damage, accounting for the bulk of the monthly increase amid the Iran war and Strait of Hormuz disruption. The hot-headline, soft-core print keeps the Federal Reserve squarely in focus before its June 16–17 meeting under new Chair Kevin Warsh, with a hold at 3.50–3.75% still widely expected even as a minority of traders price rising odds of a hike later this year. All eyes turn next to Oracle, which reports fiscal Q4 results after Wednesday's close. (Closing levels are in the Weekly Scorecard below; live quotes stream in the widgets above.)
