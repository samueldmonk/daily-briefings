Thursday, June 4 · Record Dow, Rotating Tape

Dow surges 875 points to a record as investors rotate out of AI chips after Broadcom's stumble

The Dow Jones Industrial Average jumped 874.86 points (+1.73%) to a record close of 51,561.93 Thursday, as a post-earnings plunge in Broadcom sparked a rotation out of AI-linked stocks and into health care, financials, and other non-tech corners of the market. The S&P 500 rose +0.41% to 7,584.31, while the chip-heavy Nasdaq Composite slipped -0.09% to 26,830.96, per CNBC.

Eight of the S&P 500's eleven sectors advanced, led by Health Care (+3.14%), Financials (+2.67%), and Real Estate (+1.87%). UnitedHealth led the Dow higher with a gain of more than 5%, with JPMorgan Chase up 3% and Walmart adding nearly 1%.

Movers & Drivers

Broadcom (AVGO) -15%

Fiscal Q2 revenue of $22.19B — up 48% year over year, with AI semiconductor revenue of $10.8B (+143%) — still fell short of the $22.27B analysts expected. The deeper disappointment: CEO Hock Tan didn't raise the company's $100B full-year AI chip sales target. The stock shed over $300B in market cap from Wednesday's record $495 close.

UnitedHealth (UNH) +5%

Led the Dow's record-setting advance as money rotated into health care — the day's best-performing S&P 500 sector at +3.14%.

JPMorgan Chase (JPM) +3%

Financials (+2.67%) were the second-best sector of the day as investors pared AI exposure and broadened out. Walmart also added nearly 1% to support the Dow.
