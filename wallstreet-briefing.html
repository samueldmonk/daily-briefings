Stocks snap nine-day win streak as oil and yields climb on fragile US–Iran talks

US stocks retreated from record levels Wednesday, with the S&P 500 ending a nine-session winning streak as Wall Street weighed shaky US–Iran negotiations against the still-powerful AI trade. Rising oil prices and higher Treasury yields stoked worries that the conflict could keep lifting inflation just as the Fed heads into its June 16–17 meeting. The pullback came a day after the S&P 500's first-ever close above 7,600.
