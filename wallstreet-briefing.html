The Lead
Closing report — Wednesday, June 10

Dow plunges 953 points to close near session lows as 4.2% inflation and fresh Iran strikes slam stocks; Oracle sinks after hours

The sell-off accelerated sharply into the bell after President Trump pledged further strikes on Iran ("we're going to be attacking them very hard"). At the close, the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 1.87% (−953 points, finishing near its low of the day), the S&P 500 lost 1.62% and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.98%, per CNBC and Motley Fool/Xignite closing data. The VIX jumped roughly 12%, per TheStreet.

The day's twin drivers: May CPI hit 4.2% year over year — the hottest annual reading since April 2023, with energy responsible for more than 60% of the monthly rise (gasoline +40.9% y/y) — and a renewed US–Iran exchange of strikes overnight after an Apache helicopter was downed off Oman. Notably, core CPI came in soft at +0.2% m/m, below the 0.3% estimate. Caterpillar (−6.3%) was the Dow's biggest drag; Nvidia (−3.4%) and Broadcom (−4.9%) extended the chip slide.

After the close, Oracle beat estimates with record Q4 revenue of $19.2B (+21%) and EPS of $2.11 — but the stock fell more than 7% in late trading as investors balked at plans to raise tens of billions in new capital (CNBC cited another ~$20B; capex hit $55.7B) and cloud revenue of $9.91B that landed slightly below expectations.
