The Lead
Early-afternoon report — Friday, June 12, as of ~1:30 PM ET

SpaceX joins the $2 trillion club on day one; a U.S.–Iran deal could be signed by Sunday as oil hits an eight-week low

SpaceX (SPCX) is having a historic first day on the Nasdaq. After pricing the largest IPO ever — a fixed $135 per share for 555.6 million shares, raising roughly $75 billion — the stock opened at $150 (+11%) and was trading around $171.56 (~+27%) as of early afternoon, in a day range of $150.00–$176.52 per TradingView; CNBC's live coverage flagged a pop of as much as ~30%. That puts SpaceX's market value at roughly $2 trillion fully diluted (24/7 Wall St pegged it north of $2.1 trillion at 12:30 PM ET), making it a day-one member of the $2 trillion club alongside Nvidia, Alphabet, Apple, Microsoft and Amazon — and, per TradingView, already worth more than Broadcom, Tesla or Meta. Yahoo Finance reported the debut makes Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire, with his stake worth nearly $870 billion at the IPO price alone.

The broad tape kept gyrating around the mega-listing: after a morning of swings between small gains and losses, readings as of ~1:30 PM ET showed the Dow up ~0.6% (+~312 points per 24/7 Wall St), the S&P 500 up ~0.3–0.5%, the Nasdaq Composite hovering near flat after dipping red around 1 PM, and the Russell 2000 in the green. The bigger driver: a reported 14-point draft U.S.–Iran peace agreement — including lifting oil sanctions, releasing frozen Iranian funds, a U.S. force drawdown in the region, and Tehran reopening the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days. Trump said a deal could be signed as soon as this weekend in Europe (Iranian state media said Sunday), though CNBC notes Tehran has pushed back on parts of it. WTI crude fell more than 4% to below $84, an eight-week low, and Treasury yields edged up off Thursday's slide, the 10-year at ~4.48%.

Beneath the surface, the debut is shaking out the rest of the space trade — Virgin Galactic −24%, Rocket Lab −8%, AST SpaceMobile −10% per 24/7 Wall St — while Adobe dropped ~7%. All of it lands days before Chair Kevin Warsh's June 16–17 FOMC meeting, with Thursday's hot May PPI (+6.5% y/y, the highest since November 2022) still hanging over the rate debate even as crude's slide does the Fed's work on the energy front.
