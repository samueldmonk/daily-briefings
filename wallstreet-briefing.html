The Lead

Streak snapped: Nasdaq plunges 4.2% at Friday's close as a $1 trillion chip rout and hot jobs data end the S&P 500's nine-week run

Wall Street's nine-week winning streak ended with a thud Friday. The Nasdaq Composite sank 4.18% — its worst day since April 2025 — while the S&P 500 shed 2.64% and the Dow lost 695 points (−1.35%) at the close. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index suffered its largest one-day percentage plunge since March 2020, erasing more than $1 trillion in market value, as the two-day sell-off sparked by Broadcom's AI outlook spread across the chip complex.

The macro picture piled on: May nonfarm payrolls rose 172,000, more than double the 80,000 consensus, reinforcing the view that the labor market remains resilient and dampening rate-cut hopes. The 10-year Treasury yield jumped to its highest level since May 21, and odds that the Fed hikes by year-end rose to roughly 70% per CME FedWatch — a classic "good news is bad news" session, with the S&P 500 ending its nine-week run of Friday-to-Friday gains, its longest weekly winning streak since December 2023.

Movers & Drivers
AVGO ▼

Broadcom's "merely good" guide keeps crushing chips

Broadcom posted record Q2 revenue of $22.19 billion, but its Q3 AI revenue outlook landed below Wall Street's most aggressive expectations — a fatal gap for a stock priced for perfection. Shares tumbled roughly 13% Thursday, wiping out about $286 billion in market value, and the sector-wide unwind carried into Friday's close.

SEMIS ▼

Two-day carnage across the chip complex

Over the past two sessions, Micron dropped about 17%, AMD 12.6% and Intel 9% as traders fled the AI trade. Friday alone, the Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index logged its biggest one-day percentage drop since March 2020, with more than $1 trillion in market value erased across the group.

RATES ▲ STOCKS ▼

Hot jobs report revives rate-hike fears

May payrolls of +172,000 smashed the 80,000 consensus. The 10-year Treasury yield rose more than 6 basis points to 4.544%, its highest since May 21, and CME FedWatch odds of a Fed hike by end-2026 climbed to about 70% — flipping the policy conversation from cuts to a possible hawkish pivot a week and a half before the June FOMC meeting.
