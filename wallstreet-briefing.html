The Lead

Chips crater into the weekend: Nasdaq sinks 4.2% Friday — its worst day since the early-2025 tariff turmoil — as a roughly $1 trillion semiconductor rout collides with a hot jobs report

Wall Street limped into the weekend after a violent Friday sell-off in chip stocks. The Nasdaq Composite tumbled 4.18% to 25,709.43 — its biggest single-day decline since the tariff turmoil of early 2025 — while the S&P 500 fell 2.64% to 7,383.74 and the Dow shed 695 points (−1.35%) to 50,866.78. The semiconductor slide alone wiped out roughly $1 trillion in market value as investors fled the AI trade.

The macro backdrop piled on. May nonfarm payrolls rose 172,000 — well above the roughly 80,000 consensus — reinforcing the view that the labor market remains resilient. The strong print sent Treasury yields jumping: the 10-year surged above 4.53% and the 30-year pushed back above 5%, a classic "good news is bad news" session that dampened rate-cut hopes and hardened bets the Federal Reserve stays on hold. One pocket of relief: WTI crude slid about 3% to roughly $90 a barrel as Israel–Lebanon ceasefire talks cooled energy fears. Markets are closed Saturday; the figures above reflect Friday's official close.

Movers & Drivers
SEMIS ▼

~$1 trillion erased in a chip-sector rout

The selling that started midweek accelerated Friday across the semiconductor complex. Marvell plunged about 16% and Micron roughly 13%, while Intel and AMD each lost around 11% — the heart of the Nasdaq's 4.18% drop and the exodus from AI names.

AVGO ▼

Broadcom's outlook lit the fuse

Broadcom posted record fiscal Q2 revenue of $22.19 billion but narrowly missed analyst estimates of about $22.27 billion, with a software miss and an unchanged AI outlook. For a chip group priced for perfection, "merely good" sparked the multi-day unwind — shares fell more than 7% Friday.

MEGACAP ▼

Blue-chip tech drags the tape

The damage spread well beyond pure-play chipmakers. Cisco Systems dropped 6.35%, Nvidia fell 5.93% and IBM slid 5.54%, leading the large-cap losers as the AI complex unwound across the board.

LULU ▼

Lululemon guides lower, drops ~10%

Lululemon shares plunged about 10% after the company reduced its full-year outlook, citing headwinds. Investors punished the guidance even as the broader market focused on chips — a reminder the bid for "story" stocks is thinning.
