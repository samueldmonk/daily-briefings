The Lead

Stocks close mixed after clawing back a brutal open: the Nasdaq ends down 0.97% and the S&P off 0.26%, while a defensive Dow eked out a 0.17% gain as US–Iran tensions and the chip rotation churn the tape

Wall Street closed mixed Tuesday, June 9, with the major averages recovering most of a steep intraday plunge as dip buyers stepped back into beaten-down technology names. The Nasdaq Composite finished down 0.97% at 25,678.82 — well off a session low of as much as 3.5% — while the S&P 500 slipped 0.26% (−19.27) to 7,386.46 after being down as much as 2.7%. The more defensive Dow Jones Industrial Average closed higher, up 85.12 points (+0.17%) at 50,871.13. The trigger for the morning plunge: President Trump said the US "must respond" to Iran's latest retaliation after an attack on a US helicopter, reviving worries that a move could reignite the regional conflict and reigniting the rotation out of crowded AI and chip names. Chipmakers Micron, Nvidia and Broadcom reversed Monday's bounce and turned lower; the iShares Semiconductor ETF (SOXX) shed more than 3% after a 6% rebound a day earlier.

Breadth was far less grim than the megacap-heavy indices: more than 1,300 NYSE issues advanced against roughly 550 declining, and small caps bucked the trend — the Russell 2000 held a gain of about half a percent as money rotated toward rate-sensitive corners. Homebuilders led, with the SPDR S&P Homebuilders ETF (XHB) up about 2.5% and names like LGI Homes (+5.2%) and Century Communities (+4.7%) climbing as a roughly 2% drop in oil eased inflation expectations. Traders now turn to Wednesday's May CPI (8:30 AM ET, June 10), with consensus near 4.2–4.3% year over year on the headline; a hot print would harden the case that the Fed could be forced to hike later this year. The backdrop: Monday's session was a chip-led rebound, with the Nasdaq Composite closing up 0.86% at 25,929.66 and the S&P 500 up 0.30% at 7,405.73, while the Dow slipped 0.16% to 50,786.01 — itself a partial recovery from Friday's rout, when the Nasdaq Composite fell 4.18% (its worst day since April 2025) as a chip selloff erased roughly $1 trillion after a hot May jobs report.

Movers & Drivers (June 9 — at the close)

Micron, Nvidia & Broadcom ▼

Chipmakers reversed Monday's bounce and closed lower, leading the slide as the rotation out of AI and semiconductor names resumed; the iShares Semiconductor ETF (SOXX) shed more than 3% after a 6% rebound a day earlier.

Nvidia, Oracle & AMD ▼ ~1–3%

The AI-infrastructure cohort fell between roughly 1% and 3% on lingering skepticism that part of the chip and datacenter rally has been overblown.

Apple ▼ ~3%

Lower in the wake of its developer conference — Tim Cook's last as CEO — where it unveiled a revamped "Siri AI"; reports said the new Siri won't launch in the EU over Brussels antitrust rules.

Qualcomm, Marvell & Workday ▼ ~4%

Among the steepest decliners during the session as the semiconductor cohort reversed; Qualcomm and Marvell each fell roughly 4% and Workday about 4%.

LGI Homes & Century Communities ▲ ~5.2% / 4.7%

Homebuilders led the rally as lower oil and falling inflation expectations lifted rate-sensitive housing names; the SPDR S&P Homebuilders ETF (XHB) rose about 2.5%.

J.M. Smucker ▲

Higher after results, though the packaged-foods maker warned of a full-year sales decline; a rare green spot as defensive and rate-sensitive names outperformed tech.
