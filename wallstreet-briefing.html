The Lead

Chips snap back: Nasdaq and S&P 500 rebound from Friday's rout while the Dow slips (June 8 close)

Wall Street steadied Monday, recouping part of last week's brutal slide as the semiconductor selloff reversed. The tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite led, closing up 0.86% at 25,929.66, and the S&P 500 added 0.30% to 7,405.73. The Dow bucked the trend, slipping 80.77 points (-0.16%) to 50,786.01 as its less tech-heavy makeup lagged the chip bounce. Stocks firmed on signs of a cautious de-escalation in the Middle East and a halt to the chip rout: Iran said it had ended its operation against Israel after weekend strikes, and as tensions cooled through the session crude oil gave back most of its early surge — WTI, which had spiked above $94 a barrel overnight on the strikes, faded back toward the $90–91 level.

Semiconductors did the heavy lifting. Marvell Technology jumped about 13% after S&P Dow Jones Indices said the stock will join the S&P 500 (effective June 22); Intel surged roughly 10% after reports that Alphabet's Google ordered more than three million tensor processing units from the chipmaker for 2028; Micron rebounded about 10% after sliding 13% Friday; and Cerebras Systems soared more than 17% on a wave of bullish Wall Street initiations. Corning climbed on a multibillion-dollar Amazon optical-fiber deal for data centers, and Nvidia added roughly 2%. The bounce follows Friday's rout, when the Nasdaq Composite plunged 4.18% — its worst day since April 2025 — and a chip selloff erased roughly $1 trillion in value after a hot May jobs report (+172k vs ~80k expected) fueled Fed-hike bets. The 10-year Treasury yield held near a two-week high around 4.57%, and traders now look ahead to this week's CPI print.
