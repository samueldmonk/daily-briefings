The Lead

Hot jobs report rattles the rally — Nasdaq and Russell 2000 sink nearly 3% as of ~3 PM ET

A much stronger-than-expected May employment report knocked stocks broadly lower Friday. Payrolls grew by 172,000 — roughly double the ~80,000 economists expected — while unemployment held at 4.3%, a classic "good news is bad news" session: the hot print revived fears the Federal Reserve will keep rates elevated (or even hike) to tame a resilient economy. The benchmark 10-year Treasury yield jumped to 4.54% after the release.

By midday the selling had deepened: as of 12:20 PM ET the S&P 500 was down 1.60%, the Nasdaq Composite off 2.65% and the Dow down 0.74%, per Yahoo Finance — and by mid-afternoon TheStreet's liveblog had the Nasdaq and Russell 2000 sinking nearly 3%, with the Russell off about 2.4% at last check. CNBC notes the S&P 500's nine-week winning streak is on track to end. AI and chip bellwethers led the decline for a second straight day following Broadcom's earnings.

Movers & Drivers
CHIPS / AI

Semis lead the slide ▼

Nvidia, AMD and Micron all dropped on fresh doubts around lofty AI valuations, per Yahoo Finance. In early trading Micron fell 6.3%, Broadcom slipped 3.8% and Marvell lost 8% as the sector sold off for a second day.

EARNINGS

Broadcom extends post-earnings drop ▼

Broadcom posted record Q2 revenue of $22.19B but narrowly missed analyst estimates (~$22.27B expected), with a software miss and an unchanged AI outlook. The print sparked Thursday's rotation out of AI names — and the stock kept falling Friday.

CRYPTO

Bitcoin's worst week since February ▼

Bitcoin fell 3.52% to $61,914.50 in premarket trading Friday, extending its decline amid a record streak of ETF outflows and on pace for its worst week since February, per Yahoo Finance.
