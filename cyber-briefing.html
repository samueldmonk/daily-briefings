Top Story

Check Point VPN zero-day (CVE-2026-50751) exploited to deploy ransomware — CISA orders federal agencies to patch in just three days

CVE-2026-50751, a critical authentication-bypass flaw in Check Point Remote Access VPN, Mobile Access / SSL VPN and Spark Firewall gateways configured to use the deprecated IKEv1 key exchange, is being actively exploited in the wild. Rated CVSS 9.3, the bug lets an unauthenticated remote attacker establish a VPN connection without a valid password — no privileges, no user interaction, just network reach to an exposed gateway. Check Point shipped an emergency hotfix on Monday, June 8, flagging exploitation that began on May 7 and surged over the weekend; in at least one case the access led to post-compromise activity tied to a Qilin ransomware affiliate.

CISA added the flaw to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog the same day (June 8) and — in a rare departure from the standard three-week window under BOD 22-01 — set an emergency three-day remediation deadline of June 11, 2026 for federal civilian agencies, reflecting the active ransomware exploitation. Organizations running affected, IKEv1-configured Check Point gateways should treat the hotfix as urgent. The disclosure caps a busy exploitation cycle: in the prior days CISA also flagged an actively exploited SolarWinds Serv-U denial-of-service flaw (CVE-2026-28318) and a BerriAI LiteLLM command-injection bug (CVE-2026-42271).

Breaches & Incidents
Zero-day

Check Point VPN — Qilin ransomware

Exploitation of CVE-2026-50751 has been limited to a few dozen targeted organizations globally; in at least one case the post-compromise activity was tied to a Qilin ransomware affiliate. Earliest observed exploitation dates to May 7.

Breach

Baker Distributing — ShinyHunters

Disclosed June 8: ShinyHunters compromised over 260,000 Salesforce records plus corporate data across various SharePoint sites at the HVAC/refrigeration distributor.

Breach

Instructure / Canvas — 275M records

ShinyHunters claims roughly 275M records (names, emails, student IDs, messages) and 3.65TB across ~8,800 schools tied to the Canvas LMS; Instructure reached a ransom agreement on May 11 after a second intrusion.

Ransomware

"The Gentlemen" — busy leak site

Among June 9 victims posted by the crew: Central Arkansas Pediatrics, Danzo Group, Integrated Distribution, IP Rings (India) and creative studio Empty (Spain).

Leak sites

Fresh claims posted June 9

New victims surfaced across extortion sites: 3i Infotech (Morpheus), Aegle Aviation (RansomHouse), Asia Strategic Holdings and Goldlion (NightSpire), and HRC Sicherheitsdienste (Akira).

Exploited CVE

SolarWinds Serv-U — DoS

CVE-2026-28318 (CVSS 7.5) lets an unauthenticated attacker crash the Serv-U file-transfer service via a crafted HTTP request; CISA added it to KEV (June 5) with 12,000+ servers exposed online per Shodan.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 8 (emergency): CVE-2026-50751 (Check Point Security Gateway improper authentication) added to the KEV catalog. CISA shortened the usual window to a three-day deadline of June 11, 2026 given active ransomware exploitation — patch IKEv1-configured gateways immediately.
  • June 8: CVE-2026-42271 (BerriAI LiteLLM command injection) added to the KEV catalog on evidence of active exploitation. Under BOD 22-01's standard three-week window, the federal remediation deadline lands around June 29, 2026.
  • June 3: CVE-2026-45247 (Mirasvit Full Page Cache Warmer, deserialization of untrusted data) added to the catalog. The three-week window puts the federal deadline around June 24, 2026.
  • BOD 22-01 gives federal civilian agencies a standard three-week window from a CVE's KEV addition date to remediate or stop using the affected product; CISA can shorten that window for emergencies, as it did for the Check Point flaw.
  • June 5: CVE-2026-28318 (SolarWinds Serv-U uncontrolled resource consumption / DoS, CVSS 7.5) added to the KEV catalog with a federal remediation deadline of June 19, 2026; fixed in Serv-U 15.5.4 HF1, with Shodan showing 12,000+ exposed servers.
