ShinyHunters rode an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day for two weeks — universities hit hardest

Oracle rushed out an out-of-band security alert for CVE-2026-35273, a CVSS 9.8 remote-code-execution flaw in PeopleSoft Enterprise PeopleTools (8.61/8.62) that lets an unauthenticated attacker with HTTP access fully take over the system. Google's Mandiant, which tracks the actors as UNC6240 (activity linked to ShinyHunters), dates exploitation from May 27 to June 9 — meaning the bug was a zero-day for the entire campaign, with Oracle's advisory landing June 10.

The crew claims to have hit roughly 300 PeopleSoft instances across 100+ organizations, stealing data and demanding payment to keep it private. About 68% of victims are in higher education, most in the US — PeopleSoft remains the back office for university HR and student records. If you run PeopleTools 8.61 or 8.62, apply Oracle's emergency mitigation now and hunt for compromise back to late May.

"GreatXML" BitLocker bypass drops — Defender Offline Scan is the way in

Researcher "Chaotic Eclipse" released a new Windows BitLocker bypass dubbed GreatXML: "If you ever attempted to use Windows Defender Offline Scan, you're automatically vulnerable to a BitLocker bypass." It lands days after the separate, still-unpatched "RoguePlanet" Defender privilege-escalation zero-day from "Nightmare Eclipse" — a rough week for Windows security tooling.
