Top Story

Self-replicating "Miasma" worm reaches Microsoft: GitHub disables 73 repos across four of the company's organizations

The Miasma supply-chain worm — a self-propagating credential stealer that has been mutating across npm and PyPI for weeks — hit its highest-profile target yet on June 5, when GitHub disabled 73 repositories across four Microsoft organizations (Azure, Azure-Samples, Microsoft and MicrosoftDocs) after a malicious commit was pushed to the Azure/durabletask repo using a previously compromised contributor account.

What makes Miasma notable is its targeting of AI coding workflows: the commit planted configuration files and a roughly 4.3 MB payload runner wired to execute automatically through five developer tools — Claude Code, Gemini CLI, Cursor, VS Code and the npm test script — harvesting credentials the moment a developer opens the repo. Researchers assess Miasma as a variant of the "Mini Shai-Hulud" worm released in mid-May; it has previously compromised Red Hat packages and hit TanStack, Mistral AI and UiPath. The malware creates a public repo in each victim's account ("Miasma: The Spreading Blight") and commits stolen credential JSON to randomized directories — the mechanism that lets it spread account to account.

Breaches & Incidents
BREACH

Adobe: 13M support tickets exposed

A threat actor using the handle "Mr. Racoon" claims a breach of Adobe, exposing roughly 13 million customer support tickets along with about 15,000 employee records, internal documents and bug-bounty submissions. The figures are the attacker's claims and have not been independently confirmed.

RANSOMWARE

Iron Mountain hit by Everest

The Everest ransomware gang claims to have stolen 1.4 TB of internal documents and client personal information from records-management giant Iron Mountain — a high-value target given the sensitivity of the data it stores for enterprise and government clients.

RANSOMWARE

Brightspeed: 1M+ customers claimed

The Crimson Collective extortion crew claims to have stolen data on more than a million customers of telecom provider Brightspeed. As with the other leak-site listings, the scope reflects the attacker's claims pending confirmation from the company.

EXTORTION

Fresh leak-site listings (June 5)

Trackers logged a cluster of newly listed victims on June 5, including private-aviation firm Avcon Jet (Qilin), Corley Manufacturing and the Dallis Law Firm (Play), food maker Don Don (Qilin) and Family Medical Associates of Raleigh — a reminder that data-extortion volume remains high across sectors.
