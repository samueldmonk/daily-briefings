Top Story

Three actively exploited enterprise flaws stack up into the weekend — SolarWinds Serv-U, an unpatched Cisco SD-WAN root bug and a Microsoft Exchange OWA zero-day — with June's Patch Tuesday now days away

Defenders head into the new week juggling a cluster of in-the-wild exploits. On June 5, CISA added SolarWinds Serv-U vulnerability CVE-2026-28318 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog after confirmation that attackers are crashing the widely deployed file-transfer servers — an unauthenticated denial-of-service that needs only a single crafted HTTP request. Federal civilian agencies were ordered to patch by June 19. Alongside it, Cisco's PSIRT says CVE-2026-20245 in Catalyst SD-WAN Manager has been exploited in limited real-world environments to run arbitrary commands as root, with no patch or workaround yet available.

Still unresolved from May: Microsoft's on-prem Exchange OWA spoofing zero-day CVE-2026-42897, confirmed under active exploitation and added to CISA KEV on May 15, remains without a full fix — defenders are leaning on the auto-on Exchange Emergency Mitigation Service and watching whether Microsoft's June 9 Patch Tuesday delivers a permanent patch. All of it lands against relentless extortion activity: trackers logged a fresh cluster of leak-site victims on June 5, and the ShinyHunters dump of roughly 234 GB of DentaQuest data affecting about 2.6 million people remains among the year's largest health-sector leaks.

Breaches & Incidents
HEALTHCARE

DentaQuest leak hits 2.6 million

ShinyHunters published roughly 234 GB of data stolen from DentaQuest, a Sun Life dental/vision benefits administrator, after a failed extortion attempt. Exposed records reportedly include names, dates of birth, addresses, government IDs, insurance details and Medicaid IDs — fueling phishing and identity-theft risk.

EXTORTION

Fresh leak-site listings (June 5)

Trackers logged a cluster of newly listed victims on June 5, including private-aviation firm Avcon Jet, Corley Manufacturing, the Dallis Law Firm, Slovenian food maker Don Don and Family Medical Associates of Raleigh — attributed to crews including Qilin and Play. Data-extortion volume remains high across sectors.

PRIVACY

Instagram reset-flow logic bug

Reporting on June 6 described a logic flaw in Instagram's web-based password-reset flow that exposed unredacted email addresses and phone numbers tied to user accounts — the latest in a run of 2026 incidents leaking sensitive personal identifiers.

FRAUD

World Cup 2026 fraud wave

Researchers and the FBI are warning of a surge of FIFA-themed fraud targeting World Cup 2026 fans: thousands of lookalike FIFA domains, banking malware hidden inside pirate streaming apps, and phishing kits cloning FIFA's login page ahead of the tournament.

CVE-2026-28318 7.5 SolarWinds Serv-U (file transfer) Unauthenticated denial-of-service (uncontrolled resource consumption, CWE-400). A crafted HTTP POST with a Content-Encoding: deflate header crashes the Serv-U service without credentials. Actively exploited; added to CISA KEV June 5. Fixed in Serv-U 15.5.4 Hotfix 1.
CVE-2026-20245 7.8 Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager Improper input validation in the CLI lets an authenticated attacker with netadmin rights upload a crafted file and execute arbitrary commands as root. Confirmed exploited in limited attacks; no patch or workaround yet — Cisco plans a future fix.
CVE-2026-42897 8.1 Microsoft Exchange Server (2016 / 2019 / SE) OWA spoofing flaw rooted in cross-site scripting; a crafted email opened in Outlook Web Access can run arbitrary JavaScript in the browser. Actively exploited; disclosed May 14, added to CISA KEV May 15. No full patch yet — Exchange Emergency Mitigation Service mitigates by default; June 9 Patch Tuesday is expected to address it.
CVE-2026-45247 9.8 Mirasvit Full Page Cache Warmer (Magento) Deserialization of untrusted data allows unauthenticated RCE via a crafted serialized PHP object in the CacheWarmer cookie. Actively exploited; added to CISA KEV June 3. Affects versions before 1.11.12; patched May 25.
CVE-2026-3300 9.8 Everest Forms Pro (WordPress plugin) Remote code-execution flaw under active exploitation, impacting all versions up to and including 1.9.12. Site owners should update immediately and audit for unexpected file writes.
CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 5 addition: CVE-2026-28318 (SolarWinds Serv-U — unauthenticated DoS) was added on evidence of active exploitation; CISA set a remediation deadline of June 19, 2026 for federal civilian agencies. Fix: upgrade to Serv-U 15.5.4 Hotfix 1.
  • June 3 addition: CVE-2026-45247 (Mirasvit Full Page Cache Warmer — deserialization enabling unauthenticated RCE on Magento) carries a standard three-week window, due by approximately June 24.
  • June 2 additions: CVE-2022-0492 (Linux kernel improper authentication, enabling privilege escalation / container escape) and CVE-2025-48595 (Android Framework integer overflow allowing code execution), due by approximately June 23.
  • Looking ahead — June 9 Patch Tuesday: defenders are watching whether Microsoft's June updates finally deliver a full fix for the actively exploited Exchange OWA zero-day (CVE-2026-42897), which still ships only the auto-on Emergency Mitigation Service.
  • Reminder: BOD 22-01 generally gives federal civilian agencies a three-week remediation window from a CVE's KEV-addition date; CISA sets the specific due date per entry, so always confirm the catalog deadline.
