Top Story

ShinyHunters claims a ~275 million-record haul from the education sector via Canvas instances

The extortion crew ShinyHunters says it stole roughly 275 million records tied to students, teachers and staff in what looks like one of 2026's largest education-sector breaches. The group shared a list of 8,809 school districts, universities and online-learning platforms with BleepingComputer, claiming their Canvas learning-management instances were impacted, with per-institution record counts ranging from tens of thousands to several million.

The disclosure lands amid a busy exploitation cycle. Cisco is warning that a Catalyst SD-WAN Manager zero-day (CVE-2026-20245) is under active attack with no patch yet available, attackers are hammering a critical WordPress plugin flaw (CVE-2026-3300, CVSS 9.8), and CISA added an actively exploited SolarWinds Serv-U denial-of-service bug (CVE-2026-28318) to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 5. The FBI, separately, is flagging a wave of World Cup 2026-themed fraud aimed at fans.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 5: CVE-2026-28318 (SolarWinds Serv-U, uncontrolled resource consumption / DoS) added to the KEV catalog — the standard BOD 22-01 three-week window puts the federal remediation deadline around June 26. Apply Serv-U 15.5.4 Hotfix 1.
  • Active, unpatched: Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager (CVE-2026-20245) is being exploited for root command execution with no fix yet — follow Cisco's upgrade guidance in the CVE-2026-20182 advisory.
  • Earlier KEV items: CVE-2026-41091 (Microsoft Defender, SYSTEM privileges) carried a June 3 remediation deadline that has now passed; an actively exploited Android Framework integer-overflow flaw was flagged via the June 1 Android security bulletin.
  • BOD 22-01 gives federal civilian agencies a standard three-week window from a CVE's KEV addition date; prioritize patching or mitigating the items above.
