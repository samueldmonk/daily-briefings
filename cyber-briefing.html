Europol cuts ransomware gangs off from "AudiA6," a €336M crypto-laundering pipeline

A coordinated international operation executed June 10 dismantled AudiA6, a professional crypto-mixing service that Europol says laundered more than €336 million (~$389M) for ransomware crews and other cybercriminal networks since 2021 — and is linked to more than 15 international cybercrime investigations.

Two alleged administrators — of Ukrainian and Russian nationality — were arrested in Georgia, where authorities also seized over 80 vehicles and multiple properties; 25 domains were seized and 30+ servers taken offline, with €692,000 in crypto frozen and €86,000 seized outright. Marketed on underground forums, the service returned "cleaned" funds within about an hour for a 3–10% commission, and investigators recovered 6,000+ KYC records tied to money-mule accounts.

The U.S. DOJ charged the two men arrested — Ruslan Igorevich Tkachuk, 37, and Alexander Vladimirovich Ledenev, 25 — with money-laundering conspiracy counts carrying up to 20 years. Europol called the takedown the loss of "a key financial pipeline used to wash hundreds of millions in illicit profits."

ShinyHunters' PeopleSoft zero-day spree: 100+ orgs hit

Mandiant (tracking the actor as UNC6240) says roughly 300 Oracle PeopleSoft instances across 100+ organizations were compromised between May 27 and June 9 via CVE-2026-35273 — about 68% of them universities, mostly in the US. Oracle pushed an out-of-band Security Alert on June 10. Details in the Vulnerability Watch below.

ServiceNow: "breach" now attributed to researcher activity

In a June 12 advisory update, ServiceNow said the suspicious June 2–3 activity against the unauthenticated REST endpoint it patched June 5 is now believed to be security researchers and customers testing — two researchers filed a bug-bounty report June 7. Users still allege the flaw was known since April; no confirmed criminal breach.

New victims posted June 12 include Hong Kong Parkview and Sweden's Areco Steel (DragonForce), Highwoods Properties ("TheGentlemen"), plus Astec Valves & Fittings, South Korea's Bitek System and Turkey's Çekok Gıda across DragonForce/Qilin-linked sites. Patch fallout

Record June Patch Tuesday: ~200 CVEs, three public zero-days

Microsoft's June 9 release was its largest ever (~200 CVEs, 33 critical), including three publicly disclosed — none known exploited — zero-days: a CTFMON privilege escalation, an HTTP/2 "Bomb" DoS, and a BitLocker "YellowKey" bypass. It also shipped the permanent fix for the May-exploited Exchange OWA flaw (below).

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 9 additions — CVE-2026-7473 (Arista EOS), CVE-2026-11645 (Chromium V8 out-of-bounds R/W, CVSS 8.8 — one of 74 Chrome fixes), and CVE-2026-20245 (Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager, authenticated privilege escalation). Standard BOD 22-01 three-week window: remediation due ~June 30.
  • June 8 additions — CVE-2026-50751 (Check Point) carried an explicit, shortened emergency 3-day deadline of June 11 — now passed; agencies still exposed are out of compliance. CVE-2026-42271 (BerriAI LiteLLM command injection) follows the standard window, due ~June 29.
  • PeopleSoft watch — despite active mass exploitation, CVE-2026-35273 had not been added to the KEV catalog as of this edition (latest visible additions: June 9). No federal deadline exists yet; treat Oracle's June 10 alert as the action trigger regardless.
  • Reminder — BOD 22-01 gives federal civilian agencies three weeks from a CVE's KEV addition date unless CISA explicitly states otherwise.
