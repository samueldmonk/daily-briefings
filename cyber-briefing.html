Top Story

ServiceNow discloses security incident: unauthenticated API let attackers query customer instance data

ServiceNow is warning customers of a security incident after attackers exploited an unauthenticated access flaw in a REST API endpoint to query data from customer instances. The observed malicious activity traces back to June 2–3, and the company applied a security update to hosted customer instances on June 5 that restricts the endpoint to authenticated users only.

Administrators discussing the incident say the issue appears tied to the '/api/now/related_list_edit/create' endpoint, which was reportedly configured with 'requires_authentication=false'. ServiceNow says the issue primarily impacts customers on the Australia platform release or older releases with certain configuration changes. The company has not said exactly what data was accessed, but affected instances can hold IT support tickets, employee records, internal documentation, asset inventories, and security incident reports. Admins are sharing indicators of compromise — including API requests from IP 51.159.98.241 — and urging log reviews for requests to the vulnerable endpoint.

The disclosure hit the company's stock too: ServiceNow shares fell more than 6% in Wednesday's session.

Breaches & Incidents
Zero-Day

"RoguePlanet" Defender zero-day exploit dropped hours after Patch Tuesday

Researcher "Nightmare Eclipse" publicly released an unpatched Microsoft Defender exploit that abuses a race condition to spawn a SYSTEM-level command prompt on fully patched Windows 10/11 — hours after Microsoft fixed two of the researcher's previously disclosed flaws. The local privilege-escalation exploit is hit-or-miss (race-condition dependent), and the release continues the researcher's running dispute with Microsoft over disclosure and bug-bounty practices.

Government

France's Tchap government messenger breached via hijacked account

French authorities warned that hackers used a hijacked user account to breach Tchap, the French government's encrypted messaging platform used by officials and civil servants.

Supply Chain

Open-source compromises ripple downstream to OpenAI and Vercel

TechCrunch's mid-year breach roundup highlights compromises of major open-source projects — including Aqua Security's Trivy, Bitwarden, and Checkmarx — that let hackers steal passwords and credentials from users, enabling downstream compromises at companies including OpenAI and Vercel.

Ransomware

Brain Cipher claims 350+ GB from Australian regional newspaper

The Brain Cipher ransomware group claimed an attack on The Adviser, a regional Victorian newspaper, saying it stole more than 350 GB of data. The episode fits 2026's broader trend: extortion-first operations and "EDR killer" tooling to neutralize endpoint defenses before payload execution.
