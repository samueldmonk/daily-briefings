Top Story

No patch yet: Cisco warns of an actively exploited root-level flaw in Catalyst SD-WAN Manager

Cisco has disclosed that CVE-2026-20245, a high-severity vulnerability in the CLI of Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager, is being exploited in the wild — and there is no patch or workaround available yet. The flaw (CVSS 7.8) stems from insufficient validation of user-supplied input and lets an authenticated local attacker with netadmin privileges upload a crafted file to execute arbitrary commands as root.

Cisco said it has observed limited cases where exploitation resulted in a configuration change being pushed down to edge devices, and noted the bug affects all deployment types — on-prem, Cloud-Pro, Cisco-managed cloud and FedRAMP. The flaw was discovered and reported by Google Mandiant researchers. With a fix still pending, defenders running SD-WAN Manager should tighten access controls and watch for unexpected configuration pushes.

Breaches & Incidents
BREACH

DentaQuest: 2.6M accounts, ShinyHunters leak

Dental benefits administrator DentaQuest confirmed on June 2 that its network was breached. Extortion group ShinyHunters listed the company and claimed to have stolen more than 234 GB of data; reporting puts the exposure at roughly 2.6 million accounts, including names, addresses, dates of birth, government IDs and health-insurance information.

SUPPLY CHAIN

IronWorm worms its way into npm

A Rust-based, self-propagating infostealer dubbed IronWorm infected 36 packages on npm, targeting AWS, OpenAI, Anthropic and npm credentials, vault configs, SSH keys and crypto-wallet files. It abuses npm's Trusted Publishing workflow to spread; researchers say it was caught early before reaching popular packages.

FRAUD

World Cup 2026 fraud wave

Researchers and the FBI are warning of a surge of FIFA-themed fraud targeting World Cup 2026 fans — thousands of lookalike FIFA domains, banking malware hidden inside pirate streaming apps, and operations cloning FIFA's login page.

BREACH

Aflac: 22.6M notified over 2025 hack

Insurer Aflac is notifying more than 22.6 million customers, beneficiaries, employees and agents that the June 2025 breach by the Scattered Spider group exposed their data — including names, Social Security numbers, dates of birth and health-insurance details. The intrusion relied on social engineering rather than malware.
