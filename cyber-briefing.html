Top Story

ShinyHunters claims a ~275 million-record haul from the education sector via Canvas instances

The extortion crew ShinyHunters says it stole roughly 275 million records tied to students, teachers and staff in what looks like one of 2026's largest education-sector breaches. The group claims the Canvas learning-management instances run by Instructure were impacted; Instructure has confirmed it is investigating a cyber incident affecting its cloud-hosted environment.

The disclosure lands amid a busy exploitation cycle. A Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager zero-day (CVE-2026-20245, CVSS 7.8) is under active attack with no patch yet available; CISA added an actively exploited SolarWinds Serv-U denial-of-service bug (CVE-2026-28318) to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 5; insurer Aflac disclosed a June intrusion exposing data on roughly 22.7 million people; and the FBI is flagging a wave of World Cup 2026-themed fraud aimed at fans ahead of the June 11 kickoff.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 5: CVE-2026-28318 (SolarWinds Serv-U, uncontrolled resource consumption / DoS) added to the KEV catalog after confirmed active exploitation. CISA set the federal remediation deadline at June 19, 2026; apply Serv-U 15.5.4 Hotfix 1.
  • June 3: CVE-2026-45247 (Mirasvit Full Page Cache Warmer, deserialization of untrusted data) added — the standard BOD 22-01 three-week window puts the federal deadline around June 24.
  • Active, no patch: CVE-2026-20245 (Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager) is being exploited in the wild with no fix available — mitigate by tightly restricting netadmin access and watching for unauthorized configuration pushes.
  • Recently added: CVE-2025-48595 (Android Framework integer overflow) enables code execution and local privilege escalation; apply current Android security updates.
  • BOD 22-01 gives federal civilian agencies a standard three-week window from a CVE's KEV addition date to remediate or stop using the affected product.
