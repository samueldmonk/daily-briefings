Top Story
Emergency KEV deadline — due tomorrow

Federal clock runs out Thursday on exploited Check Point VPN bypass

CISA's rare three-day emergency remediation deadline for CVE-2026-50751 expires tomorrow, June 11 — a deliberate exception to the standard three-week BOD 22-01 window. The CVSS 9.3 improper-authentication flaw in Check Point's IKEv1-configured Remote Access VPN, Mobile Access and Spark firewall deployments lets an unauthenticated attacker bypass user authentication and establish a VPN session without a valid password.

Exploitation has been traced back to around May 7, with post-compromise activity tied to a Qilin ransomware affiliate. Check Point shipped hotfixes on June 8, the same day CISA added the bug to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. Federal agencies — and any defender treating KEV as a to-do list — have hours, not weeks, to patch or pull IKEv1 remote access.

ServiceNow: unauthenticated API abused to query customer instances

ServiceNow disclosed that attackers exploited an unauthenticated REST endpoint (/api/now/related_list_edit/create) on June 2–3 to query data from customer instance tables. A fix went to hosted instances June 5. Exposure centers on the Australia platform release and older releases with certain config changes; the company is still deciding whether to issue a CVE. Shares fell ~6% Wednesday on the disclosure.

zero-dayunpatched

"RoguePlanet" Defender zero-day drops right after Patch Tuesday

Researcher "Nightmare Eclipse" publicly released an exploit for an unpatched Microsoft Defender race condition hours after June's record Patch Tuesday. The local privilege-escalation bug yields SYSTEM on fully patched Windows 10/11. No official CVE or fix yet; no confirmed in-the-wild exploitation beyond the public release — but the window is open.

data leak

ShinyHunters publishes Baker Distributing data

After claiming a May 23 attack on the HVAC/refrigeration distributor and threatening to leak 260,000+ Salesforce records, ShinyHunters published data from Baker's Salesforce and SharePoint environments in early June. Have I Been Pwned indexed 102,935 accounts — emails, names, addresses, phone numbers and contractor support tickets; largely corporate contact data of limited sensitivity, per Cybernews.

research

PoC AI worm navigates networks with a local LLM

Researchers built and tested a proof-of-concept worm that uses a locally hosted large language model to reason its way through a network and replicate. Across 15 isolated runs on a vulnerable 33-host network it found an average of 31.3 vulnerabilities and gained elevated access on 23.1 hosts — a preview of autonomous tradecraft defenders may soon face.
