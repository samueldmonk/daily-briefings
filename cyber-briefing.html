"Agentjacking": a fake bug report can hijack your AI coding agent — and nothing in the stack catches it

Researchers at Tenet Security disclosed a new attack class they call Agentjacking that turns a public error-tracking credential into remote code execution on developer machines — with no malware, no stolen password, and no breach of the target. The trick: an attacker submits a crafted error event to a project's Sentry instance (anyone holding the DSN can post events), and when an AI coding agent such as Claude Code or Cursor reads that error through the Sentry MCP server, it treats the attacker-controlled payload as trusted diagnostic output and follows the embedded "remediation steps," executing the attacker's code.

What makes it dangerous is that every step is technically authorized. Tenet calls it the "Authorized Intent Chain": because the prevailing security model is built to flag unauthorized behavior, an attack containing none sails past EDR, WAF, IAM, VPN, Cloudflare and firewalls. The Cloud Security Alliance published a corroborating research note on June 12 framing it as MCP-injection.

Tenet says it reported the issue to Sentry on June 3; Sentry acknowledged it the same day but declined to fix it at the root, describing the attack class as "technically not defensible" at the platform level. The practical takeaway: treat third-party data surfaced to AI agents (error reports, tickets, issues) as untrusted input, and don't let agents act on it without a human in the loop.

Europol dismantles "AudiA6," ransomware's €336M laundromat

An 11-nation operation executed June 10 — led by the U.S. Secret Service and IRS Criminal Investigation with Polish police — seized 25 domains and 30+ servers behind a laundering service that washed ~10,333 BTC (€336M / ~$389M historically) for ransomware crews since 2021, charging 3–10% commissions. Two alleged admins were arrested in Georgia; the DOJ charged Ruslan Igorevich Tkachuk, 37, and Alexander Vladimirovich Ledenev, 25, with counts carrying up to 20 years. 80+ vehicles confiscated; €692k in crypto frozen.

UpdateDe-escalation

ServiceNow: "breach" now attributed to researcher activity

In a June 12 advisory update, ServiceNow said the suspicious June 2–3 activity against the unauthenticated REST endpoint it patched June 5 is now believed to be security researchers and customers testing — two researchers filed a bug-bounty report June 7. Users still allege the flaw was known since April; no confirmed criminal breach.

Zero-dayKEV

PeopleSoft zero-day: ShinyHunters hit 100+ orgs, now on the KEV clock

Mandiant and Google Threat Intelligence (tracking the actor as UNC6240, linked to ShinyHunters) say roughly 300 PeopleSoft instances across 100+ organizations were compromised between May 27 and June 9 via CVE-2026-35273 — about 68% higher-education, mostly US. Oracle shipped an out-of-band alert June 10; CISA added the bug to KEV June 12 with a July 3 federal deadline.

Law enforcementProcess

Conti guilty plea; Maine pulls its breach portal offline

A Ukrainian national extradited to the U.S. last year pleaded guilty to conspiracy charges tied to the Conti ransomware operation. Separately, Maine took its public data-breach reporting portal offline on June 13 after fraudulent breach disclosures were published to the state site, and is reviewing its submission procedures to prevent abuse.
