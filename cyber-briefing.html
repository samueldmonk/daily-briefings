Top Story

ShinyHunters dumps DentaQuest: 234 GB of data exposing 2.6 million people in one of 2026's largest health-sector leaks

The digital-extortion group ShinyHunters has published roughly 234 gigabytes of data it says was stolen from DentaQuest, a Sun Life subsidiary and one of the largest U.S. dental and vision benefits administrators, serving Medicaid, Medicare Advantage and commercial members across all 50 states. DentaQuest confirmed the breach on June 2; the leak affects approximately 2.6 million individuals and ranks among the largest health-sector incidents reported so far this year.

Exposed records reportedly include names, dates of birth, home and email addresses, phone numbers, government-issued IDs, health-insurance information and Medicaid IDs — a potent mix for identity theft and targeted fraud. According to reporting, the data was published after ShinyHunters failed to extort a payment, with the trove posted to the group's dark-web leak site. DentaQuest is now the subject of breach investigations and consumer-notification efforts.

RANSOMWARE

Abyss hits German county administration

The Limburg-Weilburg county administration in Germany was struck by the Abyss ransomware group, which claims to have stolen and encrypted roughly 132 GB of data — the latest in a steady run of attacks on local-government and public-sector targets.

FRAUD

World Cup 2026 fraud wave

Researchers and the FBI are warning of a surge of FIFA-themed fraud targeting World Cup 2026 fans: thousands of lookalike FIFA domains, banking malware hidden inside pirate streaming apps, and phishing kits cloning FIFA's login page ahead of the tournament.

HEALTHCARE

DentaQuest fallout widens

Beyond the headline 2.6 million figure, the DentaQuest leak has triggered privacy-alert filings and law-firm investigations. With PII and PHI in the wild, affected members face an elevated phishing and identity-theft risk in the weeks ahead.
