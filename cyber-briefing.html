Top Story

No patch yet: Cisco warns of an actively exploited root-level zero-day in Catalyst SD-WAN Manager

Cisco has disclosed that CVE-2026-20245, a high-severity zero-day (CVSS 7.8) in the Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager, is being exploited in the wild — and there is no patch or workaround available yet. The flaw stems from insufficient validation of user-supplied input and lets a low-privileged local attacker upload a crafted file to perform command injection and execute arbitrary commands as root.

Cisco's PSIRT said it became aware of exploitation in June after Google Mandiant reported the bug, and observed limited cases where exploitation resulted in a configuration change being pushed down to edge devices. The flaw affects all deployment types — on-prem, Cloud-Pro, Cisco-managed cloud and FedRAMP (Government). With a fix still pending, defenders should tighten access controls, restrict who can reach the management plane, and watch for unexpected configuration pushes. SecurityWeek notes it is at least the seventh SD-WAN zero-day Cisco has flagged as exploited in 2026.

Breaches & Incidents
BREACH

DentaQuest: ~2.6M hit, ShinyHunters leak

Dental and vision benefits administrator DentaQuest was breached, with extortion crew ShinyHunters publishing roughly 234 GB of stolen data. Reporting puts the exposure at about 2.6 million people — one of the largest breaches reported so far in 2026 — including personal and health-insurance information.

SUPPLY CHAIN

IronWorm worms its way into npm

A Rust-based, self-propagating infostealer dubbed IronWorm infected 36 npm packages, targeting AWS, OpenAI, Anthropic and npm credentials, vault configs, SSH keys and crypto-wallet files. It abuses npm's Trusted Publishing workflow to spread; researchers say it was caught before reaching more popular packages (~32K combined monthly downloads).

FRAUD

World Cup 2026 fraud wave

Researchers and the FBI warn of a surge of FIFA-themed fraud days before the June 11 kickoff — thousands of lookalike FIFA domains, banking malware hidden inside pirate streaming apps, and operations cloning FIFA's login page to hijack accounts.

EXTORTION

Fresh leak-site listings (June 5)

Trackers logged a cluster of newly listed victims on June 5, including private-aviation firm Avcon Jet, Corley Manufacturing, the Dallis Law Firm, food maker Don Don, Family Medical Associates of Raleigh and Trican Well Service — a reminder that data-extortion volume remains high across sectors.
