ShinyHunters exploited an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day for two weeks before anyone knew — universities hit hardest

The extortion crew tracked by Mandiant as UNC6240 (ShinyHunters) exploited CVE-2026-35273, a CVSS 9.8 unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in PeopleSoft Enterprise PeopleTools 8.61/8.62, to break into enterprise systems, steal data, and demand payment to keep it private. Activity ran from May 27 to June 9 — Oracle's out-of-band advisory didn't land until June 10, meaning the bug was a zero-day the entire campaign.

Roughly 300 instances across 100+ organizations are believed affected, about 68% of them in higher education (mostly US). Exploitation requires no login and no user interaction — just HTTP access to the server. Notably, the flaw had still not appeared in CISA's KEV catalog as of this edition, so no federal remediation clock has started; admins should treat Oracle's June 10 alert as the deadline.
