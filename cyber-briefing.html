Top Story

Windows Netlogon flaw (CVE-2026-41089) is being exploited in the wild — unpatched domain controllers face credential-free takeover

CVE-2026-41089, a critical stack-based buffer overflow in the Windows Netlogon RPC service rated CVSS 9.8, is now under active exploitation. Microsoft's Windows Attack Research and Protection (WARP) team found the bug and Microsoft patched it on May 12 as part of May Patch Tuesday — but on June 1, Belgium's Centre for Cybersecurity (CCB) issued a public warning that it is "now actively exploited in the wild and could lead to RCE." The flaw needs no authentication and no user interaction: every supported Windows Server is affected, and exploitation requires only network reachability to a domain controller, leaving unpatched DCs exposed to full SYSTEM-level compromise.

As of the latest reporting the vulnerability is not yet listed in CISA's KEV catalog, but researchers expect a listing with a federal deadline to follow. The disclosure lands amid a busy exploitation cycle: SAP patched a critical NetWeaver ABAP kernel bug (CVE-2026-27671, CVSS 9.8) in its June Security Patch Day, Microsoft addressed a near-perfect Dynamics 365 on-prem RCE (CVE-2026-42898, CVSS 9.9), and CISA added Linux Kernel and Android Framework flaws to the KEV catalog in early June.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 3: CVE-2026-45247 (Mirasvit Full Page Cache Warmer, deserialization of untrusted data) added to the KEV catalog on evidence of active exploitation. Under BOD 22-01's standard three-week window, the federal remediation deadline lands around June 24, 2026.
  • June 2: CISA added CVE-2025-48595 (Android Framework integer overflow) and CVE-2022-0492 (Linux Kernel improper authentication). The three-week window puts the federal deadline around June 23, 2026; apply current platform updates.
  • Active, patch available: CVE-2026-41089 (Windows Netlogon) is being exploited in the wild — patch domain controllers immediately and watch for a forthcoming KEV listing. Microsoft shipped the fix on May 12.
  • Highest severity this month: CVE-2026-42898 (Microsoft Dynamics 365 on-prem, CVSS 9.9 RCE) and CVE-2026-27671 (SAP NetWeaver ABAP, CVSS 9.8) — both fixed by vendor advisories; prioritize internet-facing and identity-adjacent systems.
  • BOD 22-01 gives federal civilian agencies a standard three-week window from a CVE's KEV addition date to remediate or stop using the affected product.
