Top Story

Check Point patches an actively exploited VPN zero-day (CVE-2026-50751) tied to a Qilin ransomware affiliate

On June 8, Check Point published an emergency advisory and hotfixes for CVE-2026-50751, a critical authentication-bypass flaw (CVSS 9.3) in its Remote Access VPN, Mobile Access and Spark Firewall products. The bug lives in how gateways validate certificates during the deprecated IKEv1 key exchange; an unauthenticated attacker can stand up a VPN session without valid credentials. Check Point says it is being exploited in the wild with activity dating back to May 7 and rising in early June — a campaign it characterizes as limited in scope, hitting several dozen organizations, with at least one incident linked (medium confidence) to a Qilin ransomware affiliate.

The disclosure caps a heavy exploitation cycle. A critical Everest Forms Pro WordPress flaw (CVE-2026-3300, CVSS 9.8) is under active attack for unauthenticated remote code execution and full site takeover; a Microsoft Exchange Server spoofing bug (CVE-2026-42897, CVSS 8.1) has no patch yet and relies on the Exchange Emergency Mitigation Service; the Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager zero-day (CVE-2026-20245) remains exploited with mitigations only; and insurer Aflac is still working through a June intrusion exposing data on roughly 22.7 million people.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 3: CVE-2026-45247 (Mirasvit Full Page Cache Warmer, deserialization of untrusted data) added to the KEV catalog on evidence of active exploitation. Under BOD 22-01's standard three-week window, the federal remediation deadline lands around June 24, 2026.
  • June 2: CISA added CVE-2025-48595 (Android Framework integer overflow, enables code execution & local privilege escalation) and CVE-2022-0492 (Linux Kernel improper authentication). The three-week window puts the federal deadline around June 23, 2026; apply current platform updates.
  • Active, hotfix available: CVE-2026-50751 (Check Point VPN) is being exploited in the wild — patch immediately, audit logs back to May 7, and look for the IOCs in Check Point's advisory even after applying the fix.
  • Active, no patch: CVE-2026-42897 (Microsoft Exchange OWA spoofing) and CVE-2026-20245 (Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager) are being exploited with no full fix — rely on vendor mitigations and tighten access.
  • BOD 22-01 gives federal civilian agencies a standard three-week window from a CVE's KEV addition date to remediate or stop using the affected product.
