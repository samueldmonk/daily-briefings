Top Story

The clock runs out tomorrow: federal agencies have until June 11 to patch the actively exploited Check Point VPN zero-day (CVE-2026-50751)

CVE-2026-50751, a critical authentication-bypass flaw in Check Point Remote Access VPN, Mobile Access / SSL VPN and Spark Firewall gateways configured to use the deprecated IKEv1 key exchange, is being actively exploited in the wild. Rated CVSS 9.3, the bug lets an unauthenticated remote attacker establish a VPN connection without a valid password — no privileges, no user interaction, just network reach to an exposed gateway. Check Point shipped an emergency hotfix on Monday, June 8, flagging exploitation that began on May 7 and surged over the prior weekend; in at least one case the access led to post-compromise activity tied to a Qilin ransomware affiliate.

CISA added the flaw to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog the same day (June 8) and — in a rare departure from the standard three-week window under BOD 22-01 — set an emergency three-day remediation deadline of June 11, 2026, which is tomorrow. Any federal civilian agency (and prudently, any organization) running affected IKEv1-configured Check Point gateways should treat the hotfix as urgent. The deadline lands amid an unusually heavy patch cycle: Microsoft's June Patch Tuesday fixed a record ~200 flaws on June 9, and separately, an actively exploited Windows Netlogon RCE (CVE-2026-41089, CVSS 9.8) and an unpatched Cisco SD-WAN Manager flaw (CVE-2026-20245) are both under attack.

Breaches & Incidents
Zero-day

Check Point VPN — Qilin ransomware

Exploitation of CVE-2026-50751 has been observed against targeted organizations; in at least one case the post-compromise activity was tied to a Qilin ransomware affiliate. Earliest observed exploitation dates to May 7; CISA's federal patch deadline is June 11.

Patch Tuesday

Microsoft fixes a record ~200 flaws

June 9's Patch Tuesday was Microsoft's largest ever — ~200 CVEs (beating the prior record of 167), including 33 critical. Three publicly disclosed zero-days were fixed (none yet exploited): CTFMON EoP (CVE-2026-45586), the HTTP/2 "Bomb" DoS (CVE-2026-49160) and BitLocker "YellowKey" (CVE-2026-50507).

Exploited CVE

Windows Netlogon — domain-controller RCE

CVE-2026-41089 (CVSS 9.8), a stack buffer overflow in Netlogon, is exploited in the wild and grants SYSTEM-level code execution on domain controllers across Windows Server 2012–2025. Belgium's CCB and others have warned DCs are at risk; patch immediately.

Exploited CVE

Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager

CVE-2026-20245 (CVSS 7.8), an authenticated privilege-escalation flaw, is being exploited for root-level command execution. Cisco confirmed active exploitation with no patch available as of June 6 — apply the vendor's mitigations.

Breach

Baker Distributing — ShinyHunters

Disclosed June 8: ShinyHunters compromised over 260,000 Salesforce records plus corporate data at the HVAC/refrigeration distributor — part of the group's broader Salesforce-focused extortion campaign.

Exploited CVE

SolarWinds Serv-U — DoS

CVE-2026-28318 (CVSS 7.5) lets an unauthenticated attacker crash the Serv-U file-transfer service via a crafted POST request; CISA added it to KEV (June 5) with a federal deadline of June 19. Fixed in Serv-U 15.5.4 HF1.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
  • June 8 (emergency): CVE-2026-50751 (Check Point Security Gateway improper authentication) added to the KEV catalog with a shortened three-day deadline of June 11, 2026 — i.e. tomorrow — given active ransomware exploitation. Patch IKEv1-configured gateways now.
  • June 8: CVE-2026-42271 (BerriAI LiteLLM command injection) added on evidence of active exploitation. Under BOD 22-01's standard three-week window, the federal remediation deadline lands around June 29, 2026.
  • June 5: CVE-2026-28318 (SolarWinds Serv-U uncontrolled resource consumption / DoS, CVSS 7.5) added with a federal remediation deadline of June 19, 2026; fixed in Serv-U 15.5.4 HF1.
  • June 3: CVE-2026-45247 (Mirasvit Full Page Cache Warmer, deserialization of untrusted data) added on evidence of active exploitation; the standard three-week window puts the federal deadline around June 24, 2026.
  • BOD 22-01 gives federal civilian agencies a standard three-week window from a CVE's KEV addition date to remediate or stop using the affected product; CISA can shorten that window for emergencies, as it did for the Check Point flaw (3 days).
