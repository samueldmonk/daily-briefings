Top Story

Nine days out: UFC Freedom 250 brings Topuria–Gaethje title unification to the White House South Lawn

The first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence is set for Sunday, June 14 — Flag Day and the 250th-anniversary year of the United States. UFC Freedom 250 is headlined by a lightweight title unification bout between champion Ilia Topuria and two-time interim champion Justin Gaethje, with former two-division champion Alex Pereira making his heavyweight debut in pursuit of the interim heavyweight title in the co-main event.

Originally announced as a six-fight card, the event now boasts seven bouts after a Josh Hokit–Derrick Lewis heavyweight clash was added. The card airs on Paramount+, with a limited number of preliminary bouts also broadcast on CBS.

Prospect Watch
PROSPECT

Quillan Salkilld (LW, Australia)

The unbeaten-in-the-UFC lightweight (5-0 in the promotion) entered the rankings by stopping longtime contender Beneil Dariush via first-round TKO (3:29) at UFC Perth on May 2 — the biggest win of his career. His next bout is not yet booked; a ranked matchup before year-end looks likely.

PROSPECT

Ateba Gautier (MW, Cameroon)

"The Storm" (11-1) is 2-0 in 2026: a decision over Andrey Pulyaev at UFC 324 in January, then a second-round KO (1:06) of Ozzy Diaz at UFC 328 on May 9 — an overhand right to the temple followed by hammerfists for another highlight-reel finish.

FREE AGENT

Salahdine Parnasse (Europe)

The highly touted European standout impressed with a second-round knockout at Jake Paul's Most Valuable Promotions event — and both the UFC and PFL are reportedly pursuing his signature.

Around the Sport
  • Paramount era in full swing — all 2026 UFC events stream on Paramount+ with no pay-per-view requirement, under the seven-year, $7.7 billion media rights deal signed with Paramount Skydance in August 2025. The White House card is one of four big events per year slated for CBS broadcast.
  • Roster purge continues — the UFC released four more fighters, including an 18-1 standout, as it clears space for emerging talent; veteran Max Griffin and Australian lightweight Jamie Mullarkey (cut after his UFC 325 loss to Quillan Salkilld) are among recent departures.
  • Power rankings churn — with Topuria–Gaethje unifying the lightweight belts and Pereira moving to heavyweight, June's results will reshuffle multiple divisional pictures heading into UFC 329 in July.
