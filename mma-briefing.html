Top Story

Four days out: UFC Freedom 250 fight week builds — as a federal lawsuit tries to block the White House card

The UFC's most audacious event yet — UFC Freedom 250 on the South Lawn of the White House — is set for Sunday, June 14, billed as the first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence and timed to the United States' 250th anniversary. The seven-fight card is anchored by two title bouts: undisputed lightweight champion Ilia Topuria defends his belt against former interim lightweight champion Justin Gaethje in the main event, while former two-division champ Alex Pereira meets Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight belt in the co-main. If Pereira wins, he becomes the first fighter to hold UFC titles in three divisions.

The rest of the card features Sean O'Malley (19-3) vs. Aiemann Zahabi (14-2) at bantamweight, Bo Nickal (8-1) vs. Kyle Daukaus (17-4) at middleweight, Mauricio Ruffy (13-2) vs. Michael Chandler (23-10) at lightweight, Diego Lopes (27-8) vs. Steve Garcia (19-5) at featherweight and Derrick Lewis (29-13) vs. Josh Hokit (9-0) at heavyweight. The main card begins at 8 PM ET and streams on Paramount+ with a CBS simulcast — but it isn't a lock: on June 6, the anti-corruption watchdog Public Integrity Project filed a federal suit in the U.S. District Court for D.C. seeking to block the fights, arguing the South Lawn structure was erected without Congressional approval or environmental review. A Trump administration official called the suit "obstructionist" and "baseless," and the promotion is still proceeding for June 14.
