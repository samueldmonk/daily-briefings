Top Story
Four days out — and one judge to convince

White House card awaits a court ruling: judge asked to decide by Thursday on bid to halt UFC Freedom 250

UFC Freedom 250 — the first professional sporting event ever staged at the White House — is set for Sunday, June 14, 8 PM ET on the South Lawn (Paramount+, with a CBS simulcast), with CNN reporting more than 90,000 people expected on and around the grounds for a card tied to America's 250th-birthday celebrations and President Trump's 80th birthday. One thing stands between fight week and the walkouts: a federal lawsuit.

The Public Integrity Project, which sued on June 6 claiming the event was improperly permitted, skipped environmental review and unlawfully erected structures on public land without congressional sign-off, has asked the judge — an Obama appointee — to rule on its emergency injunction request by Thursday. The Justice Department fired back this week that the challenge simply came too late. As of the latest reports, no hearing had been scheduled.

If the cage door does swing open, the stakes are enormous: Ilia Topuria defends the undisputed lightweight title against former interim champ Justin Gaethje, who punched his ticket by upsetting Paddy Pimblett. In the co-main, Alex Pereira meets Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title — a Pereira win would make him the UFC's first three-division champion.

Tom Nolan — five straight, calling for Moicano

Daniel Marcos — 18-1 and out the door to PFL

The Peruvian bantamweight (18-1, 1 NC overall; 5-1, 1 NC in the UFC) was removed from the roster May 29 after his contract ran out, then signed with PFL on June 1. He exits off a submission win over Miles Johns — a head-scratcher of a departure.
