Top Story

Bonfim shocks Belal: Brazilian sweeps the cards 50-45 in Vegas — and the road now runs to the White House lawn

Gabriel Bonfim delivered the breakout night of his career on Saturday at the APEX in Las Vegas, dominating former welterweight champion Belal Muhammad for a clean-sweep unanimous decision — 50-45 on all three scorecards. The Brazilian was the sharper striker from the opening bell, leaning on calf kicks that repeatedly forced Muhammad to switch stances and kept the ex-titleholder off the grappling that defined his title run. Bonfim moves to 20-1; for Muhammad, it is a third consecutive loss.

The result reshapes the welterweight picture just as the sport's biggest week of the year approaches. Seven days out, UFC Freedom 250 — tied to the United States' 250th anniversary — lands on the South Lawn of the White House on Sunday, June 14, the first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence. Undisputed lightweight champion Ilia Topuria defends his belt against interim champ Justin Gaethje in the main event, while Alex Pereira faces Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title in the co-main.

Prospect Watch
PROSPECT

Gabriel Bonfim arrives as a contender

The Brazilian welterweight pushed his record to 20-1 with the biggest win of his career, picking apart a former champion over five rounds. At just the start of his prime, Bonfim now sits squarely in the divisional mix.

PROSPECT

Iwo Baraniewski cashes a bonus

The young Polish prospect needed just 85 seconds to dispatch Junior Tafa by first-round TKO on the June 6 main card, banking a $100K Performance of the Night check in the process.

PROSPECT

Ateba Gautier stays unbeaten in 2026

The Cameroonian middleweight is 11-1 and a perfect 2-0 on the year — a decision over Andrey Pulyaev in January followed by a second-round KO/TKO of Ozzy Diaz at UFC 328 on May 9. One of the division's fastest-rising names.
