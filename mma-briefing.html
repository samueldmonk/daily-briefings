June 14 · White House South Lawn

Ten days out: UFC Freedom 250 brings a title unification — and 90,000 fans — to the White House

UFC Freedom 250 lands Sunday, June 14 on the White House grounds — the first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence, timed to Flag Day and President Trump's 80th birthday. CNN reports the South Lawn event is expected to draw more than 90,000 spectators.

At the top of the card, reigning lightweight champion Ilia Topuria meets interim champ Justin Gaethje to unify the belts. In the co-main, former two-division champion Alex Pereira makes his heavyweight debut in pursuit of the interim heavyweight title.

Prospect Watch
prospect

Quillan Salkilld — 4-0 in the UFC and hunting a ranked name

The lightweight prospect is 4-0 in the promotion after submitting Jamie Mullarkey (first-round rear-naked choke) at UFC 325 in Sydney on January 31 — a $100,000 Performance of the Night. He's said he wants a top-15 opponent next and to fight a couple more times this year; no bout announced yet.

free agent

Salahdine Parnasse — UFC, PFL and MVP chase MMA's hottest free agent

The former two-division KSW champion TKO'd Kenneth Cross in the first round at MVP's Rousey vs. Carano card on May 16. His manager says his destination will be decided within a month — UFC, PFL, and a France homecoming with MVP are all in play.

roster moves

UFC roster purge continues

The UFC released four more fighters — including an 18-1 standout — as cuts continue, with veteran Max Griffin among recent departures, a sign the promotion is clearing space for younger talent.
