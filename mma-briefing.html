Top Story

Eight days out: UFC Freedom 250 takes Topuria–Gaethje to the White House lawn

The first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence is nearly here. UFC Freedom 250 — named for the 250th anniversary of the U.S. Declaration of Independence — lands on the South Lawn of the White House on Sunday, June 14. Lightweight champion Ilia Topuria puts his title on the line against Justin Gaethje in the main event, while former two-division champion Alex Pereira makes his heavyweight debut against Ciryl Gane for an interim title in the co-main.

The seven-fight card also features Sean O'Malley vs. Aiemann Zahabi, Mauricio Ruffy vs. Michael Chandler, Bo Nickal vs. Kyle Daukaus and Diego Lopes vs. Steve Garcia, per ESPN and Tapology listings. First, though, there's business tonight: UFC Fight Night caps the week at the APEX with Belal Muhammad facing Gabriel Bonfim at welterweight.

PROSPECT

Salahdine Parnasse — the free agent everyone wants

The French-Moroccan standout scored a second-round knockout of Kenneth Cross at Most Valuable Promotions' inaugural event and entered free agency. Both the UFC and PFL have reportedly expressed interest in signing him.

PROSPECT

Quillan Salkilld keeps climbing at lightweight

The unbeaten Australian is 5-0 in the UFC. His latest statement: a first-round TKO of longtime contender Beneil Dariush at UFC Perth on May 2 — the kind of win that turns a prospect into a ranked name.

PROSPECT

Rei Tsuruya shines in Macau
