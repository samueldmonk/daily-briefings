Top Story

Nine days out: UFC Freedom 250 brings title unification — and a triple-champ bid — to the White House South Lawn

The first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence lands Sunday, June 14: a seven-fight card headlined by lightweight champion Ilia Topuria unifying belts with interim champ Justin Gaethje. Topuria says he'll finish Gaethje in the first round — and per SI, a win would fuel his push for a historic bid at a third divisional title.

In the co-main, former two-division champion Alex Pereira makes his heavyweight debut against Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title — victory would make him the first three-division champion in UFC history. The UFC is erecting a 4,000-plus-seat stadium with a 92-foot stage on the South Lawn, and President Trump has floated keeping the arena permanently. Sean O'Malley, Michael Chandler and Diego Lopes also feature, with Josh Hokit vs. Derrick Lewis a late addition.

Sources: ESPN, Yahoo Sports, ABC News, Sports Illustrated

Quillan Salkilld (Australia)

Now 5-0 in the UFC. The 26-year-old survived an early knockdown to storm back and stop longtime contender Beneil Dariush by first-round TKO at UFC Perth on May 2, earning Performance of the Night.

"The Storm" is 11-1 and 2-0 in 2026 — a decision over Andrey Pulyaev at UFC 324 in January, then a highlight-reel KO of Ozzy Diaz at UFC 328 on May 9, landing a monstrous overhand right 1:06 into Round 2.

Salahdine Parnasse in demand

Around the Sport
  • The Paramount+ era is in full swing — all 13 flagship events and 30 Fight Nights a year stream on Paramount+ under the seven-year deal, with no pay-per-view; UFC 329: McGregor vs. Holloway 2 is among the marquee cards slated for the platform.
  • Roster purge continues — the UFC released fighters on six separate dates in the second half of May, including veteran Max Griffin and, per EssentiallySports, an 18-1 standout, as the promotion clears space for emerging talent.
  • White House logistics — the Freedom 250 build-out features a 92-foot-tall stage normally reserved for music-festival headliners, and Trump has compared the South Lawn arena to the Eiffel Tower, suggesting it could become permanent.
