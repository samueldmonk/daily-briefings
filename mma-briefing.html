Top Story

Six days out: UFC Freedom 250 brings two title fights to the White House South Lawn

The UFC's most audacious card ever — UFC Freedom 250 on the South Lawn of the White House — is set for Sunday, June 14, the first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence. It is built on two title fights: reigning lightweight champion Ilia Topuria meets interim champ Justin Gaethje in a unification main event, while Alex Pereira faces Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight belt in the co-main.

The seven-fight card is set to play out in front of roughly 4,300 spectators on the South Lawn, with another 85,000 tickets issued for a viewing party at the nearby Ellipse. The main card is slated for 8 p.m. ET, expected to stream on Paramount+ with a limited number of prelims simulcast on CBS — the centerpiece of the UFC's new media era.
