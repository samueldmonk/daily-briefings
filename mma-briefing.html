Top Story

Five days out: UFC Freedom 250 fight week ramps up — even as a federal lawsuit tries to block the White House card

The UFC's most audacious event yet — UFC Freedom 250 on the South Lawn of the White House — is set for Sunday, June 14, billed as the first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence. The card is anchored by two title fights: reigning lightweight champion (and former featherweight champ) Ilia Topuria meets two-time interim champ Justin Gaethje in a unification main event, while former two-division champ Alex Pereira faces two-time title challenger Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight belt in the co-main.

The stacked card also features Sean O'Malley vs. Aiemann Zahabi (bantamweight), Bo Nickal vs. Kyle Daukaus (middleweight), Mauricio Ruffy vs. Michael Chandler (lightweight), Diego Lopes vs. Steve Garcia (featherweight) and Derrick Lewis vs. Josh Hokit (heavyweight). If Pereira beats Gane, he becomes the only fighter to win UFC titles in three divisions. The event streams on Paramount+ with no pay-per-view — but it isn't a done deal: a federal lawsuit filed this week seeks to block the fights from being held on the South Lawn, arguing the event's organizing was unlawful.
