Freedom 250 · Cleared for Sunday

Green light: federal judge rejects bid to stop the White House card — Freedom 250 is ON

U.S. District Judge Amit Mehta on Friday rejected the emergency request to block UFC Freedom 250, the June 14 card on the White House South Lawn. Mehta ruled the two Virginia residents who sued lacked standing, found no showing of irreparable harm, and cited their "unreasonable delay" in filing — agreeing with the government that an eleventh-hour cancellation would cause "substantial harm" given the planning and cost already sunk into the event, which The Hill reports has run federal agencies and the UFC at least $60 million.

That clears the stage for Sunday, 8 PM ET: Ilia Topuria defends the undisputed lightweight title against Justin Gaethje, the former interim lightweight champ, while Alex Pereira meets Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title — a win would make Pereira the first three-division champion in UFC history. Sean O'Malley, Michael Chandler, Diego Lopes, Bo Nickal and Derrick Lewis also feature. The Secret Service says security will be at Super Bowl level.

Fight week is already underway downtown — the pre-event press conference takes place in front of the Lincoln Memorial tonight at 8:30 PM ET.
