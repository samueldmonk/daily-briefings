UFC Freedom 250 · White House South Lawn · Sun Jun 14

The greatest upset in UFC history: Justin Gaethje breaks unbeaten Ilia Topuria to claim the undisputed lightweight crown on the White House lawn

It actually happened — and the favorite fell. In the main event of UFC Freedom 250, the first card ever staged on the South Lawn of the White House, a heavy underdog Justin Gaethje outslugged and outlasted Ilia Topuria, forcing the previously unbeaten champion to retire on his stool after the fourth round to capture the undisputed UFC lightweight championship. Topuria, 17-0 coming in and one of the sport's biggest stars, gave Gaethje hell in the second round, but Gaethje refused to fade and piled on the punishment until Topuria — battered and out of answers — could not continue. The two-time former interim lightweight champion finally has the undisputed belt that long eluded him, in front of President Trump and a crowd ESPN put at over 4,000, with an estimated 80,000 more on the Ellipse.

The co-main delivered a second stunner: Ciryl Gane knocked out Alex Pereira in the second round — dropping the kickboxing icon and swarming with strikes until referee Herb Dean stepped in — to win the interim heavyweight title and end Pereira's bid to become the first three-division champion in UFC history. Up and down the seven-fight main card, the finishes kept coming: Sean O'Malley walked off Aiemann Zahabi with a second-round knockout, Josh Hokit brutalized knockout king Derrick Lewis to move to 10-0, Mauricio Ruffy spun a wheel kick through Michael Chandler, and Bo Nickal blew out Kyle Daukaus in the first.

The spectacle matched the violence. Fights took place under "The Claw," a 600-ton, four-pronged steel arena 154 feet wide and 92 feet tall — 22 feet taller than the White House — suspended over a 13-ton cage; per CBS Sports, the UFC will spend $700,000 restoring the South Lawn grass. The event streamed exclusively on Paramount+ (no pay-per-view) after U.S. District Judge Amit Mehta's Friday ruling cleared away a last-minute legal challenge. Full results below.

Prospect Watch
ProspectTitle contender?

Josh Hokit — 10-0, the heavyweight wrecking ball

Still undefeated and now 4-0 in the UFC, Hokit followed his record-setting April win over Curtis Blaydes by brutalizing knockout king Derrick Lewis on the White House lawn (TKO, R2). MMA Mania flagged the loud-mouthed heavyweight as a possible next contender for the title. Trump personally pushed for the booking after watching the Blaydes fight.

ProspectStatement win

Mauricio Ruffy — 14-2, a problem at lightweight

The #9-ranked Brazilian proved the hype, fighting with his hands down before spinning a wheel kick through Michael Chandler for a first-round TKO at Freedom 250 — his second straight highlight-reel finish after stopping Rafael Fiziev in February. The win pushes him toward the top five.

ProspectCareer-best

Bo Nickal — 9-1, back in the win column in style

After his first pro loss, the former NCAA wrestling champion answered with the most dominant performance of his career, slamming and then stopping Kyle Daukaus late in the first round (TKO) at the White House. The finish reasserts Nickal as a middleweight to watch.

Around the Sport
  • Topuria's aura, gone: the loss is the first of Ilia Topuria's professional career, dropping the two-division star to 17-1 and stripping him of the lightweight belt he won by knocking out Charles Oliveira a year ago. The man billed as the sport's next pound-for-pound king was retired on his stool — the defining result of the Freedom 250 night.
  • Pereira's history bid falls short: Alex Pereira's attempt to become the UFC's first-ever three-division champion ended with Ciryl Gane's second-round knockout. Gane, twice a heavyweight title challenger before, finally has gold around his waist as interim champ and a unification fight with the division's kingpin to chase.
  • McGregor is next on the marquee: attention now turns to UFC 329 on July 11 at T-Mobile Arena, where Conor McGregor returns for the first time since July 2021 in a welterweight rematch with Max Holloway — 13 years after McGregor won their 2013 meeting by decision.
  • The grass bill: per CBS Sports, the UFC will spend $700,000 restoring the South Lawn after the event — a line item inside what The Hill reported was an at-least-$60M combined cost to federal agencies and the promotion for the historic card.
  • Chandler's skid deepens: Michael Chandler's first-round loss to Ruffy is his fourth straight inside the Octagon, leaving the former Bellator champ at 2-6 in the UFC and raising fresh questions about his future at the top of the lightweight division.
