Top Story

Nine days out: UFC Freedom 250 brings Topuria vs. Gaethje to the White House South Lawn

The first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence is almost here. UFC Freedom 250 lands Sunday, June 14 on the White House grounds, headlined by a lightweight title unification bout between reigning champion Ilia Topuria and interim champion Justin Gaethje. In the co-main, former two-division champion Alex Pereira makes his heavyweight debut in pursuit of the interim title.

Per CNN, the card is expected to draw more than 90,000 spectators to the South Lawn, coincides with President Trump's 80th birthday and Flag Day, and will feature concerts and ceremonial weigh-ins in the lead-up — with the UFC covering the estimated $60 million cost, including a massive lighting structure called The Claw. First, though: fight night tomorrow at the Apex.

PROSPECT

Quillan Salkilld (12-1) — lightweight, Australia

The unbeaten-in-the-UFC Aussie survived an early knockdown and stormed back to stop contender Beneil Dariush by first-round TKO at UFC Perth on May 2, earning another Performance of the Night. A ranked number should be next.

PROSPECT

Ateba Gautier (11-1) — middleweight, Cameroon

"The Silent Assassin" is 2-0 in 2026: a decision over Andrey Pulyaev at UFC 324 in January, then a second-round TKO of Ozzy Diaz at UFC 328 on May 9 — an overhand right and hammerfists at 1:06 of R2. Nine of his eleven wins are by KO.

FREE AGENT

Salahdine Parnasse — the man everyone wants

The former two-division KSW champion crushed Kenneth Cross by first-round TKO in his US debut at MVP's Rousey vs. Carano card on May 16. UFC, PFL and MVP are all chasing his signature; his manager says a decision is coming within the month.
