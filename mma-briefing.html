Top Story
Fight eve · Everyone made weight

The Claw is up, the lawsuit is dead, and all 14 fighters are on weight — Topuria vs. Gaethje goes Sunday at the White House

The last box is checked. All 14 fighters hit the scale on Saturday and every one made weight at the official White House weigh-ins: Ilia Topuria and Justin Gaethje both came in at the 155-pound lightweight title limit, and the interim heavyweight headliners cleared with room to spare — Alex Pereira at 251, Ciryl Gane at 248. (Diego Lopes weighed in twice, hitting 146 for his featherweight bout before stepping back on the scale at 154 as a standby alternate for the main event.) With U.S. District Judge Amit Mehta's Friday ruling clearing the last legal obstacle — he found the two Virginia plaintiffs lacked standing, showed no irreparable harm, and waited too long to sue — the only thing between the UFC and its White House card is Sunday's opening bell. Ceremonial weigh-ins run Saturday evening at the fan park on the Ellipse.

Looming over it all — literally — is "The Claw," the 600-ton, four-pronged steel arena now standing on the South Lawn: 154 feet wide and 92 feet tall, 22 feet taller than the White House itself, suspended over a 13-ton cage. ESPN reports Dana White rejected earlier designs because he wanted broadcast sight lines to the White House and the Washington Monument. Roughly 5,000 invitation-only spectators will watch in person per CNN — 1,200 of those seats are reserved for active-duty military, per Al Jazeera — while TIME reports about 85,000 free tickets were made available for the watch party on the Ellipse.

Sunday, 8 PM ET on Paramount+: Ilia Topuria (17-0) defends the undisputed lightweight title against interim champion Justin Gaethje — the unbeaten two-division champ's first walk since his June 2025 knockout of Charles Oliveira won him the belt, against the man who claimed the interim strap by outclassing Paddy Pimblett over five rounds at UFC 324 in January. Co-main: Alex Pereira meets Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title — a Pereira win would make him the first three-division champion in UFC history.

Prospect Watch
ProspectFights Sunday

Josh Hokit — 9-0, from Blaydes to the White House lawn

The undefeated heavyweight (3-0 in the UFC) ground out a 29-28 ×3 decision over Curtis Blaydes at UFC 327 in Miami on April 11 — a three-round war that set the heavyweight record for combined significant strikes (354, per CBS). Trump was in the building that night and pushed for the booking: per ESPN, he asked why Derrick Lewis wasn't on the White House card, and Dana White made Lewis–Hokit on the spot.

ProspectFights Sunday

Mauricio Ruffy — 13-2, the leap to contention

The #9-ranked lightweight, fresh off a second-round TKO of Rafael Fiziev at UFC 325 on February 1, meets Michael Chandler on the White House card — a step-up fight with top-five implications.

Prospect

Iwo Baraniewski — 9-0, nine first-round finishes

The Polish light heavyweight blitzed Junior Tafa in 85 seconds on June 6, moving to 3-0 in the UFC with every career win by first-round stoppage. One of the cleanest finishing résumés on the roster.

Around the Sport
  • The grass bill: Dana White says the UFC will spend $700,000 on new grass to restore the South Lawn after the event — a line item inside what The Hill reports is an at-least-$60M cost to federal agencies and the UFC combined.
  • Paramount+ only: Freedom 250 is not a pay-per-view and won't be simulcast — White confirmed the event streams exclusively on Paramount+, after a CBS simulcast had been floated earlier in planning. Weather reports have even raised the possibility of start-time adjustments for the open-air card.
  • Super Bowl-level lockdown: the Secret Service told ABC News that security for Sunday's card will be at Super Bowl level.
  • Callout corner: Tom Nolan (11-1), riding a five-fight streak after his June 6 decision over Farès Ziam, says he wants Renato Moicano next.
  • Roster move: bantamweight Daniel Marcos (18-1, 1 NC overall) left the UFC after his contract was completed in late May — not cut mid-deal — and signed with the PFL on June 1, departing on a submission win over Miles Johns after going 5-1 (1 NC) in the promotion.
