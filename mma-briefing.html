The White House card is ON: judge lets UFC Freedom 250 proceed as fight week takes over the National Mall

The last legal roadblock is gone — a federal judge declined to halt Sunday's UFC Freedom 250 on the White House South Lawn, rejecting the emergency bid by two Virginia residents (represented by the Public Integrity Project) who argued the event skipped environmental review and congressional approval. U.S. District Judge Amit P. Mehta had been asked to rule by Thursday; the DOJ called the claims "meritless" and argued the suit simply came too late. Per The Hill, the spectacle is costing federal agencies and the UFC at least $60 million.

Fight week is now in full Washington bloom: all 14 fighters hit a media day on the South Lawn, and tonight's pre-fight press conference stages in front of the Lincoln Memorial at 8:30 PM ET. About 4,000 invited guests will watch Sunday as Ilia Topuria defends the undisputed lightweight title against former interim champ Justin Gaethje — 8 PM ET on Paramount+, simulcast on CBS, on a card timed to America's 250th anniversary celebrations (and coinciding with President Trump's 80th birthday).
