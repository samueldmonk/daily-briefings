Judge rejects bid to block the White House card: UFC Freedom 250 is ON for Sunday

The last legal obstacle is gone. A federal judge rejected the request to halt UFC Freedom 250, CNN reported Friday, in the case brought by two Virginia residents — represented by the Public Integrity Project — who argued the privately run event couldn't lawfully be staged on the South Lawn or at the Lincoln Memorial. The Justice Department had called the claims "meritless," arguing federal law doesn't require congressional approval for "temporary structures" and that the challenge came too late; the case sits before U.S. District Judge Amit P. Mehta, who per Newsweek has ruled against Trump in past high-profile disputes. Per CNN, parts of the event roll out at the Lincoln Memorial today ahead of Sunday's main card, and The Hill reports the spectacle has cost federal agencies and the UFC at least $60 million.

So the first professional sporting event at the White House proceeds: Sunday, June 14, 8 PM ET on the South Lawn (Paramount+, CBS simulcast), tied to America's 250th-birthday festivities and President Trump's 80th birthday, with CNN previously reporting 90,000+ people expected. Ilia Topuria defends the undisputed lightweight title against former interim champ Justin Gaethje, and Alex Pereira meets Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title — a Pereira win makes him the UFC's first three-division champion.
