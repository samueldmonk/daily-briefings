Ten days out: UFC Freedom 250 at the White House — Topuria vs. Gaethje, Pereira's heavyweight debut

The first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence is set for Sunday, June 14. UFC Freedom 250 is headlined by a lightweight title unification bout between reigning champ Ilia Topuria and interim champ Justin Gaethje, with former two-division champion Alex Pereira making his heavyweight debut in the co-main in pursuit of an interim heavyweight title. But first: fight week starts now, with Belal Muhammad vs. Gabriel Bonfim at the APEX this Saturday.

Around the Sport
  • Paramount+ era pays off: UFC parent TKO posted Q1 revenue of $1.6B (+26%), with UFC’s growth driven by a $50M+ media-rights jump from the seven-year, ~$1.1B-per-year Paramount deal. UFC 326 drew nearly 2.5M viewers on CBS — the most-watched UFC linear telecast in a decade.
  • Younger eyeballs: Paramount says UFC viewers on Paramount+ average 15 years younger than the platform's pre-UFC audience.
  • Roster reshuffle continues: The UFC released four more fighters — including an 18-1 standout — as the promotion clears space for emerging talent in the new broadcast era. Canada's PPV model also ends in January 2027 as numbered cards move to Paramount+.
