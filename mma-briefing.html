Top Story

Four days out: UFC's White House card battles a lawsuit, the heat, and history

UFC Freedom 250 lands Sunday, June 14, on the White House South Lawn — timed to President Trump's 80th birthday and America's 250th-anniversary celebrations — with Ilia Topuria defending the undisputed lightweight title against former interim champ Justin Gaethje, and Alex Pereira vs. Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight title in the co-main. A Pereira win would make him the UFC's first three-division champion. The seven-fight card starts at 8 PM ET on Paramount+ with a CBS simulcast.

The run-up is anything but quiet. The Public Integrity Project's federal lawsuit in D.C. — seeking to halt the event over claims the Interior Department and National Park Service unlawfully staged a private sporting event on public property without congressional consent or environmental review — is still pending; legal experts say a judge would have to block the event for it to succeed at this late stage. Meanwhile, the forecast calls for partly cloudy skies with high heat, humidity, and a slight risk of pop-up thunderstorms — the UFC says the show goes on regardless of weather, lightning excepted — and D.C. is bracing for extensive road closures around the White House all weekend.
