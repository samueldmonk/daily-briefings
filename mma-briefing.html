Top Story

Five days out: UFC Freedom 250 fight week builds — as a federal lawsuit tries to block the White House card

The UFC's most audacious event yet — UFC Freedom 250 on the South Lawn of the White House — is set for Sunday, June 14, billed as the first professional sporting event ever staged at the presidential residence. The seven-fight card is anchored by two title bouts: undisputed lightweight champion Ilia Topuria defends his belt against former interim lightweight champion Justin Gaethje in the main event, while former two-division champ Alex Pereira meets two-time heavyweight title challenger Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight belt in the co-main.

The rest of the card features Sean O'Malley vs. Aiemann Zahabi (bantamweight), Bo Nickal vs. Kyle Daukaus (middleweight), Mauricio Ruffy vs. Michael Chandler (lightweight), Diego Lopes vs. Steve Garcia (featherweight) and Derrick Lewis vs. Josh Hokit (heavyweight). If Pereira beats Gane, he becomes the first fighter to win UFC titles in three divisions. The event streams on Paramount+ with no pay-per-view — but it isn't a lock: on June 6, the anti-corruption watchdog Public Integrity Project filed a federal suit in the U.S. District Court for D.C. seeking to block the fights, arguing the event violates National Park Service rules, lacked Congressional approval and skipped a required environmental review. The promotion is still proceeding for June 14.

Around the Sport
  • Legal cloud over the lawn: the Public Integrity Project's June 6 federal suit seeks to halt UFC Freedom 250, contending the White House event was authorized unlawfully — the promotion is still proceeding for June 14.
  • Gabriel Bonfim's 50-45 shutout handed former welterweight champion Belal Muhammad a third straight loss on June 6, reshuffling the top of a crowded 170-pound division.
  • Three-division stakes: Alex Pereira can become the first fighter to hold UFC titles in three weight classes if he beats Ciryl Gane for the interim heavyweight belt.
  • The UFC's Paramount media era continues — Freedom 250 streams on Paramount+ with no traditional U.S. pay-per-view.
