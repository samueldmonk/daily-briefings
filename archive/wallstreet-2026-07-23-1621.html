The Lead

At the close: oil above $100 and AI capex angst sink stocks — Nasdaq drops 2.2%, Dow sheds 507

Wall Street closed broadly lower Thursday. The S&P 500 fell 1.2%, the Nasdaq Composite lost 2.2% and the Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 507 points, or 1% (CNBC). A surge in oil spurred by the escalating Iran war sent stocks and bonds lower, with the Street also rattled by renewed concerns over whether massive artificial-intelligence investments will pay off (Bloomberg).

The Nasdaq was bogged down by a roughly 7% drop in Alphabet and a 14% slide in Tesla following their earnings reports. Alphabet's quarter was fundamentally strong — but its capital-spending forecast was raised again, and Tesla's profit fell even after record deliveries, with Elon Musk calling 2026 a "massive capex year" (CNBC/Yahoo Finance).

Brent crude topped $100 a barrel for the first time since May 26 after Iran-backed Houthis said they attacked two Saudi tankers in the Red Sea; WTI traded near $90. The oil spike stoked inflation fears and drove Treasury yields to their highest levels of the year, pushing hike expectations — not cuts — for the Fed's next move. One bright spot: initial jobless claims fell to 187,000 versus 210,000 expected.

Movers & Drivers

Tumbled after Q2 profit fell despite record deliveries of more than 480,000 vehicles. EPS missed, and Musk said 2026 will be a "massive capex year" focused on Optimus robots, robotaxis and data centers (CNBC/Yahoo Finance).

Surged after a Q2 beat — EPS $7.94 vs. $7.19 expected, revenue $20.06B — and a raised full-year outlook as Mideast escalation lifts defense names. Quoted +11.3% on Yahoo's morning board.
