Top Story

McGregor's comeback meets Holloway's blueprint at UFC 329

Conor McGregor's long-awaited return headlines UFC 329 on July 11 at Las Vegas's T-Mobile Arena, and he draws one of the sport's most durable technicians. The former two-division champion meets No. 4-ranked welterweight Max Holloway in a 170-pound main event — a rematch more than a decade in the making, after McGregor won their 2013 meeting by unanimous decision — in what would be his first walk to the Octagon since a multi-year layoff. The bout headlines International Fight Week.

Thirteen years on, the market sees it differently: bookmakers install Holloway as the favorite at around −240, with McGregor a live underdog. Holloway opened as high as −400 a month ago and money poured in on McGregor's return — tightening the line to about −210 by early July before it drifted back toward −240 into fight week.
