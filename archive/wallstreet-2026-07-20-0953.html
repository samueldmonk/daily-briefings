The Lead

Stocks open higher as chips rebound and oil gives back its $90 spike — Big Tech earnings are the week's test

U.S. stocks rose Monday morning, with chip stocks advancing ahead of a heavy week of Big Tech results. Shortly after the open the Dow Jones Industrial Average was up about 0.3%, the S&P 500 about 0.5% and the Nasdaq Composite almost 0.8%, per Yahoo Finance's live coverage. That followed a firm pre-open: at roughly 7:30 AM ET S&P futures were +0.35%, Dow futures +0.23%, Nasdaq futures +0.76% and Russell 2000 futures +0.39%, with the VIX at 18.21, down 2.98%.

The geopolitical backdrop got worse over the weekend and the market shrugged. Yahoo Finance reports the U.S.–Iran war escalated again as the U.S. carried out its ninth straight day of attacks and Iran retaliated by bombing U.S. allies in the region, primarily Kuwait, with the two sides still far apart on a ceasefire. TheStreet, citing CNN, reports the death of a third American service member, killed in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone. What cooled the oil bid was diplomacy: Iranian state news agency IRNA, citing Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei, said Iran has received proposals from mediators about resuming negotiations with the U.S., and that negotiations could still be pursued if they align with Iran's national interests, while Iran would continue to defend itself "resolutely."

Crude reversed accordingly. As of about 7:21 AM ET, West Texas Intermediate was down 0.17% at $82.35 a barrel while Brent was up 0.50% at $88.54, having earlier surpassed $90. "Investors appear to be distinguishing between elevated geopolitical risk and the likelihood of a wider systemic shock," said Daniela Hathorn, senior market analyst at Capital.com. "For now, markets continue to view higher energy prices as the primary transmission mechanism, with inflation implications taking precedence over broader concerns about global growth." On the week ahead, Hathorn added that after last week's sharp rotation out of AI-related stocks, "investors will be looking for evidence that earnings can continue to justify elevated valuations," with geopolitics, central bank expectations and corporate earnings all competing for attention.

Movers & Drivers

UpNew

AMC Entertainment — the morning's biggest gainer

Shares of the world's largest movie theatre operator soared in pre-market trading after it beat Wall Street's quarterly earnings estimates. Yahoo Finance put the move at +16.4%; a separate pre-market screen had it at +17.7% and credited record Q2 revenue and adjusted EBITDA. Both figures are pre-market, not a session close.

UpNew

Steel Dynamics and Domino's Pizza

Steel Dynamics (STLD) rose nearly 10% pre-market ahead of its earnings report. Domino's Pizza (DPZ) gained 7.2% after topping Wall Street's revenue estimates — two of the clearest single-name moves in a session otherwise waiting on the megacaps.

UpNew

SpaceX sets a new Starship attempt for Thursday

SpaceX (SPCX) rose 1.62% to $125.99 in pre-market trading. The company has rescheduled the 13th test flight of its Starship rocket for Thursday, July 23, after a Raptor 3 engine ignition failure forced an automatic abort of the earlier attempt. SpaceX completed the largest IPO in history on June 12, raising $75 billion and briefly pushing its valuation above $2 trillion; the stock hit an all-time intraday high of $225.64 just three trading days later, well above where it trades now.

Down

Software and energy names on the other side

The pre-market losers were led by software and an oil producer: HubSpot (HUBS) fell 3.2%, Diamondback Energy (FANG) slipped 2.9% and Atlassian (TEAM) lost 2.8%.

On the Radar

  • Big Tech earnings all week. Alphabet, Tesla, Intel and IBM report later this week. Wall Street has raised its expectations, and the question investors are asking is whether these companies are monetizing AI at a pace that matches the build-out spending.
  • Intel is the sharpest single test. Q2 consensus sits at 22¢ EPS against a year-ago loss of 10¢, on revenue of roughly $14.4 billion, up about 11.6% year over year.
  • No Fed meeting this week. The FOMC decision comes July 28–29, at 2:00 PM ET, with a press conference at 2:30 and no Summary of Economic Projections at that meeting.
  • SpaceX Starship flight 13, Thursday, July 23 — rescheduled after a Raptor 3 ignition failure triggered an automatic abort.
  • The Iran conflict remains the swing factor for energy. Brent's move above and then back below $90 in a single overnight session is the clearest signal of how headline-driven the oil tape has become.
