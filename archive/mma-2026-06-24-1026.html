Fight week: Rafael Fiziev meets Manuel Torres atop UFC Baku on Saturday

The UFC returns to Azerbaijan this Saturday, June 27 with a lightweight main event between 11th-ranked Rafael Fiziev (13–5) and 15th-ranked Manuel Torres (17–3) at the National Gymnastics Arena in Baku. It is effectively a homecoming for the Azerbaijan-born Fiziev, who looks to rebound from a second-round knockout loss to Mauricio Ruffy at UFC 325 in January against a dangerous Torres — the Mexican finisher rides back-to-back first-round TKOs (Grant Dawson at UFC 323, Drew Dober before that). Bookmakers expect fireworks: the total is set at just 1.5 rounds, with the over juiced to −140. The line is tight: DraftKings lists it near pick'em at Fiziev −112 / Torres −108 this run, while Odds Shark's opening number had Fiziev a wider −135 favorite over Torres at +114. The main card streams on Paramount+ around 3 p.m. ET, with prelims earlier in the day.

The co-main is a middleweight striking clash between Shara "Bullet" Magomedov (16–1) and Michel Pereira, with a lightweight bout between Nazim Sadykhov and Matheus Camilo also on the main card. It follows Saturday's UFC Vegas 119, where Manel Kape avenged a prior loss to Kyoji Horiguchi and extended his finishing streak.

Prospect Watch

Murtazali Magomedov Prospect

The featherweight announced himself on his UFC debut at Vegas 119 with only the fourth twister finish in UFC history — and, per Yahoo Sports, the fastest — submitting Melsik Baghdasaryan in 1:17 of Round 1 and banking a $100,000 Performance of the Night bonus.

Ateba Gautier Prospect

The Cameroonian middleweight (11–1) is 2–0 in 2026 — a decision over Andrey Pulyaev at UFC 324 (Jan 24) and a second-round TKO of Ozzy Diaz at UFC 328 (May 9) — and remains one of the division's fastest risers.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement: Manel Kape pushes toward a flyweight title shot with a fourth consecutive finish, while debutant Murtazali Magomedov makes an immediate statement with a record-fast twister at featherweight.

Business & broadcast: The UFC's U.S. home is now Paramount+ under a seven-year, $7.7 billion agreement with TKO Group (about $1.1 billion in average annual value), carrying all 13 numbered events and 30 Fight Nights — with select numbered cards simulcast on CBS, ending the pay-per-view model. The deal is already showing up in results: TKO reported UFC revenue rose 12% year-over-year to $401.2 million in Q1 2026, lifted by higher media-rights fees as the Paramount agreement took effect in January.
