Top Story

Ransomware negotiator sentenced to 70 months for working with BlackCat New

Angelo Martino, 41, of Land O'Lakes, Florida — a professional ransomware negotiator — was sentenced to 70 months (nearly six years) in prison for conspiring with operators of the ALPHV/BlackCat ransomware group. Per the Justice Department, Martino negotiated on behalf of five ransomware victims while secretly feeding BlackCat their confidential negotiating positions, including insurance policy limits, so the attackers could maximize ransom demands.

Prosecutors said Martino also colluded with two other cybersecurity professionals — Ryan Goldberg, 41, of Georgia, and Kevin Martin, 36, of Texas — to deploy BlackCat ransomware against multiple U.S. victims between April and November 2023, extorting one victim for roughly $1.2 million in Bitcoin and splitting the proceeds. Law enforcement has seized about $10 million in assets from Martino to date, including cryptocurrency, vehicles, a food truck and a luxury fishing boat.

Sources: U.S. Department of Justice; The Hacker News (July 10)

⚠ Patch Priority — deadline today

Adobe ColdFusion — CVE-2026-48282 (CVSS 10.0): path traversal in the RDS FILEIO handler enabling unauthenticated remote code execution on ColdFusion 2025.9, 2023.20 and earlier. In-the-wild exploitation was captured within two hours of public details. Adobe's fix shipped June 30 in bulletin APSB26-68; CISA added the flaw to the KEV catalog on July 7 with a federal remediation deadline of today, July 10. Patch now.

Breaches & Incidents

FortiBleed: 86,644 firewalls compromised across 194 countries Campaign

The credential-compromise campaign against Fortinet FortiGate devices now counts 86,644 compromised firewalls across 194 countries — roughly half of all internet-facing FortiGates, per this week's research. Attackers assembled credentials from old breach dumps and infostealer logs, tested them at scale, then extracted config backups and cracked stored hashes offline. CISA urges immediate credential resets and session termination; prior sourced reporting links access sales to INC Ransom and Lynx affiliates.

Dutch regulator warns of AI-fueled attack surge New

A Dutch regulator warned July 10 of a massive surge in cyberattacks fueled by AI — the latest official body to flag machine-assisted phishing, reconnaissance and intrusion tooling as a step-change in attacker productivity.

Free VPN apps flunk privacy testing New

Researchers tested 281 of the most popular free VPN apps on the Google Play Store and found many fail at keeping user traffic private and secure — a reminder that "free" network security tooling is often the opposite.

Conduent: 62.2M people affected Ongoing

Business-services giant Conduent's breach disclosure — covering 62.2 million people, per reporting carried in prior editions this week — remains among the largest incidents of 2026 as notifications continue.
