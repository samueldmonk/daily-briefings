The Lead

Chips drag the Nasdaq to a 1.7% loss at the close as TSMC's record quarter draws selling — healthcare earnings cushion the Dow

For the second time in three days, blockbuster results from a dominant chipmaker were met with selling rather than buying. Taiwan Semiconductor reported record second-quarter results — profit up roughly 77% year over year — and raised its capital-spending outlook, but the stock fell more than 4% as investors questioned whether AI-driven valuations and heavy capex can keep being rewarded (Yahoo Finance/TheStreet). The drag rippled across the complex, and the Nasdaq Composite closed down 1.73% while the S&P 500 lost 0.78%. The Cboe Volatility Index rose about 6% to 16.73.

Healthcare kept the damage contained. Abbott jumped 12.5% — the day's biggest S&P mover — after beating on second-quarter earnings and raising full-year guidance; DexCom climbed about 7% and UnitedHealth added 5.6% on its own beat and raised outlook, holding the Dow's loss to just 0.48%. Technology was the worst sector (roughly −2.3%), while Consumer Staples, Health Care and Real Estate led the green; 358 S&P 500 names still finished higher. The backdrop stayed geopolitical, with the U.S. having struck Iran on Wednesday and oil holding near one-month highs. Netflix headlined the after-close calendar — and fell hard on soft guidance (Yahoo Finance/TheStreet/CNBC).
