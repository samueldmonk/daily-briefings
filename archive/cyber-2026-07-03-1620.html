Top Story

FortiBleed confirmed as a ransomware pipeline: stolen FortiGate credentials now feeding INC and Lynx

Researchers this week confirmed that the sprawling FortiBleed credential-theft campaign is feeding ransomware operations. Attackers run a custom Golang packet-sniffing tool dubbed "FortiGate Sniffer," which abuses FortiOS's built-in diagnose sniffer packet feature to intercept VPN and other authentication traffic directly off compromised Fortinet firewalls. The campaign has targeted roughly 430,000 FortiGate firewalls globally and harvested more than 110 million credentials; investigators tracked scanning against about 11,250 portals in 150+ countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets, and completed the full attack chain on 354 of them.

The ransomware link is the new part. According to The Hacker News, BleepingComputer and SOCRadar, an operator with access to FortiBleed infrastructure was found logged in to the negotiation panels of both the INC Ransom and Lynx crews — with INC's victim list overlapping the campaign's data — the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been tied directly to ransomware deployment. At least 12 ransomware deployments have followed, encrypting hundreds of endpoints, and internal documents point to a structured operation of roughly 20 people. Defenders should treat exposed appliances as compromised: rotate all FortiGate admin and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, review logs for the sniffer, and update FortiOS.

Patch Priority

Patch now: SimpleHelp CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0) — CISA deadline has passed

A maximum-severity OIDC authentication-bypass in SimpleHelp remote-support software accepts unsigned identity tokens, letting a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a fully authenticated technician session and stand up an admin account across managed endpoints. It is being actively exploited to deploy the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer (Arctic Wolf, Help Net Security and The Hacker News report in-the-wild attacks). Affected: SimpleHelp 5.5.15 and earlier and 6.0 pre-release builds; upgrade to the patched 5.5.16 / 6.0 build. The federal remediation deadline has already lapsed, so any unpatched, internet-exposed server should be treated as an emergency — and MSPs are a supply-chain amplifier here, since one compromised SimpleHelp server can reach every downstream customer.

CISA KEV: added Jun 29, 2026 → remediation was due July 2, 2026 (now overdue). Next imminent federal deadline: SharePoint CVE-2026-45659 — July 4. Risk-based BOD 26-04 assignment.

Threat Actor Spotlight

The FortiBleed broker → INC Ransom & Lynx Initial-Access Broker

The actor behind FortiBleed operates as an initial-access broker (IAB) — harvesting, cracking and stockpiling corporate credentials, then handing that access to ransomware crews. Investigators found a single operator running victim-negotiation panels for both INC Ransom and Lynx, and tied the stolen FortiGate access to real ransomware intrusions — at least 12 deployments so far. Leaked internal documents describe a structured, roughly 20-person operation, with a small core of primary operators driving the high-impact intrusions and a back-office layer of junior operators and support. The takeaway for defenders: any organization whose FortiGate credentials appear in the FortiBleed dataset should assume it is now a ransomware target, not merely a credential-exposure statistic.

Breaches & Incidents

ChocoPoC RAT hides in fake exploit code

YesWeHack and Sekoia disclosed ChocoPoC (Jul 1): a Python remote-access trojan smuggled into trojanized proof-of-concept exploit repos on GitHub. Instead of embedding malware in the exploit itself, it adds a malicious PyPI dependency, so it slips past a quick code review. At least 7 PoC repos — posing as exploits for FortiWeb, PAN-OS, Ivanti Sentry, Check Point VPN and more — target vulnerability researchers, lifting browser passwords, cookies and files and using a Mapbox dataset as a dead-drop C2. Servers were still live as of the report.

SimpleHelp exploited to drop Djinn Stealer

The CVSS 10.0 SimpleHelp auth bypass (CVE-2026-48558) is being exploited in the wild to plant two malware families — the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer, which harvests cloud, source-control, browser, SSH and crypto-wallet credentials across Windows, macOS and Linux (Arctic Wolf, The Hacker News). CISA's remediation deadline passed July 2 and is now overdue.

Microsoft SharePoint under active attack

CISA added SharePoint RCE CVE-2026-45659 to its KEV catalog after confirming exploitation. Any authenticated attacker — no elevated privileges required — can achieve remote code execution via unsafe deserialization; Microsoft shipped fixes in its May 2026 updates. Federal deadline: July 4 (tomorrow).

RCEKEV

Scattered Spider suspect extradited

Peter Stokes, 19, a dual U.S.–Estonian citizen accused of belonging to the Scattered Spider crew, was extradited from Finland to the U.S. and appeared in a Chicago federal court to face conspiracy, computer-intrusion and fraud charges (DOJ, July 1). Prosecutors tie the group — linked to 100+ intrusions and $100M+ in extortion — to a May 2025 raid on a luxury-jewelry retailer that carried an $8M ransom demand.
