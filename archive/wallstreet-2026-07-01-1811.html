The Lead

Dow taps a record, then fades to flat as Burry's Caterpillar short and a chip selloff cap Wall Street's Q3 open

U.S. stocks opened the second half on a mixed-to-lower note Wednesday, a day after the major indexes wrapped their strongest quarter since 2020. The Dow Jones Industrial Average tagged a fresh intraday record — 52,742.66 — before fading to essentially unchanged at the close (52,305.24, −0.03%); the S&P 500 slipped about 0.2%; and the Nasdaq Composite was the clear laggard (−0.66% to 26,040.03) as investors dumped semiconductors to lock in a blistering first-half run. Communication-services and financial names did the lifting, while chip and AI-infrastructure stocks did the dragging.

The marquee move belonged to Meta, up roughly 8–9% (as much as ~10% intraday) after it unveiled plans to sell excess AI computing capacity through a new cloud unit — a pivot that pressured "neocloud" rivals CoreWeave (~−11%) and Nebius (~−13%) and glass-and-hardware supplier Corning (down double digits). The Dow was knocked off its high by Caterpillar, which sank about 6.9% after "Big Short" investor Michael Burry disclosed a bet against the stock — even though CAT remains up ~73% year-to-date on demand for the power gear that feeds AI data centers. Traders also digested a cooler June ADP payrolls report (+98,000 vs. ~113,000 expected), a softer ISM manufacturing read (PMI 53.3; prices-paid down to 73.0 from 82.1), oil near a four-month low, and remarks from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh at the ECB's Sintra forum on central banking.
