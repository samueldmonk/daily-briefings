Top Story

ShinyHunters weaponize an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day against higher education

Google's Mandiant and Threat Intelligence Group have tied a sweeping intrusion-and-extortion campaign to UNC6240 — the group known as ShinyHunters — which exploited CVE-2026-35273, an unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw (CVSS 9.8) in Oracle PeopleSoft's PeopleTools. The bug requires no login and no user interaction — only network access over HTTP — to fully take over the server.

The attackers were active between May 27 and June 9, weeks before Oracle pushed an out-of-band advisory on June 10. ShinyHunters claims to have compromised over 100 organizations across roughly 300 PeopleSoft instances, with about 68% being universities and colleges, predominantly in the United States. The University of Nottingham was the first to publicly confirm a breach, with 454,600 current and former students' personal and billing records — some 40 GB — already posted to the group's leak site.

CISA added CVE-2026-35273 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 12, with a federal remediation deadline of July 3. Defenders running PeopleTools 8.61/8.62 should patch and hunt for prior compromise immediately.

Splunk Enterprise — CVE-2026-20253 (CVSS 9.8)

A missing-authentication flaw (CWE-306) in Splunk Enterprise's PostgreSQL sidecar lets an unauthenticated, network-reachable attacker create or truncate arbitrary files — a path to remote code execution. CISA confirmed active exploitation and added it to the KEV catalog on June 18 — the first Splunk CVE ever listed — with a remediation deadline of Sunday, June 21. Patch affected 10.0 / 10.2 branches to 10.0.7 / 10.2.4 this weekend. Also note the maximum-severity Joomla JCE flaw (CVE-2026-48907, CVSS 10.0) is now past its June 19 deadline — remediate immediately if still unpatched.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters / UNC6240

The data-extortion crew behind the PeopleSoft campaign continues an aggressive 2026, leaning on zero-days and stolen OAuth tokens to mass-harvest enterprise data before naming victims on its leak infrastructure. Beyond the 100-plus PeopleSoft targets, the group is listing additional alleged victims — including an unverified claim of 8.8 TB stolen from Amazon's One Medical, with a June 22 negotiation deadline (Amazon has not confirmed it), and a Kodak data set (2.2 million records, which Kodak characterizes as "limited"). Mandiant tracks the activity as UNC6240. A separate Salesforce OAuth-theft wave was attributed this week to a distinct actor, "Icarus" — not ShinyHunters.

"FortiBleed" — Fortinet credential exposure

A sweeping campaign exposed admin and SSL-VPN credentials cracked from config files on internet-facing FortiGate firewalls; counts reached 86,644 devices across 194 countries by June 19, and CISA issued a hardening advisory June 18 urging credential resets, session termination and phishing-resistant MFA. Attributed to Russian-speaking actors.

Salesforce disabled the Klue Battlecards app integration after detecting unusual activity tied to a June 11 security incident at the competitive-intelligence firm that may have exposed a subset of customer data. Huntress is among the named victims; tracked as actor "Icarus."
