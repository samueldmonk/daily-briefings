Top Story

ShinyHunters' PeopleSoft zero-day keeps tearing through higher education

Google's Mandiant has tied a sprawling extortion campaign to UNC6240 (ShinyHunters) exploiting CVE-2026-35273, a critical (CVSS 9.8) flaw in Oracle PeopleSoft that requires no login and no user interaction. The bug was a true zero-day — abused between May 27 and June 9, before Oracle published its advisory on June 10.

The group claims more than 100 organisations compromised across roughly 300 PeopleSoft instances, with about two-thirds being universities and colleges, predominantly in the U.S. The University of Nottingham has acknowledged a breach, and the group has already published 454,600 current and former students' records to its leak site. CISA added the CVE to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 12, with a federal remediation deadline of July 3.

Splunk Enterprise — CVE-2026-20253 (CVSS 9.8)

A missing-authentication flaw in a PostgreSQL sidecar service lets an unauthenticated, remote attacker create or truncate files — chainable to pre-auth remote code execution. It is actively exploited (the first Splunk bug ever added to CISA's KEV) and the federal remediation deadline is Sunday, June 21. Affected: Splunk Enterprise 10.0.0–10.0.6 and 10.2.0–10.2.3; patch to 10.0.7 / 10.2.4 / 10.4.0. The gap from public proof-of-concept (June 12) to confirmed exploitation was under a week. (Joomla JCE and LiteSpeed cPanel deadlines, below, are already overdue.)

Threat Actor Spotlight

The Gentlemen (RaaS) — a homegrown EDR-killing arsenal

New ESET research details The Gentlemen, a ransomware-as-a-service crew that emerged in late 2025 and became one of 2026's most active gangs. Its centerpiece is GentleKiller, an in-house framework with at least eight variants that abuse vulnerable or malicious drivers (BYOVD) to blind security software — targeting more than 400 processes across roughly 48 security products (Microsoft, CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Sophos, ESET, Kaspersky and more). It also bundles three external EDR killers: HexKiller (abuses a Baidu Antivirus driver), ThrottleBlood (a TechPowerUp driver) and HavocKiller (first disclosed by Huntress in March 2026).

The crew has been tied to roughly 478 victims with a globally distributed victimology skewed toward Southeast Asia, South America and Western Europe rather than the usual U.S.-heavy targeting, and runs an aggressive affiliate model advertising a 90% cut of ransoms. Investigative reporter Brian Krebs has published evidence pointing to the operator's real-world identity.

Breaches & Incidents

Malicious JetBrains Marketplace plugins steal AI keys

Aikido Security flagged 15 plugins across 7 vendor accounts — posing as AI coding assistants and Git tools — that exfiltrate developers' AI provider API keys over HTTP. Downloaded close to 70,000 times since October 2025 and pushed as recently as June 10; JetBrains has since pulled all 15 and terminated the seven vendor accounts.

Klue OAuth breach → "Icarus" Salesforce theft

Competitive-intelligence platform Klue was breached via a long-dormant API credential; the actor tracked as Icarus abused OAuth tokens from Klue's Battlecards integration to pull customers' Salesforce CRM data. Huntress is among the victims; Salesforce disabled the Klue integration.

ShinyHunters extortion: Kodak listed, Nottingham data dumped

Beyond the PeopleSoft campaign, ShinyHunters' leak site lists Kodak among extortion targets — a claimed 2.2M+ records, which Kodak characterizes as limited data — while the group has already published 454,600 University of Nottingham student records from the PeopleSoft breach.

"FortiBleed" exposes Fortinet credentials at scale

Researchers tallied roughly 86,644 FortiGate devices across 194 countries with credentials recoverable from configuration files; CISA issued an advisory (~74,000 devices) on June 18. A large share are internet-facing.
