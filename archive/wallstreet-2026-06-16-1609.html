The Lead

A split close: the Dow rings the bell at a record while tech drags the S&P 500 and Nasdaq to the day's lows

Wall Street split sharply down the middle Tuesday as a rotation out of last week's technology leaders deepened into the close. The Dow Jones Industrial Average finished as the only major index in the green — up about 0.74% to a fresh record high — while the S&P 500 (down ~0.40%), the Nasdaq Composite (down ~0.81%) and the Russell 2000 (down ~0.63%) all closed at session lows as megacap technology and chip names sold off. In the words of one market wrap, "today is the opposite of yesterday": after tech jumped Monday on the Iran framework, it was virtually every other sector — financials, industrials and cyclicals — making a leg higher Tuesday, with Dow components Caterpillar (~+2.2%), American Express (~+1.7%) and Goldman Sachs (~+1.3%) doing the heavy lifting.

The pullback caps a roaring start to the holiday-shortened week. On Monday, the S&P 500 jumped 1.65%, the Nasdaq Composite surged 3.07% and the Dow climbed 0.92% (+468.77 points), as President Trump announced an agreement to wind down the U.S.–Iran conflict and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, sending oil sharply lower and easing the inflation worries hanging over the rate path. Reports Tuesday said the two sides had signed a memorandum of understanding to solidify the ceasefire and frame further talks — though a formal signing ceremony is still reported for Friday in Switzerland, and Iran has not publicly confirmed final terms.

The bigger event lands Wednesday, when the FOMC delivers its decision alongside a fresh "dot plot" and new Chair Kevin Warsh holds his first press conference. Futures markets price a near-certain hold of the target rate at 3.50–3.75%, leaving traders to parse the tone of Warsh's debut rather than the level. A weak May housing-starts report — down 15.4% to the slowest pace since 2020 — added a note of caution beneath the calm, and May retail-sales data is due Wednesday morning.

Movers & Drivers

SpaceX (SPCX) ▲ ~20%

The newly public rocket maker soared about 20% intraday — its market value topping $2.8 trillion and overtaking Amazon — after agreeing to acquire Anysphere, the startup behind the AI coding tool Cursor, for $60 billion in an all-stock deal expected to close in the third quarter pending regulatory approval. The surge bucked a softer tape and extended a blistering post-IPO run; Cursor carries about $2.6 billion in annualized revenue.

Fox (FOXA) New ▼ ~15%

Fox tumbled roughly 15% after unveiling a $22 billion cash-and-stock deal to acquire streaming platform Roku. Roku holders would receive $160 a share ($96 in cash plus Fox stock), with Fox shareholders owning about 73% of the combined company. The deal isn't expected to close until the first half of 2027 and needs regulatory and shareholder approval.

CoreWeave (CRWV) New ▲ ~9.6%

The AI-infrastructure provider jumped about 9.6% to roughly $117 after confirmation it will join the Nasdaq-100, effective June 22 — a move that should draw fresh index-fund and ETF buying. The company is carrying a near-$100B revenue backlog but also heavy leverage after a recent $3.5B high-yield note sale.

Yum! Brands (YUM) New

Yum agreed to sell Pizza Hut for about $2.7 billion in two deals: roughly $1.5 billion for the ex-China business to private-equity firm LongRange Capital (plus a potential $75M earn-out) and about $1.2 billion for Pizza Hut China to Yum China. Yum expects ~$2.3 billion in net proceeds; the transactions are slated to close in the third quarter, pending regulatory approval.

Airlines & cruises ▲ ~3–4%

Fuel-sensitive travel names rallied as oil held near a two-month low: United Airlines climbed about 3.9%, Norwegian Cruise Line added ~3.7% and Carnival rose ~3.2%, helping cyclical and value corners offset the tech slide.

Robinhood (HOOD) ▼ ~1.5%

The trading platform slipped about 1.5% after saying it would cut roughly 10% of its full-time workforce — about 290 jobs — in a cost-cutting move.
