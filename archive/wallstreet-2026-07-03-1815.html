The Lead

Wall Street is dark for Independence Day — the Dow closed the holiday week at a record even as chips dragged the Nasdaq lower

U.S. equity and bond markets are closed all day Friday, July 3 in observance of Independence Day (July 4 falls on Saturday); trading resumes Monday, July 6 at 9:30 AM ET. There is no session today, so all figures below reflect the most recent close on Thursday, July 2.

That session split the tape: the Dow rose 594.83 points (+1.14%) to a fresh record 52,900.07, while the Nasdaq Composite fell 0.80% to 25,832.67 in a second-straight semiconductor selloff and the S&P 500 finished essentially flat at 7,483.24. The driver: a much weaker-than-expected June jobs report — payrolls grew just 57,000 versus roughly 115,000 expected, with unemployment ticking down to 4.2% — which pushed traders to unwind bets on a near-term Fed rate hike and sent money rotating out of AI chips and into Dow cyclicals.
