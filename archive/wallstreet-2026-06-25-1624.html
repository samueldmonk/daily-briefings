The Lead
As of the close · ~4:00 PM ET

The Dow prints a record the Nasdaq paid for

Wall Street split down the middle to end Thursday. The Dow closed at a fresh all-time high — up roughly 0.6% (about +314 points), touching an intraday peak near 52,655 that eclipsed the June 16 record (51,999.67) — powered by a rotation out of megacap technology and into industrials, financials and health care. The Nasdaq Composite slipped about 0.46% and the S&P 500 finished around the flat line.

The drag came from the Magnificent Seven: Apple fell roughly 6% after raising MacBook and iPad prices to offset surging memory costs, and Microsoft dropped about 3% on its own Xbox and consumer price hikes, with Nvidia, Oracle and Amazon also down ~2%. Offsetting them, Caterpillar jumped ~5%, with JPMorgan (~+2%) and Johnson & Johnson (~+1%) carrying the value trade. A blowout from Micron kept the chip rally alive. On the data front, May PCE inflation rose to a 4.1% headline rate (its highest since April 2023) while core held at 3.4%, in line with forecasts.
