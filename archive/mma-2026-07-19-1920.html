Top Story

Du Plessis batters Usman in Oklahoma City — and walks into a title queue that already has a line

Dricus Du Plessis made what UFC.com called "a hasty return to the win column," taking a unanimous decision over Kamaru Usman on Saturday night at Paycom Center. The scorecards read 50-45, 49-46, 49-46. It was Du Plessis's first appearance since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev last August.

The fight was one-sided in the way that matters. Per UFC's recap, the South African's physicality and power were too much: he wobbled Usman multiple times in the first two rounds with clubbing strikes and well-timed knees up the middle, and although Usman weathered the storm and warmed into the fight, "Stillknocks" stayed on him, hurting him again in the fourth and holding his edge to the final horn. Usman is a former welterweight champion who defended that title five times, with signature wins over Jorge Masvidal and Colby Covington.

The awkward part is what comes next. UFC's own recap lists "a potential trilogy bout with current champ Sean Strickland, whom he's beaten twice before" among Du Plessis's options — and Du Plessis has argued that the winner of his next bout should get the belt, leaning on those two prior wins over the champion. But Nassourdine Imavov has been waiting close to a year on the strength of a five-fight win streak, and he has publicly dismissed the suggestion that Du Plessis leapfrogs him, maintaining he was promised the first shot at Strickland after the belt changed hands in Newark in May.

No title fight has been booked. What exists is a champion with two losses to a contender who just looked excellent, and a separate contender with a stronger queue position and a year of waiting behind him.

Prospect Watch

ProspectStrawweight

Fatima Kline punches her ticket to the top 10

"The Archangel" swept Tabatha Ricci 30-27 across all three cards, landing the better strikes while mixing in timely takedowns. She is now 4-0 since returning to 115 pounds following a promotional debut a division up, and UFC expects another step up in competition in the back half of the year.

ProspectFeatherweight

Tommy McMillen sets a record on the way to a third-round finish

The undefeated featherweight pressed a breakneck pace for three rounds against Alberto Montes, mixing in body shots before forcing the late stoppage. UFC credits him with the record for most significant strikes landed in a three-round bout at 145 pounds.

ProspectLight heavyweight

Felipe Franco stops Levi Rodrigues Jr. in the second

UFC's own bonus coverage calls Franco "a prospect to keep an eye on at 205 pounds." He left Oklahoma City with a Performance of the Night award for the finish.

ProspectHeavyweight

The undefeated heavyweight, 27 and only around 16 months into his professional career, needed 1:40 to stop Hines by strikes in his first Octagon appearance.

Around the Sport

  • Christian Leroy Duncan is on a five-fight run. The Gloucester middleweight out-worked longtime contender Jared Cannonier for what UFC called the biggest win of his career, posting consecutive victories over ranked opponents. UFC expects him to "fight forward again next time out."
  • Chase Hooper went back to grappling and it worked immediately. A straight left set up the level change; from there he took the back, laced a body triangle and finished. It snapped a two-fight slide and was his ninth UFC win and seventh finish in the Octagon.
  • Jared Cannonier's slide continues. The former title challenger had his moments and steadied himself repeatedly, but per UFC's recap there was simply too much offence coming from Duncan to overcome.
  • The Paris card still has no main event. UFC's events listing carries a September 5 Fight Night at the Accor Arena with the headline bout listed as TBD vs TBD, though Michael Page and Nursulton Ruziboev appear among the athletes attached to the card.
