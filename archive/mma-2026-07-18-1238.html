Top Story

Du Plessis vs. Usman headlines the UFC's first Oklahoma City card — two former champions, one middleweight crossroads

UFC Fight Night: du Plessis vs. Usman takes place tonight, July 18, at the Paycom Center in Oklahoma City. It is a 12-fight card, with prelims from 5 PM ET and the main card at 8 PM ET on Paramount+.

The main event pairs two former champions at middleweight. Dricus du Plessis, ranked second at 185 pounds, fights for the first time since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev at UFC 319 — a belt Chimaev has since lost to Sean Strickland. Opposite him is Kamaru Usman, the former welterweight king, ranked ninth, stepping up to middleweight for his first bout in over a year.

Du Plessis is the favourite, but the line has tightened significantly through fight week. He is currently −205 at DraftKings with Usman at +170, having opened as steep as −245 at some books. The betting split is instructive: du Plessis is drawing about 67% of bets, but Usman is taking roughly 51% of the handle — the sharper money is on the underdog.

The co-main event is another middleweight bout, Jared Cannonier (+310) vs. Christian Leroy Duncan (−395).

Around the Sport

  • Tonight is a first for Oklahoma City. The UFC has never staged an event at Paycom Center before.
  • Usman's weight-class move. The former welterweight champion competes at middleweight tonight, in his first appearance in over a year.
  • Du Plessis has unfinished business at 185. He enters ranked second, with the belt now held by Sean Strickland, who took it from the man who beat du Plessis.
  • Ankalaev's opponent changed. Bogdan Guskov replaced Khalil Rountree Jr. for the July 25 Abu Dhabi main event after Rountree withdrew injured.
  • The McGregor era question. After a 69-second loss in his comeback, the post-UFC 329 conversation has turned to whether McGregor fights again at all.

Rankings & Business

  • Rankings movement: tonight's main event is a #2 vs. #9 middleweight matchup — du Plessis ranked second, Usman ninth.
  • Business: UFC 329 set a promotional record gate of $26,430,566, surpassing the previous high set at UFC 306 in September 2024.
  • Broadcast: tonight's card streams on Paramount+, with prelims at 5 PM ET and the main card at 8 PM ET.
  • Champions in waiting: light heavyweight champion Carlos Ulberg has not defended his title since winning it in April; he tore his ACL shortly after UFC 327.
