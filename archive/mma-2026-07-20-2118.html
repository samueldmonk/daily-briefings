Top Story

Guskov in, Rountree out: Abu Dhabi’s main event changes hands on under two weeks’ notice

The light heavyweight bout headlining UFC Fight Night 282 this Saturday was booked in June as Magomed Ankalaev vs. Khalil Rountree Jr. It is no longer that fight. Less than two weeks out, Rountree withdrew with an injury, and Bogdan Guskov has stepped in opposite Ankalaev — a change UFC.com, Yahoo Sports, Sports Illustrated and The National all confirm.

Guskov was not the promotion’s first call. Former UFC light heavyweight champion Jan Błachowicz, who had been scheduled to face Guskov the following week, was asked to move up on short notice but said he could not make the weight limit with one week to go before his own booking. That is how the fight fell to Guskov instead.

The Uzbek is ranked 10th at 205 pounds and arrives with a striking profile: 18 wins, every one of them by finish, including 13 first-round stoppages, and he is unbeaten in his last five. The betting market is unmoved by that resume against this opponent — Ankalaev opened around −500 at DraftKings with Guskov +340, and other books list roughly −460 / +360, implied probabilities near 80% / 20%.

A note on sourcing: several fight-card references still list Rountree as the headliner — including the event’s own Wikipedia page as fetched this run, which carries the original June booking in its main-event line. That listing predates the withdrawal and is not used here. The main event on this page follows UFC.com’s announcement.

Prospect Watch

ProspectLight heavyweight

Felipe Franco

Took Performance of the Night on his return to 205 pounds with a dominant second-round stoppage of Levi Rodrigues Jr. After settling in, Franco mixed striking with timely takedowns and repeatedly advanced to dominant positions on the mat. It was his first UFC win, and UFC.com’s own write-up calls him “a prospect to keep an eye on at 205 pounds.”

ProspectFeatherweight

Tommy McMillen

Kept the pressure on from the opening bell and wore down Alberto Montes for a third-round TKO on the prelims. Montes had moments early, but McMillen’s pace, body work and high-volume striking took over as the fight went on, with a late surge sealing the stoppage. UFC.com describes it as a record-setting performance from the unbeaten featherweight.

NewLight heavyweight

Bogdan Guskov

Not a newcomer, but a fighter about to get the biggest platform of his career on ten days’ notice. Ranked 10th at light heavyweight, the Uzbek carries 18 wins, all by finish — 13 of them in the first round — and is unbeaten in his last five. He is a heavy underdog on Saturday; his path is the one it has always been.

Quillan Salkilld was surfaced again this run by a prospect search citing his UFC 325 submission of Jamie Mullarkey as his most recent win. That is not current — his latest fight is a first-round TKO of Beneil Dariush at UFC Perth on May 2, 2026 — so the item was rejected rather than published.

Around the Sport

  • Irene Aldana has retired at 38. Her record stands at 15–8 overall, 8–5 in the UFC. ESPN Deportes reported it first; Aldana then confirmed and released the letter she had sent Dana White back in April. The decision traces to Noche UFC 2024 and a unanimous-decision loss to Norma Dumont in which she suffered one of the worst cuts in UFC history, requiring multiple surgeries. She is no longer ranking-eligible and has been removed from the top 15.
  • Jan Błachowicz was the first call for Abu Dhabi. The former light heavyweight champion — already booked against Guskov the following week — was asked to step up on short notice against Ankalaev but said he could not make the weight limit with a week to go before his own fight.
  • Khalil Rountree Jr.’s withdrawal is the second high-profile main-event reshuffle of the month and leaves Ankalaev, a former champion, facing a stylistically different opponent than the one he prepared for.
