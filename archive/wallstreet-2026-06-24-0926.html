The Lead

Wall Street points higher at Wednesday's open as memory chips rebound from a two-day rout — all eyes on Micron's earnings after the bell (morning, June 24)

U.S. stocks are set to try to claw back ground Wednesday morning after a two-session, AI-driven selloff, with stock-index futures pointing higher — Nasdaq 100 futures up about 0.4% and S&P 500 futures slightly higher — as battered memory-chip and semiconductor shares stabilize ahead of the cash open. The bounce is led by Micron, which rose roughly 4% in pre-market trading after closing down about 11.4% Tuesday (around 13% at its intraday low); the memory maker is up more than 250% year to date and reports fiscal third-quarter results after today's close, the week's marquee corporate catalyst. The setup follows Tuesday's losses, when the Nasdaq Composite closed down 2.21% at 25,587.04, the S&P 500 fell 1.44% to 7,365.46, the Dow finished essentially flat (−0.09%) at 51,666.84 and the Russell 2000 slipped 0.78% to 2,980.82, dropping back below 3,000.

The morning's biggest single-stock story is well away from the AI trade: Wendy's surged about 24% in pre-market trading after appointing former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer and becoming the latest target of a retail "meme-stock" short squeeze, ranking among the most-mentioned names on Reddit trading forums. Sunrun jumped about 19% and Churchill Downs added about 7%, while on the downside Hertz tumbled about 22% after launching a $100 million stock offering, Silgan fell about 9.5% and Cerebras Systems slid about 9%, extending its post-earnings drop. Macro and rates stay in the frame: Treasury yields remain elevated — the 10-year hovers near 4.5% — after Bank of America this week became the latest house to call for three quarter-point Fed rate hikes in 2026, while oil stays soft, with WTI near $74, after Washington granted Tehran a 60-day oil-sales waiver amid reported U.S.–Iran progress. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said Wednesday he is confident the economy can return to 3% growth as the Iran conflict winds down. Beyond Micron tonight, the week's key macro test is Thursday's May PCE inflation report.

Movers & Drivers

Wendy's — meme squeeze + new CFO ▲ ≈ +24% pre-mktNew

The burger chain soared about 24% in pre-market trading on a combination of a retail "meme-stock" short squeeze and the appointment of former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer. It ranked among the most-mentioned tickers on Reddit trading forums over the prior 24 hours.

Micron — rebound before tonight's report ▲ ≈ +4% pre-mkt

Micron rose roughly 4% pre-market, recovering part of Tuesday's ~11.4% closing slide, ahead of its fiscal-Q3 earnings after today's close. FactSet consensus models about $20.83 adjusted EPS on ~$35.75B revenue, with a record gross margin near 81% and HBM capacity sold out through 2026.

Hertz — stock offering ≈ −22% pre-mktNew

Hertz Global tumbled about 22% pre-market after announcing a SEC-registered common-stock offering of roughly $100 million, diluting holders. Sunrun (+19%) and Churchill Downs (+7%) were among the other big pre-market gainers.

Cerebras — post-earnings hangover ≈ −9% pre-mkt

The newly public AI-chip maker fell about 9% pre-market, extending losses after its first quarterly report since its May IPO — a 94% revenue jump to $193.4M and a raised full-year outlook still undercut by softer gross-margin guidance and a stretched post-IPO valuation.
