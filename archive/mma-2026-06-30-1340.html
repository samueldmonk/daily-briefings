McGregor Returns After Five Years; Challenges Holloway in Welterweight Debut at UFC 329

Jul 11, 2026 · T-Mobile Arena, Las Vegas
Conor McGregor will return to competition for the first time since breaking his leg at UFC 264 (Jul 2021) when he faces former featherweight and lightweight champion Max Holloway in a welterweight bout headlining UFC 329 during International Fight Week in Las Vegas. McGregor's comeback has been the subject of intense speculation and fan anticipation; the matchup with Holloway marks a historic clash of former champions and a potential springboard for either fighter into title contention. UFC Hall of Fame induction ceremony scheduled Jul 9 (inductees: Dominick Cruz, Demetrious Johnson). Full Fight Week runs Jul 9–12.

AROUND THE SPORT
  • Freedom 250 Historic Event (Jun 14): UFC hosted first-ever professional sporting event at the White House grounds. Main card: Ilia Topuria lost lightweight title to Justin Gaethje (TKO4); co-main saw Alex Pereira fall to Ciryl Gane (KO2) in interim heavyweight title bout. A Pereira win would have made him the first three-division UFC champion.
  • Lightweight Title Change: Justin Gaethje dethroned Ilia Topuria at Freedom 250, claiming the undisputed lightweight belt. Gaethje moves to prominent contention with the title; Topuria faces a potential move up or title rematch bid.
  • Interim Heavyweight New Champ: Ciryl Gane earned the interim heavyweight title by defeating Alex Pereira (KO2) at Freedom 250. Gane's interim status may be resolved by undisputed champ Tom Aspinall's next title defense, potentially setting up a unification bout in late 2026.
  • Hall of Fame Class 2026: Dominick Cruz and Demetrious Johnson inducted Jul 9 during International Fight Week. Both legends remain active in UFC media and analysis post-fighting careers.
