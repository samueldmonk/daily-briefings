The Lead

Memory chips power a broad rebound — Nasdaq closes up 1.29%, snapping a three-day slide

US stocks finished higher across the board Tuesday, ending a three-session losing streak. At the close the Nasdaq Composite rose 1.29% to 25,837.21, the S&P 500 added 0.89% to 7,509.20, and the Dow gained 385.38 points, or 0.74%, to 52,224.64. The Cboe Volatility Index eased about 5.6% to roughly 17.7 — the risk-on signature confirmed across the tape.

The engine was memory. Micron jumped about 12% — Morgan Stanley told clients memory prices could rise as much as 25% on AI demand and lifted its price target to $1,200 (Overweight) — helping push the PHLX Semiconductor Index up about 4.9%, a second straight advance, with Teradyne +11.2% and Western Digital +10.7% riding the same memory bid. Earnings did the rest of the work: 3M surged more than 7% after a beat, and General Motors rose about 2% after topping its second-quarter estimates, with a broad run of profit beats reinforcing the bid.

Geopolitics stayed the counterweight. The US has now carried out a 10th consecutive day of strikes on Iran, with another tanker reportedly hit near the Strait of Hormuz — enough to push Brent up about 2.1% to roughly $91 and drive the 10-year Treasury yield to a two-month high near 4.64% on renewed inflation worry. That rate backup was the tape's main headwind; even so, investors are positioning into a heavy earnings week, with Texas Instruments due Wednesday and Alphabet, IBM and Tesla all reporting in the days ahead.

Movers & Drivers

New▲ ~90%

Utz Brands — taken private by Intersnack at a 91% premium

Shares of the snack maker surged almost 90% to about $14 Tuesday morning after agreeing to be acquired by Germany's Intersnack Group for $14.25 a share in cash — a premium of roughly 91% to Monday's close, valuing the transaction at approximately $2.9 billion enterprise value. On completion Utz becomes privately held, jointly owned 50/50 by Intersnack and the Rice and Lissette family entities, and delists from the NYSE. Intersnack, founded in Germany in 1968, reported roughly $5 billion of 2025 sales across 31 countries and currently has no US presence. The deal is expected to close in Q4, subject to regulatory approval.

▲ ~12%Chips

Micron leads a memory melt-up

Micron jumped about 12% after Morgan Stanley told clients memory prices could rise as much as 25% on AI demand and raised its price target to $1,200 (Overweight). The call powered a broad memory bid: Teradyne rose 11.2%, Western Digital gained 10.7%, SanDisk soared about 14% and AMD rose roughly 8% on a Microsoft AI deal, lifting the PHLX Semiconductor Index about 4.9% — its second straight advance.

New▲ 7%Earnings

3M surges on an earnings beat

3M gained more than 7% — among the Dow's biggest boosts on the day — after reporting quarterly results that exceeded expectations. It was one of a run of industrial and chip beats that, alongside the memory rally, powered Tuesday's advance.

New▲ ~17%

Nebius rallies on an Nvidia stake

The Amsterdam-based AI cloud company surged about 17% after Nvidia disclosed a 9.3% ownership stake in a Schedule 13G filing. Nebius has become one of Europe's leading "neoclouds" supplying AI compute, a category the market has been repricing sharply on any sign of hyperscaler or chipmaker endorsement.

New▼ ~7%Earnings

Equifax slides on soft guidance

Equifax fell about 7% after issuing third-quarter guidance below Wall Street expectations — one of the day's few notable decliners against a broadly higher tape.

New▲ 7%Earnings

Hasbro beats and raises

Hasbro gained about 7% after a second-quarter beat released before the open — adjusted EPS $1.28 vs $1.14 expected and revenue up 16% to $1.14 billion.

▲ ~2%Earnings

General Motors beats on its Q2 print

GM rose about 2% to roughly $77 after beating Wall Street's second-quarter top- and bottom-line estimates and raising its full-year outlook, per this run's close coverage — a steady gain on an earnings beat rather than one of the day's outsized movers.

New▼ ~14%Earnings

Danaher and MSCI tumble on results

Danaher dropped about 13.7% after issuing weaker-than-expected third-quarter revenue guidance, and MSCI fell roughly 10.7% after missing second-quarter estimates — the two sharpest decliners on an otherwise higher tape, alongside Equifax's soft-guidance slide.

After-Hours Movers

NewEarnings

Capital One beats on strong credit

Capital One (COF) posted a clear second-quarter beat after the close: adjusted EPS of $5.81 topped the roughly $4.77 consensus by about 22%, on revenue of $15.85 billion (up about 27% year over year) and an improved 3.23% net charge-off rate as the lender integrates Discover. On the Radar

  • FOMC, July 28–29. Officials have entered the pre-meeting blackout period. Policymakers are widely expected to leave the federal funds rate unchanged at 3.50–3.75%.
  • The market is pricing a hike, not a cut. Traders continue to price one Fed rate increase this year, with the probability of a September move holding above 60% — a repricing driven by the oil shock rather than by growth data.
  • Big Tech earnings. A heavy week of technology results is the near-term catalyst investors are positioning into; today's chip strength is partly anticipation.
  • Iran & oil. A 10th consecutive day of US strikes on Iran — plus another reported tanker hit near the Strait of Hormuz — pushed Brent up about 2.1% to roughly $91. Oil is the transmission channel into the two-month-high 10-year yield.
