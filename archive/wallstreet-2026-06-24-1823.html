The Lead

Wall Street drifts to a mixed close, then Micron detonates after the bell

Stocks finished little changed and split on Wednesday, June 24: the Dow Jones Industrial Average added 182.06 points (+0.35%) to 51,848.90, but the S&P 500 eased 0.10% to 7,358.22 and the Nasdaq Composite slipped 0.43% to 25,476.64 as energy, communication-services and technology names dragged into the close. The whole session was a holding pattern ahead of memory giant Micron's report — and traders were rewarded.

After the bell, Micron posted a blowout: record fiscal-third-quarter revenue of about $41.46 billion (versus roughly $35.8 billion expected), a record 84.9% gross margin, and fiscal-fourth-quarter guidance of around $50 billion in revenue, far above estimates, on an AI-driven data-center surge. The stock jumped more than 12% in extended trading (briefly up ~16%), pulling chip peers higher. The backdrop: a hawkish Fed — which held rates at 3.50%–3.75% on June 17 but flipped its dot plot to signal a hike this year — plus a sharp drop in oil to pre-war lows, with WTI crude down about 4% near $70 and the 10-year Treasury yield sliding below 4.5% to roughly 4.41%.

Movers & Drivers

Wendy's (WEN) +25.7%New

The latest meme-stock target closed up 25.7% at $7.86 — spiking as much as ~42% on heavy volume (trading was halted at one point) — after naming former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer. It ranked among the most-mentioned tickers on Reddit trading forums.

Sunrun (RUN) surgedNew

The residential-solar company jumped roughly 19% intraday after unveiling a partnership with Tesla and Renew Home to provide more than 16 gigawatts of flexible energy capacity to utilities and AI data centers amid surging electricity demand.

Micron (MU) +12%+ AHAfter Hours

Shares leapt more than 12% in extended trading (briefly ~16%) on a record fiscal-Q3 (~$41.5B revenue, 84.9% gross margin) and ~$50B Q4 revenue guidance — the day's marquee event, lifting the semiconductor complex into Thursday.

Energy & chips lagged

Energy was the day's weakest corner as crude tumbled to pre-war lows, and semiconductors were heavy into the Micron print — the drag that kept the S&P 500 and Nasdaq in the red despite the Dow's gain.
