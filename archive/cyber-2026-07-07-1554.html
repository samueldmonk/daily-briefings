Top Story

FortiBleed credential theft is now a confirmed pipeline into INC and Lynx ransomware

Researchers tracking the FortiBleed campaign say a mass harvest of Fortinet FortiGate credentials has become a direct route into ransomware. The operation collected more than 110 million credentials from roughly 430,000 internet-facing FortiGate firewalls, using a custom Golang tool dubbed "FortigateSniffer" that abuses FortiOS's native diagnose sniffer-packet command to passively intercept VPN logins and other authentication traffic across two dozen protocols.

Investigators logged scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals in more than 150 countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets and completed the full attack chain on 354 of them. One operator was found logged into both the INC Ransom and Lynx negotiation panels, and at least 12 ransomware deployments — encrypting hundreds of endpoints — have been tied to the access. The links surfaced after an operational-security slip left one of about 200 FortiBleed servers exposed on the internet.

An internal tracking document points to an operation of roughly 20 people — some running high-impact intrusions, others providing technical support — marking the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been linked directly to ransomware deployment. Defenders should treat any exposed FortiGate credentials as compromised: rotate all device and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, and apply Fortinet's latest FortiOS updates.

Patch Priority

Patch SimpleHelp now — CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0) is actively exploited and past its federal deadline

An authentication-bypass flaw in SimpleHelp's OIDC login accepts identity tokens without verifying their signature, letting an unauthenticated attacker forge a token, seize a full technician session and — in some setups — bypass MFA. It is being used to drop TaskWeaver and Djinn Stealer malware to steal credentials, SSH keys and crypto wallets. CISA set a July 2, 2026 KEV remediation deadline (now past), and because managed service providers rely on SimpleHelp, one compromised server can reach every downstream client. Fix: upgrade to 5.5.16 or 6.0 RC2 and rotate secrets.

Threat Actor Spotlight

The FortiBleed → INC / Lynx nexus

The operation behind the FortiBleed-to-ransomware pipeline is assessed at roughly 20 people, split between operators running high-impact intrusions and others providing technical support. The tell that links it directly to ransomware: a single operator was seen logged into both the INC Ransom and Lynx negotiation panels, and victims listed by INC Ransom overlap with data from the campaign. INC and Lynx are human-operated, double-extortion ransomware brands; at least 12 deployments, encrypting hundreds of endpoints, have been traced to the stolen FortiGate access.

Breaches & Incidents

Adobe ColdFusion max-severity flaw exploited within ~2 hoursExploited

Adobe's June 30 update fixed a CVSS 10.0 RDS path-traversal flaw in ColdFusion (CVE-2026-48282) that lets an unauthenticated attacker perform arbitrary file operations and escalate to RCE. KEVIntel honeypots logged exploitation on July 2, minutes after watchTowr published a technical analysis. Affected: ColdFusion 2025.9, 2023.20 and earlier — apply 2025 Update 10 / 2023 Update 21 now.

New CitrixBleed-style NetScaler flaw exploited within hoursNew

Citrix patched six NetScaler flaws on June 30, including CVE-2026-8451 (CVSS 8.8), a pre-auth memory over-read via the SAML IdP config that leaks appliance memory — session tokens, cookies, credentials — in the same class as "CitrixBleed." CrowdSec observed the first in-the-wild exploitation on July 2, a window measured in hours, not weeks. Attackers can hijack live sessions and walk past MFA; patch immediately.

ShinyHunters breach 100+ orgs via an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-dayExploited

The ShinyHunters extortion crew exploited CVE-2026-35273, an unauthenticated deserialization RCE in Oracle PeopleSoft (CVSS 9.8), as a zero-day from May 27 to June 9 — before Oracle's June 10 out-of-band patch — hitting 300+ instances across 100+ organizations, 68% in higher education. On July 3, Nissan, Kubota North America and Aflac's Japan unit all confirmed data theft; Nissan says employee SSNs, banking and tax data may be exposed.

Huntress: 81M-attempt Azure CLI password spray bypasses MFANew

Huntress documented an automated password-spray campaign against Microsoft's Azure command-line interface that generated more than 81 million login attempts between June 12 and 26, compromising at least 78 accounts across 64 organizations. The attackers abused the ROPC OAuth flow to slip past multi-factor authentication. Defenders should disable ROPC where possible and hunt for anomalous Azure CLI sign-ins.
