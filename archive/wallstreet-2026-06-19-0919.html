The Lead

Markets shut for Juneteenth after Thursday's broad rebound — small caps and chips led, Intel rocketed to a record

U.S. stock and bond markets are closed Friday, June 19 for the Juneteenth federal holiday, so there is no regular session today; the next one is Monday, June 22. The most recent read is Thursday's broad-based rebound from Wednesday's hawkish-Fed selloff: the small-cap Russell 2000 led the way, up about 2.12%, the Nasdaq Composite climbed roughly 1.91%, the S&P 500 added about 1.08% and the megacap-heavy Dow lagged at +0.14% — a comeback fueled by steadier-to-lower Treasury yields. The standout single name was Intel, which spiked more than 12% to an all-time high before settling up about 10%, after President Trump claimed on Truth Social that Apple has agreed to design and build chips with Intel in the United States; neither company confirmed the deal and Apple shares moved only about 0.6%. The semiconductor complex rallied alongside it, while Accenture cratered about 16% after cutting its full-year revenue outlook, and oil has eased roughly 10% on the week as a U.S.–Iran deal reopened the Strait of Hormuz.
