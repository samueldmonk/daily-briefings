The Lead

At the close: S&P 500 sheds 1.21% to 7,408 as oil tops $100 — then Intel rips ~11% higher after hours

Wall Street closed broadly lower Thursday. The S&P 500 lost 1.21% to end at 7,408.30, the Nasdaq Composite fell 2.15% to 25,137.69, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average shed 506.93 points, or 0.97%, to 51,711.65 (CNBC). Surging oil prices amid the escalating Mideast conflict pressured stocks and bonds, while Alphabet's results fueled fresh concern about the payoff timeline on massive artificial-intelligence spending.

The Nasdaq was bogged down by a roughly 7% drop in Alphabet and a 14% slide in Tesla following their earnings reports. Alphabet's quarter was fundamentally strong — but its capital-spending forecast was raised again, and Tesla's profit fell even after record deliveries, with Elon Musk calling 2026 a "massive capex year" (CNBC/Yahoo Finance).

Brent crude topped $100 a barrel for the first time since May 26 after Iran-backed Houthis said they attacked two Saudi tankers in the Red Sea; WTI traded near $90. The oil spike stoked inflation fears and kept Treasury yields at their highest levels of the year. One bright spot: initial jobless claims fell to 187,000 versus 210,000 expected — and after the bell, Intel's blowout quarter sent its shares up about 11% (see After-Hours Movers).

Movers & Drivers

Tesla (TSLA) −14%

Tumbled after Q2 profit fell despite record deliveries of more than 480,000 vehicles. EPS missed, and Musk said 2026 will be a "massive capex year" focused on Optimus robots, robotaxis and data centers (CNBC/Yahoo Finance).

Alphabet (GOOGL) ~−7%

Sank despite a Q2 beat — revenue $119.8B, EPS $2.26, Cloud up 82% to $24.8B — after raising 2026 capex guidance to $195–205B. Investors are scrutinizing AI's return on investment (CNBC; Bloomberg cited a 6.5% drop).

Super Micro (SMCI) +24%New

Surged 24% (TradingKey) after revealing a record $60 billion backlog and nearly doubling gross-margin guidance to 15–17% from 8.2–8.4%, even with revenue near the low end; Benzinga reported the stock soaring post-results on more than $60 billion in new orders and plans for an AI data center with SpaceX.

Intel (INTC) −8.4%

Slid roughly 8.4% in the regular session (Finviz quote board) as traders de-risked ahead of the print — CNBC noted the stock entered earnings down 27% from its June record high. Then came the report: see After-Hours Movers below.

T-Mobile (TMUS) −6.61%New

Fell 6.61% Thursday on softened postpaid-subscriber-growth expectations, with rising capex and marketing costs compressing free-cash-flow margins ahead of its imminent Q2 report — consensus $2.59 EPS on $22.94B revenue (TradingKey).

Lockheed Martin (LMT) +11% AM

Surged after a Q2 beat — EPS $7.94 vs. $7.19 expected, revenue $20.06B — and a raised full-year outlook as Mideast escalation lifts defense names. Quoted +11.3% on Yahoo's morning board.

Energy leads: OXY, XOM +1.8%

Occidental and Exxon each gained about 1.8% as Brent crossed $100. Energy was the session's standout while tech lagged badly (24/7 Wall St/Yahoo).

VIX back above 19

Wall Street's fear gauge jumped more than 15% intraday to above 19 (Yahoo quote board) as the oil shock and megacap earnings reaction rippled through risk assets.

After-Hours Movers

Intel (INTC) — blowout Q2, stock jumps ~11% AH
