The Lead

Chips power a broad rally as stocks shrug off a second day of Iran strikes — S&P 500 +0.8%, Nasdaq +1.2% as of ~3:40 PM ET

As of ~3:40 PM ET Thursday, July 9 — markets open. A semiconductor surge drove the major averages higher, with the Nasdaq Composite up about 1.2%, the S&P 500 up about 0.8% and the Dow up about 0.2%. Investors leaned back into the AI trade ahead of SK Hynix's U.S. trading debut Friday — an ADR offering reported to be more than seven times oversubscribed — even as the U.S. struck Iran for a second straight day (U.S. Central Command said it hit about 90 targets Wednesday) and Tehran retaliated against U.S. bases in Bahrain, Kuwait and Qatar.

The chip complex led broadly: a gauge of semiconductor firms rose about 4.5% and the iShares Semiconductor ETF climbed roughly 5%. Micron jumped about 8% after pledging up to $3 billion to strengthen the U.S. semiconductor supply chain, part of a plan to invest more than $250 billion over the next decade; AMD gained about 7% and Broadcom about 3%. A persistent shortage of high-bandwidth memory for AI data centers — expected to run through 2027 — kept memory names bid.

Not everything joined in. PepsiCo slipped after Q2 earnings missed by a penny (adjusted $2.20 vs. $2.21 expected) even as revenue beat, AstraZeneca fell about 6% after its heart-disease drug Wainua missed a late-stage trial, and Paramount Skydance dropped about 6% on a report that several U.S. states plan an antitrust suit over its Warner Bros. Discovery acquisition. Oil stayed elevated — Brent near $78.5 and WTI near $74, with tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz slowing to a near standstill — keeping the 10-year Treasury yield up around 4.57%, close to a two-month high.
