Top Story

Ankalaev headlines Abu Dhabi against a short-notice Guskov — and a heavy favourite's price says everything

UFC Fight Night: Ankalaev vs. Guskov lands Saturday at the Etihad Arena in Abu Dhabi, the promotion's 23rd visit to the city and its first since UFC 321 in October 2025. The main event was booked as former light heavyweight champion Magomed Ankalaev against Khalil Rountree Jr. — but Rountree withdrew injured with under two weeks to go, and Bogdan Guskov stepped in.

Guskov is a live replacement rather than a body. He is ninth-ranked, has 18 wins all by finish including 13 first-round stoppages. The books still make it lopsided: Ankalaev opened at −500 with Guskov +340 at DraftKings and the line has since moved further toward the former champion, to roughly −535 / +400 (UFC.com's line is −550 / +400) — implied odds of about 85% to 15%.

Ankalaev (21-2-1) arrives with something to answer for: he returns from an ~80-second TKO loss to Alex Pereira at UFC 320 that cost him the light heavyweight title. He is publicly treating Guskov as a “tuneup” — “this is just a fight to be back,” he said this week — and a win would put him next in line for the belt now held by Carlos Ulberg. Against a finisher who does nearly all of his work in round one, that framing is the fight's whole risk.

A scheduling note that will catch people out: this card runs on Abu Dhabi time. Prelims start at 9 AM ET / 6 AM PT and the main card at 12 PM ET / 9 AM PT.

A sourcing note: the Wikipedia event page for this card still carried the superseded Rountree booking in its main-event line when checked. UFC.com's own announcement and event page govern here.
