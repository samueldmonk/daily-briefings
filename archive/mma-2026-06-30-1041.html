TOP STORY

McGregor Returns at UFC 329

Conor McGregor steps back into the Octagon at UFC 329 on July 11 in Las Vegas to face Max Holloway in the most anticipated matchup of the summer. McGregor opens as a slight underdog (−180 to +180 at various books, ranging to −298 to +240 at FanDuel), marking his return after an extended absence due to injury recovery and legal matters. Holloway, more active with eight fights since McGregor's last appearance (going 5–3), is favored by oddsmakers. The card anchors UFC International Fight Week and kicks off a robust summer schedule. Paddy Pimblett and Benoit Saint-Denis square off in the co-main event.

LAST EVENT — RESULTS (JUNE 20 UFC FIGHT NIGHT: KAPE VS. HORIGUCHI)
Result Bout Method
Manel Kape vs. Kyoji Horiguchi — Flyweight TKO, R3 2:42 (punches)
Murtazali Magomedov vs. Melsik Baghdasaryan — Featherweight Sub, R1 1:17 (twister)

Kape's statement finish: After a competitive early-fight exchange, Kape rallied in Round 3 to drop Horiguchi with a crushing right hand, then unleashed vicious ground-and-pound including an uppercut that faceplanted the former Bellator and Rizin champion. Horiguchi's corner called time at 2:42. Magomedov's debut: The Russian phenom locked up a rare Scottish twister to tap Baghdasaryan at 1:17 R1—the first UFC twister of that variety ever recorded. Magomedov remains undefeated (11–0) and instantly put the featherweight division on notice.

AROUND THE SPORT
  • Meta UFC Rankings Debut: On June 20, the UFC replaced its media voting panel with the Meta UFC Rankings, an Elo-based mathematical model designed to prioritize objectivity and remove pound-for-pound rankings. First full rankings cycle underway.
  • Gaethje's Upset Historic: Justin Gaethje's TKO4 victory over Ilia Topuria at Freedom 250 (Jun 14, White House) makes him the first undisputed champion to be defeated since Conor McGregor vs. Eddie Alvarez (2016) at lightweight. Gaethje is now the undisputed lightweight champion.
  • Gane Captures Interim HW Gold: At Freedom 250, Ciryl Gane dispatched Alex Pereira via TKO2 to capture the interim heavyweight title. Gane is now positioned as Tom Aspinall's mandatory challenger, setting up a title-unification bout later in 2026.
  • Conor's Rust Factor: McGregor hasn't fought since a knockout loss to Dustin Poirier (Jan 2021); his extended absence, recovery from leg injury, and legal matters all factoring into his underdog status vs. the more active Holloway.
RANKINGS & BUSINESS

Lightweight Division Reshuffle: With Gaethje now champion, Topuria vacating after his TKO4 loss, and Islam Makhachev holding the welterweight belt (two-division champ), the division looks wide open. Former title challengers and the meta-ranked contenders are jockeying for position under the new Elo-based system. McGregor's return and potential fast-track if he wins could accelerate interest in a title bout by late 2026 or early 2027.

Broadcast & Viewership: Freedom 250 (White House event, Jun 14) was Paramount+ exclusive—no PPV or CBS simulcast. Historical context drove strong early numbers; UFC International Fight Week (Jul 9–11) expected to draw significant attention with McGregor's return as the marquee draw.
