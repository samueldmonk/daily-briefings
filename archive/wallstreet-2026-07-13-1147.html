The Lead

A whipsaw Monday: stocks fall on the Hormuz blockade order, then fight back as Trump says Iran "wants to talk" (as of ~noon ET)

Wall Street opened a heavyweight week on the back foot — and has been trading headline to headline since. The S&P 500 fell after President Trump reinstated the Strait of Hormuz blockade on Iranian ships, with SK Hynix leading chip stocks lower (CNBC), reviving the U.S.–Iran standoff that markets had hoped was winding down. By late morning the tape was fighting back: Yahoo Finance's newest banner has the Dow, S&P 500 and Nasdaq edging higher as Trump says Iran "wants to talk." Crude holds its jump either way — WTI traded above $75 (CNBC) and Brent crossed $80 (Yahoo Finance) — after the weekend's exchange of strikes, in which Tehran hit U.S. facilities in Gulf countries including Kuwait, Jordan and Qatar, and Iran's IRGC declared the strait "closed until further notice" while Washington insists it remains open.

Tech carried the early pain: Nasdaq futures fell nearly 1% before the bell as memory stocks slumped following SK Hynix's plunge in Seoul, which dragged the Kospi down 9% and triggered a market-wide trading halt on fears Korea's AI rally had grown overstretched (Investrade/Hammerstone). The inflation stakes are immediate: oil's climb lands the day before Tuesday's CPI print, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first congressional testimony, and the unofficial kickoff of Q2 earnings season led by JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America, Citigroup and Wells Fargo (Yahoo Finance/Schwab).

Movers & Drivers

memory rout

SK Hynix plunge halts Seoul, drags U.S. memory names

Days after its record $26.5B U.S. ADR debut — priced at $149, opened at $170, settled near $168 — SK Hynix plunged, pulling the Kospi down 9% and triggering a market-wide trading halt on fears South Korea's AI rally is overstretched. Its U.S. shares are leading chip stocks lower Monday (CNBC), with memory names Micron and SanDisk lower in sympathy (Investrade/Hammerstone).

legal

Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade-secret theft

Apple filed suit in the Northern District of California alleging the AI lab attempted to steal confidential information, naming two former Apple employees — Chang Liu and Tang Yew Tan — as defendants alongside OpenAI (Investrade/Hammerstone).

AI demand

TSMC Q2 revenue tops estimates on AI chips

Taiwan Semiconductor's Q2 revenue reached NT$1.27T (~$39.63B), up 36% year over year and ahead of the ~$39.31B consensus, inside its guided range — a fresh signal on AI demand ahead of its full report Thursday (Investrade/Hammerstone).

upgrades

American Express, Humana catch big upgrades

JPMorgan lifted American Express to Overweight with a $400 target, calling its premium warranted given defensive revenues; Wells Fargo raised Humana to Overweight with a $502 target ahead of managed-care Q2 reports (Investrade/Hammerstone).

AI buildout

Meta supersizes Louisiana data center to 5 GW

Meta said its Richland Parish, Louisiana data center will expand to 5 gigawatts of compute for its AI ambitions; local businesses have received more than $1.6B in contracts since the December 2024 groundbreaking (Investrade/Hammerstone).
