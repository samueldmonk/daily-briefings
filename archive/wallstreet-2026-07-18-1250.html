The Lead

Semiconductors fall into a bear market as a Chinese model rattles the AI trade

U.S. markets are closed for the weekend. The figures below are Friday, July 17's official closes. The S&P 500 fell 1.01% to 7,457.69, the Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.40% to 25,520.24, and the Dow lost 406.55 points, or 0.77%, to 52,146.42. For the week the S&P shed more than 1.5%, the Nasdaq 2.9%, and the Dow nearly 1%.

The damage was concentrated in chips. The PHLX Semiconductor Index closed 1.6% lower and 20.2% below its June 22 record close — the conventional 20% threshold, which puts the sector formally in a bear market. The VanEck Semiconductor ETF posted its third weekly decline in four, down almost 9% over the period. The index did close off its session lows as investors bought the dip.

The catalyst was competitive, not macro. Chinese startup Moonshot AI released Kimi K3, claiming it narrows the gap with leading U.S. systems; on benchmark tests it reportedly matched or outperformed several leading U.S. models, trailing only Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and OpenAI's GPT-5.6. That fed the argument that stiffer competition could soften future demand for advanced AI chips and slow the pace of technology capital spending — the thesis that has carried this market for a year. Renewed U.S.–Iran escalation pushed oil sharply higher at the same time, and energy was the only sector to finish higher, with technology and communication services the worst hit.

DownEarnings

Fell more than 10% early Friday. Q2 EPS of $0.80 slightly topped expectations, but revenue of $12.56 billion narrowly missed a $12.6 billion consensus.

NewUpInsurance

Travelers — best-in-market gain on a Q2 beat

Rose about 9% after results driven by lower catastrophe losses and higher net investment income. Raymond James lifted its price target from $350 to $400 and reiterated Strong Buy, citing high-teens ROE potential through 2028 with lower earnings volatility.

NewUpEnergy

Energy complex — the session's only green sector

Valero, ConocoPhillips and ExxonMobil each gained about 2% ahead of the open as Middle East escalation drove crude roughly 4% higher.

DownSector

Technology and communication services led the downside

The rotation out of tech was broad rather than name-specific, with the AI-adjacent complex absorbing the bulk of the selling while defensives and energy held up.
