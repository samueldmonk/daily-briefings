The Lead

Chips lead a Monday snapback — but the Dow is the laggard, as of midday ET

Wall Street's main indexes rose on Monday, led by recovering chip stocks, while investors awaited a key slate of earnings from major technology companies that have powered the market's AI-driven rally, Reuters reported. At 9:50 a.m. ET, Reuters put the Dow Jones Industrial Average up 78.39 points, or 0.15%, the S&P 500 up 44.81 points, or 0.60%, and the Nasdaq Composite up 234.45 points, or 0.92%. Those three point-and-percent pairs reconcile exactly against Friday's closes, so they are the anchor figures for this edition.

Two earlier reads are published alongside rather than blended into them: Bloomberg had the S&P 500 up 0.6% as of 9:35 a.m. in New York, the Nasdaq 100 up 1.3% led by semiconductors after its worst week in almost a month, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) up 3.1%. Reuters, capturing slightly later, had the same SOX gauge up 2.5%. Investing.com's live quote strip at the time this edition was built showed the SOX +2.78%, the Nasdaq Composite +0.89% and the S&P 500 +0.45% — with the Dow slipping to −0.18%. The direction of travel through the morning is a narrowing, chip-led tape in which the Dow has given up its early gain.

The rebound follows a bruising week. Reuters notes the Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index ended Friday more than 20% below its late-June record high, confirming a bear-market decline. Monday's catalysts are specific: AMD jumped on a wide-scale Microsoft Azure deployment deal, and Alphabet added 3.4% after a report said the Google parent is developing new chips to run its AI models. Micron and SanDisk were up 5.1% and 5.4% respectively, leading gains among chipmakers.

"There's just a little less room for error in the market at this point. Any sort of events or earnings news could probably move the market down," Jack Herr, senior investment analyst at GuideStone Funds, told Reuters. "As we move into the second half of the year, market expectations are higher than they were before." Markets are pricing in about a 15% chance of a quarter-point rate hike at the Fed's July meeting, according to CME's FedWatch tool, and expect S&P 500 second-quarter earnings growth of 26% year-on-year, up from an earlier estimate of 23.7%, per data compiled by LSEG.

A note on one figure not published here: a search synthesis this run reported the Dow as having fallen 0.2% at midday. A direct fetch of the underlying Yahoo Finance article showed it to be a 7:24 a.m. ET pre-open piece about index futures ("U.S. markets open in 1h 56m"), in which Dow futures were up 0.2%. The synthesis had inverted the sign and relabelled futures as cash indexes. Rejected.

Movers & Drivers

+5%New

AMD — Microsoft locks in Helios for Azure

AMD shares jumped about 5% on Monday after Microsoft committed to wide-scale deployment of the AMD Helios Rackscale Solution for frontier-model AI inference across Azure. Helios is an integrated power rack pairing Instinct MI455X GPUs, 6th-Gen EPYC "Venice" CPUs, Pensando networking and ROCm software, scheduled to begin shipping in the second half of 2026. Azure is launching two Venice-powered VM lines — HDv2 for agentic AI and data processing, HXv2 for semiconductor design.

+3.4%New

Alphabet — building its own AI chips

Alphabet added 3.4% by Reuters' 9:50 a.m. ET mark after a report said the Google parent is developing new chips to run its AI models. Investing.com's later quote strip showed GOOGL at +2.62%, so the gain faded somewhat through the morning. Alphabet reports quarterly results on Wednesday, July 22 — the week's most closely watched print.

Memory leadsNew

Micron & SanDisk out front

Reuters named Micron (+5.1%) and SanDisk (+5.4%) as the leaders among chipmakers at 9:50 a.m. ET. Investing.com's live strip later showed MU +5.67% and SNDK +6.92%, making memory the strongest corner of the semiconductor rebound on both reads.

+4.32%

Intel — ahead of its own print

Intel showed +4.32% on Investing.com's live quote strip. Reuters flags Intel and Texas Instruments (+2.15% on the same strip) as the two reports investors will watch for signals on whether the semiconductor sector can regain momentum after its sharp pullback.

+3.6%New

Domino's Pizza — revenue beat

Domino's Pizza gained 3.6% at Reuters' 9:50 a.m. ET mark after the pizza chain's quarterly revenue edged past Wall Street estimates. The gain had narrowed to +1.96% on Investing.com's later strip — both readings are Monday, captured at different times.

−1.66%

Tesla — the other Mag 7 print

Tesla showed −1.66% on Investing.com's live strip, an outlier in an otherwise firm tech tape. Tesla is one of four megacap reports due later this week alongside Alphabet, Intel and IBM (+0.47%), as investors look for signs that companies are monetizing their AI build-out spending.

Chart of the Day — AMD

AMD takes the chart slot as the best-corroborated single-name move of the session with a named catalyst — roughly +5% on the Microsoft Helios/Azure deal, reported independently by Yahoo Finance, Investing.com, Invezz, Stocktwits and Blockonomi. On Investing.com's live strip, SanDisk (+6.92%) and Micron (+5.67%) were running higher; those are single-widget readings rather than multi-source confirmations, so they are reported above but do not take the chart.

Sector Heat — live

Reuters reports that information technology and communication services were leading gains on the benchmark S&P 500 — the first sourced sector read this briefing has been able to publish in several editions. No Monday intraday sector percentages were confirmed, so none are given. Breadth was positive but thin: advancing issues outnumbered decliners by 1.14-to-1 on the NYSE and 1.11-to-1 on the Nasdaq; the S&P 500 posted 8 new 52-week highs and 1 new low, while the Nasdaq Composite recorded 31 new highs and 55 new lows. The VIX was 18.21, −2.98% on a pre-open Yahoo Finance capture.

On the Radar

  • Megacap earnings week. Alphabet reports Wednesday, July 22, with Tesla, Intel and IBM also due. Reuters frames Intel and Texas Instruments as the tell for whether semiconductors can regain momentum; Alphabet's data-centre spending is the tell for whether hyperscalers keep funding the AI build-out.
  • AMD's Advancing AI 2026 event, July 22–23. CNBC reports Meta, OpenAI and Oracle have already committed as early Helios platform adopters, and Jefferies analysts flagged Anthropic as a likely next AMD customer announcement.
  • FOMC meets July 28–29. CME's FedWatch tool puts the odds of a quarter-point hike at this month's meeting at about 15%. Reuters notes last week's declines came despite benign inflation data that had eased some concerns about a Fed rate increase.
  • A new front in the Middle East. Yemen's Iran-aligned Houthis said they are imposing a naval blockade on Saudi Arabia, opening a new front in the war and increasing the threat to global energy supplies and trade.
  • U.S.–Iran escalation. The conflict escalated again over the weekend, with the U.S. engaged in a ninth straight day of attacks and Iran retaliating by bombing U.S. allies in the region, primarily Kuwait. Reuters describes a nearly five-month-old conflict whose recent escalation has stoked inflation fears; Yahoo Finance reports both sides remain far apart on a ceasefire as the death toll rises.
  • Earnings expectations have been marked up. LSEG data has S&P 500 Q2 earnings growth at 26% year-on-year, raised from an earlier 23.7% estimate — a higher bar for the reports still to come.
