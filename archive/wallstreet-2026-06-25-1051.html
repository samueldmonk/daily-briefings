The Lead

Micron's blowout reignites the AI trade; stocks rise in early trading as PCE runs to a 3-year high

Wall Street climbed Thursday, June 25, with the rebound led by chips after memory giant Micron's after-the-bell blowout the night before. In late-morning trading (as of about 10:30 a.m. ET) the S&P 500 was up 0.52%, the Dow Jones Industrial Average 0.65%, the Nasdaq Composite 0.24% and the Russell 2000 0.37%. Micron crushed estimates with record fiscal-third-quarter revenue of about $41.46 billion (versus roughly $35.69 billion expected) and adjusted EPS of $25.11 (well above the ~$20.78 consensus) — revenue up about 346% from $9.3 billion a year earlier — and paired the print with a current-quarter sales forecast that topped Wall Street, reiterating that its HBM memory capacity is sold out through 2026 and that customers have committed about $22 billion to lock in memory supply. The stock jumped about 18% and dragged the semiconductor complex higher (the iShares Semiconductor ETF rose about 5%). This follows a mixed Wednesday close: the Dow rose 182.06 points (+0.35%) to 51,848.90 while the S&P 500 eased 0.10% to 7,358.22 and the Nasdaq Composite slipped 0.43% to 25,476.64.

The other marquee number landed at 8:30 a.m.: the Fed's preferred inflation gauge ran hot. May PCE rose 0.4% on the month and 4.1% year over year — the highest since April 2023; core PCE rose 0.3% on the month and 3.4% from a year ago, its highest since October 2023, with both readings broadly in line with forecasts. Even so, the chip rally dominated the morning: Qualcomm climbed about 10% after nearly doubling its fiscal-2029 non-handset revenue forecast to roughly $40 billion, and memory and chip-equipment peers — SanDisk, Western Digital, Lam Research, KLA and Applied Materials — rose in sympathy. The macro backdrop stays unusual — the Fed held its target at 3.50%–3.75% on June 17 and rate-path chatter lingers, while oil sits at pre-war lows (WTI settled near $70 on Wednesday) and the 10-year Treasury yield hovers below 4.5%. Alphabet replaces Verizon in the Dow before the June 29 open.

Movers & Drivers

Micron (MU) ~+18%

The day's marquee mover. Record fiscal-Q3 revenue of about $41.46B (vs ~$35.69B expected), adjusted EPS of $25.11 (vs ~$20.78), revenue up about 346% from a year ago, and a current-quarter forecast that topped estimates — with HBM sold out through 2026 and ~$22B of customer commitments — sent shares up roughly 18% and reset sentiment on AI memory.

Qualcomm (QCOM) ~+10%

The chipmaker rallied about 10% after nearly doubling its projection for fiscal-2029 non-handset revenue to roughly $40 billion, reinforcing the AI/data-center growth story alongside Micron.

Bio-Techne (TECH) ~+19.6%

The day's biggest single mover — the life-sciences tools maker agreed to be acquired by Germany's Merck KGaA for $73 a share in cash, an enterprise value of about $11.3 billion and roughly a 36% premium to its one-month average price. Shares jumped about 20% toward the deal price; the transaction is expected to close in late 2026 or early 2027.

Laggards lower

Against the broad bid, Dell Technologies (DELL) fell about 6.7%, Apple (AAPL) slipped roughly 4.7% and Albemarle (ALB) lost about 4.4% — the standout decliners as money rotated toward AI-memory and chip names.
