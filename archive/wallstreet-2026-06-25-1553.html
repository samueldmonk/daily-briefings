The Lead
As of ~3:30 PM ET · markets open

Dow taps a record as Wall Street splits down the middle

A blowout earnings report from Micron reignited the artificial-intelligence trade, but the day's real story was a rotation out of megacap tech and into the rest of the market. The Dow climbed to a fresh all-time intraday high — up roughly 0.6% — powered by industrials, banks and health care, while the Nasdaq Composite slipped about 0.6% and the S&P 500 hovered near the flat line.

The drag came from the Magnificent Seven: Apple fell roughly 6% after raising MacBook and iPad prices to offset surging memory costs, and Microsoft dropped about 4% on its own consumer price hikes, with Nvidia, Amazon, Alphabet and Oracle also lower. Offsetting them, banks rose after clearing the Federal Reserve's annual stress test and signaling bigger dividends ahead. May PCE inflation landed in line (4.1% headline, 3.4% core), keeping the focus on the tape rather than the data.
