The Lead

Wall Street reopens with a tech-led bounce; chips lead, Microsoft slides on layoffs

U.S. markets came back from the long Independence Day weekend on the front foot Monday. As of roughly 10:20 AM ET, the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite was up about 0.8% (the Nasdaq-100 about 1.3%), the S&P 500 about 0.5% and the Dow about 0.2% — a mirror image of Thursday's session, when a chip sell-off had pinned tech even as the blue-chip Dow closed at a record.

The catalyst was a rebound in semiconductors: the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) was up about 2.7% at the open, with AMD up roughly 8.5%, easing the late-June jitters over the AI trade. The standout laggard was Microsoft, off about 1.7% after it announced it is cutting 4,800 jobs — about 2.1% of its workforce — in its biggest-ever Xbox restructuring, including plans to spin off four gaming studios. Meanwhile oil eased after OPEC+ agreed to another monthly output increase, and investors turned to a week of services data and Wednesday's Fed minutes for direction on rate-cut — or rate-hike — odds.

Movers & Drivers

Semiconductors (SMH)

▲ ~+2.7%

The chip complex led the rebound: the VanEck Semiconductor ETF was up about 2.7% at the opening bell, clawing back some of the late-June AI-trade slump as faith in memory and AI-compute demand returned.

AMD

▲ ~+8.5%

Advanced Micro Devices led individual chip names higher, up about 8.5%, while Intel added roughly 3% (near $124) as dip-buyers returned to the sector after its June slump.

Microsoft New

▼ ~−1.7%

Microsoft slid about 1.7% after announcing 4,800 job cuts (~2.1% of staff) and a sweeping Xbox overhaul — about 1,600 gaming roles now, heading toward ~3,200 this fiscal year — plus plans to spin off four studios. It remains 2026's worst megacap performer (−19% YTD as of Friday).

WTI Crude Oil

▼ ~−0.2%

WTI slipped to about $68.58 (Brent −0.3% near $71.88) after OPEC+ agreed to raise August output by a combined +188,000 bpd (led by Saudi Arabia and Russia) and flows kept recovering through a reopened Strait of Hormuz.
