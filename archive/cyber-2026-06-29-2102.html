Top Story

ShinyHunters Targets Oracle PeopleSoft Across Higher Ed
The ShinyHunters cybercrime group has compromised Oracle PeopleSoft servers at 100+ organizations, primarily colleges and universities, stealing hundreds of thousands of student records containing names, addresses, phone numbers, emails, birthdates, ethnicity, enrollment status, GPAs, and student IDs. The campaign represents a coordinated attack on ERP infrastructure commonly deployed in the education sector.
Patch Priority
Microsoft Office RCE (CVSS 8.4, CVE-2026-45461/45463/45472/45474) — four critical use-after-free and integer-underflow flaws in Office products allowing arbitrary code execution via Preview Pane. June 2026 Patch Tuesday. Actively exploited via malicious documents.

Breaches & Incidents

Tchap French Government Messaging Hacked New
Tchap, a messaging platform for the French government, was compromised in June with an attacker claiming to have stolen 13.5 GB of data: 73,467 user accounts (including French Ministry personnel), 643,459 messages, 876 chat rooms, and 59,386 shared media files.
TVING South Korean Streaming Service Breach New
TVING confirmed personal information of users was leaked on June 3, including IDs, names, birthdates, phone numbers, emails, passwords, and refund account numbers.
Texas Parks & Wildlife Department Exposes 3M+ Records New
More than three million Texans' personal data was exposed in a breach affecting the Texas Parks and Wildlife Department hunting/fishing license system vendor, detected by Texas Cyber Command.
Klue Supply Chain Attack via Salesforce New
Market intelligence platform Klue disclosed on June 19 that hackers stole data from an undisclosed number of customers in a cyberattack between June 11–12, targeting Klue's Salesforce integration and exfiltrating data from multiple customers' Salesforce environments.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Cl0p Remains Dominant Extortion Force
Cl0p, a Russian-speaking financially motivated cybercriminal collective, is likely to remain a top-tier global extortion actor in 2026. The group has shifted toward an extortion-only model with executive-focused pressure campaigns tied to enterprise software exposure, particularly targeting Serv-U and similar ERP/productivity platforms.
