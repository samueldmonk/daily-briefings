Top Story

FortiBleed confirmed as a ransomware pipeline: credentials from ~74,000 FortiGate firewalls now feeding INC and Lynx

Researchers this week confirmed that the sprawling FortiBleed credential-theft campaign — running for months — is feeding ransomware operations. Attackers ran a custom Golang packet-sniffing tool dubbed "FortiGate Sniffer," which abuses FortiOS's built-in diagnose sniffer packet feature to intercept VPN and other authentication traffic directly off compromised Fortinet firewalls. Recorded Future puts the exposed dataset at valid administrative and SSL-VPN credentials for 73,932 FortiGate systems across 194 countries and more than 21,600 domains; the broader operation is assessed to have targeted 430,000+ firewalls and harvested over 110 million credentials.

The ransomware link is the new part. According to The Hacker News and BleepingComputer, an operator with access to FortiBleed infrastructure was found logged in to the negotiation panels of both the INC Ransom and Lynx ransomware crews — with INC's victim list overlapping the campaign's data — the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been tied directly to ransomware deployment. Tooling carrying Cyrillic-language comments points to a likely Russian-speaking initial-access broker. Defenders should treat exposed appliances as compromised: rotate all FortiGate admin and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, review logs for the sniffer, and update FortiOS.

Patch Priority

Patch now: SimpleHelp CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0) — CISA deadline is TODAY

A maximum-severity OIDC authentication-bypass in SimpleHelp remote-support software accepts unsigned identity tokens, letting a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a valid technician session and create an admin account across every managed endpoint. It is being actively exploited to deploy the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer (Horizon3.ai has published IoCs). Affected: SimpleHelp 5.5.15 and earlier and 6.0 pre-release builds. Fix: upgrade to 5.5.16 / 6.0 RC2 immediately — MSPs are a supply-chain amplifier here, with ~14,000 servers exposed and ~1,000 assessed vulnerable.

CISA KEV: added Jun 29, 2026 → remediation due July 2, 2026 (today). Risk-based BOD 26-04 assignment (3-day window).

Threat Actor Spotlight

The FortiBleed broker → INC Ransom & Lynx Initial-Access Broker

The actor behind FortiBleed operates as an initial-access broker (IAB) — harvesting, cracking and stockpiling corporate credentials, then handing that access to ransomware crews. Investigators found a single operator running victim-negotiation panels for both INC Ransom and Lynx, and tied the stolen FortiGate access to real ransomware intrusions. Cyrillic-language comments in the custom sniffer tool suggest a Russian-speaking origin. The takeaway for defenders: any organization whose FortiGate credentials appear in the FortiBleed dataset should assume it is now a ransomware target, not merely a credential-exposure statistic.

Breaches & Incidents

FortiBleed → INC & Lynx ransomware

Credentials harvested from FortiGate firewalls across 194 countries are being used to stage ransomware; researchers confirmed a direct operator link to the INC Ransom and Lynx crews this week — the fresh escalation in a campaign that has been running for months.

KDDI email breach — up to 14.2M exposed

Japanese telecom KDDI disclosed a breach of a shared email platform used by six ISPs, exposing up to 14.22 million email addresses and passwords. KDDI detected and blocked unauthorized access on June 17 and disclosed it on June 23; a third-party software flaw — not ransomware — was the cause.

BreachTelecom

Novo Nordisk — clinical-trial data leaking

The Ozempic maker disclosed (June 11) that intruders copied non-public data from its IT systems. Extortion crew FulcrumSec claims more than 1 TB — source code, marketed and unreleased drug details, clinical-trial and patient/physician data, and internal AI drug-development models — and, after a rejected $25 million ransom demand, is now threatening to sell it.

Microsoft SharePoint under active attack

CISA added SharePoint RCE CVE-2026-45659 to its KEV catalog after confirming exploitation. An authenticated attacker with Site Member permissions can achieve remote code execution; Microsoft shipped fixes in its May 2026 updates. Federal deadline: July 4.
