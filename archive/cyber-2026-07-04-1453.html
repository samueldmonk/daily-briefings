Top Story

‘FortiBleed’ credential-harvesting campaign linked to INC and Lynx ransomware

SOCRadar's Threat Research Unit says a large-scale Fortinet credential-harvesting operation dubbed FortiBleed has targeted more than 430,000 FortiGate firewalls worldwide. The actor operates as an initial access broker, using a custom Golang tool called FortigateSniffer that abuses FortiOS's native diagnose sniffer-packet command to passively intercept authentication traffic. SOCRadar tracked scanning against roughly 11,250 FortiGate portals in 150+ countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets, and completed the full attack chain on 354 of them.

The stolen access is not academic: at least 12 ransomware deployments have resulted from it, and SOCRadar tied the campaign to the INC Ransom and Lynx ransomware operations after finding a single operator logged into both gangs' negotiation panels — the clearest evidence yet that harvested FortiGate credentials are being handed off for ransomware deployment. An internal tracking document points to a structured operation of roughly 20 people. (SOCRadar, The Hacker News, BleepingComputer, SecurityWeek, DarkReading)

Patch Priority
⚠ Most urgent action today

Patch SimpleHelp now — CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0) is exploited and past deadline

When OpenID Connect (OIDC) is configured, SimpleHelp accepts identity tokens without verifying their signature (CWE-347), letting a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a token and gain a full technician session. It affects SimpleHelp 5.5.15 and earlier plus 6.0 pre-release builds; update to the patched release (5.5.16 or later). CISA added it to the KEV catalog on June 29 with a July 2 remediation deadline — now overdue. It is being actively exploited to deploy malware and steal credentials, and SimpleHelp's heavy use among MSPs makes each compromised server a supply-chain risk. Microsoft SharePoint's CVE-2026-45659 also carries a KEV deadline of today, July 4. (Arctic Wolf, Help Net Security, CISA)

INC Ransom & Lynx

SOCRadar links the FortiBleed credential trove to both the INC and Lynx ransomware operations — two closely connected, data-theft-plus-encryption crews (Lynx is widely tracked as an INC successor). Investigators found a single operator logged into both gangs' negotiation panels from FortiBleed-linked infrastructure, turning the FortiGate credential haul into ready-made ransomware footholds. (SOCRadar, BleepingComputer, The Hacker News)

Medtronic warns patients of ShinyHunters breach

The medical-device maker is notifying 3.8 million individuals after the extortion group ShinyHunters accessed its corporate IT systems between April 13 and 19, 2026 and claimed to have stolen more than 9 million records. Potentially exposed data includes names, contact details, dates of birth, Social Security numbers and health information; Medtronic says its medical devices are unaffected.
