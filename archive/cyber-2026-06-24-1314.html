Top Story

"FortiBleed" campaign holds working credentials for tens of thousands of Fortinet firewalls across 194 countries

Researchers are tracking a large-scale, automated credential-harvesting campaign — dubbed FortiBleed — against internet-facing Fortinet FortiGate firewalls and SSL-VPN gateways worldwide. An attacker-controlled database holds valid administrator credentials for tens of thousands of devices: Recorded Future counts 73,932 firewalls, SOCRadar's tally reaches 86,644, and Arctic Wolf maps the activity across 194 countries — roughly half of all FortiGates reachable on the internet. Verified working credentials have been confirmed for an estimated 30,000 to 75,000 devices, and the exposed victim set reads like a who's-who of the Fortune Global 500 — Chevron, Samsung, AT&T, Toyota, Mercedes-Benz, Oracle, Accenture and FedEx among the names that appear.

FortiBleed does not rely on a malware payload or a new product vulnerability. Instead it runs a credential pipeline — credential stuffing and password spraying using data from earlier breach dumps and infostealer logs, configuration harvesting, offline hash-cracking, and post-authentication capture of further credentials. Independent researcher Bob Diachenko identified the exposed threat-actor server on June 13, 2026, finding a structured database of validated FortiGate credentials alongside automation tooling, and attributed the operation to a Russian-speaking threat group. Fortinet characterizes the activity as credential reuse and brute-forcing rather than a new CVE; CISA's June 18 advisory urges organizations to rotate FortiGate and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, and harden management interfaces.

Patch Priority

Patch Ubiquiti UniFi OS now — CVE-2026-34908 / -34909 / -34910 (CVSS 10.0) are exploited; federal deadline June 26

The single most urgent action for defenders today is the max-severity (CVSS 10.0) Ubiquiti UniFi OS set — an improper-access-control flaw (CVE-2026-34908) plus a path-traversal bug (CVE-2026-34909) and an improper-input-validation flaw (CVE-2026-34910) that researchers at Bishop Fox showed can be chained to full remote code execution with elevated privileges. CISA added all three to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 23 based on evidence of active exploitation; under BOD 26-04, federal agencies must apply Ubiquiti's fixes or discontinue the affected UniFi OS products by June 26, 2026 (two days out). Apply the vendor updates immediately. Note: the actively-exploited Splunk Enterprise RCE (CVE-2026-20253) is also already past its June 21 deadline — patch it if you have not.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters — data-theft & extortion crew

The extortion group ShinyHunters is the actor behind the year's largest breach by record count — the Instructure / Canvas incident. The crew exfiltrated roughly 3.65 TB of data and about 275 million records — usernames, email addresses, course names, enrollment information and messages — affecting nearly 8,800 educational institutions worldwide, making it the largest education-sector breach on record. ShinyHunters runs a pure data-theft-and-extortion model rather than file-encrypting ransomware: it steals data, threatens to leak it, and negotiates payment. After defacing Canvas login portals with extortion messages in early May, the group reached a ransom agreement with Instructure on May 11, which says the stolen data was destroyed.

Breaches & Incidents

Instructure / Canvas — largest education breach on record

ShinyHunters stole ~275 million records (3.65 TB) tied to students, teachers and staff across roughly 8,800 institutions worldwide — Canvas is used by 41% of U.S. higher-ed institutions. Unauthorized access began April 25; a second extortion wave on May 7 defaced login portals at about 330 institutions before a May 11 ransom agreement.

LastPass & others via the Klue supply chain New

LastPass disclosed (alerted June 12) a breach via third-party market-intelligence vendor Klue, claimed by a threat actor tracked as "Icarus." The same supply-chain compromise reached customer data at Recorded Future, Tanium, Jamf, Sprout Social, Gong and Insurity — a reminder of how a single SaaS vendor can expose a long chain of downstream organizations.

June 23 ransomware wave — banking, academia, finance New

A fresh batch of ransomware claims surfaced June 23: the Central Bank of Libya (claimed by Qilin), India's BITS Pilani university (DragonForce), and Belpointe Asset Management (INC Ransom) — spanning critical financial, education and asset-management targets.

Ransomware-as-a-service keeps scaling

"The Gentlemen" has rapidly become the second-most-active ransomware gang by victim count, recruiting affiliates with a 90% cut of any ransom paid, while INC has claimed 830+ victims since August 2023 — evidence that the RaaS affiliate model continues to drive volume across 2026.
