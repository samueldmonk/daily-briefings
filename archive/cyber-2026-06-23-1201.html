Top Story

"FortiBleed" campaign compromises ~86,600 Fortinet firewalls across 194 countries

Researchers are tracking a large-scale, fully automated campaign — dubbed FortiBleed by SOCRadar — that has harvested working login credentials from internet-facing Fortinet FortiGate firewall and SSL-VPN devices worldwide. An attacker-controlled database holds valid credentials for more than 86,600 FortiGate devices spanning 194 countries — roughly half of all Fortinet firewalls reachable on the internet, based on Shodan polling — belonging to companies and government organizations, including (per Hudson Rock) major government entities and critical-infrastructure providers.

The operation runs on automation: attackers scan the internet for FortiGate devices, try curated lists of known passwords, and log every success; a compromised firewall is then used as a listening post to skim further credentials from SSL-VPN traffic, which are fed back into the scanner. SOCRadar estimates roughly 1.16 billion credential attempts against 320,000+ FortiGate targets (plus 2.1 billion brute-force attempts against 160,000+ MSSQL servers). Attribution is ongoing, but SOCRadar says the tooling and targeting are consistent with Russian-speaking threat actors, and the campaign remains active. Defenders should assume exposure: rotate FortiGate and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, and check leaked-credential exposure now.

Patch Priority

Patch Splunk Enterprise now — CVE-2026-20253 (CVSS 9.8) is exploited and past its federal deadline

The single most urgent action for defenders today is the unauthenticated remote-code-execution / arbitrary file create-or-truncate flaw in Splunk Enterprise (CVE-2026-20253, CVSS 9.8) — the first Splunk bug ever added to CISA's KEV catalog. It is actively exploited, and its federal remediation deadline of June 21 has already passed (overdue). Affected: Splunk Enterprise 10.2.0–10.2.3 and 10.0.0–10.0.6. Upgrade to 10.4.0, 10.2.4, 10.0.7 or later immediately. Splunk Cloud and Enterprise 9.4 and earlier are not affected.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Icarus — emerging data-theft & extortion crew

A newly emerged extortion group, Icarus, has publicly claimed the Klue/Salesforce data-theft campaign, pressuring victims through extortion emails and a leak site. By its own account it has been active since April 28, 2026 and initially listed only a couple of victims. It runs a pure data-theft-and-leak model — no file-encrypting ransomware — and set a June 22 deadline to negotiate; with that deadline lapsed, stolen data has begun appearing on its leak site.

Breaches & Incidents

Tata Electronics — Apple/Tesla data New

The Indian Apple/Tesla supplier confirmed a "cybersecurity incident" on June 22 after the World Leaks group published 200,000+ files (~630GB) — including documents purportedly tied to iPhone and Tesla Model 3 designs plus employee passport scans. Tata says operations are unaffected; a ransom was demanded.

10+ firms hit via Klue → Salesforce

HackerOne, Huntress, Jamf, Recorded Future, Tanium, Snyk, OneTrust, Sprout Social, Insurity and Gong have confirmed business data was exfiltrated through the compromised Klue integration; LastPass also disclosed exposure (vaults and products unaffected).

Splunk Enterprise exploited in days

Splunk shipped fixes for CVE-2026-20253 on June 10; CISA confirmed active exploitation on June 18 — about eight days later — and added it to the KEV catalog as the first-ever Splunk entry.

Microsoft June Patch Tuesday — record

Microsoft patched a record ~208 CVEs this month, including an actively-exploited Microsoft Defender elevation-of-privilege zero-day (CVE-2026-41091) and a wormable, unauthenticated Windows kernel RCE (CVE-2026-45657, CVSS 9.8).
