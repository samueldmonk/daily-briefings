The Lead

Dow tops 53,000 for the first time as a chip rebound powers Wall Street back from the holiday

U.S. stocks came back from the long Independence Day weekend broadly higher on Monday, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average closing above 53,000 for the first time — up 0.29% to a record — barely a week after Alphabet replaced Verizon in the 30-stock average. The S&P 500 added 0.72% and the Nasdaq Composite climbed 1.12%, both lifted by a rebound in beaten-down memory-chip and AI names.

The mood brightened after Nvidia supplier Hon Hai (Foxconn) reported stronger-than-expected quarterly sales on Sunday, a fresh signal of durable AI demand ahead of Samsung Electronics' preliminary results on Tuesday. Seven of the eleven S&P 500 sectors finished higher, led by technology, financials and industrials; the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) rose about 2.4%.

A Bank of America Street-high target hike on Astera Labs, plus gains across AMD, Western Digital and Micron, drove the semis, while soft June payrolls kept September rate-cut hopes alive. Retail and healthcare names lagged, and oil slipped again after OPEC+ agreed to another output increase.
