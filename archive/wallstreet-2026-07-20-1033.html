The Lead

Alphabet jumps on a "Frozen v2" chip report as the tape leans back into semis, two days ahead of its own earnings

Stocks are higher in Monday morning trade with chips out front. TheStreet's updated July 20 session coverage has the S&P 500 +0.63%, the Nasdaq +1.02% and the Dow +0.23%. Earlier, just after the open, Yahoo Finance had the Dow up 0.3%, the S&P 500 up 0.5% and the Nasdaq Composite up almost 0.8% — both readings are published here with their source, because neither carried a clock time we could verify.

The single biggest identified driver is Alphabet. The Information reported the company is working on a new server chip — referred to as "Frozen v2" — intended to increase AI model efficiency and boost Gemini's efficiency in particular. Alphabet gained about 1% in premarket trading on the report (Seeking Alpha) and was up roughly 3.5%–3.7% in Monday morning trading; both figures appeared in this run's results and both are shown rather than picking one. The move lands two days before Alphabet's own quarterly report.

Alphabet's Q2 print is due Wednesday, July 22. Analysts are looking for about $116.9 billion in revenue and $2.90 EPS, with earnings and revenue growth expected above 20% and Google Cloud's growth rate — 63% year over year in the first quarter — the central question. Sentiment going in is lopsided: 49 of 54 analysts rate the stock Buy or Strong Buy with zero Sells and a mean target near $434, against a recent close of $346.77 after a $4.67 billion EU antitrust fine, Digital Markets Act data-sharing compliance orders and delays to the Gemini 3.5 Pro model.

The broader setup is the same one that has framed the week: Wall Street is hunting the next catalyst for the AI trade after weeks of sector rotation, with Alphabet, Intel, IBM and Tesla all reporting. "After last week's sharp rotation out of AI-related stocks, investors will be looking for evidence that earnings can continue to justify elevated valuations," said Daniela Hathorn, senior market analyst at Capital.com, who added that markets "are likely to remain highly headline-driven throughout the week."

Geopolitics stayed in the frame without setting the direction. The U.S. carried out its ninth straight day of attacks on Iran over the weekend and Iran retaliated by bombing U.S. allies in the region, primarily Kuwait, with the two sides still far apart on a ceasefire (Yahoo Finance, citing Bloomberg). TheStreet, citing CNN, reported a third American service member killed in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone. On the other side of the ledger, Iranian state agency IRNA — citing Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei, whom TheStreet also renders "Esmail Baghaei" in the same article — said Iran has received proposals from mediators about resuming talks and would pursue them if they align with national interests, while defending itself "resolutely." Oil reversed on that: crude eased after touching $90, and Brent futures remain well below their April and May peaks.

Climbed 4.5% in premarket trading after AMD and Microsoft announced Microsoft will use AMD's Helios rack-scale system in its data centers. AMD begins shipping Helios to customers, including Microsoft, later this year.

▲ 1.62% premkt

SpaceX (SPCX)

Rose 1.62% to $125.99 premarket. SpaceX rescheduled the 13th Starship test flight for Thursday, July 23 after a Raptor 3 engine ignition failure forced an automatic abort. Its June 12 IPO raised $75 billion — the largest in history — and briefly pushed valuation above $2 trillion; the stock hit an all-time intraday high of $225.64 three trading days later.
