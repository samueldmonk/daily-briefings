Top Story

Du Plessis outmuscles Usman in Oklahoma City and points at a Strickland trilogy

Dricus Du Plessis made a fast return to the win column on Saturday night, taking a unanimous decision over Kamaru Usman — 50-45, 49-46, 49-46 — in the main event of UFC Fight Night: Du Plessis vs Usman at the Paycom Center in Oklahoma City. It was his first bout since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev last August.

UFC's own recap put it plainly: the South African's physicality and power were too much, and he wobbled Usman multiple times in the first two rounds with clubbing strikes and well-timed knees up the middle. Usman weathered it and warmed into the fight, but "Stillknocks" stayed on him, hurting him again in the fourth and holding his edge to the final horn. The two shared Fight of the Night honours.

Usman arrived as a former welterweight champion who defended that title five times, with signature wins over Jorge Masvidal and Colby Covington. Du Plessis leaves with options — UFC flagged a potential trilogy bout with current champion Sean Strickland, whom he has beaten twice.

Du Plessis has already made his case against the alternative. On a Strickland–Chimaev rematch he said: "Between the two of them, they have an amazing zero title defenses. So, how do you warrant a rematch? In that case, I have two, so give me a rematch then. I have two wins over the current champion. It makes zero sense."

Prospect Watch

ProspectNewHeavyweight

RJ Harris — debut finish in 100 seconds

"The Hammer" was patient in his first Octagon appearance, then uncorked an uppercut that took Alvin Hines off his feet and kicked off the finishing sequence, stopping him at 1:40 of round one. He kept his undefeated record intact — and per UFC he is 27 years old and only 16 months into his professional career.

ProspectNewLight heavyweight

Felipe Franco — first UFC win, first bonus

The Salvador, Bahia native (10-2) mixed striking with timely takedowns and repeatedly advanced to dominant positions before stopping the previously unbeaten Levi Rodrigues Jr. in round two. UFC called it evidence of "why he's a prospect to keep an eye on at 205 pounds," and paid him a Performance of the Night bonus for it.

ProspectNewStrawweight

Fatima Kline — 4-0 back at 115

"The Archangel" swept the cards against Tabatha Ricci, landing the better strikes while mixing in timely takedowns. She is now 4-0 since returning to the 115-pound division following a promotional debut a weight class up, and UFC says the win punches her ticket to the strawweight Top 10.

ProspectNewMiddleweight

Christian Leroy Duncan — five straight

The Gloucester man collected what UFC called the biggest win of his career, out-working longtime contender Jared Cannonier for a clear decision. That is five consecutive victories overall and back-to-back wins over ranked opponents after an uneven start to his UFC tenure.

Around the Sport

  • Tommy McMillen's third-round stoppage of Alberto Montes came with a record attached: UFC says the undefeated featherweight set the mark for the most significant strikes landed in a three-round bout in the 145-pound weight class.
  • Du Plessis pushed back publicly on a Strickland–Chimaev rematch, arguing that neither man has defended the middleweight belt while he holds two wins over the current champion — a direct pitch for a third fight with Strickland.
  • Dione Barbosa's first-round submission of short-notice newcomer Anna Melisano snapped a run of alternating results across her first five UFC appearances. She trains alongside fellow Contender Series graduate and Oklahoma City cardmate Jean-Paul Lebosnoyani at The Q in Las Vegas.
  • Chase Hooper said he wanted to get back to his grappling roots and did exactly that, taking Mitch Ramirez down and finishing him in under two and a half minutes for his ninth UFC win and seventh finish in the Octagon — ending a two-fight slide.
