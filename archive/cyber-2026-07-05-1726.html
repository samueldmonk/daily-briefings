Top Story

FortiBleed becomes a ransomware pipeline: one operator ran both INC and Lynx panels

Researchers have connected the FortiBleed credential-theft campaign directly to the INC and Lynx ransomware operations — the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been tied straight to ransomware deployment. A custom packet-sniffing tool dubbed "FortiGate Sniffer", planted on compromised firewalls, intercepted VPN and authentication traffic and helped harvest more than 110 million credentials across a campaign scoped to roughly 430,000 FortiGate devices.

The activity was exposed by an attacker operational-security slip — a server holding stolen credentials was left open on the internet. Analysts tracked scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals in more than 150 countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets, completed the full attack chain on 354, and traced at least a dozen ransomware deployments to the access. A single operator was found working negotiation panels for both INC and Lynx, and an internal document points to a structured crew of roughly 20 people. FortiBleed itself involves no new CVE — Fortinet has moved administrator password hashing to PBKDF2 in FortiOS 7.2.11, 7.4.8 and 7.6.1.

Patch Priority

⚠ Patch now: CVE-2026-45659 — Microsoft SharePoint Server (on-prem)

A CVSS 8.8 remote-code-execution flaw (deserialization of untrusted data) is being actively exploited by Storm-2603 to deploy Warlock ransomware. CISA's federal remediation deadline was July 4, 2026 — now overdue. Any authenticated user with Site Member permissions can trigger it; fixes shipped in Microsoft's May 2026 updates for SharePoint Subscription Edition, 2019 and 2016. On-prem SharePoint operators should patch and hunt immediately.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Storm-2603  Ransomware

Storm-2603 has been deploying Warlock ransomware (and, previously, LockBit) by exploiting on-prem SharePoint flaws since mid-2025. The group is now weaponizing CVE-2026-45659 against unpatched SharePoint farms, and leans on Cloudflare tunnels, Zoho Assist and VS Code SSH sessions for persistence that survives single-tool eviction — meaning a clean-up that removes just one foothold is likely to leave the actor with a way back in.
