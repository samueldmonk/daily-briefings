Top Story

Fight Week looms as White House fallout rolls on: McGregor's return, FBI arrests, and an Aspinall–Gane push

With International Fight Week now days away, the sport's attention is split between what's ahead and what just happened. Ahead: Conor McGregor returns at UFC 329 on July 11 — his first fight since UFC 264 in July 2021 — to rematch Max Holloway. Behind: two-plus weeks after the UFC's unprecedented Freedom 250 card on the White House grounds (June 14), the aftershocks are still front-page news. This week the FBI revealed it arrested suspects who allegedly plotted to attack the event — investigators say a mother's tip led them to the group.

The night rewrote two divisions and the maneuvering has already begun. Justin Gaethje stunned Ilia Topuria to take the lightweight title, and Ciryl Gane knocked out Alex Pereira to claim the interim heavyweight belt. Undisputed heavyweight champ Tom Aspinall accepted Gane's challenge to unify — "I'll be there" — while Topuria congratulated Gaethje and insisted there were "no excuses" in defeat. Dana White called the White House spectacle "great" but said the promotion would "never again" stage one there. ESPN's updated divisional rankings summed up the moment bluntly: "Gaethje, Gane are second to none."

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement

ESPN's post-White House divisional update leads with the two men who seized their moment: "Gaethje, Gane are second to none." Gaethje's upset vaults him up the lightweight and pound-for-pound picture, while Gane's finish of Pereira reestablishes him at the top of a suddenly crowded heavyweight class.

Business & broadcast

The needle-mover ahead is Conor McGregor's return at UFC 329 (July 11) — his first bout in nearly five years and the marquee draw of International Fight Week for the UFC and parent TKO Group Holdings. Under the promotion's new broadcast era, its cards — UFC 329 included — stream on Paramount+ rather than traditional pay-per-view.
