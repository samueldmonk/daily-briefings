The Lead

Dow briefly tops 53,000 for the first time, then fades; chips lead a tech rebound

U.S. markets came back from the long Independence Day weekend on the front foot Monday. Around midday, the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite was up about 1.1% and the S&P 500 about 0.6%, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average briefly punched above 53,000 for the first time in its history — trading as high as 53,052.70 — before slipping back toward the flat line as Nike and Amgen weighed on the blue-chip average. Thursday's 52,900.07 close remains the last official finish.

The engine was a rebound in semiconductors after last week's slump — the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) was up about 2.7% at the open. Astera Labs led the way, surging to a fresh all-time high — up roughly 8% to about $493, with an intraday peak of $499.48 — after Bank of America lifted its price target to a Street-high $450 (from $240) and the AI-connectivity chipmaker joined the Nasdaq-100. Lam Research rose more than 4% as Morgan Stanley raised targets across chip-equipment names, Taiwan Semiconductor climbed about 5%, and Dell gained about 7.7% after President Trump promoted its computers at a White House event. The notable laggard was Microsoft, cutting 4,800 jobs — roughly 3,200 Xbox roles through fiscal 2027 — after a first half in which its shares fell nearly 23%. Oil eased after OPEC+ approved another August output hike, and investors looked to Wednesday's Fed minutes for direction.

Movers & Drivers

Astera Labs (ALAB)

▲ record high

The fabless AI-connectivity chipmaker surged to a fresh all-time high — up roughly 8% to ~$493, with an intraday peak of $499.48 — after Bank of America lifted its target to a Street-high $450 (from $240) and the stock joined the Nasdaq-100. The day's biggest mover.

Lam Research (LRCX) New

▲ ~+4%

Lam Research rose more than 4% as Morgan Stanley hiked price targets across chip-equipment names; Applied Materials and KLA also gained, and Taiwan Semiconductor climbed about 5% as the AI-hardware trade reignited.

Dell (DELL)

▲ ~+7.7%

Dell surged about 7.7% after President Trump promoted the company's computers during a White House event Monday. Western Digital (+9.2%), AMD (+8.5%) and the broader SMH chip ETF (+2.7% at the open) rounded out the AI-hardware rebound.

Microsoft (MSFT)

▼ −23% H1

Microsoft is cutting 4,800 jobs (~2.1% of staff), including roughly 3,200 Xbox roles through fiscal 2027 — about 20% of the division. Its shares fell nearly 23% in the first half of 2026, their worst first-half since 2022. Elsewhere, Nike −3.7% and Visa −2.9% were among the laggards.

WTI Crude Oil

▼ ~−0.5%

WTI fell about 0.5% to $68.33 (Brent −0.5% to $71.76) after OPEC+ agreed Sunday to raise August output by a combined +188,000 bpd — a fifth straight monthly increase.

Gold & Silver

▲ +0.9% / +2.4%

Precious metals firmed: gold futures rose about 0.9% to ~$4,164 and silver jumped 2.4% to ~$62.55, holding near record territory as a soft June jobs report supports the case for patient Fed policy.
