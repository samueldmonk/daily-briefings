The Lead

Chips power a broad rebound to the close as Intel rockets to a record on a Trump–Apple chip claim

Wall Street rebounded from Wednesday's hawkish-Fed selloff on Thursday, with the S&P 500 closing up about 1.15%, the Nasdaq Composite roughly +1.5% and the Dow near +0.8% — though the Russell 2000 slipped about 0.72%, underscoring leadership concentrated in megacap chips rather than a broad-based advance. The session's driver was Intel, which surged about 9% to a record after President Trump claimed on Truth Social that Apple has agreed to design and build chips with Intel in the United States; neither company confirmed the deal and Apple shares moved only about 0.6%. The chip complex did the heavy lifting — the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) climbed roughly 6% to a record high, with Micron and Broadcom among the top S&P 500 gainers — while Accenture cratered about 16% after cutting its full-year outlook, and oil slid below $75 on the signed US–Iran deal.
