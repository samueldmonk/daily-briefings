Top Story

"Icarus" threatens to publish Klue supply-chain loot today

The extortion group Icarus, which emerged in late April 2026, has claimed the breach of competitive-intelligence platform Klue and threatened to release data stolen from Klue customers' Salesforce instances today, June 22, unless affected organisations negotiate. Klue says attackers gained access to its backend around June 11 using a long-dormant API credential created for an abandoned integration prototype, then pushed a malicious code update that stole the OAuth tokens customers use to connect Klue's Battlecards product to third-party platforms — most notably Salesforce — and queried those CRM environments directly. The activity was detected on June 12.

The victim list is unusually security-heavy: HackerOne, Huntress, Jamf, OneTrust, Recorded Future, Snyk and Tanium are among affected customers, with Sprout Social, Gong and Insurity also disclosing impact. Stolen data is business CRM information — sales-account records, contacts, price quotes and competitive-intelligence reports — rather than credentials or source code. Huntress said it received extortion contact on June 16 (a threat to "go public within 24 hours") via the Session messaging app from an actor using the alias "mr bean"; on June 20 Icarus issued a blanket deadline to every Klue client it claims to have contacted, giving them until today, June 22 to respond before data is published. As of Monday evening the deadline had arrived with no affected firm signaling any intent to negotiate, and no mass leak had yet been confirmed. Salesforce has disabled the Klue Battlecards integration while the breach is investigated.

Splunk Enterprise — CVE-2026-20253 (CVSS 9.8)

A missing-authentication flaw in a PostgreSQL sidecar service lets an unauthenticated, remote attacker create or truncate files — chainable to pre-auth remote code execution as the Splunk user. It is actively exploited (the first Splunk bug ever added to CISA's KEV, on June 18) and the federal remediation deadline of Sunday, June 21 has now passed — patch immediately. Affected: Splunk Enterprise 10.0.0–10.0.6 and 10.2.0–10.2.3; fix to 10.0.7 / 10.2.4. Patches shipped June 10 and in-the-wild exploitation was confirmed roughly eight days later.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Icarus — OAuth-token theft, no malware required

Icarus is a data-theft and extortion crew that, by its own leak-site claim, has been active since April 28, 2026, and lists only two prior victims — making Klue its highest-profile hit. Rather than deploying ransomware, it monetises stolen business data: in the Klue campaign it leveraged a dormant credential and stolen OAuth tokens from a trusted SaaS integration to siphon Salesforce CRM records from a chain of downstream customers — a clean supply-chain pivot that sidesteps endpoint defences entirely.

The group negotiates over the Session messenger (using the alias "mr bean") and pressures victims with public leak-site countdowns. Its targeting of security vendors — HackerOne, Huntress, Snyk, Tanium and others — makes the Klue incident a notable case of attackers turning a defender's own SaaS supply chain against it.

Breaches & Incidents

Microsoft ships a record June Patch Tuesday

Microsoft's June update fixed a record 208 CVEs — 33 rated Critical — including one actively-exploited zero-day: a Defender elevation-of-privilege flaw (CVE-2026-41091, CVSS 7.8). The headline patched flaw is a wormable Windows kernel use-after-free (CVE-2026-45657, CVSS 9.8, no auth or user interaction). Separately, researchers published proof-of-concept code for "RoguePlanet," a new Defender zero-day that abuses a race condition to spawn a SYSTEM-level shell on fully patched Windows 10/11; a fix is still in development.

Check Point VPN zero-day tied to Qilin

An improper-authentication flaw in Check Point Remote/Mobile Access VPN (CVE-2026-50751) has been exploited in the wild since around May 7, with activity rising in early June; Check Point observed post-exploitation payload retrieval and linked the binaries to the Qilin ransomware operation. It sits on CISA's KEV with a now-passed deadline.

ShinyHunters hit Oracle PeopleSoft

The ShinyHunters extortion crew is exploiting an unauthenticated Oracle PeopleSoft PeopleTools flaw (CVE-2026-35273, CVSS 9.8) to break into enterprise systems, steal data and demand payment in a widespread campaign — with universities and large enterprises among those hit.

ExtortionOracle PeopleSoft

Chrome V8 zero-day patched in emergency fix

Google shipped an emergency update on June 9 for a V8 engine flaw (CVE-2026-11645) that was already being exploited in the wild. It is on CISA's KEV with a federal remediation deadline of June 23 — update Chrome/Chromium browsers now.

Exploited 0-dayGoogle Chrome

F5 ships out-of-band NGINX patches

F5 issued an out-of-band notification on June 17 and emergency patches on June 18 for two critical NGINX flaws (CVE-2026-42530 and CVE-2026-42055, both CVSS 9.2) that let an unauthenticated, remote attacker crash NGINX or — where ASLR is disabled or bypassed — run code via crafted HTTP/3 or HTTP/2 traffic. Affected: NGINX 1.13.10–1.31.1 (Open Source, Plus, Gateway Fabric, Instance Manager); fix to 1.30.3 / 1.31.2. No in-the-wild exploitation reported yet.
