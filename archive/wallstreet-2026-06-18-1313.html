The Lead

Stocks rebound at midday as Intel rockets to a record on a Trump–Apple chip claim

Wall Street clawed back Wednesday's hawkish-Fed losses on Thursday, with the S&P 500 up roughly 1%, the Nasdaq Composite about +1.5% and the Dow near +0.8% as of midday ET — though the Russell 2000 lagged, underscoring a narrow, megacap-chip-led move rather than a broad rally. The session's driver was Intel, which surged about 9% to an intraday record (near $133) after President Trump claimed on Truth Social that Apple has agreed to design and build chips with Intel on its 18A process; neither company has confirmed the deal, and Apple shares barely moved. The rebound also rode a signed US–Iran interim deal that sent oil tumbling, even as a hotter-than-expected May producer-price report kept the Fed's hawkish tilt in view.
