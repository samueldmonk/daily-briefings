Top Story

Du Plessis batters Usman over five rounds, and the Strickland trilogy comes into focus

Dricus Du Plessis defeated Kamaru Usman by unanimous decision (50-45, 49-46, 49-46) in the main event of UFC Fight Night: Du Plessis vs Usman at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City on Saturday, July 18 — his first bout since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev last August.

UFC's own account of the fight is unambiguous about how it went: "The South African's physicality and power were just too much for Usman, as Du Plessis wobbled him multiple times in the first two rounds with clubbing strikes and well-timed knees up the middle." Usman — a former welterweight champion who defended that title five times, with signature wins over Jorge Masvidal and Colby Covington — weathered it and warmed into the fight, but Du Plessis hurt him again in the fourth and held his edge to the final horn.

The bout earned Fight of the Night, worth an extra $100,000 to each man. And it reset the middleweight picture: UFC notes that among Du Plessis's options going forward is "a potential trilogy bout with current champ Sean Strickland, whom he's beaten twice before." Strickland took the belt from Chimaev by split decision at UFC 328 in May — meaning the man who beat Du Plessis for the title no longer holds it, and the man who does has already lost to him twice.

Usman, for his part, told TMZ he believes the performance sets him up for a title fight of his own.

Prospect Watch

ProspectLight heavyweight

Felipe Franco (10-2)

The Salvador, Bahia native took Performance of the Night on his return to 205 pounds, mixing striking with timely takedowns and repeatedly advancing to dominant positions before finishing in the second. It was his first UFC win — UFC's own write-up calls him "a prospect to keep an eye on at 205 pounds."

ProspectHeavyweight

RJ Harris — debut, first-round finish

"The Hammer" was patient before uncorking an uppercut that dropped Alvin Hines and started the finishing sequence, at 1:40 of round one. The 27-year-old heavyweight is undefeated, and — the striking detail — only 16 months into his professional career, already with a first-round UFC finish on debut.

ProspectFeatherweight

Tommy McMillen — a record-setting night

The unbeaten featherweight set a UFC record for the most significant strikes landed in a three-round bout at 145 pounds, pressing forward from the opening bell and eventually overwhelming Alberto Montes late in the third for the TKO and a $100,000 bonus.

Around the Sport

  • Christian Leroy Duncan is on a five-fight run. The Gloucester middleweight took the biggest win of his career in the co-main, out-working Jared Cannonier for a clean decision and posting back-to-back wins over ranked opponents.
  • Fatima Kline is knocking on the Top 10. "The Archangel" is now 4-0 since returning to the 115-pound division following a promotional debut a weight class up, and UFC says the win over Tabatha Ricci punches her ticket to the strawweight Top 10.
  • Chase Hooper stopped the slide. The lightweight said he wanted to get back to his grappling roots and did exactly that, taking Mitch Ramirez down and submitting him inside a round for his ninth UFC win and seventh finish — snapping a two-fight losing run.
  • The middleweight logjam is real. Du Plessis has beaten champion Sean Strickland twice; Strickland took the belt from Khamzat Chimaev, who took it from Du Plessis. Every path at 185 now runs back through fights that have already happened once.
  • Rankings note: coverage ahead of the main event listed Du Plessis at No. 2 and Usman at No. 9 at middleweight, but this came from search coverage rather than a fetched rankings page — treat the numbers as indicative only.
