The Lead

Wall Street starts the second half in the red — as of ~10:30 AM ET

Stocks edged lower Wednesday morning to open the third quarter, a day after major indexes wrapped their strongest quarter since 2020. As of roughly 10:30 AM ET the S&P 500 was down about 0.2% (~17 points), the Dow off about 0.25% (~143 points) and the Nasdaq Composite the laggard at roughly −0.8% (~247 points), while the small-cap Russell 2000 bucked the trade with a ~0.4% gain.

The pressure came from the same chip and AI-infrastructure names that led the second-quarter rally, as investors locked in profits and turned selective. The marquee move belonged to Meta, which jumped about 8% on a Bloomberg report that it plans to sell excess AI computing capacity through a new cloud unit — a shift that lifted the stock but weighed on rival "neocloud" providers. Traders are also weighing the June ADP payrolls report, the ISM manufacturing index and remarks from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh at the ECB's forum in Sintra, Portugal, where he offered no early signal on rates.
