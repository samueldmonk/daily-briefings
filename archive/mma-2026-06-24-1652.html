Top Story

UFC scraps its media panel for the Elo-based "Meta UFC Rankings"

The UFC has begun replacing its long-running media-panel ranking system with the Meta UFC Rankings, an Elo-based mathematical model built with Meta that determines a fighter's place almost entirely from fight data. The framework weighs opponent quality, activity and decay for long layoffs or outdated wins, plus metrics such as outcome probability, win type and recent trajectory. Crucially, it removes the pound-for-pound rankings and minimizes human involvement, with the media panel staying on only through a transition period. Officials stress it is a math algorithm, not artificial intelligence, despite some early reports.

The change has teeth: the first automated rankings published June 23, and 27 fighters — including three former champions — lost their ranking in the initial update, per Bloody Elbow (Robert Whittaker among them; Raquel Pennington and Jan Blachowicz tumbled toward the bottom of their divisions). The overhaul has reopened the perennial debate over how much the official numbers matter for title shots and matchmaking. It lands in a busy stretch on the calendar — the UFC is in Azerbaijan this Saturday for UFC Baku: Fiziev vs. Torres, with Conor McGregor's return against Max Holloway headlining UFC 329 two weeks later.

Sat, Jun 27 · National Gymnastics Arena, Baku

UFC Baku: Fiziev vs. Torres Next

Lightweights Rafael Fiziev (13–5) and Manuel Torres (17–3) headline, with Fiziev — who fights out of Azerbaijan — in a Baku homecoming. The new Meta rankings reshuffled the pair: Torres climbed to No. 13 while Fiziev slipped outside the top 15 — a flip of the outgoing media rankings, where Fiziev sat at No. 11 and Torres four spots back. Both are finishers: Torres rides back-to-back first-round TKOs (Grant Dawson at UFC 323 in December; Drew Dober in March) and is 5–1 in the UFC with every promotional win by first-round stoppage, while Fiziev's last outing was a second-round TKO loss to Mauricio Ruffy at UFC 325 in late January. Streams on Paramount+ (no PPV), afternoon ET.

Prospect Watch

Murtazali Magomedov Prospect

The undefeated featherweight (11–0) announced himself on his UFC debut at Vegas 119, submitting Melsik Baghdasaryan with a twister in 1:17 of Round 1 — only the fourth twister finish in UFC history — and banking a $100,000 Performance of the Night bonus.

Navajo Stirling Prospect

The DWCS graduate stopped veteran Ion Cutelaba in Round 2 for another finish — extending a long unbeaten run — and cementing himself as a light-heavyweight to watch in a division that just crowned a new champion in Carlos Ulberg.

Vinicius "LokDog" Oliveira Prospect

In his first featherweight appearance after moving up from bantamweight, Oliveira weathered early adversity to stop divisional mainstay Andre Fili in Round 2 — a Fight-of-the-Night, $100,000 effort that adds another threat to the 145-pound mix.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement: the UFC's first automated Meta UFC Rankings update (June 23) removed 27 fighters — including three former champions — and scrapped the pound-for-pound list; among the active names, Manel Kape's fourth straight finish bolsters his flyweight title case while debutant Murtazali Magomedov forces his way into the featherweight conversation.

Business & broadcast: the UFC's U.S. home is now Paramount+ — in year one of a seven-year, $7.7 billion media-rights deal (the UFC is owned by TKO Group) — where cards stream without the traditional pay-per-view surcharge. The June 14 White House card, UFC Freedom 250, became Paramount+'s most-watched live event, averaging about 8.2 million viewers (roughly 7M in the U.S.) and totaling around 17 million, though TKO president Mark Shapiro said the ~$60M event isn't expected to turn a profit, with the goal of recouping about half via sponsors. This Saturday's UFC Baku, UFC 329, and the July 18 (Oklahoma City) and July 25 (Abu Dhabi) Fight Nights all stream on Paramount+ with no PPV.
