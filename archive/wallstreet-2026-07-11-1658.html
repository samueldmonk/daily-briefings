The Lead

Weekend edition: Friday's rise caps a choppy week, with CPI and the banks up next

U.S. markets are closed for the weekend. In Friday's session — the most recent close — the S&P 500 rose 31.75 points (+0.42%) to 7,575.39, the Nasdaq Composite added 74.72 (+0.29%) to 26,281.61, and the Dow gained 149.60 (+0.29%) to 52,637.01, with the S&P ending near its session highs as investors monitored U.S.–Iran ceasefire talks (Yahoo Finance). The index finished roughly 40 points shy of its June 2 record close (Investrade).

The day belonged to SK Hynix: the Korean chipmaker's $26.5 billion U.S. share sale — the largest-ever American listing by a foreign company — priced at $149, opened at $170 and finished its first session up 13% (Investrade/Motley Fool). Meta climbed about 6%, capping a roughly 15% week — its best since early 2024, per CNBC — and Nvidia rose about 4%. For the week, the S&P 500 gained about 1.2% and the Nasdaq 1.7%, while the Dow slipped about 0.5%. The next test comes Tuesday: June CPI at 8:30 AM ET and Q2 kickoff earnings from the biggest banks.

Sources: WTOP/AP, Yahoo Finance, CNBC, Investrade — figures are Friday, July 10 official closes

Meta (META) ~+6%

The week's megacap standout

Meta jumped about 6% Friday and roughly 15% on the week — its best weekly gain since early 2024, per CNBC.

Circle (CRCL) +5–14%

OCC clears stablecoin issuer as a trust bank

Circle rallied after the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency granted final approval Friday to establish First National Digital Currency Bank — operating as a national trust bank for USDC's reserve and custody infrastructure. The pop faded through the session: up 14% early to $71.93, +12% per Benzinga, +8.2% in Seeking Alpha's recap, with one late tally near +5% (Yahoo Finance/Benzinga/Stocktwits).

Fermi (FRMI) −17.3%

Convertible offering dilutes the story

Fermi tumbled 17.3% after pricing an upsized $375 million offering of 5.00% convertible senior notes due 2031 (Seeking Alpha).

Ionis (IONS) −7.6%

Wainua heart-drug trial fallout

Ionis remained under pressure after shares plunged 24% on Thursday's news that the Phase 3 CARDIO-TTRansform trial for Wainua with partner AstraZeneca failed (Investrade); Seeking Alpha's Friday recap had it down a further 7.6%, even as the company reiterated its 2026 revenue guidance of $875M–$900M.

Vodafone (VOD) +12%

Emirates Telecom stake sale at a premium

Vodafone shares jumped 12% after Emirates Telecom agreed to sell its stake to Xavier Niel's Vega for $5.95 billion, a price above market (Investrade/CNBC midday movers).

SK Hynix (ADR) +13%

Record foreign debut pops

SK Hynix priced at $149, opened at $170 and finished its first Nasdaq session up about 13%, after raising $26.5 billion — the largest-ever U.S. listing by a foreign company (Investrade/Motley Fool). CEO Kwak Noh-Jung says memory-chip shortages could persist beyond 2030.
