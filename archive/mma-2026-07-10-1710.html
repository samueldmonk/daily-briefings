Top Story

It's official: McGregor and Holloway make weight for Saturday's rematch, 13 years in the making New

Conor McGregor stepped on the scale Friday morning in Las Vegas at 170.5 pounds and Max Holloway hit 170 on the nose — making Saturday's UFC 329 welterweight main event at T-Mobile Arena official. It's McGregor's first MMA bout in five years and Holloway's debut at 170 pounds; McGregor (22-6) won their 2013 first meeting by decision, and per this week's sourced records Holloway stands 27-9.

The betting line has been one of the stories of fight week: Holloway opened as high as −550 (McGregor near +420) but money has poured in on the Irishman all week, compressing the line to Holloway −225 / McGregor +185 by Friday, with books ranging −210 to −240. McGregor showed no mic rust at the pre-fight press conference — TMZ reports he belted Oasis' "Wonderwall" and yanked Holloway's glasses in a night heavy on theater. Every fighter on the card made weight.

Sources: UFC.com official weigh-in results; CBS Sports; Yahoo Sports; Sportsbook Review; Sherdog; TMZ (July 10)
