Top Story

Klue OAuth breach cascades into Salesforce data theft at HackerOne, Huntress, Gong and more

Supply ChainOAuthExtortion

A supply-chain compromise at Klue, a competitive- and sales-intelligence platform, has cascaded into Salesforce data theft across at least eleven organizations — many of them well-known technology and security companies. Klue says it detected unauthorized activity in its integration infrastructure on June 12, 2026, traced to a long-disused legacy credential — originally created for an abandoned third-party integration prototype — that let attackers abuse the OAuth tokens Klue uses to connect into customers' environments and quietly exfiltrate CRM data through those integrations. Salesforce disabled the Klue Battlecards integration on June 11.

A newly surfaced extortion group calling itself Icarus — first observed in April 2026 — has claimed responsibility and begun leaking data. Firms that have disclosed impact include HackerOne, Huntress, Tanium, Jamf, Snyk, Kudelski Security, Recorded Future, Insurity, Sprout Social, BeyondTrust and LastPass; Gong said attackers may have accessed "internal licensed user data" — usernames, business titles and emails — for a subset of customers that used the Klue integration. The stolen material spans business contacts, sales communications, price quotes, competitive-intelligence reports and account data. Icarus began sending victims 48-hour extortion demands — Huntress reported receiving such messages on June 16 — and formally listed Klue on its leak site on June 19, a reminder that OAuth tokens into SaaS platforms are now a prime target for supply-chain intrusions.

Patch Priority
⚠ Federal deadline now PASSED — patch immediately

Ubiquiti UniFi OS — CVE-2026-34910 (CVSS 10.0)

An improper-input-validation flaw lets an attacker inject and run arbitrary OS commands. Researchers showed it chains with two other max-severity bugs — CVE-2026-34908 (access-control bypass) and CVE-2026-34909 (path traversal) — so a network-based attacker can reach unauthenticated, root-level remote code execution on UniFi OS devices. All three are being actively exploited in the wild. Fix: apply Ubiquiti's patched UniFi OS build immediately. CISA added the trio (plus a Lantronix EDS5000 flaw) to the KEV catalog on June 23 with a federal remediation deadline of June 26, 2026 under BOD 26-04 — that deadline has now passed; any unpatched device should be remediated immediately. Next up: actively-exploited flaws in Cisco Unified CM (CVE-2026-20230, CVSS 8.6) and PTC Windchill / FlexPLM (CVE-2026-12569, CVSS 9.3) are both due tomorrow, June 28.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Icarus — the extortion crew behind the Klue Salesforce supply-chain theft

First observed around April 2026, the extortion group calling itself Icarus has claimed responsibility for the Klue breach, exfiltrating Salesforce CRM data through the OAuth tokens Klue uses to connect into customer environments. The crew formally listed Klue on its leak site on June 19 and has issued short-fuse extortion demands to downstream victims — security vendor Huntress reported receiving a 48-hour demand on June 16. In an ironic twist, researchers have been able to identify additional victims because the group's own operations were exposed — SecurityWeek's reporting on the widening cascade ran under the banner "hackers get hacked." Icarus's target list skews unusually security-savvy: HackerOne, BeyondTrust, LastPass, Tanium, Jamf, Snyk, Kudelski Security, Recorded Future, Insurity, Sprout Social and Gong have all been named.

Breaches & Incidents

Operation Endgame strikes again — StealC, Amadey & SocGholish dismantled Law Enforcement

Europol and Eurojust announced on June 24 that the latest phase of Operation Endgame neutralized 326 servers and 142 domains behind the StealC and Amadey infostealer/loader networks and the SocGholish "fake browser update" operation. Investigators recovered roughly 27 million stolen credentials from 385,000+ systems and froze over €41 million (~$46.5 million) in criminal crypto. Partners spanned Canada, Denmark, Germany, the Netherlands, the UK and the US, plus Microsoft, Proofpoint, ESET, IBM X-Force and others; 106 SocGholish servers were seized and 14,971 hijacked WordPress sites disinfected.

Cisco Unified CM exploited in the wild

CISA added CVE-2026-20230 (CVSS 8.6), a server-side request forgery flaw in Cisco Unified Communications Manager, to its KEV catalog on June 25 after confirmed exploitation. Attackers abuse it to write arbitrary files to the underlying OS and escalate to root, with public exploit code now circulating; Cisco says there are no workarounds. Federal agencies must remediate by June 28.

Polymarket to reimburse ~$3M after a vendor-breach code injection Web3

Prediction-market platform Polymarket says it will fully reimburse customers who lost an estimated $3 million after attackers injected malicious code into the site following a breach of a third-party vendor. The incident underscores how front-end and supply-chain compromises — not just direct intrusions — can drain user funds, and how platforms are increasingly absorbing the losses to retain trust.

Texas Parks & Wildlife — 3M+ exposed via license vendor

The Texas Parks and Wildlife Department disclosed a breach affecting roughly 3 million people after a third-party vendor that sells hunting and fishing licenses was compromised. Exposed data includes names, email and physical addresses, phone numbers, and driver's license and passport numbers — but not Social Security numbers, dates of birth or financial data. Texas Cyber Command flagged the intrusion; TPWD was notified May 13, filed its breach notice June 12 and disclosed publicly June 18, offering a free year of credit monitoring (enroll by Sept. 14).

"Mistic" backdoor hits multiple sectors in financially motivated attacks

Researchers are tracking a stealthy new backdoor dubbed Mistic, deployed in financially motivated intrusions against organizations in insurance, education, IT and professional services since April 2026. Separately, a phishing campaign has been hitting hotel and hospitality firms across Europe and Asia since April, using photo-themed ZIP files to drop a Node.js implant — a reminder that loaders and implants remain the entry point for follow-on theft and extortion.

Klue / Icarus — security & tech firms swept up

The Klue OAuth breach (see Top Story) has pulled in an unusually security-savvy roster of victims via stolen Salesforce data: HackerOne, Huntress, Tanium, Jamf, Snyk, Kudelski Security, Recorded Future, Insurity, Sprout Social and Gong. The Icarus group is leaking and extorting; reporting suggests the underlying Klue intrusion may have touched many downstream customers.
