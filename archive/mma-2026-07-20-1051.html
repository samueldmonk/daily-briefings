Top Story

Du Plessis removes himself from the middleweight queue — and hands the argument to Imavov

The cleanest path to the next middleweight title fight just got redrawn by the man who had the best claim to it. After beating Kamaru Usman by unanimous decision on Saturday, Dricus du Plessis said Sean Strickland should fight someone else — specifically that Nassourdine Imavov has earned the right to challenge next. Du Plessis also dismissed the alternative bluntly, calling a Strickland–Chimaev rematch "senseless."

That leaves two names and no decision. Khamzat Chimaev, ranked No. 1, is demanding an immediate rematch after losing the belt to Strickland by split decision at UFC 328 in May. Imavov sits at No. 3 on five consecutive wins, with Caio Borralho, Israel Adesanya and Brendan Allen in his last three. Du Plessis, now 24-3 and No. 2 after scorecards of 50-45, 49-46 and 49-46, has effectively vacated his own turn in the line.

Dana White has still not named a challenger. Usman, at No. 9, offered his own read on where the division should look next: "It's either Sean Strickland or Islam" Makhachev.

A note on a recurring trap: several articles circulating today under headlines like "Sean Strickland's next opponent confirmed" and "Strickland vs Imavov next as Chimaev eyes 205" carry May bylines and quote Strickland saying he would "support my boy Alex at the White House" — an event that happened on June 14. They describe the post-UFC 328 picture, not a booking, and are not treated as news here.

Ankalaev vs Guskov

UFC.com bills it as "a former champion and rising star face off in Abu Dhabi." Prospect Watch

Prospect

Tommy "Gun" McMillen — featherweight

28, now 2-0 in the UFC and still undefeated. UFC.com called the Montes win "a record-setting performance for the unbeaten featherweight" — he set the record for most significant strikes landed in a three-round bout at 145 pounds, and took a $100,000 Performance of the Night bonus for it.

Prospect

Felipe "Negão" Franco — light heavyweight

25 and 11-2, with his first UFC victory on Saturday. Back at 205 after debuting on short notice at heavyweight in March against Mario Pinto, he took the back, moved to mount and finished with hammer punches at 1:40 of round two. Also a $100,000 Performance of the Night winner.

Prospect

Ezra Elliott — featherweight

8-0 and unbeaten on debut under difficult circumstances: he took the fight on Tuesday morning and missed weight on Friday. He still set the UFC record for most takedowns in a featherweight fight.

Prospect

RJ "The Hammer" Harris — heavyweight

27, undefeated, and only 16 months into his professional career. He won his UFC debut by TKO at 1:40 of round one.
