Top Story

Public exploit code lands for “wp2shell,” the WordPress Core pre-auth RCE chain

The unauthenticated remote code execution chain in WordPress Core disclosed last week has escalated: BleepingComputer reports the wp2shell flaws now have public exploits, and coverage this weekend describes the full mechanism as published with a working proof-of-concept posted to GitHub. That is a material change from the initial disclosure, when Rapid7 said it was not aware of publicly confirmed in-the-wild exploitation and The Hacker News reported no exploitation attempts as of July 18.

The chain pairs CVE-2026-63030, a REST API batch-route confusion issue, with CVE-2026-60137, a SQL injection in WordPress core; chained, they carry an anonymous request through to code execution. Affected versions are WordPress 6.9.0 through 6.9.4 and 7.0.0 through 7.0.1. WordPress shipped 6.8.6, 6.9.5 and 7.0.2 on July 17, each closing the issues relevant to its branch, and the security team force-pushed automatic updates to affected sites that had auto-updates enabled. Cloudflare deployed WAF rules and reported the vulnerable path is reachable when a persistent object cache is not in use.

Scoring is still split and worth noting rather than resolving: Tenable records an NVD CVSS v3 base score of 7.5 (High) for CVE-2026-63030, while the GitHub Security Advisory published July 17 rates it Critical. The flaw is not in CISA’s KEV catalog, which requires confirmed exploitation, and no in-the-wild exploitation had been reported as of July 18 — but with the chain public, patched and effective against a default setting, exposure is now a function of patch speed.

Patch Priority

Overdue — act now

CVE-2026-58644 — Microsoft SharePoint Server — federal deadline has passed

A deserialization-of-untrusted-data flaw carrying a CVSS of 9.8 that lets an unauthorized attacker execute arbitrary code. CISA added it to the KEV catalog on July 16 and set the FCEB remediation deadline at July 19, 2026 — which has now elapsed. It affects all supported on-premises versions (Subscription Edition, 2019 and 2016), was exploited in the wild before patches were available, and was fixed in Microsoft’s July 2026 security updates. CISA notes post-exploitation activity including theft of IIS machine keys for persistence and malware deployment, and issued a separate July 14 alert urging SharePoint hardening.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters

The data-extortion group remains the through-line across this month’s biggest incident clusters. Its Oracle PeopleSoft campaign — consistent with exploitation of CVE-2026-35273, a critical RCE in the Environment Management component carrying a CVSS of 9.8 — ran between May 27 and June 9, 2026, with extortion messages reaching victims from June 9, before public reporting. The group claims data from more than 100 organizations across roughly 300 compromised PeopleSoft instances, weighted heavily toward the education sector. On July 3 a cluster of notifications landed together: Nissan, Kubota North America and Aflac’s Japan subsidiary.

Separately, in research published July 13, Microsoft mapped a year of activity matching ShinyHunters’ methods against corporate Salesforce environments to three distinct techniques — campaigns that ran from mid-2025 into mid-2026 and that, notably, did not exploit a flaw in the Salesforce platform itself, instead abusing OAuth connections to third-party apps and vendors.

Breaches & Incidents

NewThird partyTax data

Ernst & Young notifies clients after IT support platform breach

EY is notifying clients that an unauthorized third party breached a support ticket platform used by its IT staff and downloaded documents containing client tax data. EY identified anomalous activity on April 23, 2026; the investigation placed unauthorized access between March 28 and April 12, 2026. Compromised documents contained personal information tied to individuals’ investment holdings with EY’s institutional clients along with financial information used to prepare tax filings, and may include Social Security numbers, financial account codes and credit and debit account information. EY filed breach notifications with the California Attorney General on July 15 and began notifying individuals on July 13, offering identity monitoring through Experian IdentityWorks. EY says it has no current evidence of misuse.

NewEnergyExtortion

Ecopetrol says attackers stole data tied to ~3,300 accounts

Colombia’s state-controlled oil company disclosed the incident in a formal statement on July 17, confirming unauthorized access to cloud file-storage environments by an external actor who has not been identified. Data tied to about 3,300 user accounts was stolen, affecting the cloud file-storage environments of 15 subsidiaries including Ecopetrol itself. The company says it prevented an attempted ransomware attack; the attacker has communicated extortion demands and threatened to publish the data. Ecopetrol reported no critical disruption to operations, production capacity or direct financial impact as of Friday, and said no stolen data had been disclosed by the hacker.

Zero-dayEmployee data

Nissan employee data taken through Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day

Nissan disclosed a breach of employee data via the PeopleSoft zero-day CVE-2026-35273, exploited between May 27 and June 9, 2026 — before Oracle’s emergency June 10 patch. Current and former employees in the US, Canada, Mexico and Brazil are affected, with data potentially including contact, banking, Social Security and Social Insurance numbers, national ID, financial and tax records and dependent or beneficiary information. The disclosure came in the July 3 cluster alongside Kubota North America and Aflac Japan.

SaaSResearch

Microsoft maps a year of Salesforce intrusions to three attack paths

In research published July 13, Microsoft documented campaigns running from mid-2025 into mid-2026 in which attackers whose tradecraft matches ShinyHunters entered corporate Salesforce environments without exploiting any flaw in the platform. The route in was trust the organization had already extended — typically OAuth connections binding Salesforce to surrounding apps and third-party vendors — which Microsoft resolved into three distinct techniques.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines

  • CVE-2026-58644 — Microsoft SharePoint Server, added to KEV July 16, 2026. FCEB remediation due July 19, 2026.
  • CVE-2026-25089 and CVE-2026-39808 — Fortinet FortiSandbox OS command injection. FCEB remediation due July 19, 2026.
  • CVE-2026-46817 — Oracle E-Business Suite, assigned under BOD 26-04. FCEB remediation was due July 18, 2026.
  • Deadline rule: KEV remediation dates are now risk-based and assigned per CVE under BOD 26-04. The old flat “three weeks from add date” heuristic from BOD 22-01 is superseded — always use the date CISA states for the specific CVE.
