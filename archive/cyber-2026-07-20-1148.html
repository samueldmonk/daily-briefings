Top Story

SonicWall's SMA1000 zero-days now carry an expired federal deadline

CISA added CVE-2026-15409 and CVE-2026-15410 to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on July 14 and ordered Federal Civilian Executive Branch agencies to remediate them by July 17, 2026. That date has passed. This is the first edition able to publish a CISA-stated due date for these two CVEs — six previous runs carried SonicWall without one because no fetched source stated it.

CVE-2026-15409 (CVSS 10.0) is a server-side request forgery flaw in the SMA1000 Appliance Work Place interface that a remote, unauthenticated attacker can use to force the appliance to make requests to unintended locations. CVE-2026-15410 (CVSS 7.2) is a post-authentication code injection flaw in the Appliance Management Console that lets an authenticated administrator execute arbitrary operating system commands.

Rapid7's MDR team discovered the activity, and telemetry ties it to a threat actor connected to the Inc ransomware-as-a-service operation using both flaws as zero-days — burrowing into enterprise networks, sweeping credentials and staging ransomware. The detail that matters for remediation: after gaining a foothold the actor extracts high-value credentials, active session databases and TOTP multi-factor authentication seed configurations, which is why applying the patch does not by itself evict an intruder who already has a foothold.

SonicWall has released a hotfix for both CVEs, per Check Point Research's July 20 threat intelligence report. Organisations that were exposed should assume session and MFA-seed compromise and rotate accordingly.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Ransomware-as-a-serviceZero-day operator

Inc Ransomware

Runs a ransomware-as-a-service model and is the group behind the SonicWall SMA1000 zero-day campaign, per Rapid7 telemetry and Check Point's July 20 reporting. Its tradecraft is notable for what it takes before deploying an encryptor: credentials, active session databases and TOTP MFA seed configurations, engineered for durable re-entry rather than a single smash-and-grab. Inc has also been linked, alongside Lynx, to the FortiBleed leak of roughly 74,000 FortiGate credentials.

Breaches & Incidents

NewSupply chain

Jscrambler npm package compromised

Five malicious releases of the jscrambler npm package — 8.14.0, 8.16.0, 8.17.0, 8.18.0 and 8.20.0 — were published on July 11 by an attacker using a stolen npm publishing credential, which Jscrambler has confirmed as the cause. Each carries a cross-platform Rust infostealer with Windows, macOS and Linux builds that harvests credentials and secrets from cloud providers, cryptocurrency wallets and AI coding assistants. In 8.14.0 the dropper runs from a preinstall hook; in 8.18.0 and 8.20.0 it was moved into the package's main code and CLI, so npm install --ignore-scripts does not stop it. Socket flagged the release six minutes after publication. Jscrambler and Socket both point to 8.22.0 as the clean version. The package sees more than 15,000 weekly downloads.

RansomwareManufacturing

Fairlife (Coca-Cola)

Coca-Cola's U.S. dairy subsidiary confirmed a ransomware attack that temporarily halted production across the United States. Attackers reached systems supporting manufacturing operations, prompting the company to activate incident response and business continuity procedures. Coca-Cola filed an SEC Form 8-K dated 2026-07-16.

RansomwareRust

Spirals ransomware

A Rust-based family documented by Check Point Research against a South Asian IT services company. The operators moved from initial access to network-wide encryption in under 24 hours, using an IIS web shell, WMI and PsExec to spread while disabling security services and disrupting backups.

AI platformAutonomous agent

Hugging Face

The open-source AI platform was breached by an autonomous AI agent system targeting its production infrastructure. Disclosure came July 16 with scope confirmation on July 20; the intrusion generated more than 17,000 recorded events and exposed an asymmetry between the guardrails applied to human and agentic activity.

Third partyProfessional services

Ernst & Young

An unauthorized third party breached an IT-staff support-ticket platform. Anomalous activity was identified April 23, 2026 for an access window of March 28 to April 12. Tickets held client tax documents plus personal information tied to investment holdings, potentially including Social Security numbers and financial account details. California AG notification was filed July 15 and individual notifications began July 13; Experian IdentityWorks was offered, with no current evidence of misuse.

ExtortionEnergy

Ecopetrol

A formal statement on July 17 disclosed unauthorized access to the cloud file-storage environments of 15 subsidiaries, with data tied to roughly 3,300 user accounts stolen. An attempted ransomware deployment was prevented. An unidentified actor issued extortion demands and threatened publication; the company reported no critical operational, production or financial impact and no data published as of Friday.
