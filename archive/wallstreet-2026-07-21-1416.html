The Lead

Memory chips snap a three-day slide — Nasdaq up about 1.3% as of roughly 2 PM ET

US stocks are higher across the board on Tuesday, ending a three-session losing streak. As of approximately 2 PM ET the Nasdaq Composite is up about 1.3% and the S&P 500 about 0.9%, with the Dow up 355.17 points as of 2:06 PM ET — roughly +0.7% against Monday's close, though several outlets rounded the Dow's move to +0.8% during the session.

The engine is memory. Micron climbed 10.4% to $955.79 in midday trading, dragging the PHLX Semiconductor Index up 4.9%, after guiding fiscal Q4 revenue to $50 billion with a 23.5% increase in non-GAAP EPS to $31.00. Western Digital and Seagate each added about 12%, and the Roundhill Memory ETF (DRAM) rose 11%. Nvidia added 1.6%.

Two other forces are in the tape. Earnings are landing well — investors are positioning ahead of a heavy Big Tech week. And geopolitics remains the counterweight: the US has now carried out 10 consecutive nights of strikes on Iran, but reports that mediators are pushing for a 10-day ceasefire eased oil in early Tuesday trading before crude turned higher again. Monday also brought a 50% US tariff on most Canadian goods, which has not visibly derailed the rebound.

Note on precision: reported percentage moves for the Dow varied across sources during the session — a 2:03 PM read of +1.38% is inconsistent with the +355.17-point figure timestamped three minutes later and is not used here. Movers & Drivers

New▲ ~90%

Utz Brands — taken private by Intersnack at a 91% premium

Shares of the snack maker surged almost 90% to about $14 Tuesday morning after agreeing to be acquired by Germany's Intersnack Group for $14.25 a share in cash — a premium of roughly 91% to Monday's close, valuing the transaction at approximately $2.9 billion enterprise value. On completion Utz becomes privately held, jointly owned 50/50 by Intersnack and the Rice and Lissette family entities, and delists from the NYSE. Intersnack, founded in Germany in 1968, reported roughly $5 billion of 2025 sales across 31 countries and currently has no US presence. The deal is expected to close in Q4, subject to regulatory approval.

New▲ 10.4%Guidance

Micron leads a memory melt-up

Micron rose 10.4% to $955.79 midday on fiscal Q4 guidance of $50 billion revenue and non-GAAP EPS up 23.5% to $31.00, lifting the PHLX Semiconductor Index 4.9%. Western Digital and Seagate each gained about 12%; the Roundhill Memory ETF (DRAM) added 11%. Context worth keeping: Micron's stock had fallen 29% since June 25 heading into this move.

New▲ 15%

Nebius rallies on an Nvidia stake

The Amsterdam-based AI cloud company jumped 15% after Nvidia disclosed a 9.3% stake. Nebius has become one of Europe's leading "neoclouds" supplying AI compute — a category the market has been repricing sharply on any sign of hyperscaler or chipmaker endorsement.

▲ Crypto

Crypto-linked names run with the risk bid

Coinbase advanced almost 12%, trading app Robinhood rose 9%, and bitcoin miner TeraWulf gained 8% — the clearest read that Tuesday's move is a broad risk-on rotation rather than a chips-only story.

On the Radar

  • FOMC, July 28–29. Officials have entered the pre-meeting blackout period. Policymakers are widely expected to leave the federal funds rate unchanged at 3.50–3.75%.
  • The market is pricing a hike, not a cut. Traders continue to price one Fed rate increase this year, with the probability of a September move holding above 60% — a repricing driven by the oil shock rather than by growth data.
  • Big Tech earnings. A heavy week of technology results is the near-term catalyst investors are positioning into; today's chip strength is partly anticipation.
  • Iran. Ten consecutive nights of US strikes, with mediators reported to be pushing a 10-day ceasefire. Oil is the transmission channel — Brent is up 2.11% on the day even with ceasefire headlines circulating.
  • Canada tariffs. A 50% US tariff on most Canadian goods was instituted Monday; the earnings-driven rebound has so far absorbed it.
