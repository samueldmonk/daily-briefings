The Notorious returns: McGregor–Holloway 2 headlines UFC 329 in Las Vegas

The fight world's attention turns to Las Vegas as UFC 329 (Saturday, July 11, T-Mobile Arena) marks the return of former two-division champion Conor McGregor — his first MMA bout since breaking his leg against Dustin Poirier at UFC 264 in July 2021 — against Max Holloway in a welterweight main event, a rematch of their 2013 featherweight fight that McGregor won by decision. Paddy Pimblett meets Benoît Saint-Denis in the co-main event on a 13-fight card.

Oddsmakers install Holloway as the clear favorite given McGregor's long layoff — a consensus of about −240 with McGregor a +180 underdog, per Covers, Odds Shark and Yahoo Sports (FanDuel opened Holloway −298 / McGregor +240; Kalshi prices Holloway near a 73% chance). Holloway opened as short as −400 a month ago before money on the returning Irishman tightened the line. The card streams on Paramount+.
