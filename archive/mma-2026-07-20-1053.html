Top Story

Du Plessis batters Usman over five rounds — and puts himself back at the front of the middleweight queue

Dricus Du Plessis defeated Kamaru Usman by unanimous decision on Saturday in the main event of UFC Fight Night at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City, taking the cards 50-45, 49-46, 49-46. It was the former middleweight champion’s first bout since losing the title to Khamzat Chimaev last August, and it was a meeting of two men — a former middleweight titleholder and a former welterweight champion — trying to establish themselves in the 185-pound picture.

Du Plessis left no doubt about what he wants next. “I said a win wouldn’t get me a title shot, but, damn, a performance like that will,” he said afterward. The argument has teeth: he has already beaten reigning champion Sean Strickland twice, and the first defense of Strickland’s second reign has not yet been announced. Ahead of the fight, Du Plessis had also dismissed the idea of a Strickland–Chimaev rematch as “senseless.”

The bout earned Fight of the Night, with Du Plessis and Usman each banking an extra $100,000.

Around the Sport

  • The middleweight logjam. Sean Strickland has not yet booked the first defense of his second reign. Du Plessis has beaten him twice and just beat Usman; Du Plessis has separately called a Strickland–Chimaev rematch “senseless.”
  • Oklahoma City delivered. The card was the UFC’s debut at Paycom Center and its first event in Oklahoma City in almost 10 years, and the main card followed an already lively set of prelims.
  • Heavyweight’s unification question. Undisputed champion Tom Aspinall has accepted a challenge from interim titleholder Ciryl Gane, saying “I’ll be there” — the belts have been split since Gane stopped Alex Pereira at UFC Freedom 250 in June.
