The Lead

Stocks stay split as oil sinks and Wall Street awaits Micron (as of ~2:15 PM ET, June 24)

A morning bounce gave way to a choppy, mixed tape. As of about 2:15 PM ET, the Dow is up roughly 0.3% (about +126 points), while the S&P 500 has slipped ~0.3% and the Nasdaq Composite is off ~0.4% — both reversing gains of more than half a percent earlier in the session. The split reflects a rotation rather than a rout: tumbling oil dragged Treasury yields lower and lifted rate-sensitive and cyclical names (industrials and consumer-cyclicals led), while semiconductors softened again and Micron eased about 3% ahead of fiscal third-quarter results after today's close — the week's marquee corporate catalyst and, in many eyes, the next big test of the AI trade. The session follows Tuesday's deep, chip-led selloff, when the Nasdaq Composite closed −2.21% at 25,587.04, the S&P 500 fell 1.44% to 7,365.46, the Dow finished essentially flat (−0.09%) at 51,666.84, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index dropped more than 8%; the rout went global overnight, with South Korea's KOSPI sliding nearly 10%.

Expectations into the Micron print are high: FactSet consensus is about $20.83 adjusted EPS on roughly $35.75B revenue — a vast jump from $1.91 EPS on about $9.3B a year ago — with a record gross margin near 81% and high-bandwidth memory (HBM) commentary the swing factor; Micron says its HBM capacity is sold out through 2026, and analysts are split, from TD Cowen's $1,500 buy-rated target to Goldman Sachs' $900 neutral (Rosenblatt expects a "beat and raise"). The day's standout tech story is in custom silicon: Broadcom climbed after it and OpenAI unveiled "Jalapeño," OpenAI's first in-house AI chip — an LLM-inference accelerator built by Broadcom that CEO Hock Tan says runs roughly 50% cheaper than typical GPUs, designed end-to-end in about nine months, with initial deployment targeted for late 2026. Away from chips, the eye-catching mover is Wendy's (WEN), up more than 40% intraday before paring on a "Save Wendy's" retail meme squeeze and the hire of Steve Cirulis (ex-Potbelly CFO/chief strategy officer) as CFO and chief strategy officer. Other gainers included Sunrun (+22%), Builders FirstSource (+9.7%) and Chemours (on a $450M environmental settlement), while Robinhood fell on reports Meta is building a rival prediction-markets app and Hertz dropped ~27% on a roughly $100M stock offering, with Cerebras (−16%) and Casey's (−7%) also lower. In an index shake-up, Alphabet (GOOGL) will join the Dow before Monday, June 29's open, replacing Verizon. On the macro side, the Fed held its target at 3.50–3.75% on June 17 — a fourth straight hold and the first meeting under new Chair Kevin Warsh — and Bank of America and Deutsche Bank now expect three quarter-point hikes in 2026 (September, October, December). WTI crude slid toward $70 — its lowest since early March, before the U.S.–Iran conflict — after Washington granted Tehran a 60-day license to sell oil internationally and Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic picked up; the slide dragged the 10-year Treasury yield below 4.5% (around 4.48%), while gold tumbled ~3% to about $4,000, its lowest since November 2025.

Broadcom — OpenAI's "Jalapeño" chip ▲New

CEO Hock Tan said it runs roughly 50% cheaper than typical GPUs; OpenAI says it was designed end-to-end in about nine months, with first deployments targeted for late 2026 — a direct challenge to Nvidia in inference.

Micron — eases before tonight's report ▼ ≈ 3%

Micron slipped about 3% into the afternoon — after rebounding ~4% pre-market from Tuesday's ~13% slide — ahead of fiscal-Q3 earnings after today's close. FactSet consensus is about $20.83 adjusted EPS on ~$35.75B revenue, up from $1.91 a year earlier, with a record gross margin near 81% and HBM (sold out through 2026) the key tell for the AI-memory trade.

Decliners — Hertz, Cerebras, Robinhood ▼New

Hertz (HTZ) fell about 27% on a roughly $100M stock offering; AI-chip startup Cerebras (CBRS) dropped ~16% and Casey's (CASY) ~7%. Robinhood slid on reports that Meta is developing a rival prediction-markets application — pockets of weakness against an otherwise rotation-driven tape.
