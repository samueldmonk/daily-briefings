The Lead

Wall Street reopens with a tech-led bounce; chips lead as oil slides

U.S. markets came back from the long Independence Day weekend on the front foot Monday. As of roughly 9:50 AM ET, the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite was up about 0.8%, the S&P 500 about 0.5% and the Dow about 0.2% — a mirror image of Thursday's session, when a chip sell-off had pinned tech even as the blue-chip Dow closed at a record.

The catalyst was a rebound in semiconductors: the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) was up about 2.4% heading into the open, easing the late-June jitters over the AI trade. Optimism was fed by a stronger-than-expected quarterly sales report from Nvidia supplier Hon Hai (Foxconn) over the weekend and anticipation of Samsung Electronics' Q2 results on Tuesday. Meanwhile oil fell after OPEC+ agreed to a fifth straight monthly output increase, and investors turned to a week of services data and Wednesday's Fed minutes for direction on rate-cut — or rate-hike — odds.

Movers & Drivers

Semiconductors (SMH)

▲ ~+2.4%

The chip complex led the rebound: the VanEck Semiconductor ETF was up about 2.4% ahead of the bell, clawing back some of the late-June AI-trade slump as faith in memory and AI-compute demand returned.

Intel

▲ +3%

Intel rose about 3% in premarket trading (near $124) as chip names bounced broadly. Western Digital (+6.3%) and Universal Display (+8%) were among the other premarket standouts in the tech complex.

WTI Crude Oil

▼ −0.5%

WTI slipped about 0.5% to $68.33 (Brent −0.5% to $71.76) after OPEC+ agreed Sunday to raise August output — seven members plan a combined +188,000 bpd — and flows resumed through a reopened Strait of Hormuz.

Dow Jones

▲ ~+0.2%

The blue-chip average lagged the tech-led tape early, up about 0.2%, after doing the heavy lifting Thursday with a record close of 52,900.07 (+1.14%).
