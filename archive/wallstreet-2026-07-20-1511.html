The Lead

As of ~2:42 PM ET: the morning's chip rally has faded to a split, sagging tape

Monday opened with conviction. By 9:34 AM ET the Associated Press had the S&P 500 up 0.6%, the Dow up 190 points and the Nasdaq Composite up 0.8%, as semiconductors bounced off a brutal week. By 11:49 AM, Reuters marked the Dow already red — 52,022.54, −123.88 points, −0.24% — with the S&P at 7,478.57 (+20.88, +0.28%) and the Nasdaq Composite at 25,667.46 (+147.22, +0.58%).

It has kept slipping. The most recent timestamped reading this run, at 2:42 PM ET, puts the Dow at 51,857.63, down 288.79 points (−0.55%) — a loss that has more than doubled since midday — with the S&P 500 up about 0.1% and the Nasdaq Composite up about 0.3%. Both are barely holding green. The arc of the day is the story: 9:34 AM → 11:49 AM → 12:54 PM → 2:42 PM is a straight line downhill from a strong open.

The rally that did happen was narrow and specific. Reuters had the Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index up 1.9% after it closed Friday more than 20% below its late-June record — a confirmed bear market — with memory names leading: Micron +4.8% and SanDisk +6%. Bloomberg, at 12:36 PM, had the semiconductor gauge +2.3% and only four of 11 sectors higher. That is a market being carried by two sectors: Reuters put information technology +0.8% and communication services +1.8%, the latter powered by Alphabet.

Underneath, breadth was thin rather than broken — advancers led decliners just 1.17-to-1 on the NYSE and 1.37-to-1 on the Nasdaq — but the new-lows tally is the tell: the Nasdaq Composite logged 39 new 52-week highs against 129 new lows. Positioning ahead of Alphabet, Tesla, Intel and IBM this week looks less like conviction than like caution with a chip hedge on.

Two figures circulating today were rejected as this session's cash reading and are named here so they are not mistaken for it. (1) "Dow −0.2% / S&P +0.3% / Nasdaq +0.6%" is the 7:24 AM pre-open futures piece — re-proven by direct fetch this run, whose own header reads "U.S. markets open in 1h 56m" (ES=F +0.35%, YM=F +0.23%, NQ=F +0.76%). (2) "S&P +0.63% / Dow +0.23% / Nasdaq +1.02%" was served again today without a timestamp; those are earlier figures and are contradicted by every timestamped reading above. Separately, a stockanalysis.com movers page and a TradingEconomics index page both returned stale renders (dated June 18 and June 9 respectively) and were discarded rather than published.

Movers & Drivers

+2.9% to +3%New

Alphabet — a Gemini-specific chip called "Frozen v2"

The clearest single driver on the tape. The Information reports Google is developing a server chip internally dubbed Frozen v2 that would permanently embed parts of Gemini's architecture directly into silicon. Google engineers project it could serve six to ten times more tokens per unit of power than the company's newest TPUs, and respond to queries faster. It is separate from the TPU line, may deploy as soon as 2028, and only works with future Gemini models if Google keeps the same underlying architecture — Google reportedly views it partly as a trial run and does not plan TPU-scale production. Reuters marked the stock +2.9% at 11:49 AM; Yahoo Finance reported +3%. Alphabet reports Q2 results Wednesday, and Bloomberg reported last week that it is behind schedule delivering Gemini 3.5 Pro.

MU +4.8% · SNDK +6%

Memory leads the semiconductor bounce

Reuters, at 11:49 AM: memory chipmakers led the gains among semiconductors, with Micron Technology +4.8% and SanDisk +6%. The context is a market repairing damage rather than making new highs — the Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index ended Friday more than 20% below its late-June record high, confirming a bear-market decline, and was up 1.9% Monday morning (Bloomberg had it +2.3% at 12:36 PM). A 2% bounce against a 20% drawdown recovers a small fraction of it.

+17.5% → +21.1%

AMC Entertainment — record quarter, and a stock still down 99% over five years

The session's biggest verified mover. Yahoo Finance trending tickers had AMC at $2.2799, +$0.3399 (+17.52%) on an 11:19 AM capture, and $2.3501, +$0.4101 (+21.14%) at 12:54 PM. The company posted record quarterly revenue of $1.6 billion and an all-time-high adjusted EBITDA; domestic ticket revenue rose 11.4% and European attendance 18% year on year. CEO Adam Aron: "In AMC's entire 106-year history, there has never been a quarter like this one." The scale check matters — the stock is up roughly 49% year to date and down about 99% over five years against a 2021 high near $450. Notably, the quarter does not include the weekend in which 4.3 million people watched The Odyssey, with six theaters running it 85 hours straight.

On the Radar

  • Alphabet reports Q2 on Wednesday, with Tesla, Intel and IBM also due this week. Reuters notes investors will watch Intel and Texas Instruments most closely for signs the semiconductor slide has bottomed.
  • Earnings expectations have been marked up, not down. LSEG-compiled data has S&P 500 Q2 earnings growth at 26% year on year, up from an earlier 23.7% estimate — which, as Northlight Asset Management CIO Chris Zaccarelli put it, sets "such a high bar at this point" that good results may not be good enough.
  • The rate risk runs the wrong way. CME FedWatch has traders pricing a roughly 14% chance of a quarter-point rate increase at the Fed's July meeting and 55% for September. Zaccarelli: "Higher interest rates could be a real Achilles' heel for the market."
  • Geopolitics stays in the tape. The nearly five-month-old US-Israeli war with Iran escalated over the weekend, and Yemen's Iran-aligned Houthis said they were imposing a naval blockade on Saudi Arabia. Iran has signalled that mediated exchanges with Washington will continue.
  • SpaceX (SPCX) rose 1.62% to $125.99 in premarket trade; the 13th Starship test flight has been rescheduled to Thursday, July 23 after a Raptor 3 engine ignition failure forced an automatic abort. Its June 12 IPO raised $75 billion, the largest on record, and the stock hit an all-time intraday high of $225.64 three trading days later. No intraday SPCX figure was verified this run.
