Top Story

CISA flags a 10.0 SimpleHelp flaw as attackers weaponize remote-support servers

CISA has added CVE-2026-48558, a critical authentication-bypass vulnerability in the SimpleHelp remote-support platform, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. The flaw (CVSS v3.1 10.0) affects SimpleHelp 5.5.15 and earlier and 6.0 pre-release builds: when OIDC authentication is enabled, the server validates identity-provider assertions without verifying their cryptographic signature, letting a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a token and log in as a fully privileged "Technician." In deployments that enforce MFA, the attacker can still bypass it because technicians can self-register their own MFA method on first login.

Because SimpleHelp is widely deployed by IT departments, managed service providers and help desks, a compromised server hands attackers the same reach as a legitimate technician across every managed endpoint. Blackpoint Cyber's Adversary Pursuit Group observed in-the-wild intrusions that began with confirmed exploitation of this flaw and then abused SimpleHelp's own remote-execution features to deploy two previously undiscovered malware families — the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer. Internet scans indicate roughly 14,000 SimpleHelp servers are externally exposed, with an estimated 1,000 directly vulnerable — and because MSPs sit upstream of many clients, a single compromise can cascade across every organization a provider manages.

Patch SimpleHelp now — CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0)

A max-severity flaw is being actively exploited, and CISA orders federal civilian agencies to remediate by July 2, 2026 (tomorrow). Upgrade to SimpleHelp's fixed build (5.5.16 / 6.0 RC2); if you can't patch immediately, disable the OIDC "Allow group authenticated logins" option that the attack requires, then hunt for rogue Technician accounts and TaskWeaver / Djinn Stealer artifacts.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Qilin (Agenda) — 2026's most prolific ransomware-as-a-service crew

The Qilin ransomware group (also tracked as Agenda) has kept up a relentless pace: it has claimed roughly 1,500 victims since launch, more than 500 of them in 2026 alone — making it the single most active ransomware operation researchers are tracking, as overall ransomware activity climbs about 30% year-over-year led by Qilin and INC Ransom. Healthcare has been especially hard hit: by June 2026 Qilin had accumulated about 168 confirmed healthcare victims, trailing only manufacturing (~291) and business services (~245); patient records can sell for up to ten times the price of financial records on darknet markets. Operating as a ransomware-as-a-service platform, Qilin recruits affiliates who run the intrusions with its builder and infrastructure — leaning on phished credentials, exposed VPN appliances and remote-management tools plus fast double-extortion leaks — making it a useful barometer of the ransomware threat this summer.

Breaches & Incidents

Kemp LoadMaster under attack

A second pre-auth RCE surfaced this week: Progress Kemp LoadMaster (CVE-2026-8037, CVSS 9.8) lets an unauthenticated attacker run root commands. eSentire's Threat Response Unit reports active exploitation attempts began June 29; patched firmware is available.

NewExploited

KDDI — up to 14.22M accounts

Japanese telecom KDDI disclosed an email-system breach (intrusion detected June 17, disclosed June 23) that may have exposed up to 14.22 million email addresses and passwords across six Japanese ISPs it hosts.

Data breach

Sysco — 61M records claimed

The ShinyHunters extortion crew claims it stole more than 61 million Salesforce records — employee, customer and internal corporate data — from food-distribution giant Sysco, part of a wider Salesforce-data extortion wave.

NewExtortion

Oracle E-Business Suite exploited

Attackers are actively exploiting Oracle E-Business Suite flaw CVE-2026-46817 (Oracle Payments); more than 900 EBS instances remain exposed online. Admins should apply Oracle's fixes and hunt for compromise.
