Top Story

Du Plessis blanks Usman and re-enters the title queue — but nobody has told Imavov to step aside

Dricus Du Plessis made a hasty return to the win column at UFC Oklahoma City, taking a five-round unanimous decision over Kamaru Usman on scorecards of 50-45, 49-46 and 49-46. UFC.com's recap describes a fight in which the South African's physicality and power were simply too much: he wobbled Usman multiple times in the first two rounds with clubbing strikes and well-timed knees up the middle, and after Usman warmed into it, hurt him again in the fourth. It was Du Plessis's first outing since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev last August, and it moves him to 24-3.

What it buys him is less clear. Dana White says the matchmakers have not decided who Sean Strickland's next challenger is. Du Plessis himself shut down the idea of an immediate Strickland–Chimaev rematch as senseless, and argued that Strickland should defend against Nassourdine Imavov first — then, he says, he would take Strickland a third time, having beaten him twice already. Chimaev, still ranked at the top of the division, has been demanding an immediate rematch of his own after the split-decision loss at UFC 328.

Imavov is not treating the queue as an open question. He publicly shut down the idea of Du Plessis leapfrogging him, responding to Du Plessis's dismissive "he's still fighting?" remark. Du Plessis, for his part, was reflective afterwards about the belt he lost — "I hate not being champion."

Prospect Watch

ProspectNew

Ezra Elliott — 8-0, and already in the record book

The MMA Lab representative took the fight on Tuesday morning, missed weight on Friday, and then went a full three rounds for the first time in his career to beat Damien Anderson in their shared UFC debuts. He now holds the UFC record for the most takedowns in a featherweight fight. Worth watching with an actual camp behind him.

ProspectNew

RJ Harris "The Hammer" — heavyweight, 27, undefeated

A patient debut that ended fast: a right hand backed Alvin Hines up, and an uppercut on the re-engage started the finishing sequence at 1:40 of the first. Harris is only 16 months into his professional career and already owns a first-round UFC finish.

Prospect

Felipe Franco — first UFC win, back at 205

Franco debuted on short notice at heavyweight in March, pushing prospect Mario Pinto to a decision. Back in his natural division he mauled compatriot Levi Rodrigues Jr., working to mount twice and finishing with heavy ground-and-pound in the second. UFC.com flags him as a light-heavyweight prospect to watch.

Prospect

Tommy McMillen — undefeated, and record-setting

McMillen and Alberto Montes went all gas, no brakes in the main-card opener. McMillen set the pace, mixed in body shots as the fight wore on, hurt Montes late in the third and forced the stoppage — setting a record for the most significant strikes landed in a three-round bout at featherweight along the way.
