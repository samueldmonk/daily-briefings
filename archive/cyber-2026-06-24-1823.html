Top Story

ShinyHunters mass-exploit an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day, hammering universities

Google's Mandiant and Oracle confirmed that the extortion group ShinyHunters (tracked as UNC6240) exploited a critical, unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in Oracle PeopleSoft Enterprise PeopleTools — CVE-2026-35273, CVSS 9.8 — as a zero-day. Activity ran between May 27 and June 9, two weeks before Oracle published an out-of-band advisory on June 10, so the bug was unpatched the entire time.

The group claims roughly 300 compromised instances across more than 100 organizations, with the education sector disproportionately hit; stolen student records began appearing on the ShinyHunters data-leak site on June 9. Mandiant observed post-exploitation deployment of MeshCentral remote-management agents disguised as Microsoft Azure services. The flaw affects PeopleTools 8.61 and 8.62; CISA added it to the KEV catalog on June 12 with a June 15 federal deadline that is now overdue.

Patch Priority

⚠ Ubiquiti UniFi OS — CVE-2026-34910 (CVSS 10.0) · Federal deadline June 26 (2 days left)

CISA added three max-severity (CVSS 10.0) Ubiquiti UniFi OS flaws plus a Lantronix EDS5000 bug to the KEV catalog on June 23, all confirmed actively exploited. CVE-2026-34910 is an unauthenticated command-injection flaw that can be chained to root-level remote code execution when the management interface is reachable. Update UniFi OS Server to 5.0.8+ and UDM/UDM-Pro/UDM-SE/UDM-Pro-Max devices to 5.1.12+ before the June 26 federal due date. (Also patch the overdue Oracle PeopleSoft RCE above.)

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters (UNC6240)

A high-volume data-theft and extortion collective that runs its own data-leak site and monetizes stolen corporate data. Beyond the Oracle PeopleSoft education-sector campaign, the group this month published roughly 45 GB of Madison Square Garden data — including facial-recognition surveillance records — after a missed June 15 ransom deadline, claiming about 26 million records (the figure is unverified). It is the second major MSG breach in under a year. ShinyHunters' calling card is opportunistic exploitation of enterprise platforms (Salesforce, Oracle) followed by named-victim extortion.

Breaches & Incidents

Oracle PeopleSoft / Education sectorNew

ShinyHunters breached 100+ organizations (~300 instances) via CVE-2026-35273, with universities hardest hit; stolen student records (names, IDs, GPAs, contact data) posted to the group's leak site.

Madison Square GardenNew

ShinyHunters published ~45 GB — claiming 26 million customer and corporate records, including facial-recognition surveillance data — after MSG missed a June 15 ransom deadline. The 26M figure is unverified; it is MSG's second breach in under a year.

June 23 ransomware victims

Fresh listings surfaced: the Central Bank of Libya (Qilin), BITS Pilani (DragonForce), Belpointe Asset Management (INC Ransom) and Aerospace & Advanced Composites GmbH (Aur0ra).

Tchap (French government messaging)

A threat actor exfiltrated 13.5 GB from the French government's secure messenger — 73,467 user accounts (including personnel from French ministries) and 643,459 messages.
