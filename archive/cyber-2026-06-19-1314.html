Top Story

ShinyHunters' PeopleSoft zero-day breached 100+ organizations before Oracle had a fix

CVE-2026-35273 — a missing-authentication flaw in Oracle PeopleSoft Enterprise PeopleTools rated CVSS 9.8 — was exploited as a zero-day ahead of Oracle's advisory, enabling unauthenticated remote code execution and full takeover of PeopleSoft instances. Arctic Wolf and Google's Mandiant tie the widespread data-theft campaign to the financially-motivated ShinyHunters (tracked as UNC6240), which claims to have hit roughly 300 instances across more than 100 organizations, with the education sector disproportionately impacted — including a published trove of 454,600 records tied to Nottingham alongside a separate claim of 61 million records from Sysco. Oracle has released mitigations but has not publicly confirmed in-the-wild exploitation. CISA added the bug to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 12; the federal remediation deadline is July 3.

Patch Priority
⚠ Most urgent action today

CVE-2026-48907 — Joomla Content Editor (JCE before 2.9.99.5): this CVSS 10.0 improper-access-control flaw lets an unauthenticated attacker upload arbitrary files and execute PHP code on a vulnerable server, and it is being actively exploited with a public exploit and automated scanning. CISA's KEV remediation deadline for federal agencies is TODAY, June 19 (0 days left). Any affected site should be upgraded immediately to JCE 2.9.99.5 or later (released June 3) and audited for rogue admin profiles or web shells. Two other KEV clocks are also live: the LiteSpeed cPanel flaw (CVE-2026-54420) is now overdue (was due June 18) and the newly-added, actively-exploited Splunk Enterprise zero-day (CVE-2026-20253) is due June 21.

Threat Actor Spotlight
Threat Actor

ShinyHunters (UNC6240)

A financially-motivated extortion collective with a long track record of large-scale data theft. Google's Mandiant and Arctic Wolf tie the group to the PeopleSoft zero-day campaign, and it is also behind the recent Kodak listing. Its 2026 spree already includes claimed breaches of Instructure Canvas (reportedly affecting up to 9,000 educational institutions) and Charter Communications (about 42 million alleged records). ShinyHunters typically uses social engineering and vishing to harvest employee credentials, and has a documented pattern of following through on leak threats when ransoms go unpaid. Fresh this morning (June 19): researchers say the group has rolled out new leak infrastructure and vowed that stolen data will stay online indefinitely "until the end of time," and it has now listed Amazon One Medical with a claimed 8.8 TB trove and a June 22 contact deadline.

Breaches & Incidents
Supply Chain

"Icarus" abuses Klue OAuth token to loot Salesforce data

The newer extortion crew "Icarus" breached competitive-intelligence platform Klue and stole Salesforce CRM data from multiple customer organizations. Researchers say the attackers reached Klue's backend on June 11 through a long-dormant API credential, then abused OAuth tokens from Klue's Battlecards integration to query customers' Salesforce instances via the REST API. Security firms ReliaQuest and Huntress confirmed the campaign — Huntress says its own Salesforce data was taken and that the actors threatened to leak it — and Salesforce has since disabled the Klue integration.

Zero-Day

Splunk Enterprise zero-day now exploited, added to KEV

CISA added CVE-2026-20253 to its KEV catalog on June 18 after confirming active exploitation. The CVSS 9.8 flaw is a missing-authentication weakness in a PostgreSQL sidecar endpoint that lets an unauthenticated remote attacker create or truncate arbitrary files — a path to pre-auth code execution — on Splunk Enterprise 10.0 (before 10.0.7) and 10.2 (before 10.2.4). Patches shipped June 10; federal agencies must remediate by June 21.

Ransomware

Qilin ransomware exploits Check Point VPN zero-day

Qilin affiliates have been exploiting CVE-2026-50751, a CVSS 9.3 improper-authentication bypass in Check Point Remote Access / Mobile Access VPNs, since early May. The logic flaw in IKEv1 certificate validation lets an unauthenticated attacker establish a VPN session without valid credentials; the campaign is described as limited to several dozen organizations. CISA added it to the KEV catalog on June 8.

Campaign

"FortiBleed" exposes ~75,000 Fortinet firewalls

Researchers (SOCRadar, Hudson Rock, Kevin Beaumont; first flagged by Volodymyr Diachenko) say a credential-harvesting operation cracked admin and SSL-VPN passwords from configuration files on internet-facing FortiGate firewalls — about 75,000 devices across 194 countries and 21,000+ domains, roughly half of all internet-facing Fortinet firewalls on Shodan — and access is now being sold. It is not a single CVE; older password hashing made many devices easier to crack. Defenders should assume exposure, rotate credentials, enforce MFA and upgrade FortiOS.

Breach

Kodak confirms breach amid ShinyHunters extortion

Eastman Kodak confirmed an incident after ShinyHunters listed it on June 15 claiming 2.2M customer records, with a leak threat timed to a June 18 deadline. Kodak says an unauthorized third party briefly accessed "a limited amount" of data; full scope is unverified and no proof samples have been posted.

Also tracked this run: an international takedown cleaned roughly 15,000 malware-infected WordPress sites and seized 100+ servers tied to the SocGholish operation and the Evil Corp cybercrime group, per Cybersecurity News.
