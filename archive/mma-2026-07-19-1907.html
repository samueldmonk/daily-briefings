Top Story

Du Plessis beats Usman — then hands the next title shot to Imavov

At UFC Fight Night: Du Plessis vs. Usman in Oklahoma City on Saturday, Dricus Du Plessis defeated Kamaru Usman by unanimous decision, 50-45, 49-46, 49-46. It was Du Plessis's first bout since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev last August, and UFC's own recap flags a potential trilogy with current champion Sean Strickland, whom Du Plessis has beaten twice.

What he did next was the surprise. Rather than press for the immediate rematch, Du Plessis said he is willing to wait — that Nassourdine Imavov deserves the shot first. Strickland, he said, would be ready around December, and Imavov should get that defence: "He earned that," followed by "after that I'll come and take my belt back."

Imavov's case is strong. He is on a five-fight win streak, including a TKO of Israel Adesanya at UFC Fight Night 250 and a unanimous-decision win over Caio Borralho at UFC Paris scored 50-45, 49-46, 49-46, and he has spent close to a year waiting on a title shot. Du Plessis separately dismissed the idea of an immediate Strickland–Chimaev rematch as "senseless" — Chimaev lost the belt to Strickland at UFC 328 without a successful defence.

Usman, a former welterweight champion with five title defences and wins over Jorge Masvidal and Colby Covington, had been angling for a title shot of his own.

Around the Sport

  • Christian Leroy Duncan has now won five straight after handing Jared Cannonier a unanimous-decision loss — the biggest win of his career to date.
  • Fatima Kline is 4-0 since returning to strawweight following her decision over Tabatha Ricci, and moves into the divisional conversation.
  • Du Plessis on a Strickland–Chimaev rematch: he called the idea "senseless." Chimaev took the middleweight belt from Du Plessis last August and then lost it to Strickland at UFC 328 without defending it, which leaves little obvious case for running it back immediately.
  • Imavov is campaigning publicly. He has used social media to press the UFC to name him the official number one contender for Strickland's belt, citing what he says was a promise he would be next.
  • Heavyweight is queued up. Interim champion Ciryl Gane has challenged undisputed champion Tom Aspinall, and Aspinall has publicly accepted — "I'll be there."
