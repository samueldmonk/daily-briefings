Top Story

ShinyHunters' PeopleSoft zero-day breached 100+ organizations before Oracle had a fix

Mandiant says CVE-2026-35273 — a missing-authentication flaw in Oracle PeopleSoft Enterprise PeopleTools rated CVSS 9.8 — was exploited as a zero-day between roughly May 27 and June 9, 2026, two weeks ahead of Oracle's advisory. The campaign, attributed to the financially-motivated UNC6240 (ShinyHunters), enabled full takeover of PeopleSoft instances and has now affected more than 100 organizations, including a published trove of 454,600 records tied to Nottingham, alongside a separate claim of 61 million records from Sysco. CISA added the bug to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 12; federal agencies face a remediation deadline of July 3.

Patch Priority
⚠ Most urgent action today

CVE-2026-48172 — LiteSpeed cPanel plugin (v10.0): this privilege-escalation flaw was added to CISA's KEV catalog and the federal remediation deadline of June 16 has now passed — it is OVERDUE (2 days past). Any internet-facing cPanel/shared-hosting server still running the vulnerable LiteSpeed plugin should be patched immediately, as the bug lets any cPanel user execute scripts with root privileges. Joomla's JCE flaw (CVE-2026-48907) is the next deadline at July 7.

Threat Actor Spotlight
Threat Actor

ShinyHunters (UNC6240)

A financially-motivated extortion collective with a long track record of large-scale data theft. Mandiant ties the group to the PeopleSoft zero-day campaign, and it is also behind the fresh Kodak listing. ShinyHunters typically uses social engineering and vishing to harvest employee credentials, and has a documented pattern of following through on leak threats when ransoms go unpaid — as when it published a 9.4 GB archive after 7-Eleven missed an April 21 deadline.

Breaches & Incidents
NewBreach

Kodak confirms breach amid ShinyHunters extortion

Eastman Kodak confirmed an incident after ShinyHunters listed it on June 15 claiming 2.2M customer records, with a leak threat timed to a June 18 deadline. Kodak says an unauthorized third party briefly accessed "a limited amount" of data; full scope is unverified and no proof samples have been posted.

Breach

PeopleSoft victims mount: Nottingham, Sysco claims

The ShinyHunters PeopleSoft campaign continues to surface victims — 454,600 records tied to Nottingham have been published and the group claims roughly 61 million records from Sysco, part of the 100+ organizations swept up before Oracle issued a fix.

Vuln

Microsoft Defender zero-day "RoguePlanet"

CVE-2026-50656, a CVSS 7.8 privilege-escalation zero-day in Microsoft Defender dubbed "RoguePlanet," has been disclosed with a patch still pending; it is not yet listed in CISA's KEV catalog. Defenders should watch for the forthcoming fix.

Loaders

ClickFix loader wave continues

Researchers continue tracking ClickFix-style social-engineering loaders — including campaigns dubbed BabaDeda, Lorem Ipsum and Potemkin — that trick users into pasting attacker commands, underscoring the persistent low-tech initial-access threat.
