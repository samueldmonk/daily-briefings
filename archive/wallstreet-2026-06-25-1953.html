The Lead
As of the close · ~4:00 PM ET

The Dow prints a record the Nasdaq paid for

Wall Street split down the middle to end Thursday. The Dow carved out a fresh all-time intraday high before closing up 0.14% (+71.72 points) at 51,920.62, powered by a rotation out of megacap technology and into industrials, financials and health care. The Nasdaq Composite fell 0.46% to 25,358.60 — its fourth straight down day, the longest losing streak since February — while the S&P 500 finished essentially flat at 7,357.49 (-0.01%).

The drag came from the Magnificent Seven: Apple fell about 6% after raising MacBook and iPad prices to offset surging memory costs, and Microsoft dropped roughly 3% on its own Xbox and consumer price hikes, with Nvidia and Amazon also down more than 2%. Offsetting them, banks rallied after passing the Fed's stress test (clearing the way for dividend hikes), Caterpillar jumped about 6% and Johnson & Johnson added ~1%. A blowout from Micron kept the chip rally alive. The day's marquee macro print: May PCE inflation rose to a 4.1% headline rate (the highest since April 2023) with core at 3.4% (just above the 3.3% forecast and the hottest since October 2023), with markets now pricing roughly a 37% chance of a rate hike at the Fed's late-July meeting (about 63% still expect a hold), per CME FedWatch.
