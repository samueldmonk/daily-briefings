The Lead

Big Tech drags the S&P and Nasdaq lower at the close while the Dow gains and small caps make history

Wall Street closed split on Monday. The Dow rose 0.29% — led by a nearly 4% gain in Caterpillar — and the small-cap Russell 2000 climbed about 0.8% to close above 3,000 for the first time ever, but the S&P 500 slipped 0.37% and the Nasdaq Composite fell 1.32%, dragged by a selloff in the megacap technology names. Alphabet fell about 5% at the close — having been down as much as ~7% during the session, and among the biggest single drags on both indexes — amid AI-spending anxiety and reports of AI-talent departures (Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer to OpenAI, DeepMind Nobel laureate John Jumper to Anthropic). Amazon (≈−4.8%) and Meta (≈−2.3%) were also lower, and Moderna fell about 9.5%. Newly public SpaceX tumbled about 16.4% — its worst day since its June 12 debut and a third straight decline, leaving the stock up only about 14% from its $135 IPO price — after confirming its first-ever bond sale to repay a bridge loan and after MSCI assigned it a bottom-tier "CCC" ESG rating.

Gold miners were the day's worst pocket — but on company news, not a bullion crash: Alamos Gold sank about 14.6% after slashing its Q2 production guidance (seismic activity forced a slowdown at its Young-Davidson mine in Ontario; Bank of America cut its price target to $50), and Gold Fields fell about 9.7% in sympathy — even as spot gold held near $4,190 an ounce, edging back toward $4,200. Dealmaking and biotech supplied the upside. Apogee Therapeutics soared nearly 47% after AbbVie agreed to acquire it for $135.11 a share in cash, valuing the biotech at about $10.9 billion, while Definium Therapeutics rocketed about 55% — the day's biggest percentage gainer — after its Phase 3 Emerge trial of DT120 met its primary endpoint in major depression. Chip and hardware names bucked the tape: Super Micro Computer jumped about 15%, Sandisk rose about 7% and Micron added about 5% ahead of its earnings later this week. Oil eased — WTI down about 3% near $74, its lowest since early March — as U.S. and Iranian negotiators, with Qatari and Pakistani mediation, agreed a roadmap toward a final deal within 60 days. Investors look ahead to Thursday's May PCE inflation report, the key macro test after last week's hawkish Fed.

Movers & Drivers

The day's biggest percentage gainer. The biotech rocketed after its Phase 3 Emerge study of DT120 met its primary endpoint in major depressive disorder — an 8.1-point placebo-adjusted MADRS improvement at week six (35% response vs 7% on placebo).
