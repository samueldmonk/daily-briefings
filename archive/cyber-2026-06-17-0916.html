Top Story

ShinyHunters burns an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day to breach 100+ organizations — universities hit hardest

The extortion crew ShinyHunters (tracked as UNC6240) exploited CVE-2026-35273, a critical, unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in Oracle PeopleSoft Enterprise PeopleTools (CVSS 9.8), as a zero-day to compromise more than 100 organizations across roughly 300 exposed instances. About two-thirds (68%) of the victims are universities and colleges, predominantly in the United States.

Among the named victims, the University of Nottingham had roughly 40 GB of personal data and billing records — belonging to hundreds of thousands of current and former students — stolen. Researchers tracked exploitation between May 27 and June 9; Oracle did not publish its out-of-band advisory until June 10, meaning the bug was a true zero-day for the duration of the campaign. CISA added it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 12 with a federal remediation deadline of July 3.

Patch Priority

Overdue — patch immediately: CVE-2026-48172 — LiteSpeed User-End cPanel Plugin (CVSS 10.0), actively exploited for root-level script execution; the CISA KEV federal remediation deadline was June 16 and has now lapsed. Fix: WHM Plugin v5.3.1.0 (cPanel plugin v2.4.7+).

New max-severity: CVE-2026-48907 (Joomla Content Editor / JCE Widget Factory, CVSS 10.0) — unauthenticated PHP code execution, actively exploited; KEV-added June 16, federal deadline July 7. Fix: JCE 2.9.99.5 (hardening in 2.9.99.6).

Next up: CVE-2026-35273 (Oracle PeopleSoft, CVSS 9.8) — KEV-listed, federal deadline July 3.

Breaches & Incidents
Extortion

ShinyHunters targets Sysco — 61M records claimed

The extortion crew ShinyHunters claims to have stolen more than 61 million Salesforce records from Sysco, the world's largest food distributor — said to include customer information, employee records and internal corporate data. It marks a second cyber-extortion threat against Sysco in weeks, following a Qilin ransomware claim.

Supply chain

140+ malicious npm packages flood the Mastra scope New

A single npm account ("ehindero") mass-published more than 140 malicious packages across the Mastra scope on June 17, the latest in a run of open-source registry poisoning aimed at developers. Teams pulling AI-agent dependencies should pin versions and audit recent installs.

Law enforcement

Conti operator pleads guilty

A Conti ransomware operator pleaded guilty to wire-fraud conspiracy and a cyber-extortion scheme, the latest courtroom result against the defunct-but-influential ransomware crew whose alumni seeded several of today's most active gangs.

Takedown

Europol disrupts "AudiA6" laundering service

Europol cut off AudiA6, a cryptocurrency-laundering service used by ransomware gangs and cybercrime networks, calling it a key financial pipeline. The service is estimated to have washed more than €336 million (~$389 million) in illicit profits.

Breach

Council of Europe investigates breach claim

The continent's oldest intergovernmental body is probing claims of a data breach made by the ShinyHunters extortion group, the same crew behind the PeopleSoft campaign.

Ransomware

"The Gentlemen" tied to RaaS ecosystem

New analysis indicates The Gentlemen operation began as an affiliate running double-extortion attacks while leveraging resources from ransomware-as-a-service schemes including LockBit, Qilin and Medusa.

Exploit

Palo Alto PAN-OS portals targeted

Palo Alto Networks confirmed active exploitation of CVE-2026-0257, an authentication-bypass flaw in PAN-OS GlobalProtect, by an unknown actor to gain unauthorized access. Initial activity was observed back on May 17.
