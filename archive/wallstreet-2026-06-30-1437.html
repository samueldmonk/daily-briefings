The Lead

Q2 Rally Gathers Strength as Fed Holds, Iran Tensions Cool

As of midday June 30, 2026, the S&P 500 is up +0.69%, the Nasdaq 100 is up +1.47%, and the Dow is up +0.21% on track for what is shaping up to be the best quarter for major U.S. stock indexes in six years. Yesterday's bounce (June 29: S&P +1.18%, Nasdaq +2.07%, Dow +0.59%, closing at record high of 52,182.74) has carried into today amid optimism over the Fed's continued hold on rates and easing Middle East geopolitical tensions.

Earnings & Market Drivers

Nike (NKE) Earnings This Afternoon: Wall Street is bracing for weak results. Consensus expects Q4 FY2026 EPS of $0.12 (down 14.2% y/y) on revenue of $10.85B (down 2.2% y/y). Analysts have slashed expectations 45.5% from $0.22 just three months ago, citing weak global demand, North American promotions, and Greater China inventory clearance. Stock down ~35% this year; options imply large post-earnings move.

Fed & Rates

No Change at Jun 18 FOMC Meeting: Fed held rates steady at 3.50%–3.75% under new Chair Kevin Warsh, marking the fourth consecutive hold. However, market expectations have shifted sharply: traders are now pricing in a possible rate hike as early as October. June economic projections show 9 officials seeing at least one hike this year; 6 anticipating two. PCE inflation was revised sharply higher to 3.6% for 2026 (from 2.7%), while 2027 guidance also rose to 3.3%.

Geopolitics: Iran Deal Hopes

Draft 14-point Iran MOU circulating includes U.S. oil sanctions relief, unfrozen Iranian funds, and Hormuz reopening within 30 days. Potential signing this weekend in Europe (Geneva floated) has cooled oil volatility. WTI trading below $85/bbl, down from $90+ levels earlier June. Brent similarly lower. The easing has supported risk appetite, particularly in cyclical sectors, though equity valuations remain elevated.
