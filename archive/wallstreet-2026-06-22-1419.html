The Lead

Alphabet and SpaceX drag the Nasdaq lower while the Dow stands alone — as of ~2 PM ET

Wall Street split into two tapes Monday afternoon. As of about 2 PM ET the Dow was up roughly 0.4%, while the S&P 500 slipped about 0.3% and the Nasdaq Composite fell roughly 1.1%, dragged by a selloff in the megacap technology names. Alphabet tumbled about 7% — erasing roughly $254 billion of market value — on reports of AI-talent departures, led by Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer's exit for OpenAI, plus unease over its roughly $180–190 billion AI capital-spending plan; Amazon (≈−4%), Meta (≈−3%) and Microsoft (≈−2%) were also lower and Moderna fell about 9.5%, the S&P 500's steepest decliner. Newly public SpaceX dropped about 10% — its worst day since its June 12 debut and a third straight decline, leaving it down roughly 16% from last Tuesday's high — after launching a bond offering. It is the first regular session of a holiday-shortened week, and the first under June's quarterly index rebalance, effective at the open.

Dealmaking supplied the day's upside. Apogee Therapeutics soared nearly 47% after AbbVie agreed to acquire it for $135.11 a share in cash, valuing the biotech at about $10.9 billion. Chip and hardware names also bucked the tape: Super Micro Computer jumped about 15%, Sandisk rose about 7% and Micron added about 4% ahead of its earnings later this week, while EV maker Lucid slipped about 2% after announcing it would cut roughly 18% of its U.S. workforce. Oil eased — WTI down about 3% near $74, its lowest since early March, and Brent off about 3% near $77 — as U.S. and Iranian negotiators cited "encouraging progress" in Switzerland toward a final deal within 60 days, a roadmap brokered with help from Qatar and Pakistan. Investors look ahead to Tuesday's S&P Global flash PMIs and Thursday's May PCE inflation report, the key macro test after last week's hawkish Fed meeting.

Movers & Drivers

Apogee Therapeutics ≈ +47%

Super Micro Computer ≈ +15%

Led the S&P 500's gainers, jumping about 15% as the AI-server maker rallied alongside Sandisk (≈+7%) — a pocket of chip-and-hardware strength that stood out against the megacap-tech weakness.

Alphabet ≈ −7%

Led the megacap-tech retreat, falling about 7% — roughly $254 billion of market value erased — after reports that Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer is leaving for OpenAI and DeepMind Nobel laureate John Jumper for Anthropic, on top of unease over a ~$180–190B AI capex plan — the single biggest drag on the Nasdaq and S&P 500.

SpaceX ≈ −10%

The recently public company dropped about 10% — its worst day since debuting on the U.S. tape on June 12 — after launching a bond offering. It was a third straight daily decline, leaving the stock roughly 16% below last Tuesday's high.

Micron ≈ +4%

Bucked the tech weakness, rising about 4% ahead of its earnings report due this week — one of the day's chip outperformers as the semiconductor complex held up better than the megacaps.

Index newcomers RKLB ≈ −8% · CRWV ≈ −9%

Rocket Lab and CoreWeave — both newly added to the Nasdaq-100 in June's quarterly reshuffle — sank near session lows in their first day in the index, weighing on the tech tape; fellow new entrant Astera Labs bucked the trend, up about 3%.

Lucid Group ≈ −2%

The EV maker eased after saying it will cut about 18% of its U.S. workforce as part of a broader cost-savings push expected to generate roughly $158 million in annualized savings.
