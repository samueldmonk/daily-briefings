Top Story

Klue OAuth breach cascades into Salesforce data theft at HackerOne, Huntress, Gong and more

NewSupply ChainOAuthExtortion

A supply-chain compromise at Klue, a competitive- and sales-intelligence platform, has cascaded into Salesforce data theft across at least nine organizations — many of them well-known technology and security companies. Klue says it detected unauthorized activity in its integration infrastructure on June 12, 2026, traced to a compromised legacy credential that let attackers abuse the OAuth tokens Klue uses to connect into customers' environments and quietly exfiltrate CRM data through those third-party integrations. Salesforce has since disabled the Klue integration.

A newly surfaced extortion group calling itself Icarus — first observed in April 2026 — has claimed responsibility and begun leaking data. Firms that have disclosed impact include HackerOne, Huntress, Tanium, Jamf, Snyk, OneTrust, Recorded Future, Insurity and Sprout Social; on Friday, Gong said attackers may have accessed "internal licensed user data" — usernames, business titles and emails — for a subset of customers that used the Klue integration. The stolen material spans business contacts, sales communications, price quotes, competitive-intelligence reports and account data. In a further twist, Klue says one set of attackers is now deleting the stolen data even as a second group has emerged with fresh extortion demands — a reminder that OAuth tokens into SaaS platforms are now a prime target for supply-chain intrusions.

Patch Priority
⚠ Federal deadline now PASSED — patch immediately

Ubiquiti UniFi OS — CVE-2026-34910 (CVSS 10.0)

An improper-input-validation flaw lets an attacker inject and run arbitrary OS commands. Researchers showed it chains with two other max-severity bugs — CVE-2026-34908 (access-control bypass) and CVE-2026-34909 (path traversal) — so a network-based attacker can reach unauthenticated, root-level remote code execution on UniFi OS devices. All three are being actively exploited in the wild. Fix: update to UniFi OS Server 5.0.8. CISA added the trio to the KEV catalog on June 23 with a federal remediation deadline of June 26, 2026 under BOD 26-04 — that deadline has now passed; any unpatched device should be remediated immediately. Next up: actively-exploited flaws in Cisco Unified CM (CVE-2026-20230, CVSS 8.6) and PTC Windchill / FlexPLM (CVE-2026-12569, CVSS 9.3) are both due tomorrow, June 28.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters (UNC6240) — the crew behind the PeopleSoft extortion spree

Tracked by Google's Mandiant as UNC6240, ShinyHunters ran a compromise-and-extortion campaign against internet-facing Oracle PeopleSoft servers between May 27 and June 9, exploiting the zero-day CVE-2026-35273 (CVSS 9.8) before Oracle's June 10 advisory. After breaking in, the group automated SSH credential-spraying against internal hosts and dropped a defacement-and-extortion marker file — README-IF-YOU-SEE-THIS-YOUVE-BEEN-HACKED.TXT — into WebLogic and Process Scheduler directories. ShinyHunters claims roughly 300 instances across 100+ organizations — about 68% in higher education, most in the US — with the University of Nottingham among the first confirmed victims and more than 454,600 current and former students' records already posted to the group's leak site.

Breaches & Incidents

Operation Endgame strikes again — StealC, Amadey & SocGholish dismantled Law Enforcement

Europol and Eurojust announced on June 24 that the latest phase of Operation Endgame neutralized 326 servers and 142 domains behind the StealC and Amadey infostealer/loader networks and the SocGholish "fake browser update" operation. Investigators recovered roughly 27 million stolen credentials from 385,000+ systems and froze over €41 million (~$46.5 million) in criminal crypto. Partners spanned Canada, Denmark, Germany, the Netherlands, the UK and the US, plus Microsoft, Proofpoint, ESET, IBM X-Force and others; 106 SocGholish servers were seized and 14,971 hijacked WordPress sites disinfected.

Cisco Unified CM exploited in the wild

CISA added CVE-2026-20230 (CVSS 8.6), a server-side request forgery flaw in Cisco Unified Communications Manager, to its KEV catalog on June 25 after confirmed exploitation. Attackers chain it to write arbitrary files to the underlying OS and escalate to root, with researchers reporting web shells dropped on compromised call-manager servers; Cisco says there are no workarounds. Federal agencies must remediate by June 28.

Universities worldwide hit in PeopleSoft data-theft wave

The ShinyHunters PeopleSoft campaign is landing on the education sector hardest: the group claims 300 instances across 100+ organizations, with the University of Nottingham among the first confirmed victims — more than 454,600 current and former students' records already published on its leak site. About 68% of victims are in higher education, exploited via the unauthenticated CVE-2026-35273 PeopleTools flaw.

Texas Parks & Wildlife — 3M+ exposed via license vendor

The Texas Parks and Wildlife Department disclosed a breach affecting roughly 3 million people after a third-party vendor that sells hunting and fishing licenses was compromised. Exposed data includes names, email and physical addresses, phone numbers, and driver's license and passport numbers — but not Social Security numbers, dates of birth or financial data. Texas Cyber Command flagged the intrusion; TPWD was notified May 13, filed its breach notice June 12 and disclosed publicly June 18, offering a free year of credit monitoring (enroll by Sept. 14).

Aflac — 22.7M people being notified of data theft

Insurance giant Aflac has begun notifying nearly 22.7 million customers, beneficiaries, employees and agents that their data — including Social Security numbers and health information — was stolen in an attack on its U.S. operations first detected June 12, 2025 and linked to the Scattered Spider crew. The notifications, rolling into 2026, underscore the long tail of social-engineering-led intrusions across financial services.

Klue / Icarus — security & tech firms swept up New

The Klue OAuth breach (see Top Story) has pulled in an unusually security-savvy roster of victims via stolen Salesforce data: HackerOne, Huntress, Tanium, Jamf, Snyk, OneTrust, Recorded Future, Insurity, Sprout Social and, as of Friday, Gong. The Icarus group is leaking and extorting; The Register reports the underlying Klue intrusion may have touched "hundreds" of downstream customers.
