Top Story

Du Plessis batters Usman — then hands Imavov the title shot he could have taken

Dricus Du Plessis comprehensively outpointed Kamaru Usman over five rounds at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City on Saturday, winning by unanimous decision at 50-45, 49-46, 49-46. It was his first bout since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev last August. Per UFC's own recap, Du Plessis wobbled Usman repeatedly in the first two rounds with clubbing strikes and knees up the middle, hurt him again in the fourth, and held his edge to the final horn.

The win put him straight back into the 185-pound title conversation, and plenty of people argued he should leapfrog Nassourdine Imavov for the next crack at champion Sean Strickland — a man Du Plessis has already beaten twice. Instead he deferred: “Fight Imavov, get a title defense. He earned that. I will give him that. And after that I'll come and take my belt back.”

Imavov, for his part, was in no mood to entertain the debate, pointing to the fact that he knocked out Israel Adesanya and most recently outpointed Caio Borralho. UFC's recap flags the obvious alternative on the table: a trilogy bout with Strickland.

Usman remains a former welterweight champion who defended that title five times, with signature wins over Jorge Masvidal and Colby Covington.
