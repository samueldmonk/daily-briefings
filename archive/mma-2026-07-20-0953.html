Top Story

Du Plessis beats Usman and reopens the middleweight queue — Imavov isn't moving

Dricus Du Plessis took a unanimous decision over Kamaru Usman in Saturday's UFC Oklahoma City main event, winning 50-45, 49-46 and 49-46 to move his professional record to 24-3. UFC's own recap has Du Plessis controlling much of the action and hurting Usman early, with the former welterweight champion rallying as the fight progressed before Du Plessis reasserted himself through the championship rounds. It was emphatic enough that some observers immediately argued the former champion should leapfrog Nassourdine Imavov and be next in line for a shot at Sean Strickland.

Imavov is not entertaining it. Responding to talk that Du Plessis could take his place — and to Du Plessis's "he's still fighting?" jab — the Frenchman shut the suggestion down publicly, projecting confidence that he will get the shot he believes he has earned. Dana White, for his part, has said the matchmakers have not yet decided who Strickland will face next, so the queue is genuinely open rather than merely disputed.

Du Plessis's own stated preference splits the difference: he likes the idea of a Strickland–Imavov booking, after which he wants Strickland a third time to take the belt back. Speaking after the win, he reflected on his first loss in eight years and put it plainly — "I hate not being champion."

Prospect Watch

ProspectNew

Tommy McMillen — 2-0 in the UFC at 28

"Gun" wore down Alberto Montes across fifteen minutes of stand-up trading before a late surge sealed a third-round TKO. UFC's recap calls it a record-setting performance for the unbeaten featherweight, credits his pace, body work and volume, and he took Performance of the Night for it. Fans had picked him for the bonus beforehand — he is becoming a favourite fast.

ProspectNew

Felipe Franco — first UFC win, and a bonus with it

"Negão", 25 and 11-2, returned to light heavyweight and dominated Levi Rodrigues Jr., mixing striking with timely takedowns, advancing to mount and hammering down punches for a second-round stoppage at 1:40. UFC's write-up flags him as a prospect to watch at 205 pounds.

Prospect

Bogdan Guskov — a main event on short notice

Ranked No. 10 at light heavyweight, Guskov gets the biggest opportunity of his UFC career on Saturday after stepping in for the injured Khalil Rountree Jr. against the No. 1-ranked Ankalaev, less than two weeks out. The betting market makes him a heavy underdog at +360.

Around the Sport

  • Khalil Rountree Jr. is out of the Abu Dhabi main event. UFC's own announcement cites injury; Guskov replaces him opposite Ankalaev. Beware secondary listings still carrying the old matchup — the event is Ankalaev vs Guskov.
  • No decision yet on Strickland's next challenger. Dana White has said the matchmakers have not settled it, which is what keeps the Du Plessis–Imavov argument live rather than academic.
  • Du Plessis wants Strickland a third time. His stated route is to let Strickland and Imavov settle it, then take the belt back.
  • Imavov's answer was short. He dismissed the idea of being leapfrogged outright rather than negotiating in public.
