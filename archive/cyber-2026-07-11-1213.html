Top Story

Accenture confirms breach after hacker offers stolen data for sale

Accenture has acknowledged a security incident after a threat actor known as "888" put company data up for sale, claiming roughly 35GB of loot including source code — with the seller also claiming SSH keys and Azure tokens are in the haul. The consulting giant confirms an intrusion occurred but disputes the scale of the claimed theft, and says its investigation is ongoing. Given Accenture's position managing systems for thousands of enterprise clients, defenders are watching for downstream supply-chain exposure.

Sources: BleepingComputer, SecurityWeek, TechRadar Pro, Help Net Security, TechRepublic (key/token claims are the seller's, not confirmed by Accenture)

Patch Priority

Adobe ColdFusion — CVE-2026-48282 (CVSS 10.0): federal deadline has PASSED, exploitation ongoing

The most urgent action today: patch ColdFusion. CVE-2026-48282, a max-severity path-traversal in the RDS FILEIO handler enabling unauthenticated remote code execution on ColdFusion 2025.9 / 2023.20 and earlier, went from public technical analysis to in-the-wild exploitation in under two hours (KEVIntel honeypots, attempts observed from July 2). CISA added it to the KEV catalog July 7 with a July 10 deadline — that deadline passed yesterday. Upgrade to ColdFusion 2025 Update 10 or 2023 Update 21, and if a server is or was internet-facing, hunt for unauthorized files in the web root and /CFIDE/ directories.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters — from helpdesk vishing to a police manhunt

The crew behind February's Odido breach — 6.2 million Dutch telecom customers exposed, including bank details and BSNs, after a caller posing as internal IT tricked helpdesk staff into a fake login page — is now the subject of an escalating criminal probe. Dutch police say the recorded voice of a Dutch-speaking accomplice links local criminals to the attack, and are threatening to release the recording publicly to identify him. Odido refused the €1M ransom demand, and the data was published on the dark web. The same group is tied by Google/Mandiant to the Oracle PeopleSoft extortion campaign (CVE-2026-35273), where 100+ organizations — roughly two-thirds in higher education — have been notified.

Breaches & Incidents

Odido manhunt: police move to unmask the inside voice Breach

Dutch police believe a domestic gang was behind the 6.2M-record Odido hack and have analyzed the vishing call recording — concluding the Dutch-speaking "IT employee" voice is genuine. Investigators are now threatening to publish the suspect's voice in a bid to identify him.

"JadePuffer": first AI-run ransomware still needed a human Ransomware

Researchers documented the first known case of agentic ransomware, dubbed JadePuffer, in which an AI agent handled the technical execution of a real-world attack. TechCrunch's follow-up stresses a human operator was still very much in the loop — steering, not typing.

Ransomware negotiator gets 70 months for playing both sides Legal

Angelo Martino, 41, was sentenced to 70 months for conspiring with the now-defunct ALPHV/BlackCat operation — negotiating for five victims while secretly working with the attackers, in a scheme that netted roughly $1.2M in bitcoin from one victim. About $10M in assets were seized.

One Medical: senior-care archive breached via third party Breach

Amazon-owned primary-care provider One Medical confirmed a breach of a third-party file-storage system holding archived records for its senior-care division: unauthorized access occurred June 8–11 and was discovered June 13, per July breach trackers. Scope of affected records is still being disclosed.

Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade-secret theft Legal

Apple has sued OpenAI over alleged trade-secret theft, per Cybernews' July 11 digest — a data-protection fight between two AI heavyweights worth watching for what it reveals about insider-exfiltration claims. Details are still emerging.

FortiBleed fallout: half the internet-facing FortiGate fleet touched Breach

SOCRadar's tally of the FortiBleed campaign stands at 86,644 compromised FortiGate firewalls across 194 countries — roughly half of internet-facing FortiGates — built on more than 105 million harvested credentials, with 409 admin-level accesses and 354 full attack chains, linked to INC Ransom and Lynx. SOCRadar stresses the operation is still live, with newly compromised devices being added.
