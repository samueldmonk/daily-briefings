Fight week is here: McGregor returns at UFC 329 to face Max Holloway

The UFC's biggest draw of the summer headlines the 14th annual International Fight Week. Conor McGregor — the former two-division champion who hasn't set foot in the Octagon since 2021, when he broke his leg against Dustin Poirier at UFC 264 — returns on Saturday, July 11 to headline UFC 329 against Max Holloway in a welterweight main event at T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas. There's no title on the line; it's Holloway's welterweight debut and a rematch of their 2013 bout, which McGregor won by decision early in his UFC run.

McGregor returns as the underdog, and the market's read on a five-year layoff and a surgically repaired leg is blunt: as of fight week books have Holloway roughly −225 to −300 and McGregor about +175 to +240 — the longest underdog price of his UFC career — with the line drifting toward McGregor on heavy public money, in from an opening near −550. In the co-main event, Liverpool's Paddy Pimblett meets Benoît Saint-Denis at lightweight.
