The Lead

Chips rebound lifts the Nasdaq ~1%; the Dow taps a 53,000 record before easing

U.S. markets came back from the long Independence Day weekend on the front foot. As of ~1 PM ET, the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite was up about 1% and the S&P 500 roughly 0.6–0.7%, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average briefly punched above 53,000 for the first time in its history before slipping back toward the flat line as a few blue-chips (Nike, Amgen) dragged. Thursday's 52,900.07 close — a record — remains the last official finish.

The engine was a rebound in beaten-down memory and chip names, with the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) up about 2.4% after UBS, Citi and Bank of America turned more bullish on the memory cycle. A stronger-than-expected quarterly sales report from Nvidia supplier Hon Hai (Foxconn) reinforced the AI-demand story. Western Digital led the tape, up about 7%, as analysts piled on price-target hikes — Cantor lifted its target to $900, Bank of America to $732, and Melius launched coverage at Buy with a $1,050 target; Teradyne (+6%), Dell (+4%), SanDisk (+5%) and Micron (+3%) rounded out the bounce. The reversal of the session was Astera Labs, which fell about 5.7% on profit-taking after touching a record $499.48 at quarter-end — even as Bank of America reiterated a Street-high $450 target (up from $240). Oil eased after OPEC+ approved another August output hike, and investors looked ahead to Wednesday's June-FOMC minutes.

The chip-equipment maker jumped about 6% alongside the broader semiconductor bounce; SanDisk (~+5%) and Micron (~+3%) also rebounded as UBS, Citi and Bank of America reframed late-June's memory selloff as a buying opportunity.

Astera Labs (ALAB)

▼ ~−5.7%

Last week's chip darling reversed, sliding about 5.7% as profit-takers locked in gains after ALAB touched a record $499.48 at quarter-end. Bank of America still carries a Street-high $450 target (up from $240) on strong AI-infrastructure spend.

SpaceX (SPCX)

▲ +1.08% · $163.75

Elon Musk's SpaceX, public since June 12, edged up to $163.75 ahead of its official addition to the Nasdaq-100 index before Tuesday's open — a fast-tracked inclusion for one of the year's largest listings.

WTI Crude Oil

▼ ~−0.5%

WTI fell about 0.5% to $68.33 (Brent −0.5% to $71.76) after OPEC+ agreed Sunday to raise August output by a combined +188,000 bpd — a fifth straight monthly increase.

Gold & Silver

Precious metals firmed: gold futures rose about 0.9% to ~$4,164 and silver jumped 2.4% to ~$62.55, holding near record territory. Bitcoin added 0.2% to about $62,734.
