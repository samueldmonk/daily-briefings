Top Story

Microsoft patches a record 622 flaws — and two are already being exploited

Microsoft shipped its largest Patch Tuesday on record on July 14, fixing 622 CVEs — more than triple June's previous high of around 200. Two of the fixes close holes attackers are already using: CVE-2026-56164, an elevation-of-privilege flaw in on-premises SharePoint Server that lets an unauthenticated attacker escalate privileges over the network, and CVE-2026-56155, an elevation-of-privilege flaw in Active Directory Federation Services (The Hacker News/SecurityWeek, Jul 14–16).

Neither is a splashy remote-code-execution critical, but both sit in systems that matter more than their scores suggest — the corporate document store and the box that signs the organization's logins. Microsoft says attackers are chaining the SharePoint flaw with older SharePoint weaknesses to steal IIS machine keys, establish persistence and deploy malware. CISA has set a federal remediation deadline of Friday, July 17 for the SharePoint bug and July 28 for the AD FS bug — the first of those is tomorrow (TechTimes/Orca).

Patch Priority

Patch by tomorrow (Fri Jul 17): SonicWall SMA 1000 CVE-2026-15409 and Microsoft SharePoint CVE-2026-56164 — both exploited zero-days

Two actively exploited zero-days share a federal remediation deadline of Friday, July 17. The most severe is SonicWall SMA 1000 CVE-2026-15409 (CVSS 10.0), an unauthenticated server-side request forgery flaw in the appliance's Work Place interface with no workaround — SonicWall confirmed multiple exploitation cases, with attackers extracting credentials, active-session databases and TOTP MFA seeds for long-term access. Alongside it, patch Microsoft's on-premises SharePoint Server CVE-2026-56164, an unauthenticated privilege-escalation zero-day being chained to steal IIS machine keys. Right behind them, CISA's newly KEV-listed Oracle E-Business Suite CVE-2026-46817 (CVSS 9.8) is due Saturday, July 18. Treat internet-facing SMA 1000, SharePoint and Oracle EBS as priority targets today (SonicWall/BleepingComputer/The Hacker News/CISA).

Threat Actor Spotlight

Scattered LAPSUS$ Hunters — the vishing-first extortion collective behind 40+ breaches in 2026

Researchers describe Scattered LAPSUS$ Hunters as a joint operation of Scattered Spider, ShinyHunters and LAPSUS$ coordinating through a public Telegram channel, with a division of labor: Scattered Spider provides initial access, ShinyHunters handles data theft and publication, and LAPSUS$ members amplify extortion. The crew has sustained 40+ claimed breaches in 2026 via social-engineering (vishing) and SaaS/cloud-credential abuse rather than endpoint exploits. 2026 victims include Canvas/Instructure (~275M student records), ADT (~5.5M records), Carnival, Charter, Rockstar Games, Telus and the European Commission; in June the group was linked to an Oracle PeopleSoft data-theft campaign hitting 100+ organizations, and it is now marketing a "shinysp1d3r" ransomware-as-a-service (Resecurity/EclecticIQ/Infosecurity Magazine).

Breaches & Incidents

zero-day

SonicWall SMA 1000 zero-days exploited in the wild

SonicWall warned of active exploitation of two zero-days in its Secure Mobile Access 1000 appliances (SMA6210/7210/8200v): CVE-2026-15409 (CVSS 10.0), an unauthenticated server-side request forgery flaw, and CVE-2026-15410, which can enable administrator-level commands. Attackers used them for stealthy initial access, then extracted credentials, session databases and TOTP MFA seeds; there is no workaround (BleepingComputer/Help Net Security/The Hacker News).

KEV

CISA adds exploited Oracle E-Business Suite flaw

CISA added CVE-2026-46817 (CVSS 9.8), an improper-privilege-management flaw in Oracle E-Business Suite's Payments module, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on July 15 after confirming active exploitation that can fully take over Oracle Payments; the remediation deadline is Saturday, July 18 (CISA/cyberpress/BleepingComputer).

healthcareNew

TriWest notifies ~11,844 TRICARE beneficiaries

TriWest Healthcare Alliance notified 11,844 military TRICARE beneficiaries of an April 16 breach in which an unauthorized party downloaded names, Department of Defense Benefits Numbers and ZIP codes; in fewer than five cases the data also included Social Security numbers, addresses and dates of birth. TriWest is offering 24 months of Experian monitoring (Military Times/TechRadar).

vulnerabilityNew

Zoom patches critical Windows account-takeover flaw

Zoom disclosed CVE-2026-53412 (CVSS 9.8), an improper-input-validation flaw in the Zoom Desktop, VDI and Meeting SDK clients for Windows that could let an unauthenticated attacker take over an account over the network. It is fixed in version 7.0.0; users should update now (The Hacker News/SecurityAffairs/BleepingComputer).

supply chainNew

Critical JetBrains flaws span its on-prem ecosystem

JetBrains released fixes for a cluster of critical vulnerabilities enabling authentication bypass, account takeover and remote code execution across Hub, YouTrack, its IntelliJ-based IDEs, Kotlin, GoLand and TeamCity. A TeamCity RCE via Perforce connection settings is a notable software-supply-chain risk; on-prem administrators should patch promptly (cybersecuritynews/JetBrains security advisories).

breach

AssuranceAmerica exposes up to 6.9M records

Auto insurer AssuranceAmerica disclosed a breach affecting up to 6.9 million individuals, with attackers accessing names, contact information, driver's-license numbers, and insurance-policy and account data (SecurityWeek/breach trackers).

TuxBot v3: an IoT botnet built with an LLM's help

A new TuxBot v3 IoT botnet framework shows signs of LLM-assisted development — using AI-generated code to hijack devices and launch DDoS attacks — the latest example of criminals leaning on large language models to speed up malware creation (Unit 42/search, this run).
