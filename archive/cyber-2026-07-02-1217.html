Top Story

SimpleHelp's 10.0 auth-bypass hits its federal deadline today as attackers keep weaponizing remote-support servers

The clock runs out today on CVE-2026-48558, a critical authentication-bypass vulnerability in the SimpleHelp remote-support platform that CISA added to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. The flaw (CVSS 10.0) affects SimpleHelp 5.5.15 and earlier and 6.0 pre-release builds: when OIDC authentication is enabled, the server accepts identity-provider tokens without verifying their cryptographic signature, letting a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a token and log in as a fully privileged "Technician." Federal civilian agencies were ordered to remediate by July 2, 2026 under CISA's risk-based BOD 26-04.

Because SimpleHelp is widely deployed by IT departments, managed service providers and help desks, a compromised server hands attackers the same reach as a legitimate technician across every managed endpoint. Researchers have observed in-the-wild intrusions that began with confirmed exploitation of this flaw and then abused SimpleHelp's own remote-execution features to deliver malware — including the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer. Internet scans have indicated roughly 14,000 externally exposed SimpleHelp servers with an estimated 1,000 directly vulnerable; because MSPs sit upstream of many clients, a single compromise can cascade downstream. The fix is SimpleHelp 5.5.16 / 6.0 RC2.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Qilin (Agenda) — 2026's most prolific ransomware-as-a-service crew keeps hunting manufacturers

The Qilin ransomware group (also tracked as Agenda) remains among the busiest operations researchers track — most recently listing Calgary-based manufacturer Chemco as a victim this week. Qilin has claimed more than 1,000 victims in 2025 and over 640 more through the first half of 2026, part of a roughly 30% year-over-year rise in ransomware activity led by Qilin and INC Ransom. Manufacturing (~291 confirmed victims) and business services (~245) top its target list, with healthcare (~168) close behind — a sector where stolen patient records can fetch up to ten times the price of financial data on darknet markets. Operating as ransomware-as-a-service, Qilin arms affiliates with its builder and infrastructure and leans on phished credentials, exposed VPN appliances and remote-management tools plus fast double-extortion leaks — making it a useful barometer of the ransomware threat this summer.

Microsoft 365 hit by 81M login attempts

SharePoint RCE added to CISA KEV

CISA added Microsoft SharePoint Server flaw CVE-2026-45659 (CVSS 8.8, deserialization of untrusted data) to its KEV catalog on July 1, citing active exploitation; federal agencies must patch by July 4. Microsoft fixed it in May 2026; parallel intrusions tie to Warlock/Storm-2603 ransomware activity.

Exploited

Kemp LoadMaster under attack

Progress Kemp LoadMaster (CVE-2026-8037, vendor CVSS 9.6) lets an unauthenticated attacker run root commands via a crafted API request (an OS command-injection flaw). Researchers report active exploitation attempts began June 29; patched firmware (GA 7.2.63.2 / LTSF 7.2.54.18) is available.
