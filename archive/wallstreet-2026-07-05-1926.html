The Lead

Dow storms to a record before the long weekend; a tech drag pins the S&P flat

U.S. markets are closed for the Independence Day weekend — Friday, July 3 was the observed holiday, and trading resumes Monday, July 6 — so Thursday, July 2 stands as the tape's last word. And it belonged to the Dow: the blue-chip index jumped about 600 points, or 1.14%, to a record close of 52,900.07.

Beneath the surface it was a split session. A softer-than-expected June jobs report — nonfarm payrolls up just 57,000 versus a forecast near 115,000, with the unemployment rate at 4.2% — revived hopes the Fed can stay patient. But a tech-stock sell-off led by a slide in semiconductors and a drop in Tesla sent the Nasdaq Composite lower and left the S&P 500 essentially unchanged. Even so, all three major indexes posted gains for the week.

Movers & Drivers

Rivian

▲ +8.4%

Thursday's standout gainer closed up about 8.4% (to $18.63) after delivering 12,194 vehicles in Q2 — comfortably above its own 9,000–11,000 outlook — and raising full-year 2026 delivery guidance to 65,000–70,000 (from 62,000–67,000).

Tesla

▼ −7%+

Tesla gave up more than 7% — one of the day's biggest drags on the Nasdaq — even after reporting Q2 deliveries above 480,000, which topped Wall Street's average estimate.

Semiconductors

▼ drag

Chip stocks extended their decline, the heaviest weight on the Nasdaq Composite and the reason the S&P couldn't follow the Dow to a record.

Dow Jones

▲ +1.14%

The blue-chip average did the heavy lifting, climbing roughly 595 points to an all-time closing high of 52,900.07 as cyclical and value names outran tech.
