Top Story

Fight day in Baku: Fiziev defends home turf against Torres as the prelims roll

Live Today

The UFC is back in Baku on Saturday, June 27, and it's a homecoming for Rafael Fiziev (13-5), the No. 11 ranked lightweight, who headlines the National Gymnastics Arena against Mexico's surging finisher Manuel Torres (17-3) — who has finished 16 of his 17 wins in the first round — in a five-round lightweight main event. The bookmakers couldn't separate them: it lined as a near pick-'em. The prelims and the main card have rolled through the co-main — where Shara Magomedov edged Michel Pereira by unanimous decision — leaving only the Fiziev–Torres headliner to be settled (noon ET start, Paramount+).

The card delivered finishes early and often. On the prelims, Abdul-Rakhman Yakhyaev needed just eight seconds to flatten Julius Walker with a right hand, and Daniil Donchenko halted short-notice newcomer Theodor Berggren by second-round TKO (1:35). The main card opened with more of the same: Abus Magomedov submitted Michał Oleksiejczuk with a first-round guillotine (3:29), No. 9 flyweight Asu Almabayev tapped No. 14 Charles Johnson with a Suloev stretch in the third (3:33), and middleweight Ikram Aliskerov outworked Brunno Ferreira for a unanimous decision. In the co-main, fan-favorite Shara Magomedov survived a chaotic, foul-marred fight — eye pokes and ponytail grabs forcing referee breaks — to edge Michel Pereira by unanimous decision (29-28 ×3). Only the Fiziev–Torres headliner remained to be settled when this edition went to print.
