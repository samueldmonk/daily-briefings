Top Story

wp2shell: WordPress core goes from "no CVE" to public exploit code in 48 hours

The pre-authentication remote-code-execution chain in WordPress core known as wp2shell has moved decisively. As of The Hacker News' July 18 update, the two underlying bugs now carry CVE IDs, the full exploitation mechanism has been published, and a working proof-of-concept is public on GitHub.

It is two bugs, not one. CVE-2026-63030 is the REST API batch-route confusion; CVE-2026-60137 is a SQL injection in WordPress core. Chained, they carry an anonymous HTTP request all the way to code execution. The flaw is in core, so a bare install with zero plugins is exploitable — every 6.9 and 7.0 site was in range until WordPress shipped 6.9.5 and 7.0.2 on Friday and enabled what it calls forced updates through its auto-update system.

The batch-route bug was found by Adam Kues at Assetnote, Searchlight Cyber's attack-surface-management arm, and reported through WordPress's HackerOne programme. A persistent-object-cache condition affecting reachability of the vulnerable path has also surfaced since the original disclosure.

Reporting note: THN's article carries a July 17 dateline with a July 18 update. Its original text stated no CVE had been assigned and no exploitation had been reported — both superseded by the CVE assignments and by BleepingComputer's July 18 reporting that public exploits are circulating and watchTowr is seeing the first signs of in-the-wild exploitation.

Patch Priority

Do this first — deadline is today

CVE-2026-58644 — Microsoft SharePoint Server (Subscription Edition, 2019, Enterprise Server 2016). A CVSS 9.8 deserialization-of-untrusted-data flaw allowing remote code execution, weaponised as a zero-day before the July 14 fix shipped. CISA added it to the KEV catalog and the FCEB remediation deadline is July 19, 2026 — today. Microsoft warns it is remotely exploitable over the internet with low attack complexity. Patch or discontinue use. Straight after that: the two Fortinet FortiSandbox command-injection flaws sharing today's deadline, then WordPress 6.9.5 / 7.0.2.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Certificate theftChina-nexusNew

GoldenEyeDog — and the subgroup that walked out of DigiCert with code-signing certificates

Expel has attributed the April 2026 DigiCert security incident to a threat activity cluster it calls CylindricalCanine, a sub-group of GoldenEyeDog — also tracked as APT-Q-27, Dragon Breath and Miuuti Group — a Chinese cybercrime operation known for targeting the gambling and gaming sectors with counterfeit websites pushing malware-laced software, and active since at least 2015.

"In April 2026, GoldenEyeDog used their malware to access a support member's device at DigiCert, a code-signing certificate provider, and leveraged their access to steal certificates intended for DigiCert customers," Expel security researcher Aaron Walton wrote. Central to the group's operations is a modified version of Gh0st RAT (aka Farfli). Stolen code-signing certificates are worth flagging to defenders precisely because they let later payloads inherit trust that endpoint tooling is inclined to honour.

Breaches & Incidents

NewSentencing

Two Scattered Spider hackers get 5.5 years each for the TfL attack

Owen Flowers, 18, and Thalha Jubair, 20, were each sentenced to five and a half years at Woolwich Crown Court on Thursday, July 16, for the 2024 hack of Transport for London. The attack left 148 TfL systems inoperable and forced all 27,000 employees into an office to reset passwords in person. The NCA and CPS both put losses and recovery costs at £29 million. Both pleaded guilty on June 22 under Section 3ZA of the Computer Misuse Act 1990 — the Act's most serious charge, and per the CPS the first successful prosecution under it.

NewCloud credentials

NadMesh botnet hunts exposed AI services for cloud keys

A Go botnet called NadMesh turned up in early July scanning for exposed AI services, with the operator's own dashboard claiming 3,811 unique AWS keys. A Shodan harvester keeps the queue stocked with ComfyUI, Ollama, n8n, Open WebUI, Langflow and Gradio — the model runners and workflow builders teams stand up fast and firewall late. QiAnXin's XLab published the report on Friday. Treat the operator's numbers sceptically: the panel captured July 10 shows 17,700 total deploys above a funnel claiming 95,700 in 24 hours, figures that do not agree with each other.

NewSupply chain

Seven malicious npm packages target Vite with blockchain C2

Checkmarx has documented ViteVenom, a cluster of seven malicious npm packages typosquatting the Vite frontend tooling ecosystem. It marks an expansion of ChainVeil, which used a four-tier blockchain-based command-and-control infrastructure spanning Tron, Aptos and Binance Smart Chain to deliver a RAT capable of reverse shell, credential harvesting, file exfiltration and persistent backdoor injection. "This tactic makes disabling or destroying the C2 infrastructure extremely difficult," said researcher Pavan Gudimalla. Attributed to an actor tracked as SuccessKey, with wallet activity dating to February 27, 2026.

NewDPRK

Fake coding tests hide malware in SVG flag images

North Korean actors tied to the Contagious Interview campaign are using steganography in SVG image files to conceal payloads inside fake job postings and coding challenges. "Any user who ran the project ended up with a four-stage payload aligned with OTTERCOOKIE: a browser credential and crypto wallet stealer, a file stealer, a Socket.IO-based remote access trojan, and a clipboard stealer," Elastic Security Labs reported. The activity is tracked as REF9403.

NewLaw enforcement

Armenia holds a Russian tourist on a REvil warrant — lawyers say wrong man

Armenia has detained a Russian tourist named Aleksandr Ermakov since June 28 on a U.S. extradition request for a REvil ransomware suspect of the same name. His lawyers say Washington has the wrong man: the Ermakov the U.S. wants is Aleksandr Gennadievich Ermakov, sanctioned by Australia, the U.S. and the UK in January 2024 over the theft of 9.7 million records from Medibank Private, and — per TASS and case files two Russian outlets say they have read — serving a Russian sentence that bars him from leaving the country. The man in the Armenian cell, his lawyers say, is Aleksandr Yuryevich Ermakov, from Omsk.

EnergyExtortion

Ecopetrol confirms data theft across 15 subsidiaries

Colombia's state energy company disclosed on July 17 that data tied to roughly 3,300 user accounts was stolen from cloud file-storage environments across 15 subsidiaries. An attempted ransomware deployment was prevented. The attacker remains unidentified, extortion demands have been communicated and no data has been released. Ecopetrol reports no critical operational or production impact but warns of a possible material adverse effect.
