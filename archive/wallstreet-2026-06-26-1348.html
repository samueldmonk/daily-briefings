The Lead
Markets open · as of ~1:35 PM ET

Indexes split as a defensive rotation cushions an AI-cost tech sell-off — as of ~1:35 PM ET

U.S. stocks are mixed in early-afternoon trade Friday after opening sharply lower and clawing much of the way back. As of roughly 1:35 PM ET the Dow Jones Industrial Average was edging higher while the S&P 500 and the Nasdaq Composite tread water — each having pared an early drop of more than 1% — and the small-cap Russell 2000 outperformed, up about 0.7%. The move is a rotation out of technology and into defensives: consumer staples rose more than 1%, with financials (+0.8%) and utilities (+0.4%) also higher, while the technology sector was the day's clear laggard, off about 1.3%. Even so, the major indexes are still tracking a losing week, with the S&P down more than 1% and the Nasdaq off about 4%, though the Dow is pacing for a roughly 0.6% weekly gain. The trigger: mounting worries over the cost of building out artificial-intelligence infrastructure, amplified by a New York Times report that OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its IPO to 2027 — chilling AI-adjacent names — with SoftBank sliding about 9% in Tokyo.

The backdrop is a market on its back foot: on Thursday the Nasdaq Composite fell a fourth straight session — its first four-day losing streak since February — to 25,358.60, while the S&P 500 finished essentially flat at 7,357.49 and the Dow eked out a 0.14% gain to 51,920.62. Friday's single biggest mover is ON Semiconductor, down about 22.6% after announcing a roughly $7 billion deal to acquire Synaptics, leading a broad chip pullback that pulled Micron back about 2% — paring its Thursday post-earnings surge — alongside AMD (≈ −2.6%), Arm (≈ −4%) and Marvell (≈ −3.4%). Megacaps stayed heavy: Apple ≈ −6% (lineup-wide price hikes) and Microsoft ≈ −3.5% (an Xbox price increase) dragged Big Tech, while health care, financials and staples led to the upside. Crude kept sliding — WTI near $70.70, down about 1.7% and back below $71 — while the 10-year Treasury yield held near 4.38%.
