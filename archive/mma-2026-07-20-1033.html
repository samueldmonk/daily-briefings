Top Story

Du Plessis bows out of the title queue and hands the argument to Imavov — with Chimaev still demanding his rematch

Two days after beating Kamaru Usman in Oklahoma City, Dricus du Plessis has done the thing contenders almost never do: taken himself out of the running. Du Plessis says Sean Strickland has earned the right to defend the middleweight belt against Nassourdine Imavov next, rather than against him.

That leaves the champion's next opponent as a two-name argument. Khamzat Chimaev, the top-ranked middleweight, is demanding an immediate rematch after dropping a split decision to Strickland at UFC 328 in May — and du Plessis has publicly shut that idea down, calling a Strickland–Chimaev rematch "senseless." The alternative is Imavov, who sits at No. 3 on the back of five consecutive wins, with Caio Borralho, Israel Adesanya and Brendan Allen beaten in his last three fights.

Du Plessis beat Usman by unanimous decision (50-45, 49-46, 49-46) to move to 24-3 and hold the No. 2 spot. Usman, ranked No. 9, framed his own next step in similar terms afterwards: "It's either Sean Strickland or Islam" Makhachev.

What has not happened is a booking. Dana White has not named Strickland's challenger, and a widely surfacing article headlined "Sean Strickland's next opponent confirmed" carries a May 10 byline — it is not news, and nothing on this page is drawn from it.

Prospect Watch

Prospect

Tommy "Gun" McMillen — 28, 2-0 in the UFC

Undefeated featherweight. UFC.com called the Montes win "a record-setting performance for the unbeaten featherweight" — he holds the record for most significant strikes landed in a three-round bout at 145 pounds. Took a $100,000 Performance of the Night bonus.

Prospect

Felipe "Negão" Franco — 25, 11-2

First UFC victory, back at light heavyweight after debuting on short notice at heavyweight in March against Mario Pinto. Took mount and finished with hammer punches at 1:40 of round two. UFC.com flags him as a 205-pound prospect. Also a $100,000 Performance of the Night bonus.

Prospect

Ezra Elliott — 8-0, UFC debut

Took the fight Tuesday morning and missed weight Friday, then won a unanimous decision — and set the UFC record for most takedowns in a featherweight fight.

Prospect

RJ "The Hammer" Harris — 27, heavyweight debut
