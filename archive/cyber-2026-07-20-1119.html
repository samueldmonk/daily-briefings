Top Story

An Inc ransomware affiliate is inside SonicWall SMA 1000 appliances — and it is taking the MFA seeds with it

Two SonicWall SMA 1000 flaws are being exploited as zero-days by a threat actor tied to the Inc ransomware-as-a-service operation, according to telemetry from Rapid7's MDR team, which discovered the activity. CVE-2026-15409 is a critical CVSS 10.0 server-side request forgery in the SMA1000 Appliance Work Place interface that lets a remote, unauthenticated attacker force the appliance to make requests to unintended locations. CVE-2026-15410 is a high-severity CVSS 7.2 post-authentication code injection in the Appliance Management Console that lets a remote authenticated administrator run arbitrary operating system commands.

The pairing is what makes this dangerous. The attacker uses the appliances for stealthy initial access, and once a foothold is established, extracts high-value credentials, active session databases and TOTP multi-factor authentication seed configurations — the last of which converts a one-time intrusion into durable access that survives a password reset. Rapid7 describes the actor burrowing into multiple enterprise networks, sweeping up credentials and setting the stage for ransomware deployment.

Check Point's July 20 threat intelligence report lists SonicWall's hotfix for both CVEs among the week's ransomware-associated vulnerabilities, attributing the active exploitation to Inc. Treat any internet-facing SMA 1000 as compromise-assumed: patching alone does not evict an attacker who already holds your session tokens and MFA seeds.

Patch Priority

Overdue — act now

CVE-2026-58644 — Microsoft SharePoint Server (CVSS 9.8). A deserialization flaw giving unauthenticated remote code execution across all supported on-premises builds, exploited in the wild as a zero-day before patches shipped. CISA's federal remediation deadline was July 19, 2026 — that date has now passed, so unpatched federal systems are out of compliance and everyone else is behind the exploiters. Post-exploitation activity has included theft of IIS machine keys, so rotate them after patching.

Named alongside it, without a deadline: SonicWall CVE-2026-15409 (CVSS 10.0), the maximum-severity item above. It is in the KEV catalog, but no CISA-stated due date for it was confirmed from a CISA page this run, so none is asserted here. On severity and active ransomware use it is the more urgent of the two.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Ransomware-as-a-serviceNew

Inc Ransomware

Inc operates as a ransomware-as-a-service group, and an affiliate connected to it is the actor behind the SonicWall SMA 1000 zero-day campaign. The tradecraft is notable for its patience rather than its noise: rather than encrypting on entry, the affiliate uses the edge appliance purely for stealthy initial access, then harvests credentials, live session databases and TOTP MFA seeds to guarantee re-entry before ransomware is ever deployed. Check Point's July 20 report and Rapid7's MDR findings both tie the exploitation of CVE-2026-15409 and CVE-2026-15410 to Inc. Separately, the FortiBleed credential trove of 74,000 stolen FortiGate credentials has been linked to Inc and Lynx — the same pattern of ransomware crews buying or stealing their way through VPN and remote-access edge devices rather than phishing their way in.

Breaches & Incidents

NewRansomware

Spirals: a Rust ransomware family that finished the job in under a day

Researchers documented Spirals, a Rust-based ransomware family used against a South Asian IT services company. The operators moved from initial access to network-wide encryption in less than 24 hours, using an IIS web shell, WMI and PsExec to spread, disabling security services, disrupting backups and then encrypting. A sub-24-hour dwell time leaves essentially no window for a next-morning triage process. Check Point Research, July 20.

NewVulnerability

7-Zip patches an XZ decoder overflow — and the fix predates the advisory

CVE-2026-14266 is a heap-based buffer overflow in how 7-Zip processes XZ chunked data; opening a crafted archive can run code in the context of the current process. ZDI detailed it July 15 and rates it 7.0 High — not the Critical several write-ups reached for — with a local attack vector and high attack complexity. The fix shipped June 25 in version 26.02, twenty days before disclosure. Reported by Landon Peng of Lunbun LLC on June 5. As of July 20 there is no public proof-of-concept and no credible report of exploitation. Updating is a manual install, so unattended machines will not pick it up. The Hacker News, July 20.

Supply chainAI

Hugging Face: an autonomous agent ran the intrusion end to end

Hugging Face's own disclosure, published July 16, describes an intrusion into part of its production infrastructure "driven, end to end, by an autonomous AI agent system." Initial access came via a malicious dataset abusing two code-execution paths on a processing worker; the attacker escalated to node level, harvested cloud and cluster credentials and moved laterally over a weekend, with more than 17,000 recorded events in the action log. On July 20 the company confirmed the breach affected internal datasets and credentials and urged users to act. Public models, datasets and Spaces showed no evidence of tampering. Notably, frontier models behind commercial APIs refused the forensic work because the payloads were exploit code and C2 artifacts — the reconstruction ran on open-weight GLM 5.2 on Hugging Face's own infrastructure.

Supply chain

SleeperGem: three rogue RubyGems that skip CI and wait for a developer

Three malicious gems were published to RubyGems — git_credential_manager (versions 2.8.0 through 2.8.3, published July 18), Dendreo and a fastlane Firebase Test Lab plugin. StepSecurity: each release "is a loader… fetches a second stage from an attacker controlled Forgejo host, checks whether it is running in a build system and skips if it is, and on a developer machine it drops a native daemon and installs persistence." The loader scans roughly 30 CI environment variables and exits if it finds them — deliberate evasion. Version 2.8.2 stages; 2.8.3 daemonises, adds cron and a systemd user service, and where passwordless sudo exists plants a setuid root shell at a path mimicking a networking utility.

Ransomware

Coca-Cola halts U.S. fairlife production after ransomware

Per Coca-Cola's SEC Form 8-K dated 2026-07-16: unauthorized third-party access to a portion of fairlife's systems, including production-related systems. U.S. production was temporarily suspended; Canadian operations were not impacted. Incident response and business continuity were activated and law enforcement notified. Product quality and safety were not impacted. The attackers are unidentified and data theft is unconfirmed.

Extortion

Accenture confirms a breach after 35 GB is offered for sale

An actor using the alias "888" offered roughly 35 GB on PwnForums — source code, RSA and SSH keys, Azure access keys and personal access tokens, and configuration files. Accenture's statement, verbatim: "We are aware of this isolated matter, and we have remediated its source. There is no impact to Accenture operations and service delivery." The company did not confirm the volume or the types of data claimed.
