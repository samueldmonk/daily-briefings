Top Story

"FortiBleed" campaign holds working credentials for tens of thousands of Fortinet firewalls across 194 countries

Researchers are tracking a large-scale, automated credential-harvesting campaign — dubbed FortiBleed — against internet-facing Fortinet FortiGate firewalls and SSL-VPN gateways worldwide. An attacker-controlled database holds valid administrator credentials for tens of thousands of devices: Recorded Future counts 73,932 firewalls, SOCRadar's tally reaches 86,644, and Arctic Wolf maps the activity across 194 countries — roughly half of all FortiGates reachable on the internet. Verified working credentials have been confirmed for an estimated 30,000 to 75,000 devices, and SOCRadar says the operators organize their validated-credential database by country, sector and organization revenue.

FortiBleed does not rely on a malware payload or a brand-new product vulnerability. Instead it runs a credential pipeline — credential stuffing and password spraying drawn from earlier breach dumps and infostealer logs, configuration harvesting, offline hash-cracking, and post-authentication capture of further credentials. Independent researcher Bob Diachenko identified the exposed threat-actor server on June 13, 2026, and the campaign was formally disclosed on June 16 after analysis by SOCRadar and Arctic Wolf, which reverse-engineered a recovered "CyberStrike Harvester" binary and attributed the operation to a Russian-speaking threat group. Fortinet characterizes the activity as credential reuse and brute-forcing rather than a new CVE; CISA's June 18 advisory urges organizations to rotate FortiGate and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, and harden management interfaces.

Patch Priority

Patch Ubiquiti UniFi OS now — CVE-2026-34910 (CVSS 10.0) is exploited; federal deadline June 26

The single most urgent action for defenders today is the max-severity (CVSS 10.0) Ubiquiti UniFi OS set. CVE-2026-34910 is an improper-input-validation flaw that lets a network-adjacent attacker run command injection; it ships alongside an improper-access-control bug (CVE-2026-34908) and a path-traversal flaw (CVE-2026-34909) that researchers showed can be chained to full remote code execution. CISA added all three to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 23 based on evidence of active exploitation; under BOD 26-04, federal agencies must apply Ubiquiti's fixes or discontinue the affected UniFi OS products by June 26, 2026 (two days out). Apply the vendor updates immediately. Note: the actively-exploited Splunk Enterprise pre-auth RCE (CVE-2026-20253) is already past its June 21 deadline — patch it (10.4.0 / 10.2.4 / 10.0.7) if you have not.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters — data-theft & extortion crew

The extortion group ShinyHunters is the actor behind the year's largest breach by record count — the Instructure / Canvas incident. The crew claims it stole roughly 275 million records — names, email addresses, ID numbers and messages — tied to students, teachers and staff across about 8,809 school districts, universities and online-education platforms, making it the largest education-sector breach on record. ShinyHunters runs a pure data-theft-and-extortion model rather than file-encrypting ransomware: after Instructure tried to patch instead of negotiate, the group triggered an outage that displayed a ransom note to every Canvas user in early May, then reached an agreement with Instructure on May 11 — which says the stolen data was destroyed (unconfirmed reports put the payment near $10M). The FBI has warned students and staff to expect follow-on extortion and phishing.

Breaches & Incidents

LastPass & others via the Klue supply chain New

LastPass confirmed (in a June 2026 notice) that customer CRM data in its Salesforce instance was exposed through a breach of third-party vendor Klue, claimed by a new extortion group calling itself "Icarus." The same OAuth-token compromise reached BeyondTrust, Recorded Future, Tanium, Jamf, Sprout Social, Gong and Insurity. Exposed data was limited to names, contact details and support/sales records; LastPass says password vaults and master passwords were not affected, and Salesforce disabled the Klue integration.

London Hydro discloses customer data breach New

Canadian electricity provider London Hydro said on June 20 that attackers broke into its systems, potentially exposing customers' names, addresses, emails and phone numbers along with account, billing and meter details. The utility is investigating and notifying affected customers — the latest reminder that critical-infrastructure operators remain prime targets.

"Cordyceps" CI/CD supply-chain flaw New

Researchers at Novee Security flagged a new class of CI/CD workflow weakness — codenamed Cordyceps — that lets any unauthenticated user (a free account is enough) forge approvals, push code or steal credentials, threatening repositories at organizations including Microsoft, Google, Apache and Cloudflare. GitHub is hardening actions/checkout to block "pwn request" patterns by default (effective June 18).

Malicious npm packages drop a Windows RAT New

Researchers uncovered a set of malicious npm packages — published over the past month by a user named "abdrizak" — that deliver a Windows-based remote access trojan. Several remained downloadable when disclosed, underscoring the continuing risk from poisoned open-source dependencies.
