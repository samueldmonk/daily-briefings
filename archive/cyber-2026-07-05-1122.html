Top Story

FortiBleed credential theft linked to INC and Lynx ransomware

On July 2, SOCRadar researchers connected the financially-motivated "FortiBleed" campaign to the INC and Lynx ransomware operations — the first confirmed link tying mass FortiGate credential theft to actual ransomware deployment. The campaign is assessed to have targeted roughly 430,000 FortiGate firewalls worldwide and gathered more than 110 million credentials.

Researchers tracked scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals across 150+ countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets and completed the full attack chain on 354. A custom packet-sniffing tool, "FortiGate Sniffer," intercepts VPN credentials directly from network traffic. One operator tied to FortiBleed's infrastructure was found working negotiation panels for both groups; at least 12 ransomware deployments have resulted, with 200+ additional operational servers and evidence of a ~20-member crew with defined roles.

Patch Priority
Most urgent action today
Patch SimpleHelp CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0, OIDC authentication bypass) now. It is actively exploited to deliver the TaskWeaver and Djinn Stealer malware, and its CISA KEV remediation deadline of July 2, 2026 has already passed. Upgrade off SimpleHelp 5.5.15-and-earlier / 6.0 pre-release builds immediately — a single compromised SimpleHelp server can reach every downstream MSP client.
Threat Actor Spotlight
ShinyHunters
UNC6240

ShinyHunters (tracked by Mandiant/Google TIG as UNC6240) is a financially-motivated extortion group. Mandiant confirmed it exploited the Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day CVE-2026-35273 between May 27 and June 9, 2026 — before Oracle's June 10 out-of-band advisory.

Roughly 68% of targeted organizations were in higher education, most in the U.S. The same campaign is behind Nissan Americas' June 25 employee-data breach, underscoring the group's focus on high-value enterprise applications.

Breaches & Incidents
NewBreach

Nissan Americas employee data exposed

Nissan disclosed a breach (June 25) tied to the Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day (CVE-2026-35273). Exposed data includes contact info, banking details, Social Security numbers and tax records for current and former employees in the U.S., Canada, Mexico and Brazil — attributed to ShinyHunters.

Ransomware

FortiBleed feeds INC & Lynx

SOCRadar linked mass FortiGate credential theft to at least 12 ransomware deployments, with one operator working negotiation panels for both INC and Lynx and victim data overlapping the INC leak site — the first hard tie between the two.

Malware

SimpleHelp abused to drop stealers

Attackers are exploiting CVE-2026-48558 to deliver two previously-unreported malware families — TaskWeaver and Djinn Stealer — through SimpleHelp remote-management servers used by MSPs and IT providers.

Exploited

SharePoint RCE in active use

CISA confirmed active exploitation of SharePoint RCE CVE-2026-45659 (added to KEV July 1). Any authenticated user can trigger it; Microsoft fixed the flaw in May 2026 updates, and campaigns are attributed to financially-motivated and ransomware groups.
