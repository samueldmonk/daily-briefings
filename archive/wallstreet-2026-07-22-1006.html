The Lead

Stocks open lower as an oil spike greets a Big Tech earnings test — Alphabet and Tesla on deck after the bell

US stocks slipped at Wednesday's open, giving back part of Tuesday's rally as a jump in crude and cooling chip stocks offset a broad run of morning earnings beats. In early trade the S&P 500 fell about 0.3%, the Nasdaq Composite dropped roughly 0.6% and the Dow eased about 0.1%, with futures having pointed lower through the pre-market (Nasdaq 100 −0.71%, S&P −0.29%, Dow −0.10% at 5:52 a.m. ET). Figures are as of the open, roughly 9:40 a.m. ET, and will move through the session.

Oil was the catalyst. Crude spiked more than 4% early Wednesday after US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said Iran was “not serious about talks”; WTI jumped about 4.2% to roughly $88.6 a barrel and Brent rose about 1.8% to roughly $93 as an 11th straight day of US strikes on Iran and a reported attack on the Caspian Pipeline Consortium terminal on Russia's Black Sea coast stoked supply fears. The move kept upward pressure on the 10-year Treasury yield, which has been circling a two-month high near 4.6%.

Earnings cut the other way. AT&T rose about 2.6% after a second-quarter beat and stronger-than-expected subscriber growth, Super Micro firmed on a record order backlog and CME Group added about 1% on its best first half ever, even as Philip Morris slipped on soft third-quarter guidance and GE Vernova fell despite raising its full-year cash-flow outlook. The bigger test comes after the close: Alphabet and Tesla — the first two of the “Magnificent Seven” to report — headline the docket, with IBM also due, and investors are weighing whether Alphabet's AI monetization justifies its surging spend.

Movers & Drivers

New▲ ~2.6%Earnings

AT&T beats and adds subscribers

AT&T rose about 2.6% to roughly $22.84 after a second-quarter beat. Adjusted EPS was $0.65, clearing the $0.59 consensus, and the company added 432,000 postpaid phone subscribers versus roughly 338,500 expected. Revenue of $31.56 billion (up 2.3% year over year) came in a touch light against the ~$31.75 billion estimate — an EPS-and-subscriber beat with a slight top-line miss.

New▼ ~4.5%Earnings

GE Vernova falls despite a raised outlook

GE Vernova lifted its 2026 free-cash-flow outlook by about $5 billion at the midpoint, yet shares fell roughly 4.5% in early trade, reversing an earlier gain — a "sell-the-news" reaction after a strong run into the print. It was among the sharpest single-name moves on the tape at the open.

New▲AI hardware

Super Micro firms on a record backlog

Super Micro Computer gained in early trading after flagging a record order backlog, a read-through the market took as fresh evidence of durable AI-server demand. The move stood out on a morning when the broader semiconductor group was cooling after Tuesday's memory-led surge.

New▲ ~1%Earnings

CME Group rises on a record first half

CME Group added about 1% after reporting earnings and revenue above expectations, with the exchange operator calling the first six months of 2026 its best-ever first half — a volatility-and-volume story as rate and commodity swings drive trading activity.

New▼ ~0.5%Earnings

Philip Morris slips on soft guidance

Philip Morris International eased about 0.5% after issuing a weaker-than-expected third-quarter forecast, projecting earnings of $2.20 to $2.25 a share against analyst expectations near $2.42 — guidance that overshadowed the rest of the print.
