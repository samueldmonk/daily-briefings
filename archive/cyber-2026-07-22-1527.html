Top Story

'wp2shell': unauthenticated WordPress Core RCE now under mass exploitation

A critical pre-authentication remote-code-execution chain in WordPress Core — dubbed wp2shell — pairs a high-severity SQL injection (CVE-2026-60137) with a critical arbitrary-code-execution flaw (CVE-2026-63030). Chained, they let a completely unauthenticated attacker run code with no valid account, no vulnerable plugin, no special configuration and no user interaction. WordPress versions 6.9.0–6.9.4 and 7.0.0–7.0.1 are affected; the project shipped emergency releases 6.9.5 and 7.0.2 and enabled forced auto-updates given the severity. VulnCheck has verified more than two dozen unique proof-of-concepts, mass scanning is under way, and CISA added both CVEs to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on July 21, confirming active exploitation.

Patch Priority
⚠ Most urgent action today

Update WordPress to 6.9.5 (6.9.x) or 7.0.2 (7.0.x) now. The wp2shell chain (CVE-2026-63030 + CVE-2026-60137) is actively exploited and both CVEs entered CISA KEV on July 21; confirm forced auto-updates completed on every site. Next: patch on-premises SharePoint against CVE-2026-50522 (CVSS 9.8) and rotate machine keys — patching alone does not evict attackers who already stole them.

Threat Spotlight
Active campaign

SharePoint machine-key theft (CVE-2026-50522)

Following a public proof-of-concept, watchTowr observed attackers exploiting on-premises SharePoint (Enterprise Server 2016, Server 2019 and Subscription Edition) to pull machine keys via a single request. With those keys an intruder forges valid authentication tokens, impersonates users and reaches SharePoint sites and documents with the victim's privileges — persistence that survives patching. Defenders are urged to patch and then rotate credentials on any potentially exposed assets. No public attribution has been made this run.

Breaches & Incidents
Data breach

Ernst & Young (EY)

EY disclosed a breach of a third-party IT platform used to support tax work for clients. An unauthorized party accessed the platform between Mar 28 and Apr 12 (detected Apr 23) and downloaded client documents. Exposed data may include Social Security numbers, financial account codes and card details. EY says there was no malware, ransomware or extortion, and is offering 24 months of Experian monitoring (enroll by Oct 31). It's EY's third incident in under three years.

Data theft

Craneware

The Edinburgh-based healthcare financial-software vendor — supplier of billing and revenue-cycle software to roughly 2,000 U.S. hospitals and nearly 10,000 clinics and pharmacies — disclosed on July 20 that attackers viewed and exfiltrated a significant volume of file names, plus a percentage of employee data and a subset of customer and partner records. No threat actor has been named; Craneware says the intrusion is contained with no disruption to services.
