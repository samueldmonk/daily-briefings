The Lead

Chips lead the tape higher on an AMD–Microsoft AI deal as oil hands back its $90 spike — Big Tech earnings are the week's test

U.S. stocks are higher in Monday's session, with semiconductors out front ahead of a heavy week of Big Tech results. TheStreet's updated July 20 session coverage has the S&P 500 up 0.63%, the Nasdaq up 1.02% and the Dow Jones Industrial Average up 0.23%, with the Russell 2000 lower by 0.42% — small caps lagging a tech-led advance. That extends the state at the open, when Yahoo Finance's live coverage had the Dow up about 0.3%, the S&P 500 about 0.5% and the Nasdaq Composite almost 0.8%. The pre-open reading, at roughly 7:30 AM ET, was S&P futures +0.35%, Dow futures +0.23%, Nasdaq futures +0.76% and Russell 2000 futures +0.39%, with the VIX at 18.21, down 2.98%.

The single biggest driver is a chip deal. AMD launched Helios, its first rack-scale system for AI, and named Microsoft as its newest buyer: Microsoft said Monday it will deploy AMD Helios racks in its Azure data centers, joining early customers Meta, OpenAI and Oracle. Helios is a 72-GPU rack combining AMD Instinct MI455X GPUs, 6th Gen EPYC CPUs, AMD Pensando networking and the ROCm software stack, and it ships later this year. AMD told CNBC it plans to book tens of billions in data-center AI revenue starting in 2027, the majority of it from Helios.

The geopolitical backdrop got worse over the weekend and the market shrugged. Yahoo Finance reports the U.S.–Iran war escalated again as the U.S. carried out its ninth straight day of attacks and Iran retaliated by bombing U.S. allies in the region, primarily Kuwait, with the two sides still far apart on a ceasefire. TheStreet, citing CNN, reports the death of a third American service member, killed in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone. What cooled the oil bid was diplomacy: Iranian state news agency IRNA, citing Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei, said Iran has received proposals from mediators about resuming negotiations with the U.S., and that talks could still be pursued if they align with Iran's national interests, while Iran would continue to defend itself "resolutely."

Crude reversed accordingly. As of about 7:21 AM ET, West Texas Intermediate was down 0.17% at $82.35 a barrel while Brent was up 0.50% at $88.54, having earlier surpassed $90. "Investors appear to be distinguishing between elevated geopolitical risk and the likelihood of a wider systemic shock," said Daniela Hathorn, senior market analyst at Capital.com. "For now, markets continue to view higher energy prices as the primary transmission mechanism, with inflation implications taking precedence over broader concerns about global growth." On the week ahead, Hathorn added that after last week's sharp rotation out of AI-related stocks, "investors will be looking for evidence that earnings can continue to justify elevated valuations."

Rose 1.62% to $125.99 in premarket trading. Separately it is expanding beyond rockets, including plans to rent out unused computing capacity from its Colossus data-center complex.
