TOP STORY

Gaethje's Historic Upset Sends Shockwaves Through Lightweight Division

In the most stunning upset in recent UFC history, Justin Gaethje stopped undisputed lightweight champion Ilia Topuria via corner stoppage at 5:00 of round 4 on the South Lawn of the White House during UFC Freedom 250 on June 14. Gaethje, a +600 underdog and three-time title challenger, proved too durable and relentless for the previously undefeated (17-0) Topuria, forcing his corner to halt the bout when Topuria showed signs of being overwhelmed. The victory reshapes the title picture entirely, with Gaethje now holding the undisputed belt and facing new contenders emerging from the reshaped rankings.

RECENT RESULTS

Fiziev's Spinning Wheel Kick KO Stuns Torres in Baku Homecoming (June 27)

Rafael Fiziev returned to his native Azerbaijan and delivered a spectacular spinning wheel kick KO against Manuel Torres at 0:15 of round 2 in UFC Fight Night: Baku at the National Gymnastics Arena in Baku. Fiziev's dramatic finish gave him redemption after a difficult recent stretch (1-4 in last five); the victory signals a resurgent lightweight contender and could position him for a title shot later in 2026.

New

Horiguchi Retains Title Over Kape with KO Performance (June 20)

Kyoji Horiguchi successfully defended the men's flyweight title against Manel Kape, stopping him via KO in round 3 at 2:42 at UFC 330 in Las Vegas. Horiguchi's dominant performance silenced doubts about his durability at the weight class and set the stage for a busy summer of title defenses.

AROUND THE SPORT

  • White House Legacy: UFC Freedom 250 marked the first professional sporting event ever held at the White House, setting a historic precedent for combat sports in U.S. politics.
  • Interim Heavyweight Picture: With Gane holding interim honors and Aspinall holding undisputed, a heavyweight unification title bout is anticipated for late summer 2026.
