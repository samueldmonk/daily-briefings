The Lead

Dow storms to a record before the long weekend; a tech drag pins the S&P flat

With U.S. markets shut Friday for Independence Day and closed through the weekend, Thursday, July 2 stands as the tape's last word — and it belonged to the Dow. The blue-chip index climbed 1.14% (about 595 points) to a record close of 52,900.07, with 24 of its 30 members higher and Apple (+4.46%) out front.

Beneath the surface it was a split session. A softer-than-expected June jobs report — payrolls up just 57,000 versus roughly 115,000 expected — revived hopes the Fed stays on hold, but a renewed slide in semiconductors and megacaps (Tesla and Meta among them) left the S&P 500 essentially flat and dragged the Nasdaq Composite down about 0.8%. Trading resumes Monday, July 6.
