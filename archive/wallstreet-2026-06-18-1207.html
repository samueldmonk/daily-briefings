The Lead

Chips lead a rebound by midday: Intel rips to a record on a reported Apple deal as the U.S.–Iran peace deal takes effect

Wall Street carried a powerful chip-led rebound through midday Thursday, clawing back part of Wednesday's hawkish-Fed selloff. As of around midday ET, the S&P 500 was up roughly 1.2% and the Nasdaq Composite about 1.5%, while the Dow added around 0.8%. The advance was decidedly narrow: the small-cap Russell 2000 lagged, off about 0.7%, as money concentrated in the megacap semiconductor complex rather than spreading across the tape.

The spark was Intel, which surged about 9% — touching an intraday record near $132 ($131.96) — after President Trump said on Truth Social that the chipmaker had agreed to a deal with Apple to design and build chips in the United States. Neither Intel nor Apple has formally confirmed the arrangement, so the move reflects the headline rather than a signed agreement (Apple shares moved just ~0.6%). The news rippled across the sector: Micron rose more than 4%, Applied Materials added more than 4% and Lam Research climbed more than 5%, while Marvell got an extra lift from a KeyBanc price-target hike.

The bounce comes a day after the Federal Reserve held its benchmark at 3.50–3.75% but delivered a hawkish dot plot — nine of 18 officials now project a 2026 rate hike — that sank stocks and pushed Treasury yields higher. Yields were roughly flat Thursday, with the 10-year near 4.45%, even after data showed headline producer prices rose 6.5% year over year in May, the hottest reading since 2022. Crude eased as the U.S.–Iran interim peace deal was signed and took effect: the two sides released the text of a 14-point agreement extending an April ceasefire by 60 days and raising hopes for reopening the Strait of Hormuz, sending Brent toward $78 and WTI into the mid-$70s, around a three-month low. In earnings, Kroger opened the day with a revenue beat but a penny EPS miss, while Accenture is due after the close.

Movers & Drivers
Percentage moves below are sourced from Thursday's session per TheStreet/CNBC/Bloomberg/Schwab coverage; figures are intraday (around midday ET) and subject to change as the session develops.

Intel (INTC) ▲ ~9%

The standout of the session: Intel jumped about 9% to an intraday record (near $132) after President Trump said on Truth Social the company struck a deal with Apple to design and build chips in the U.S. Neither company has confirmed. Trump also referenced an Nvidia manufacturing tie-up and a U.S. stake.

Semiconductors — broad rally

The Intel headline lit up the group: Micron and Applied Materials each tacked on more than 4% and Lam Research added more than 5%. Marvell got an extra boost from a KeyBanc price-target hike. The chip complex paced the broad market's advance.

Kroger (KR) — Q1 beat, EPS miss

The grocer reported Q1 revenue of $46.12B, topping the ~$45.5B consensus, but adjusted EPS of $1.58 missed the $1.59 estimate by a penny. Identical sales ex-fuel rose 1%, e-commerce grew 19% and gross margin narrowed to 22.7% from 23%; full-year guidance ($5.10–$5.30 EPS) was maintained.

Russell 2000 — small caps lag

The narrow, chip-driven advance left small caps behind: the Russell 2000 traded lower (about -0.7%) even as the S&P 500 and Nasdaq pushed higher, a reversal of the broad-participation rallies seen earlier in the month.

Oil & the Iran deal ▼

Crude eased as the U.S.–Iran interim peace deal was signed and took effect: Brent slipped toward $78 and WTI into the mid-$70s, around a three-month low. The two sides released a 14-point text extending an April ceasefire by 60 days and raising hopes of reopening the Strait of Hormuz, deflating the energy-supply risk premium.

Rates — yields stay firm

The hangover from Wednesday's hawkish dot plot lingered in the bond market: the 10-year Treasury yield held near 4.45% and the rate-sensitive 2-year stayed elevated around 4.15%, a headwind that kept the rally concentrated rather than broad.
