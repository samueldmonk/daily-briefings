The Lead

Stocks close lower as a chip selloff drags the Nasdaq down more than 1%; oil jumps on a Hormuz tanker strike

As of Tuesday's close, July 7. Two blows to the AI trade landed at once. Samsung Electronics posted record preliminary Q2 results — operating profit surged roughly 19-fold on booming AI-memory demand — but the beat still triggered selling in chip names on worries the AI-spending boom is fully priced, and Samsung's own shares fell about 7% in Seoul. Separately, a report that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI chip to cut its reliance on outside suppliers sent a fresh chill through the complex, pressuring Nvidia.

The result was another broad chip selloff: a gauge of semiconductor stocks sank about 5.5%, the VanEck Semiconductor ETF fell more than 5%, and Micron dropped about 7% with KLA, Marvell, Broadcom and AMD also lower. At the close the Nasdaq Composite was down more than 1%, the S&P 500 finished modestly lower — most of its members actually rose in a rotation — and the Dow slipped back below 53,000 after Monday's record close.

Oil added a second worry: WTI crude rose about 2.3% to roughly $70 a barrel and Brent climbed about 2.4% to near $74 after a naval coalition raised the threat level for ships crossing the Strait of Hormuz to "severe" following Iranian attacks on tankers. Traders also looked ahead to the June FOMC minutes expected this week, while SpaceX made its debut in the Nasdaq-100.

Movers & Drivers

Crinetics Pharma (CRNX)

≈ +100%

The day's biggest mover. Shares roughly doubled after Vertex Pharmaceuticals agreed to acquire the endocrine-drug maker for $85 a share in cash — a deal valued around $10 billion and about double Crinetics' ~$42 close on Monday. The buyout, built around acromegaly drug Palsonify, lifted health care and made pharma the session's bright spot.

Micron (MU)

≈ −7%

Micron fell about 7% as Samsung's record report reignited fears that elevated memory pricing and margins are already fully priced across the memory complex — the heart of Tuesday's selloff.

Western Digital (WDC)

≈ −9%

Western Digital tanked almost 9% as investors sold memory and data-storage names across the board, extending a days-long slide in the group.

Chip Complex

Semis ≈ −5.5%

A broad reset: the semiconductor gauge sank about 5.5% and the VanEck Semiconductor ETF fell more than 5%, with KLA, Marvell, Broadcom and AMD all posting declines. Not even Samsung's record profit was enough to entice buyers.

Nvidia (NVDA)

Lower

Nvidia came under pressure after a report that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI chip to reduce reliance on outside suppliers — a fresh worry for AI-data-center demand at the center of this year's rally.

The Rotation

Broad market up

Beneath the chip selloff, most S&P 500 stocks actually rose: health care led — lifted by the Crinetics-Vertex deal — alongside energy and communication services, as investors rotated out of this year's biggest AI winners rather than fleeing the market.
