Top Story

FortiBleed credential theft confirmed as a ransomware pipeline for INC and Lynx

SOCRadar researchers on July 2 linked the financially motivated campaign dubbed FortiBleed to the INC Ransom and Lynx ransomware operations — the first confirmed instance connecting mass FortiGate credential theft to actual ransomware deployment. The campaign is assessed to have targeted roughly 430,000 FortiGate firewalls worldwide and harvested more than 110 million credentials.

Researchers tracked scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals across 150+ countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets, and completed the full attack chain on 354 of them — leading to at least 12 ransomware deployments and hundreds of encrypted endpoints. Tellingly, a single operator tied to FortiBleed's infrastructure was found working the negotiation panels for both INC and Lynx, collapsing the usual distance between a credential harvester and the ransomware crews that buy that access. Researchers characterize the crew as an initial-access-broker operation with an organized internal structure of roughly 20 people and a clear division of labor.

Patch Priority
◆ Act now — overdue & actively exploited

SimpleHelp RMM — CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0)

An OIDC authentication bypass (the server fails to verify the cryptographic signature of identity tokens, CWE-347) lets a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a token and gain a full technician session — then transfer files and run commands on every managed endpoint. It is being actively exploited to drop the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer, an MSP supply-chain risk (observed by Arctic Wolf). CISA added it to the KEV catalog under BOD 26-04 with a July 2 deadline — now overdue. Patch to SimpleHelp 5.5.16 (or 6.0 RC2) immediately, disable OIDC if unused, and audit technician accounts and endpoints for TaskWeaver.

Threat Actor Spotlight

INC Ransom & Lynx

Two ransomware-as-a-service operations that have, for the first time, been directly tied to a mass credential-theft campaign. SOCRadar's investigation found the same operator negotiating extortion cases on both groups' panels while sitting on FortiBleed's stolen-credential pipeline — evidence that the crew is not merely selling access but coordinating downstream encryption. At least a dozen ransomware deployments have already been traced to that access, so defenders running FortiGate estates should treat any exposed-credential alert as a potential precursor to an INC or Lynx intrusion.

Breaches & Incidents
Data breachHealthcare

Medtronic notifies customers

Medical-device maker Medtronic is notifying about 3.8 million people of a breach tied to the ShinyHunters extortion group, after an unauthorized actor accessed corporate IT systems between April 13 and 19. ShinyHunters claims to have stolen about 9 million records — names, contact details, dates of birth, SSNs and health data; Medtronic says products and patient safety were unaffected and is offering two years of credit monitoring.

Data extortionHealthcare

ShinyHunters targets One Medical

The same data-extortion crew is threatening to leak roughly 8.8 TB of data it claims to have stolen from primary-care provider One Medical — part of a widening ShinyHunters campaign against healthcare and enterprise SaaS victims.

RansomwareMicrosoft

SharePoint servers under active attack

CISA added SharePoint RCE CVE-2026-45659 (CVSS 8.8) to its KEV catalog after confirming active exploitation, ordering federal agencies to patch by July 4 (now overdue). The deserialization flaw was patched by Microsoft in May and can be triggered by any authenticated user — no admin rights required — across Subscription Edition, 2019 and Enterprise 2016.

MalwareMSP

SimpleHelp abused to drop stealers

Attackers exploiting the SimpleHelp auth bypass (CVE-2026-48558) are deploying the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer across endpoints managed through compromised remote-support servers — turning a single RMM into a mass malware-delivery channel.
