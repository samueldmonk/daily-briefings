The Lead

Wall Street drifts to a mixed close, then Micron detonates after the bell

Stocks finished little changed and split on Wednesday, June 24: the Dow Jones Industrial Average added 182.06 points (+0.35%) to 51,848.90, but the S&P 500 eased 0.10% to 7,358.22 and the Nasdaq Composite slipped 0.43% to 25,476.64 as energy, communication-services and technology names dragged into the close. The whole session was a holding pattern ahead of memory giant Micron's report — and traders were rewarded.

After the bell, Micron posted a blowout: record fiscal-third-quarter revenue of about $41.5 billion (versus roughly $35 billion expected), adjusted EPS of $25.11 (well above the ~$20.4 consensus), a gross margin of 84.9%, and fiscal-fourth-quarter revenue guidance of around $50 billion ± $1 billion — far above the ~$42.9 billion Street estimate — on an AI-driven memory crunch that roughly quadrupled revenue from a year earlier, with management saying HBM capacity is sold out through 2026 and more than $1 billion of HBM4 already shipped. The stock jumped about 15% in extended trading, pulling chip peers higher. The backdrop: the Fed held rates at 3.50%–3.75% on June 17 and rate-hike chatter resurfaced this week (a BofA note flagged the risk), while oil tumbled to pre-war lows — WTI crude down about 4% near $70 — the 10-year Treasury yield slid below 4.5% to roughly 4.40%, and gold fell more than 3% to about $4,000, its lowest since November 2025.

Movers & Drivers

Wendy's (WEN) +25.7%

The latest meme-stock target closed up 25.7% at $7.86 — spiking as much as ~42% on heavy volume (trading was halted at one point) — after naming former Potbelly executive Steve Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer. With short interest near 27%, it shot to the top of Reddit and Stocktwits trending boards on a "Save Wendy's" squeeze.

Micron (MU) +15% AHAfter Hours

Shares leapt about 15% in extended trading on a record fiscal-Q3 (~$41.5B revenue, EPS $25.11 vs ~$20.4 expected, 84.9% gross margin) and ~$50B Q4 revenue guidance — the day's marquee event, lifting the semiconductor complex into Thursday.

Energy & oil −4%

Energy was the day's weakest corner as crude tumbled to pre-war lows — WTI settling near $70 (−3.9%) and Brent around $73.74 (−4.3%) — on easing Middle East tensions, dragging on the S&P 500 and Nasdaq.

Semiconductors heavy, then bid

Chips were sold into the Micron print after an AI-jitters, Asia-led selloff (a BofA rate-hike note added pressure), then memory and AI-adjacent names firmed after hours on Micron's guide — the swing that kept the Dow green while the Nasdaq slipped.
