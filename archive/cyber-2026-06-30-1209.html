Top Story
Klue OAuth Compromise Exposes CRM Data Across Enterprises
Market intelligence platform Klue discovered unauthorized activity on June 12 affecting its integration infrastructure. Attackers used a compromised legacy credential to push malicious code that harvested OAuth tokens, allowing the Icarus extortion group to directly query connected Salesforce environments. Multiple enterprise victims have been identified, including Gong, Tanium, Recorded Future, Jamf, Sprout Social, and others. The attack highlights supply-chain risk: a single compromised vendor can grant attackers access to dozens of customer CRM systems without triggering endpoint detection.

Patch Priority
Urgent: June Patch Tuesday — 39 Critical CVEs
Action: Microsoft released 206 vulnerabilities on June 9 (the largest Patch Tuesday ever), including 39 critical-severity flaws and 3 zero-day exploits. Three critical Hyper-V vulnerabilities (CVE-2026-47652, CVE-2026-45641, CVE-2026-45607) enable guest-to-host escape, bypassing VM isolation entirely.
Deploy patches immediately for Hyper-V, Remote Desktop Client (11 CVEs including 4 critical), and Outlook/Word RCE variants (CVE-2026-45458, CVE-2026-45456, CVE-2026-47635).
Threat Actor Spotlight
Icarus — Supply-Chain Extortion Operator
Active since April 2026, Icarus targets supply-chain vendors (Klue, TinyPulse ecosystem) to exfiltrate customer business intelligence and CRM data for extortion. The group favors credential and API abuse over malware, reducing detection surface. Pattern: identify vendor with weak secrets management → compromise infrastructure → harvest customer tokens → monetize data via extortion to individual victims.
Breaches & Incidents
Nintendo Employee Data via TinyPulse HR Platform
ShadowByt3$ ransomware group claimed responsibility on June 12 for accessing 859 MB of internal data through the TinyPulse employee engagement platform. Data includes employee names, email addresses, IDs, and financial records (bank statement PDFs, W-9 tax forms). Ransom demand of $2M was made; Nintendo declined to negotiate. Threat actors subsequently leaked samples to dark-web platform.
Ransomware
TVING (Korean Video Streaming) — User Data Exposed
TVING confirmed on June 3 that unauthorized external access exposed personal information of its users, including IDs, names, birthdates, phone numbers, email addresses, passwords (hashed/salted), and refund account details.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines
CISA Binding Operational Directive 26-04 (effective June 11): Risk-based remediation replaces the flat BOD 22-01 three-week window. KEV CVEs now receive individualized deadlines based on exploitability and severity.

Recent KEV additions (June 2026):
  • CVE-2026-35273 (PeopleSoft, June 12) — due ~July 3 (21 days)
  • CVE-2026-45247 (Mirasvit, June 3) — deadline assigned per BOD 26-04
  • CVE-2022-0492 (Linux Kernel, June 2) — legacy but actively exploited
FCEB agencies only: federal civilian executive branch agencies must remediate KEV flaws per deadline. State/local governments and private firms are not legally bound but advisably should treat KEV as an alert system.
