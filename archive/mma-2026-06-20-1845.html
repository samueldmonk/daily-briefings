Top Story

Gaethje shocks Topuria at the White House — and a division resets

At UFC Freedom 250 on the White House South Lawn (June 14), Justin Gaethje — a heavy underdog — battered previously-unbeaten Ilia Topuria so badly over four rounds that Topuria's corner waved it off before the fifth, handing Gaethje the undisputed lightweight title by TKO. Remarkably, all seven main-card bouts ended in a knockout or TKO.

The aftermath is severe: Topuria suffered non-displaced orbital fractures in both eyes (the right took the brunt), and reports say he will not return in 2026, with a comeback now targeted for 2027. Doctors determined no surgery is needed, and the first-ever loss of his pro career leaves Gaethje anchoring a reshuffled lightweight picture as champion.

Rankings & Business

Business & broadcast. UFC Freedom 250 (June 14) was the first UFC event staged on the White House grounds. To mark it, the promotion lifted fight-night bonuses to a record $400,000 (Fight of the Night) and $425,000 (Performance of the Night). Gaethje's combined $825,000 was a promotion-record single-night bonus haul on a card where every bout ended in a finish. Tonight's UFC Vegas 119 streams on Paramount+.
