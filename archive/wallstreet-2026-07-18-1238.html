The Lead

Chip rout deepens and Netflix cracks — Wall Street closes out its first losing week in three

U.S. markets are closed for the weekend. The most recent session is Friday, July 17, and it was ugly across the board: the Nasdaq Composite fell 1.40% to 25,520.24, the S&P 500 lost 1.01% to 7,457.69, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average slipped 0.77%, or 406.55 points, to 52,146.42, as a worsening semiconductor rout outweighed strong insurance-sector earnings.

Two things drove it. First, a global sell-off in chip stocks accelerated on fears about whether AI infrastructure spending is sustainable — sharpened by news that Moonshot AI, a Chinese start-up, had released an advanced model to rival U.S. offerings. The Philadelphia Stock Exchange Semiconductor Index has fallen more than 13% in the past month, and the industry gauge closed 20.2% below its June 22 record high, confirming a bear market. For context, the same index is still up almost 63% year to date.

Second, Netflix cracked. Shares fell as much as 12.2% Friday morning after the company said it would publish its engagement report annually rather than twice a year starting in 2027 — a disclosure change some investors read as hiding something. Add a re-escalation of U.S.–Iran hostilities and the risk-off mood was complete.

For the week, the S&P 500 was off 1.6%, the Nasdaq slid 2.9% and the Dow fell 0.9%. The S&P closed out its first losing week in the last three, and only its third since March. The energy sector was Friday's only gainer, lifted by a Middle East oil spike.

Movers & Drivers

NewDown

Netflix — fell as much as 12.2% intraday

Q2 was fine: revenue of $12.56 billion matched estimates and EPS of $0.80 beat the Street by a penny, with full-year guidance unchanged at the midpoint. What spooked investors was the move to annual engagement reporting from 2027, viewing hours growing just 2% in the first half, and Q3 guidance landing a hair below consensus. Shares touched a 52-week low of $65.08 and sat around $68.70–$68.95 (roughly −7%) in late/closing prints. The stock is down 46% from its 52-week high.

NewUp

Travelers — jumped 9%

The insurer surged after reporting a significant second-quarter earnings beat. Insurance peers Progressive and Allstate also gained, making financials one of the few places to hide on Friday.

DownSemis

Nvidia, AMD and Intel — chip rout extends

Nvidia shares slid as investors rotated out of the AI bellwether, and the semiconductor sell-off weighed on Advanced Micro Devices and Intel. The SOX confirmed a bear market, closing 20.2% below its June 22 record close.

NewDown

Alphabet — Gemini 3.5 Pro delay

Alphabet shares fell on reports that the launch of its Gemini 3.5 Pro AI model would be delayed.

NewUp

Energy — the session's only winning sector

Oil's jump on Middle East escalation made energy the sole gaining sector on Friday. Communication services and consumer cyclicals were the worst hit.

Safe havenUp

Gold — $4,010.55, up 0.57%

Gold rose 0.57% to $4,010.55 as of the U.S. market close, with the 10-year Treasury yield finishing at 4.55%.

On the Radar

  • FOMC, July 28–29. The Fed's next rate decision lands in under two weeks. There is no Summary of Economic Projections at this meeting, so the statement and press conference carry more weight.
  • Q2 earnings hit their peak. FactSet forecasts the S&P 500 will report year-over-year earnings growth of 23.1% for the second quarter — a high bar, and the question is whether results clear it.
  • The Strait of Hormuz. Oil rose Friday after Kuwait said Iran attacked a power and water desalination plant, with fighting disrupting flows through a strait that typically handles around 20% of the world's oil traffic. This is now the dominant macro swing factor.
  • Is the AI trade breaking? The semiconductor index is officially in a bear market, 20.2% off its June 22 record, on doubts about the durability of AI capex. Watch whether the selling stays contained to chips or keeps broadening as it did Friday afternoon.
