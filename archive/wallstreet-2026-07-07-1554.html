The Lead

Chips slide again on Samsung's record quarter and a DeepSeek chip report; oil firms after a Hormuz tanker strike

As of about 3:40 p.m. ET, Tuesday, July 7. Two blows to the AI trade landed at once. Samsung Electronics posted record preliminary Q2 results — quarterly profit surged roughly 19-fold — but the beat still triggered selling in chip names on worries the AI-spending boom is fully priced. Separately, a Reuters report that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI inference chip to cut its reliance on Nvidia sent a fresh chill through the complex, pressuring Nvidia and its suppliers.

The result was another broad chip selloff: a semiconductor ETF fell about 6% and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index dropped around 5.5%, with Intel down roughly 8–10% (to about $110), Applied Materials off about 10%, AMD about 8% (to about $508) and Micron about 7%, while Nvidia came under pressure on the DeepSeek report. By mid-afternoon the tape had improved off its lows: as of about 3:40 p.m. ET the Nasdaq Composite was down about 0.6% (having pared a roughly 1% opening drop), the S&P 500 dipped just ~0.2% — most of its members actually rose in a rotation — and the Dow slipped about 0.4% (roughly 200 points), falling back below 53,000 after Monday's record close.

Oil added a second worry: WTI crude rose above $69 a barrel and Brent climbed toward $73 — one-week highs — after a fully laden Qatari LNG carrier was struck by a projectile near the Omani coast while exiting the Strait of Hormuz, reviving supply-disruption fears even as prices sit near their lowest since late February on ample OPEC+ supply. Traders also looked ahead to Wednesday's June FOMC minutes, while SpaceX made its debut in the Nasdaq-100.

Movers & Drivers

Crinetics Pharma (CRNX)

≈ +99%

The day's biggest mover. Shares nearly doubled to about $83.69 after Vertex Pharmaceuticals agreed to acquire the endocrine-drug maker for $85 a share in cash — a deal valued around $10 billion and a 102% premium to Monday's $42.03 close. Vertex sees the assets, led by acromegaly drug Palsonify, reaching ~$5B in peak annual revenue.

Intel (INTC)

≈ −8–10%

Among the S&P 500's biggest losers, Intel fell roughly 8–10% (to about $110). Applied Materials — exposed to AI-capex customers Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron and Intel — was hit just as hard, down about 10%, bearing the brunt of the memory/AI-capex reset.

Micron (MU)

≈ −7%

Micron fell about 7% (to roughly $1,004) as Samsung's record report reignited fears that elevated memory pricing and margins are already fully priced in across the memory complex.

AMD · Nvidia

Lower

AMD fell about 8% (to roughly $508), reversing Monday's rally, while Nvidia came under pressure after a Reuters report that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI inference chip to reduce its reliance on Nvidia — a fresh worry for AI-data-center demand.

SpaceX (SPCX)

≈ −5%

SpaceX joined the Nasdaq-100 before Tuesday's open — one of the fastest additions in the index's history under new "fast-track" rules for mega-IPOs — yet slid about 5% despite the billions in expected index-fund buying.

The Rotation

Broad market up

Beneath the chip selloff, most S&P 500 stocks actually rose: health care led — lifted by the Crinetics-Vertex deal — alongside energy and communication services, as investors rotated out of this year's biggest AI winners rather than fleeing the market.
