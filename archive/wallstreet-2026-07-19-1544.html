The Lead

Semiconductors close the week in a bear market as a Chinese model rattles the AI trade

U.S. equity markets are shut for the weekend. The most recent session was Friday, July 17, and it was an ugly one: the S&P 500 fell 1.01%, the Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.40% and the Dow slipped 0.77%, extending a tech-led sell-off as a worsening semiconductor rout outweighed strong insurance-sector earnings.

The headline damage was in chips. The PHLX Semiconductor Index entered a bear market on Friday — Yahoo Finance flagged the threshold crossing as the sell-off deepened — after falling more than 13% over the past month. The Motley Fool notes the same index is still up almost 63% year to date, which is the other half of the story: this is a violent unwind of a very large run, not a collapse from a standing start.

Two catalysts got named repeatedly. Chinese start-up Moonshot AI released an advanced model that investors fear could undercut U.S. vendors' pricing power — TheStreet identified it as Kimi K3 and reported the company's claim that it rivals leading U.S. systems. Alongside that, a re-escalation of violence between the U.S. and Iran drove a broad risk-off tone and pushed crude sharply higher.

Breadth was narrow but not uniformly grim: energy was the only gaining sector on the day, while communication stocks and consumer cyclicals took the worst of it. For the week, the S&P 500 was off 1.6%, the Nasdaq slid 2.9% and the Dow fell 0.9%.

Movers & Drivers

+9%Earnings

Travelers jumps on a big Q2 beat

The Travelers Companies climbed 9% after reporting a significant second-quarter earnings beat — the day's standout gainer. Insurance peers Progressive and Allstate also gained, making financials one of the few places to hide on an otherwise red tape.

ChipsRotation

Nvidia, AMD and Intel lead the semiconductor unwind

Nvidia shares slid as investors rotated out of the AI bellwether, and the broader semiconductor sell-off weighed on Advanced Micro Devices and Intel. The Motley Fool's quote board showed Nvidia at $203.31, down 2.0% on the session.

Product delayMega-cap

Alphabet falls on a reported Gemini delay

Alphabet shares fell on reports that the launch of its Gemini 3.5 Pro AI model would be delayed. The Fool's board showed Alphabet at $346.12, down 2.2%. Netflix also plunged during the session on its results, adding to pressure on communication-services names.

On the Radar

  • The next FOMC meeting is July 28–29. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has described an internal "family fight" over whether the central bank tightens or holds later this year; reporting this week put markets as pricing a quarter-point hike as soon as the September meeting — the opposite direction from most of the past two years.
  • Second-quarter earnings season hits its peak. FactSet forecasts the S&P 500 will report earnings growth of 23.1% year over year for Q2 — a high bar, and the gap between that expectation and actual results is the week's main single-stock risk.
  • Watch whether the chip unwind stops at the semis. The PHLX Semiconductor Index is in a bear market but still up nearly 63% year to date; whether Friday's damage stays contained to AI hardware or bleeds into the mega-cap complex is the question the tape reopens with on Monday.
  • Oil is the geopolitical tell. WTI's 13.4% five-day gain on renewed U.S.–Iran hostilities is doing double duty — supporting the only sector that gained on Friday while adding an inflation complication for a Fed already arguing with itself.
