Top Story

Law Enforcement Dismantles Amadey and StealC Malware Infrastructure

A coordinated takedown on June 24, 2026, involving Bitdefender, Bitsight, ESET, Microsoft, and law enforcement agencies disrupted the "assembly lines" powering ransomware, financial fraud, and critical infrastructure attacks. The operation significantly hampers the distribution pipeline for Amadey and StealC malware, which are often used as initial-access vectors for larger intrusions.

Patch Priority

CVE-2026-35273 (Oracle PeopleSoft) — CVSS 9.8, Actively Exploited

Affected: Oracle PeopleTools 8.61, 8.62 (HTTP → unauthenticated remote code execution, full takeover). Threat Actor: ShinyHunters (Mandiant UNC6240). Scope: ~300 instances across 100+ organizations; education sector heavily impacted (68% of known compromises). Status: Exploitation as zero-day confirmed May 27 – Jun 9, 2026; Oracle out-of-band alert issued Jun 10. Action: Prioritize patching of externally facing PeopleSoft instances immediately; audit access logs for suspicious activity May 27 onward.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters (Mandiant UNC6240)

ShinyHunters leveraged the Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day (CVE-2026-35273) as a primary intrusion vector, targeting higher education and enterprise environments. The group operated undetected for ~2 weeks post-exploitation, with evidence of data exfiltration across 100+ organizations before disclosure. ShinyHunters previously claimed responsibility for Baker Distributing (Salesforce records, early June 2026) and has a track record of targeting financial services and healthcare sectors with dual-extortion tactics.

Breaches & Incidents

Baker Distributing — Salesforce Records Leaked

ShinyHunters claimed responsibility for a breach affecting Baker Distributing on May 23, 2026 (not a June 2026 intrusion). The group publicly released the breach on early June 2026, exposing ~102,935 accounts via HIBP. Leaked data consists largely of corporate contact and support information; not high-sensitivity PII, per Cybernews. ShinyHunters' May 23 claim aligns with their pattern of claiming attacks weeks before public leak disclosure.

Ransomware-as-a-Service: Emerging Trends

Threat actors increasingly favor data-theft-only and extortion-only operations over traditional encryption-based ransomware. This operational shift reduces complexity while maintaining victim pressure via data exposure threats. Notable groups: The Gentlemen (dual-extortion, globally active, advanced evasion); SafePay (rapidly emerging, targeting US/Germany/UK/Canada/Italy). The Amadey/StealC takedown will likely accelerate this migration away from malware distribution infrastructure.

CISA KEV & Federal Deadlines

Recent Status: CISA KEV remediation deadlines are now risk-based under BOD 26-04 (Jun 10, 2026). Actively exploited, automatable vulnerabilities on internet-facing systems = 3-day federal remediation window.

  • CVE-2026-20230 (Cisco UC Manager): Jun 28, 2026 (PASSED) — SSRF-to-RCE, exploited in the wild.
  • CVE-2026-12569 (PTC Windchill): Jun 28, 2026 (PASSED) — RCE in Windchill and FlexPLM.
  • CVE-2026-11645 (Chrome V8): ~Jun 30, 2026 — Memory safety issue, part of 74-CVE Chrome patch set (Jun 2026).

Note: The old BOD 22-01 three-week window is superseded. Verify all agency deadlines against CISA's official KEV catalog for current dates and remediation windows.
