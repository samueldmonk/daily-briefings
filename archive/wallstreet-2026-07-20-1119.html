The Lead

Chips come roaring back off the bear-market line, and the tape follows

The semiconductor complex staged the snapback the market had been waiting for. The Philadelphia Stock Exchange Semiconductor Index — the SOX — gained 3.1%, two sessions after falling into a bear market on Friday, and it dragged the rest of the growth trade with it: the Nasdaq 100 climbed 1.3%, led by semiconductor companies, after its worst week in almost a month. Bloomberg had the S&P 500 up 0.6% as of 9:35 a.m. in New York.

Readings on the broad indices differ slightly by capture time, and both are published here with their attribution rather than blended: TheStreet's updated July 20 session coverage gives S&P 500 +0.63%, Nasdaq +1.02%, Dow +0.23%, with the Russell 2000 −0.42%; Yahoo Finance's post-open read gives Dow +0.3%, S&P 500 +0.5% and the Nasdaq Composite up almost 0.8%.

The names doing the work: Nvidia rose more than 2% and AMD jumped 4% on analyst price-target calls, with Western Digital up more than 4% and Intel, Seagate and SanDisk each up more than 3%. Part of the move looks mechanical — short covering in chipmakers and AI-infrastructure names ahead of a heavy megacap earnings week that begins with Alphabet on Wednesday.

The geopolitical backdrop did not go away; the market simply declined to trade on it. The U.S. carried out a ninth consecutive day of strikes on Iran and announced the death of a third American service member, killed in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone. Iran's state news agency IRNA, citing Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei, said Iran has received proposals from mediators about resuming negotiations and would pursue them if they align with its national interests, while continuing to defend itself "resolutely." Oil eased after touching $90 a barrel over the weekend.

On the Radar

  • Alphabet reports Wednesday, July 22. The first of the megacap prints and the one the chip rebound is effectively front-running. Consensus as stated by the sources: $116.9B revenue, $2.90 EPS; 49 of 54 analysts rate it Buy or Strong Buy with no Sells and a mean target near $434.
  • Tesla, Intel and IBM also report this week. Wall Street has raised expectations for all of them, looking for evidence that companies are monetizing AI to match the scale of their build-out spending.
  • More than 86% of S&P 500 companies that have reported this season have beaten expectations. The open question is whether beats are still enough, or whether the sell-the-news dynamic of recent weeks remains in charge.
  • FOMC meets July 28–29. Policymakers are expected to leave rates unchanged while preserving the option to tighten. Rate futures show more than two-thirds of the market positioned for a hike by year-end.
  • IBM's July 14 crash is still the overhang on its print. Shares fell 25.21% to close at $217.07, erasing roughly $68 billion — the largest one-day drop since at least 1968 — after preliminary Q2 revenue of $17.2B missed the $17.86B FactSet consensus and operating EPS of $2.93 missed $3.01. Oppenheimer cut it from Outperform to Perform and scrapped a $350 target. This is six days old, not today's news.
  • Alphabet's "Frozen v2" server chip. The Information reported Alphabet is building a chip that would embed parts of Gemini's architecture directly into silicon, with engineers projecting six to ten times more tokens per unit of power than the newest TPUs, targeted for 2028 deployment and treated partly as a trial run.
