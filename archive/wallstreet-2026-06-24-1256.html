The Lead

Stocks steady before Micron's after-the-bell earnings as chips try to stabilize (as of ~12:40 PM ET, June 24)

U.S. stocks bounced modestly Wednesday after a two-session, AI-driven selloff. As of about 12:40 PM ET, the Nasdaq Composite is up ~0.6%, the S&P 500 ~0.4% and the Russell 2000 ~0.4%, while the Dow slips ~0.2%, with the early bounce narrowing through the morning. The steadier tone follows a rebound attempt in battered memory and chip names: Micron, which closed down about 13% Tuesday (the worst performer in the S&P 500), rose roughly 4% pre-market and traded choppily into midday ahead of fiscal third-quarter results after today's close — the week's marquee corporate catalyst and, in many eyes, the next big test of the AI trade. Tuesday's rout ran deep: the Nasdaq Composite closed −2.21% at 25,587.04, the S&P 500 fell 1.44% to 7,365.46, the Dow finished essentially flat (−0.09%) at 51,666.84, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index dropped more than 8%.

Expectations into the print are high: FactSet consensus is about $20.83 adjusted EPS on roughly $35–35.75B revenue — a vast jump from $1.91 EPS on about $9.3B a year ago — with high-bandwidth memory (HBM) commentary the swing factor. Sell-side targets have raced higher: TD Cowen lifted its price target to $1,500 (Buy), Goldman Sachs to $900 (Neutral), and Rosenblatt expects a "beat and raise." Away from chips, Take-Two kept climbing — rallying from about $206 to roughly $247 as BTIG initiated a Buy with a $290 target into Grand Theft Auto VI pre-sales. In an index shake-up, Alphabet (GOOGL) will join the Dow before Monday, June 29's open, replacing Verizon and seating Google's parent beside Nvidia, Amazon, Apple and Microsoft; Honeywell stays in the index as it spins off its aerospace arm. On the macro side, the Fed held its target at 3.50–3.75% on June 17 — a fourth straight hold and the first meeting under new Chair Kevin Warsh — and Bank of America and Deutsche Bank now expect three quarter-point hikes in 2026 (September, October and December); the 10-year Treasury yield hovers near 4.5%. Oil keeps sliding, with WTI near $71 — briefly under $70, its lowest since late February — as U.S.–Iran peace-talk progress and heavier Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic ease supply fears, while gold tumbled below $4,000 (down ~1.7% toward a seven-month low) and silver fell about 5% on a firmer dollar and rising rate-hike bets.

Movers & Drivers

Micron — steadies before tonight's report ▲ ≈ +4% pre-mkt

Micron rose roughly 4% pre-market — recovering part of Tuesday's ~13% slide — and traded choppily into midday ahead of fiscal-Q3 earnings after today's close. FactSet consensus is about $20.83 adjusted EPS on ~$35–35.75B revenue, up from $1.91 a year earlier; TD Cowen raised its target to $1,500 (Buy) and Goldman to $900 (Neutral), with HBM guidance the key tell for the AI-memory trade.

Take-Two — GTA VI run continues ▲New

Take-Two rallied from about $206 to roughly $247 as Grand Theft Auto VI pre-sales approach. BTIG initiated coverage with a Buy rating and a $290 price target, calling the publisher "firing on all cylinders" — a rare AI-independent winner amid the week's tech turbulence.

Alphabet — joining the Dow ▲New

S&P Dow Jones Indices will add Alphabet (GOOGL) to the 30-stock Dow before Monday's open, replacing Verizon — broadening the price-weighted index's tilt toward AI, cloud and digital advertising and seating Google's parent beside Nvidia, Amazon, Apple and Microsoft. Honeywell stays in the Dow as it spins off its aerospace unit.

Semiconductors — the swing factor ▼ mixed

Chips sit at the center of the week's volatility: the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell more than 8% Tuesday as investors took profits on the market's biggest AI winners. Names are mixed Wednesday and largely waiting on Micron — a strong print and upbeat guidance could steady the group, while any disappointment risks another leg lower.
