The Lead

The Dow climbs to a fresh record to open Q3 as chipmakers drag the Nasdaq — as of ~1:40 PM ET

Stocks opened the third quarter on a split note, a day after major indexes wrapped their strongest quarter since 2020. As of roughly 1:40 PM ET the Dow Jones Industrial Average had scaled a fresh intraday record, up about 168 points (~0.3%), and the S&P 500 edged up around 0.1%, while the Nasdaq Composite lagged at about −0.2% as investors dumped semiconductor names. The small-cap Russell 2000 outperformed with a gain of roughly 0.6%, and breadth was positive as the rally broadened out of Big Tech.

The pressure came from the same chip names that led the second-quarter rally, as investors locked in profits after a first-half run that saw the group surge more than 80%. The marquee move belonged to Meta, which jumped nearly 10% on a report that it plans to sell excess AI computing capacity through a new cloud unit — lifting fellow hyperscalers Microsoft (~+3%) and Apple (~+2%) but weighing on rival "neocloud" providers such as CoreWeave and Nebius. Traders are also weighing the June ADP payrolls report, the ISM manufacturing index and remarks from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh at the ECB's forum in Sintra, Portugal.
