The Lead

Wall Street splits as chips rebound and oil climbs — all eyes on Alphabet and Tesla (as of ~1 PM ET)

U.S. stocks were diverging Wednesday afternoon. As of roughly 12:46 PM ET the Dow was up about 0.3% and the S&P 500 about 0.2%, while the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite slipped around 0.1%. A rebound in chipmakers — with Nvidia climbing about 3% — pulled the S&P off its session lows even as most megacaps fell. Rising oil prices revived inflation worries after an 11th consecutive night of U.S. strikes on Iran, and investors braced for the first two "Magnificent Seven" reports of the season: Alphabet and Tesla after the close, with IBM and Texas Instruments also on deck. Fresh U.S. tariffs on Canada added to the cautious mood.
