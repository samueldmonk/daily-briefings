Top Story

Operation Endgame strikes again — StealC, Amadey and SocGholish networks dismantled

Law EnforcementMalware TakedownEuropol

Europol and Eurojust announced on June 24 that the latest phase of Operation Endgame neutralized 326 servers and 142 domains behind three of the world's most prolific malware loaders and infostealers — StealC, Amadey and SocGholish. Investigators recovered roughly 27 million stolen credentials (about 25.6 million unique) from more than 385,000 compromised systems and froze over €41 million (about $46.5 million) in criminal crypto assets.

Law-enforcement agencies from Canada, Denmark, Germany, the Netherlands, the UK and the US took part, alongside private partners including Microsoft, Bitdefender, BitSight, ESET, IBM X-Force, Lumen, Proofpoint and Mitsui Bussan Secure Directions. Amadey and StealC — which work in tandem, with Amadey gaining initial access and StealC harvesting passwords — were linked to more than 140,000 infected computers in the first two weeks of May alone. The action also struck the infrastructure behind SocGholish, the long-running "fake browser update" operation.

Patch Priority
⚠ Patch before tomorrow's federal deadline

Ubiquiti UniFi OS — CVE-2026-34910 (CVSS 10.0)

An improper-input-validation flaw allows command injection and chains with two other maximum-severity bugs (CVE-2026-34908 and CVE-2026-34909, an NGINX authentication-gateway bypass) for unauthenticated command injection and full system takeover of UniFi OS devices. It is being actively exploited — reports describe automated reconnaissance creating rogue administrator accounts (named "John Sim"). Fix: update to UniFi OS Server 5.0.8 or later. CISA added all three to the KEV catalog on June 23 with a federal remediation deadline of June 26, 2026 — patch today.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Icarus — the SaaS-supply-chain extortionist

A newly-emerged extortion group, Icarus (active since late April 2026), claimed the supply-chain compromise of competitive-intelligence platform Klue. By abusing a legacy integration credential to harvest OAuth tokens, the attackers reached the Salesforce environments of Klue's customers — among them a striking roster of security and tech firms (HackerOne, Huntress, Snyk, Tanium, Recorded Future, Jamf, OneTrust and others). Icarus issued extortion demands threatening to publish the stolen business-contact data, underscoring how a single trusted SaaS connector can become a skeleton key across an entire customer base.

Breaches & Incidents
New

Klue — Salesforce supply-chain theft

Attackers breached competitive-intelligence platform Klue around June 11 using a compromised legacy integration credential, pushed a malicious update to harvest OAuth tokens, and siphoned Salesforce CRM data from customers. Salesforce disabled the Klue Battlecards app integration; the "Icarus" extortion crew claimed responsibility.

New

Security vendors in the blast radius

HackerOne, Huntress, Snyk, Tanium, Recorded Future, Jamf, OneTrust and Sprout Social have disclosed that their Salesforce business-contact data — names, emails, phone numbers and job titles — was accessed through the Klue integration.

New

‘Mistic’ fileless backdoor campaign

Researchers detailed a stealthy new backdoor, Mistic, used in suspected financially-motivated intrusions against insurance, education, IT and professional-services organizations since April 2026. It runs payloads entirely in memory — nothing is written to disk — and carries a self-delete kill switch, hallmarks of an operator seeking long-term, low-visibility access.

Aflac — ~22.7M people notified

Aflac's notification to nearly 22.7 million customers, beneficiaries, employees and agents stems from its 2025 Scattered Spider–linked intrusion, with identity-protection enrollment that ran into 2026 — not a new June 2026 breach.
