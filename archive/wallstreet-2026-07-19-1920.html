The Lead

Chips finish the week in a bear market, and the tape holds there until Monday

U.S. equity markets are shut for the weekend, so there is no live session to report. The most recent one was Friday, July 17, and it closed lower across the board: the S&P 500 fell 1.01% to 7,457.69, the Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.40% to 25,520.24, and the Dow gave up 406.55 points, or 0.77%, to 52,146.42.

The damage was concentrated in semiconductors. The PHLX Semiconductor Index finished 20% below its record close — the technical threshold for a bear market — capping the group's worst week since April 2025. The Motley Fool's recap notes the same index is still up roughly 63% year to date, which is the other half of the story: this is a violent unwind of an enormous run, not a collapse from a standing start.

Two catalysts came up repeatedly in the coverage. Chinese AI start-up Moonshot AI released an advanced model positioned as a rival to U.S. systems, and investors read it as a threat to American vendors' pricing power. Separately, a re-escalation of violence between the U.S. and Iran drove a broad risk-off tone and pushed crude sharply higher.

Breadth was narrow. Energy was the only S&P 500 sector to close higher, with communication stocks and consumer cyclicals hit hardest. CNBC's sector read put energy at +1.82%, information technology at −2.65% and communication services at −1.45%. For the week, the S&P 500 lost about 1.6%, the Nasdaq 2.90% and the Dow roughly 0.9%.

Movers & Drivers

+9%Earnings

Travelers jumps on a blowout second quarter

The insurer rose about 9% on a large Q2 beat — CNBC's midday read framed it as "more than 8%" with EPS of $10.04 against a $5.41 consensus and net income near $2.2 billion versus roughly $1.5 billion a year earlier. Peers Progressive and Allstate also gained.

ChipsAI

Nvidia leads the AI complex lower

Investors rotated out of the AI bellwether. The Motley Fool and TheStreet put Friday's decline at 3.5%, taking the market value to roughly $4.86 trillion; Yahoo Finance's session wrap described a smaller 2.2% loss. The two figures are not reconciled in the coverage, so both are shown here. AMD and Intel fell alongside it.

DownProduct delay

Alphabet slips on a reported Gemini delay

Shares fell on reports that the launch of its Gemini 3.5 Pro model would be pushed back. Communication services was among the weakest sectors on the day at −1.45% per CNBC.

DownMedia

Netflix plunges

Netflix was a notable decliner through Friday's session, carrying the "Netflix plunges" headline on Yahoo Finance's midday wrap. No specific percentage move was stated in the coverage retrieved, so none is published here.

On the Radar

  • FOMC, July 28–29. The decision lands Wednesday, July 29. Fed funds futures put the probability of no change at roughly 90%, and there is no Summary of Economic Projections at this meeting.
  • Does the chip unwind find a floor? The semiconductor index enters the week 20% off its record after its worst stretch since April 2025, but still up around 63% for the year. Which of those two facts the tape decides to trade on is the open question.
  • The Moonshot AI overhang. Friday's selling was tied in part to a Chinese model release read as a competitive threat to U.S. AI vendors' pricing power. Whether that thesis holds up on closer inspection is a live debate rather than a settled one.
  • Crude and the geopolitical bid. Energy was the only sector to close green, on a re-escalation between the U.S. and Iran that lifted oil. That is a tailwind for one sector and an inflation complication for everyone else.
