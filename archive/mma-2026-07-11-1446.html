Top Story

Fight night: McGregor–Holloway 2, thirteen years in the making

Conor McGregor's first fight in five years arrives tonight at UFC 329, and it's a rematch thirteen years in the making: Max Holloway, the former featherweight champion making his welterweight debut, meets the Irishman he lost to by decision back in 2013. Both men made weight Friday — McGregor at 170.5 pounds, Holloway at 170 — and all 28 fighters on the card cleared the scale.

The books make Holloway the favorite, though fight-day prices vary widely — Holloway ranges from −220 to as steep as −265 across books, with McGregor between +165 and +215 (Covers/Yahoo's fight-day surveys; DraftKings has it −250/+205). The line opened far wider: Covers had Holloway open at −400 a month out, earlier reporting had openers as wide as −550/+420, and sportsbooks now report a flood of small wagers on McGregor while Holloway draws the bigger bets. McGregor, 22-6, spent fight week in vintage form — declaring himself the greatest featherweight in UFC history and taking shots at Jon Jones and Khabib Nurmagomedov. The co-main sends Benoit Saint Denis against Paddy Pimblett in a lightweight contenders' bout — booked for three rounds rather than five, to Pimblett's public frustration; Saint Denis says he wanted 25 minutes too and is predicting Fight of the Night.

Sources: UFC.com weigh-in results, Yahoo Sports, SI/FanNation, MSN, CBS Sports, ESPN
