Top Story

CISA flags a 10.0 SimpleHelp flaw as attackers weaponize remote-support servers

CISA has added CVE-2026-48558, a critical authentication-bypass vulnerability in the SimpleHelp remote-support platform, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. The flaw (CVSS v3.1 10.0) affects SimpleHelp 5.5.15 and earlier and 6.0 pre-release builds: when OIDC authentication is enabled, the server accepts identity tokens without verifying their cryptographic signature, letting a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a token and log in as a fully privileged "Technician" — and, in some configurations, bypass multi-factor authentication entirely.

Because SimpleHelp is widely deployed by IT departments, managed service providers and help desks, a compromised server hands attackers the same reach as a legitimate technician across every managed endpoint. Blackpoint Cyber observed in-the-wild intrusions that began with confirmed exploitation of this flaw and then abused SimpleHelp's own file-transfer and remote-execution features to mass-deploy two previously unreported malware families — the TaskWeaver loader and Djinn Stealer — using an obfuscated file disguised as jQuery, pulled from a temporary Cloudflare address and run via Node.js. SimpleHelp fixed the bug in late May in versions 5.5.16 and 6.0 RC2; exposed SimpleHelp servers have grown from roughly 3,400 in January 2025 to nearly 14,000.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters — data-extortion crew running an automated PeopleSoft campaign

The prolific ShinyHunters extortion group is exploiting a critical Oracle PeopleSoft zero-day, CVE-2026-35273 (CVSS 9.8, in the Environment Management Hub), to execute code on servers with no authentication required. Per Arctic Wolf's analysis, the crew has been quietly scanning the internet and compromising vulnerable PeopleSoft deployments since at least May 27, 2026, and claims to have hit more than 300 instances across 100+ organizations using automated attack scripts rather than hand-crafted exploits. The campaign exemplifies the shift away from encryption toward pure data-theft extortion: ShinyHunters published 297 GB of Council of Europe employee data after the body missed a June 16 ransom deadline. CISA added CVE-2026-35273 to its KEV catalog in mid-June.
