Top Story

Freedom 250 at the White House — Gaethje's Stunning Upset, Gane Retains Interim HW

On June 14, 2026, the UFC held its first-ever event at the South Lawn of the White House. In the main event, Justin Gaethje shocked undisputed lightweight champion Ilia Topuria with a TKO victory at 5:00 of Round 4—a stunning upset that changed the lightweight title picture. Gaethje, a +600 underdog on many books, delivered a brutal striking assault and corner stoppage to claim the belt. In the co-main event, Ciryl Gane defeated Alex Pereira via TKO (punches) at 1:27 of Round 2 to retain the interim heavyweight championship. Pereira, who vacated the light heavyweight title to campaign at HW, fell short in his interim-title bid. All seven bouts on the card ended inside the distance. UFC CEO Dana White reported the global viewership figures as "massive."
