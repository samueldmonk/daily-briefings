Top Story

Oezdemir accepts 16-month ban after EPO turns up in blood and urine

Combat Sports Anti-Doping has announced that Volkan Oezdemir of Fribourg, Switzerland, accepted a sixteen-month period of ineligibility for a violation of the UFC Anti-Doping Policy. The former light-heavyweight title challenger tested positive for erythropoietin (EPO), a prohibited substance in the class of Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances and Mimetics.

The detection is unusually well-documented for a case of this kind: EPO was found in both urine and blood samples, collected out-of-competition on February 16, 2026 and March 5, 2026, in Le Mouret, Switzerland. His period of ineligibility runs from February 16, 2026 — the date of the first sample — and he becomes eligible to compete in UFC bouts again on June 17, 2027.

The reduction from a longer default sanction turns on how the substance reached him. Per the statement, following his most recent UFC bout in November 2025, Oezdemir was contacted unsolicited by a local pharmacist who promised he could improve his performance. Weighing all the evidence — including that Oezdemir did not seek out EPO and the level of transparency he demonstrated throughout the investigation — CSAD determined that sixteen months was the appropriate sanction. Oezdemir has publicly described it as the biggest mistake of his life.
