The Lead

Chip stocks lead a Monday snapback as Big Tech earnings loom — S&P 500 up 0.6% as of 9:35 a.m. ET

U.S. stocks advanced Monday, paring last week's slump, as chipmakers rebounded ahead of a deluge of earnings that will test whether Big Tech profits can match high expectations. Bloomberg reports the S&P 500 up 0.6% as of 9:35 a.m. in New York, the Nasdaq 100 up 1.3% led by semiconductors after its worst week in almost a month, and the Philadelphia Stock Exchange Semiconductor Index (SOX) up 3.1% after it fell into a bear market on Friday.

Other trackers give slightly different readings by capture time, and they are published separately rather than blended. TheStreet's updated July 20 coverage has the S&P 500 +0.63%, the Nasdaq +1.02%, the Dow +0.23% and the Russell 2000 −0.42%. Yahoo Finance, post-open, has the Dow +0.3%, the S&P 500 +0.5% and the Nasdaq Composite up almost 0.8%.

Geopolitics remains the live risk. The U.S. conducted a new round of airstrikes against Iran over the weekend and announced the death of a third American service member, killed in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone. Iran's state news agency IRNA, citing Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei, says Tehran has received proposals from mediators about resuming negotiations while continuing to defend itself “resolutely.” Oil eased after touching $90 a barrel.

Capital.com senior market analyst Daniela Hathorn: “Earnings season gathers pace with reports from Tesla, Alphabet, Intel and several major industrial and consumer companies. After last week's sharp rotation out of AI-related stocks, investors will be looking for evidence that earnings can continue to justify elevated valuations.”

On the Radar

  • Alphabet reports Wednesday, July 22 — consensus around $116.9B revenue and $2.90 EPS, with 49 of 54 analysts at Buy or Strong Buy and a mean target near $434. It is the first of the megacap prints this week.
  • Tesla, Intel and IBM also report this week. Wall Street has raised expectations for all four as investors look for signs that companies are monetizing AI to match their build-out spending.
  • More than 86% of S&P 500 companies that have reported so far have beaten expectations — the benchmark heading into the heart of earnings season.
  • FOMC meets July 28–29. A hold is expected this month, while futures show more than two-thirds positioned for a hike as the year-end directional move.
  • SpaceX (SPCX) rose 1.62% to $125.99 premarket. The 13th Starship test flight is rescheduled for Thursday, July 23 after a Raptor 3 engine ignition failure forced an automatic abort. SpaceX completed the largest IPO in history on June 12, raising $75 billion and briefly topping a $2 trillion valuation before an all-time intraday high of $225.64 three trading days later.
  • IBM's July 14 crash — down 25.21% to $217.07, roughly $68 billion erased, its largest one-day drop since at least 1968 — remains the backdrop to Wednesday's report. This item is six days old and is carried as context, not as today's news.
