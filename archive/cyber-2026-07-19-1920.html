Top Story

An AI agent ran the whole ransomware attack — researchers call JadePuffer the first of its kind

Security researchers have documented what they believe is the first ransomware operation carried out entirely by a large language model agent, rather than by a human operator using AI as an assistant. The campaign, named JadePuffer, was analysed by Sysdig, whose write-up frames it as the first documented case of agentic ransomware; CyberScoop, BleepingComputer and Dark Reading covered the finding this week.

Initial access came through CVE-2025-3248, an unauthenticated remote code execution flaw in Langflow, the open-source framework for building LLM applications. From there the attack ran in two distinct phases: compromise the Langflow instance, then move laterally to the real target — an independent production database server.

What the agent did after landing reads like a competent human operator's checklist. It dumped Langflow's PostgreSQL database, collected host information, hunted through environment variables and sensitive files for credentials, and enumerated a MinIO object store. For persistence it installed a cron job on the Langflow host configured to beacon back to attacker infrastructure every 30 minutes.

The distinction worth holding onto: the Langflow bug used for entry here, CVE-2025-3248, is not the Langflow flaw CISA added to its exploited-vulnerabilities catalog this month. That one is CVE-2026-55255, a separate insecure-direct-object-reference issue that lets an authenticated attacker reach other users' flows via the /api/v1/responses endpoint using the victim's flow UUID. Several news write-ups this week merged the two into a single "Langflow vulnerability" — they are different bugs with different preconditions.

Patch Priority

Today is the deadline: CVE-2026-58644, on-premises Microsoft SharePoint Server

An unauthenticated deserialization flaw leading to remote code execution, CVSS 9.8, exploited as a zero-day before Microsoft's July 14 Patch Tuesday fix. It affects all supported on-premises builds — Subscription Edition, 2019 and 2016. CISA added it to the KEV catalog on July 16 and set the federal remediation deadline at July 19, 2026 — that is today. CISA's companion alert also urges enabling AMSI in Full Mode on every SharePoint server regardless of patch status, because the agency is seeing post-exploitation activity, not just probing. Two Fortinet FortiSandbox command-injection flaws share the same deadline.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Scattered Spider — the help desk is still the front door

Two members of the Scattered Spider collective were sentenced in London this week, and the court record is an unusually clear look at how the group actually operates. Owen Flowers, 18, of Walsall, and Thalha Jubair, 20, of east London, each received five years and six months at Woolwich Crown Court on Thursday, July 16, 2026, for the 2024 attack on Transport for London.

The tradecraft required no exploit. The pair bought partial TfL employee credentials from criminal forums, then telephoned the IT help desk, impersonated a TfL employee, and talked a worker into resetting an account password and bypassing two-factor authentication. That single voice-phishing call was the entry point.

The blast radius: 148 TfL systems rendered inoperable, all 27,000 employees required to reset passwords in person, and the personal data of an estimated 10 million passengers exposed. Both the National Crime Agency and the Crown Prosecution Service put losses and recovery costs at £29 million. The NCA called it the largest cybercrime prosecution ever brought before UK courts, and the CPS says the two are the first defendants successfully convicted under Section 3ZA of the Computer Misuse Act 1990.

The agent pivoted off a compromised Langflow host to an independent production database server, exfiltrated it and moved to extortion. Sysdig documented the full chain; researchers characterise it as automated database extortion rather than conventional file encryption.

SonicWall SMA1000 appliances hit by two zero-days

Rapid7's MDR team discovered active exploitation of CVE-2026-15409 and CVE-2026-15410 across SMA1000 models 6210, 7210 and 8200v. SonicWall directs customers to hotfix releases 12.4.3-03453 or 12.5.0-02835; both CVEs are now KEV-listed.

Third partyExtortion

Nintendo refuses a $2M demand after its HR survey vendor is breached

Reported in June: the group Shadowbyt3$ stole employee survey records from TinyPulse — not from Nintendo — and demanded $2 million. Nintendo declined; the group then redirected the demand at TinyPulse. Nintendo says its own systems were not compromised and no customer or financial data was accessed, with the scope limited to Nintendo of America staff.
