The Lead

A record Dow — but chips drag the Nasdaq into the holiday break

Wall Street limped into the July 4 weekend with a split tape. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 1.14% Thursday to a record 52,900.07, with 24 of its 30 members higher and Apple (+4.46%) out front — but the S&P 500 finished essentially flat and the Nasdaq Composite fell 0.80% as semiconductors sold off for a second straight session.

The driver was a soft June jobs report. Nonfarm payrolls grew just 57,000, well below the 115,000 consensus, and May was revised down to 129,000. The unemployment rate edged down to 4.2% (below the 4.3% expected) — but largely because labor-force participation slid 0.3 point to 61.5%. Softer data revived the "higher-for-longer, but no more hikes" debate as the Fed's next decision approaches.

U.S. equity and bond markets were closed Friday, July 3, for the Independence Day holiday (observed) and remain closed over the weekend; regular trading resumes Monday, July 6.
