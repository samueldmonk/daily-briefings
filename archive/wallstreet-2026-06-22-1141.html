The Lead

Stocks mixed near records as a rebalance rattles the Nasdaq and oil eases on Iran progress — as of ~11:35 AM ET

Wall Street traded mixed in a holiday-shortened week, hovering close to record highs after last week's tech-led advance (the Nasdaq Composite rose 2.43%, the S&P 500 0.93% and the Dow 0.71%). As of roughly 11:35 AM ET the Dow was up about 0.44% and the S&P 500 up about 0.12%, while the Nasdaq Composite slipped about 0.27% — pressured as several newly-added index members sold off in their first session. Sentiment was steadied by fresh signs of de-escalation in the U.S.–Iran conflict: officials pointed to "encouraging progress" toward a final deal within 60 days, and a memorandum signed last week has the U.S. issuing waivers covering Iranian crude exports — pushing oil lower.

The session's standout was a wave of dealmaking. Apogee Therapeutics soared nearly 47% after AbbVie agreed to acquire it for $135.11 a share in cash, valuing the biotech at about $10.9 billion; Arcosa jumped roughly 7% on a $150-a-share all-cash takeover by CRH. Chipmaker Micron added about 4% ahead of its earnings later this week. Cutting the other way, fresh Nasdaq-100 entrants Rocket Lab (≈−7.6%) and CoreWeave (≈−6.7%) sank near session lows in their index debut. Investors now look to Thursday's May PCE inflation report — the key macro test after last week's hawkish Fed meeting.

Movers & Drivers

Apogee Therapeutics ≈ +47%

Rocketed after AbbVie agreed to buy the immunology drug developer for $135.11/share in cash — about $10.9B in equity value. The deal, expected to close in Q3, deepens AbbVie's respiratory and inflammation pipeline (lead asset zumilokibart, an IL-13 antibody).

Arcosa ≈ +7%

Climbed after building-materials giant CRH agreed to acquire the infrastructure-products maker in an all-cash deal valued at $150 a share — the session's second sizeable takeover.

Micron ≈ +4%

Gained ahead of its earnings report due this week as semiconductor momentum carried over; the chip complex remained a leadership group into the new session.

Lucid Group ≈ −2.1%New

Slipped in early trading after the EV maker said it would cut its U.S. workforce by about 18% as part of a broader cost-savings push — the day's notable single-name decliner outside the index reshuffle.

Rocket Lab and CoreWeave — both newly added to the Nasdaq-100 in June's quarterly reshuffle — sank near session lows in their first day in the index, weighing on the tech tape; fellow new entrant Astera Labs bucked the trend, up about 3%.
