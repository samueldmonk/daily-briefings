TOP STORY

Icarus Ransomware Weaponizes OAuth to Infiltrate Enterprise SaaS

The Icarus ransomware group executed a coordinated OAuth-based attack against Klue to gain unauthorized Salesforce access across multiple high-profile organizations including HackerOne, Gong, OneTrust, Tanium, and Huntress. The attack bypassed perimeter defenses by leveraging legitimate cloud authentication pathways, signaling a shift in ransomware group tactics toward supply-chain and OAuth abuse. No public ransom demand has been disclosed yet.

PATCH PRIORITY TODAY

CVE-2026-20230 (Cisco Unified Communications Manager SSRF) — Federal deadline: June 28, 2026 (overdue as of this run). Server-side request forgery in Cisco UCC allows unauthenticated attackers to enumerate internal services. Actively exploited in the wild. Apply patches immediately under Binding Operational Directive 26-04 (risk-based).

THREAT ACTOR SPOTLIGHT

Icarus Ransomware Group

Icarus has emerged as one of the more sophisticated operators in the ransomware ecosystem, recently demonstrating advanced OAuth exploitation skills and a focus on high-value SaaS targets. Their targeting of data aggregation and security platforms suggests a strategy to maximize the sensitivity and reach of stolen data. No known mitigations exist short of disabling OAuth integrations or implementing strict cloud access policies.

BREACHES & INCIDENTS

Texas Parks and Wildlife Vendor Breach Exposes 3M+ License Records

A cybersecurity incident at a Texas Parks and Wildlife Department vendor compromised over 3 million hunting and fishing license customer records. Exposed data includes driver's license numbers, email addresses, phone numbers, passport numbers, and residential addresses. The vendor has not disclosed whether the breach resulted from ransomware, a configuration error, or another attack vector. Affected customers are advised to monitor for phishing and identity theft.

June 2026

Amadey and StealC Malware Networks Dismantled

A coordinated law enforcement and cybersecurity industry operation, conducted in partnership with Bitdefender, Bitsight, ESET, and Microsoft, resulted in the takedown of criminal infrastructure powering the Amadey and StealC information-stealing malware networks. Recovered evidence includes 27 million stolen credentials. Victims are urged to change passwords and enable multi-factor authentication.

June 2026

npm and Go Package Supply-Chain Attack

Cybersecurity researchers identified two hijacked npm packages and a cluster of malicious Go packages designed to deploy a Python-based information stealer on compromised Windows, Linux, and macOS hosts. The packages were published to public repositories and may have been installed by developers unaware of the supply-chain compromise. Package registries have removed the malicious code; developers should audit their dependency trees.
