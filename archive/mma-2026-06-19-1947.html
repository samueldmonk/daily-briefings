Top Story

Topuria out until 2027 with orbital fractures in both eyes

Ilia Topuria suffered non-displaced orbital fractures in both eyes — the right taking the brunt — plus a broken nose in his fourth-round TKO loss to Justin Gaethje at UFC Freedom 250, the promotion's first event on the White House grounds. He lost vision in the right eye late in the fight, which is why his corner waved it off after Round 4. Because the breaks stayed in alignment, doctors say no surgery is required.

The Nevada commission handed him a six-month medical suspension plus a 60-day mandatory rest period, and he must be cleared by an oral-and-maxillofacial surgeon before returning. Reports indicate "El Matador" will not fight again until 2027. The defeat is the first of his professional career, dropping him to 17-1, and it cost him the undisputed lightweight crown.

Rankings & Business

Business & broadcast. UFC Freedom 250 (June 14) was the first UFC event staged on the White House grounds. To mark it, the promotion lifted fight-night bonuses to a record $400,000 (Fight of the Night) and $425,000 (Performance of the Night) from the usual $100,000, bankrolled by World Liberty Financial (USD1) and Crypto.com (CRO). Gaethje's combined $825,000 set a single-night record.
