Top Story

Gaethje crowns himself at the White House; Topuria out for 2026 with orbital fractures in both eyes

Justin Gaethje is the new undisputed UFC lightweight champion after stopping Ilia Topuria via corner stoppage following the fourth round at UFC Freedom 250 on June 14 — handing Topuria the first loss of his MMA career (now 17-1). On a historic card where all seven main-card bouts ended in a knockout or TKO, the UFC raised its marquee bonuses to $400,000 (Fight of the Night) and $425,000 (Performance of the Night) — presented in part by Trump-linked World Liberty Financial (paid in its USD1 stablecoin) and Crypto.com (in CRO); Gaethje swept both for an $825,000 haul, Topuria earned $400,000 for Fight of the Night, and Ciryl Gane took the other $425,000 after a second-round knockout of Alex Pereira to claim the interim heavyweight title. Topuria has since suffered orbital fractures in both eyes and, per reports, will not return in 2026; he, Gaethje and Pereira were each issued 180-day medical suspensions, pushing any rematch to late 2026 at the earliest. Topuria insists "we will have our rematch."
