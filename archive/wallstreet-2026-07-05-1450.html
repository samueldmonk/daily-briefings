The Lead

Dow storms to a record before the long weekend; a tech drag pins the S&P flat

With U.S. markets shut Friday for Independence Day and closed through the weekend, Thursday, July 2 stands as the tape's last word — and it belonged to the Dow. The blue-chip index climbed 1.14% (about 595 points) to a record close of 52,900.07, touching an intraday high of 52,903.85.

Beneath the surface it was a split session. A softer-than-expected June jobs report — payrolls up just 57,000 versus roughly 115,000 expected — revived hopes the Fed stays patient, but a renewed slide in semiconductors and a drop in Tesla left the S&P 500 essentially flat (7,483.24) and dragged the Nasdaq Composite down about 0.8% to 25,832.67. Trading resumes Monday, July 6.

Movers & Drivers

Rivian

▲ ~+13%

Thursday's standout gainer surged nearly 15% in early trading — up about 13% to $19.38 — after raising its 2026 delivery guidance to 65,000–70,000 (from 62,000–67,000). The EV maker delivered 12,194 vehicles in Q2 and produced 12,613 at its Normal, Illinois plant, citing stronger-than-expected demand.

Tech & Chips

▼ NDX −1.61%

The Nasdaq-100 fell 1.61% — down as much as 2% intraday — as semiconductors slid for a second straight day and megacap tech retreated. Meta dropped 3.78%. Technology was the session's clear drag on the broad market.

Tesla

▼ ~−7%

Tesla sank about 7% even after easily surpassing its second-quarter vehicle-delivery estimates — one of the most-cited drags on the Nasdaq, keeping the tech-heavy benchmarks red even as the Dow set a record.

June Jobs Report

driver

Payrolls rose just 57,000 in June versus the 115,000 Dow Jones consensus, with May revised down to 129,000; unemployment ticked down to 4.2% (below the 4.3% forecast) as labor-force participation fell 0.3 point to 61.5% — soft enough to keep the Fed patient.

Sector Heat — Live

On Thursday, technology was the clear laggard as semiconductors slid for a second straight day and megacap tech retreated — the Nasdaq-100 fell 1.61% while Tesla sank ~7% and Meta lost 3.78%; the record Dow close reflected a rotation into blue chips (Apple +4.46%, McDonald's +3.34%) even as the tech-heavy Nasdaq finished lower.
