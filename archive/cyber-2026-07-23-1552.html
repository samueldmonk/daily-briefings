Top Story

Romania races to restore its wiped land registry as the attacker's trail points to an access broker

Romania is still working to restore its national land registry more than a week after the e-Terra platform of the National Agency for Cadastre and Land Registration (ANCPI) was breached on July 14. The attacker, who goes by ByteToBreach, got in with valid credentials — not an exploit — conducted internal reconnaissance, and after a failed extortion attempt destroyed both production systems and backups: ANCPI had configured its backups under the same permission system as production, so the same stolen admin credentials reached the backup servers too. An offline backup has enabled partial recovery, averting a longer-term national crisis, but the wipe halted property transactions across the country — notaries could not issue land extracts, authenticate sales or register mortgages — and the actor claims access to citizen data, internal databases and copies of the agency's GitLab source code, with stolen records reportedly offered for sale online. Romanian investigators believe ByteToBreach is a financially motivated initial access broker rather than a state-backed group, and reporting this week says the persona has been linked by researchers at Kela to an alleged cybercriminal based in Algeria. It is the latest in a string of attacks on land-registry agencies — similar incidents have been reported in Poland, Slovakia, Greece, Morocco, Russia and Ukraine.

Patch Priority
⚠ Most urgent action today

Update WordPress to 6.9.5 (6.9.x) or 7.0.2 (7.0.x) now. The wp2shell chain (CVE-2026-63030 + CVE-2026-60137) is actively exploited and both CVEs entered CISA KEV on July 21; confirm forced auto-updates completed on every site. Next: on-premises SharePoint remains under active exploitation — patch CVE-2026-58644 (CVSS 9.8), whose federal deadline is already 4 days past, and rotate machine keys, since stolen keys let attackers forge valid tokens even after patching.

Threat Spotlight
Threat actor

Storm-2603 · Warlock ransomware

Microsoft-tracked Storm-2603 — assessed with moderate confidence as a China-based actor — is a hybrid financial/espionage group behind the on-premises SharePoint exploitation wave. Reporting this week ties it to the actively exploited SharePoint RCE CVE-2026-45659 (now in CISA KEV). Its playbook: plant web shells for persistence, steal SharePoint machine keys and dump credentials (Mimikatz), move laterally with PsExec/Impacket, disable endpoint defenses, then deploy Warlock (and, at times, LockBit) ransomware via Group Policy. Because the keys survive patching, defenders must patch and rotate machine keys.

Breaches & Incidents
NewChina-nexus

JadeProx espionage campaign exposed

An exposed Alibaba Cloud server in Singapore revealed a China-nexus operation Group-IB tracks as JadeProx, which used a previously undocumented Windows loader — TriBack Loader, delivered via DLL sideloading and a fake Claude installer — against government, healthcare and education targets across Asia and Latin America. Artifacts pointed to active intrusions at a Vietnamese public hospital's medical-imaging system and Malaysia's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, plus a spear-phishing package addressed to the National Congress of Honduras. The server, found in mid-April, was offline by the time Group-IB published on July 23.

Breach expanded

Conduent

The business-services giant's breach has expanded sharply: healthcare breach reporting as of July places the affected population at more than 62.2 million individuals, making it one of the largest disclosed incidents of 2026 so far.

Data breach

Ernst & Young (EY)

EY disclosed a breach of a third-party IT support platform used for client work. An unauthorized party had access between Mar 28 and Apr 12; a Vermont AG filing dated July 16 says exposed data may include Social Security numbers and financial account codes. EY reports no malware or ransomware was involved and is offering 24 months of Experian monitoring.

Data theft

Craneware

The Edinburgh-based healthcare financial-software vendor — whose billing and revenue-cycle software serves roughly 2,000 U.S. hospitals — disclosed on July 20 that attackers exfiltrated files along with employee data and a subset of customer and partner records.

Edge exploit

SonicWall SMA1000

CISA added SonicWall Secure Mobile Access flaws to KEV after confirmed exploitation: CVE-2026-15409, an unauthenticated SSRF rated a maximum CVSS 10.0, can be chained with code-injection bug CVE-2026-15410 to achieve full remote code execution on the appliance. The federal remediation deadline of July 17 has already passed.
