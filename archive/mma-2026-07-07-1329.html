Top Story

Conor McGregor returns after five years to headline UFC 329 against Max Holloway

On Saturday, July 11, former two-division champion Conor McGregor (22-6) walks to the Octagon for the first time since 2021 — his last bout was a July 2021 TKO loss to Dustin Poirier after he broke his leg — to face ex-featherweight king Max Holloway in the UFC 329 main event at T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas.

It's a rematch of their August 2013 meeting in Boston, which McGregor won by unanimous decision. The main event anchors International Fight Week and streams on Paramount+ with the main card at 9 p.m. ET.

McGregor is a +185 underdog; former featherweight champion Max Holloway is a −225 favorite — an implied win probability near 69% — though the line has drifted steadily toward McGregor through fight camp on heavy action for the returning Irishman.
