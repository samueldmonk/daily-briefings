Top Story

Romania's land registry wiped: ByteToBreach destroyed ANCPI systems — and the backups too

Romania's national land-registry agency ANCPI is still reeling from a breach of its e-Terra platform confirmed July 14. The actor, tracked as ByteToBreach, got in with valid credentials — no exploit — and after a failed extortion attempt destroyed production systems and backup servers. Notaries have been unable to issue extracts, authenticate property sales or register mortgages, and stolen records have reportedly been offered for sale online (Cybernews/The Record/Rescana).

Reporting this week added a brutal detail: the backup environment shared production's permission system, so the same stolen admin credentials that ran the wipe also formatted the backups (Cybernews). An offline backup has enabled partial recovery as the agency races to restore service (The Record). Investigators describe ByteToBreach as a financially motivated initial-access broker, suspected to be Algeria-based, with a claimed victim pattern spanning Poland, Slovakia, Greece, Morocco, Russia and Ukraine.

Defender takeaway: separate backup credentials and permissions from production, and keep at least one restore path offline — this incident is the textbook case for both.

Breaches & Incidents

Tata Electronics — 200K files posted Ransomware

Tata Electronics confirmed a June cybersecurity incident after the World Leaks extortion group published more than 200,000 alleged company files — a cache reportedly of 204,341 files totaling about 630GB (Bright Defense).

EY — third-party platform breach Breach

Ernst & Young disclosed via a Vermont AG filing (Jul 16) that a third-party IT support platform was accessed between Mar 28 and Apr 12, exposing SSNs and financial account codes; affected people get 24 months of Experian monitoring. No ransomware reported.

Craneware — healthcare fin-software Breach

The Edinburgh-based healthcare financial-software firm serving roughly 2,000 US hospitals disclosed a cybersecurity incident on Jul 20; scope of data impact still emerging.

SonicWall SMA1000 exploitation Exploited

CVE-2026-15409 (CVSS 10.0 SSRF) and companion CVE-2026-15410 are being exploited against SMA1000 appliances; both were added to CISA's KEV with a Jul 17 federal deadline (SecurityWeek/CISA).

Conduent fallout widens Breach

Conduent's breach disclosures feed a healthcare breach-reporting tally that now stands above 62.2 million individuals (Bright Defense).

JadeProx: China-nexus espionage APT

An exposed Alibaba Cloud server (found mid-April, offline by Jul 23) revealed a China-nexus operation Group-IB tracks as JadeProx: TriBack Loader via DLL sideloading and a fake Claude installer. Victims include a Vietnamese public hospital's medical-imaging system and Malaysia's MFA, with a spear-phish addressed to Honduras' National Congress (The Hacker News, Jul 23).
