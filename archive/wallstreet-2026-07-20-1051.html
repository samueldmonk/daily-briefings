The Lead

Alphabet jumps on a "Frozen v2" chip report as chip stocks swing back upward, two days ahead of its own earnings

Stocks are higher in Monday morning trade with semiconductors leading the recovery. The Associated Press framed the session as "Wall Street holds steadier, as stocks of chip companies and other winners of the artificial-intelligence boom swing back upward." On the numbers, TheStreet's updated July 20 session coverage has the S&P 500 +0.63%, the Nasdaq +1.02%, the Dow +0.23% and the Russell 2000 −0.42%; Yahoo Finance, in its post-open update, had the Dow up 0.3%, the S&P 500 up 0.5% and the Nasdaq Composite up almost 0.8%. Both readings appear here with their source, because neither carried a clock time we could verify.

The single biggest identified driver is Alphabet, whose shares climbed about 3% on Monday after The Information reported that the company is developing a new server chip, internally dubbed "Frozen v2," designed to run its Gemini models more efficiently (CNBC). One separate movers read put GOOG up 3.30%.

What makes the chip unusual is that it would permanently embed parts of Gemini's architecture directly into the silicon. Google engineers project it could serve between six and ten times more tokens per unit of power than the company's newest TPUs. The trade-off is flexibility: the chip would only work with future Gemini models if Google keeps the same underlying architecture. Google reportedly treats Frozen v2 partly as a trial run and does not plan to produce it at TPU scale, targeting 2028 for deployment. The stated motivation is a major internal compute shortage — one that has fuelled tensions inside the company and reportedly forced Google Cloud to turn away outside business.

The timing matters because Alphabet's Q2 print is due Wednesday, July 22. Analysts are looking for roughly $116.9 billion in revenue and $2.90 EPS, with earnings and revenue growth expected above 20% and Google Cloud's growth rate — 63% year over year in the first quarter — the central question. Positioning is lopsided going in: 49 of 54 analysts rate the stock Buy or Strong Buy with zero Sells and a mean target near $434, against a recent close of $346.77 after a $4.67 billion EU antitrust fine, Digital Markets Act data-sharing compliance orders and delays to the Gemini 3.5 Pro model.

Alphabet is the opening act of a heavy week — Tesla, Intel and IBM also report — and the bar is already high. More than 86% of the S&P 500 companies that have reported so far this season have beaten expectations. "After last week's sharp rotation out of AI-related stocks, investors will be looking for evidence that earnings can continue to justify elevated valuations," said Daniela Hathorn, senior market analyst at Capital.com, who added that markets "are likely to remain highly headline-driven throughout the week." TheStreet Pro contributor James "Rev Shark" DePorre put it more simply: after last week's volatility in chip and technology stocks, "we are entering the heart of earnings season."

Geopolitics stayed in the frame without setting the direction. U.S. Central Command announced a new round of strikes early Monday, the ninth consecutive night of attacks on Iran, and Iran retaliated by bombing U.S. allies in the region, primarily Kuwait. TheStreet, citing CNN, reported a third American service member killed in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone. Cutting the other way, Iranian state agency IRNA — citing Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei, whom TheStreet also renders "Esmail Baghaei" in the same article — said Iran has received proposals from mediators about resuming talks and would pursue them if they align with national interests, while continuing to defend itself "resolutely." Oil reversed on that: crude eased after touching $90 a barrel.

Up 4.5–5%AI hardware

AMD — Helios lands in Azure

AMD launched Helios, its first rack-scale AI system, and Microsoft said it will deploy Helios racks in its Azure data centers, joining early customers Meta, OpenAI and Oracle. Helios is a 72-GPU rack pairing Instinct MI455X GPUs with 6th Gen EPYC CPUs, Pensando networking and ROCm, shipping later this year. AMD told CNBC it plans to book tens of billions in data-center AI revenue starting in 2027, the majority from Helios. The stock was +4.5% premarket; one headline put the move at 5%. Published targets seen this run: Cantor Fitzgerald $700, Wells Fargo fair value $615.

Up 1.62%Space

SpaceX (SPCX) — Starship back on the pad Thursday

Rose 1.62% to $125.99 in premarket trading. The 13th Starship test flight is rescheduled for Thursday, July 23 after a Raptor 3 engine ignition failure forced an automatic abort. SpaceX completed the largest IPO in history on June 12, raising $75 billion and briefly pushing its valuation above $2 trillion; three trading days later the stock hit an all-time intraday high of $225.64. It is also moving to rent out unused compute from its Colossus data-center complex.

On the Radar

  • Alphabet Q2 earnings — Wednesday, July 22. Consensus near $116.9B revenue and $2.90 EPS, with Google Cloud's growth rate and any read-through on the compute shortage behind Frozen v2 the things to watch.
  • Tesla, Intel and IBM report this week. IBM carries the most baggage: on July 14 the stock fell 25.21% to close at $217.07, erasing roughly $68 billion of market value in a session — its largest one-day drop since at least 1968. Preliminary Q2 revenue of $17.2 billion missed the $17.86 billion FactSet consensus, and operating EPS of $2.93 came in eight cents short of the $3.01 estimate. Oppenheimer cut the stock from Outperform to Perform and scrapped its $350 target after software revenue grew just 5% against the 12% it had modelled; HSBC went to reduce with a $191 target, while Morgan Stanley lifted its target to $293 from $267 at equal weight.
  • FOMC meets July 28–29. Policymakers are expected to leave rates unchanged while preserving the option to tighten.
  • Starship's 13th test flight — Thursday, July 23, after a Raptor 3 ignition failure aborted the previous attempt.
  • The Iran track cuts both ways. A ninth straight night of U.S. strikes and Iranian retaliation against regional allies keeps an energy-price risk premium live; IRNA's confirmation that Tehran has received mediator proposals on resuming talks is the offsetting headline. Crude has already given back its push above $90.
