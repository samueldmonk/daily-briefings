Top Story

UFC and Meta scrap the media panel for an Elo-based ranking system

The UFC has rolled out the Meta UFC Rankings, retiring the long-criticized media-voting panel in favor of a statistical, Elo-based model built with Meta that updates automatically after every event — the first automated rankings published June 23. Officials stress the system is driven by math, not AI: a fighter's rating is shaped chiefly by wins and the quality of opposition, with finishes adding a small boost.

The launch arrives without a pound-for-pound list, which the UFC says needs a separate model. The first automated run reshuffled the divisions — 27 fighters, including three former champions (Robert Whittaker among them), lost a ranking — drawing a polarized reaction from fans. It is the biggest structural change to how the promotion ranks its fighters in over a decade.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement: the Meta UFC Rankings replaced the media panel this week, auto-updating after events; the system launched without a pound-for-pound list and its first automated run (June 23) cost 27 fighters a ranking, including three former champions — Robert Whittaker among them.

Business & broadcast: UFC Freedom 250 at the White House (June 14) was a Paramount+ ratings record — roughly 8.2 million average viewers (about 7 million in the U.S.) and around 17 million total — in year one of TKO's seven-year, ~$7.7 billion deal that moved the UFC to Paramount+ with no pay-per-view; the event reportedly cost about $60 million to stage. Post-fight performance bonuses now stand at $100,000, as seen at UFC Vegas 119, which paid four.
