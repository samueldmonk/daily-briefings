The Lead

A holiday pause after Thursday's chip-led rebound

With Wall Street dark for Juneteenth, the standing read is Thursday's broad advance. The S&P 500 closed +1.08% at 7,500.58, the Nasdaq Composite +1.91% at 26,517.93, the Dow +0.14% (+72) at 51,564.70, and the small-cap Russell 2000 led, +2.12% at 2,979.77 — a recovery from Wednesday's slide after Kevin Warsh's first Fed meeting landed hawkish.

The session's headline was Intel, up 10.64% to a record $133.99, after President Trump posted on Truth Social that Apple would build chips with Intel in the U.S. — a claim neither company has confirmed (Intel said it would not comment). The note lifted the whole chip complex: the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index climbed 6.3% to a record, with AMD +4.86%, Broadcom +4.70% and Nvidia +2.95%. Cutting the other way, Accenture cratered roughly 18% — its worst day on record — after cutting its full-year revenue-growth outlook.
