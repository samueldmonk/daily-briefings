The Lead

Chips tumble on Samsung's blowout as oil jumps on a Hormuz tanker strike — the Dow clings to its record

As of late morning ET. A record quarter from Samsung Electronics did what disappointing results usually do: it triggered a semiconductor selloff. Samsung's preliminary Q2 operating profit came in around $58 billion — up nearly 19-fold from a year earlier and ahead of estimates — but the stock fell roughly 7% on profit-taking after a huge run and fresh worries about AI-spending plans, and the anxiety rippled straight across the chip complex.

Micron sank about 13%, Applied Materials — which counts Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron and Intel among its customers — dropped roughly 10%, Intel fell about 9% and AMD lost around 7%, with KLA, Marvell and Broadcom also lower. That dragged the Nasdaq Composite down close to 1% and left the S&P 500 hovering near flat, though the Dow held modestly higher, extending its record run thanks to its lighter chip weighting.

Oil added a second worry: WTI crude climbed to a one-week high near $69 a barrel after Iran's Revolutionary Guard fired on two commercial vessels near the Strait of Hormuz — including a Qatari state-owned LNG carrier struck by a projectile while exiting the strait — reviving supply fears. Traders also braced for Wednesday's June FOMC minutes, while SpaceX made its debut in the Nasdaq-100.
