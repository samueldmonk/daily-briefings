McGregor returns: Conor McGregor vs. Max Holloway 2 set for UFC 329 on July 11

Conor McGregor is booked to headline UFC 329 on Saturday, July 11 at T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas — the centerpiece of International Fight Week — rematching Max Holloway in a welterweight bout. It will be McGregor's first appearance since he broke his leg at UFC 264 in July 2021, a nearly five-year absence, and marks Holloway's debut at welterweight. The two first met back in August 2013 as featherweights, a fight McGregor won by unanimous decision. The card also features a lightweight co-main between Paddy Pimblett and Benoît Saint-Denis, streaming on Paramount+ with no pay-per-view.

Rankings & Business
  • Viewership: UFC Freedom 250 (the White House card, June 14) drew 17 million total viewers across the U.S. and Latin America on Paramount+, averaging 8.2 million (7M U.S., 1.2M Latin America) over the seven-bout card — the biggest live exclusive event in Paramount+ history, per Nielsen and Adobe Analytics.
