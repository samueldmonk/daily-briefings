Top Story

Du Plessis and Usman — two former champions, one middleweight main event in Oklahoma City

The UFC returns to Oklahoma City tonight for the first time in almost 10 years, and for its debut event at the Paycom Center. Former middleweight champion Dricus du Plessis faces former welterweight champion Kamaru Usman at 185 pounds in a 12-fight card. Prelims begin at 5 p.m. ET and the main card at 8 p.m. ET, both on Paramount+.

The betting market has moved toward Usman through fight week. Du Plessis opened as steep as −245 at some sportsbooks and sits at −205 at DraftKings, with Usman at +170. It is du Plessis's first appearance since losing the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev at UFC 319, and Usman's first bout in more than a year as he moves up to middleweight.

In the co-main event, middleweight contenders Jared Cannonier (+310) and Christian Leroy Duncan (−395) meet. No results are available at publication time — this edition went out before the card began.

Prospect Watch

ProspectNew

Ezra Elliott (7-0) — UFC debut tonight

One of Sean O'Malley's main training partners at Red Hawk Academy, also training out of The MMA Lab in Glendale, Arizona. The 26-year-old holds a 100 percent finish rate — five submissions and two knockouts — and is coming off a first-round knockout of Ashton Caniglia in May. He missed weight at 147.5 pounds, a pound and a half over the featherweight non-title limit; the bout proceeds at catchweight with both fighters fined a percentage of their purses.

ProspectNew

Damien Anderson (5-0) — UFC debut tonight

A decorated grappler who was part of the B-Team Jiu-Jitsu squad alongside Craig Jones and Nick Rodriguez before the stable disbanded. He has spent most of his MMA career under the Fury FC banner, winning all five professional fights with a 100 percent finish rate — four submissions and one knockout.

Around the Sport

  • Weigh-in misses in Oklahoma City. Promotional newcomer Ezra Elliott came in at 147.5 pounds, a pound and a half over the featherweight non-title limit, and Chase Hooper was also among the fighters who missed weight. Elliott's bout proceeds at catchweight with a purse deduction.
  • A first for the venue. Tonight is the UFC's debut event at the Paycom Center and its return to Oklahoma City after nearly a decade away.
  • Elliott vs. Anderson was a late addition. The two undefeated newcomers were booked on short notice, giving the prelims an undefeated-versus-undefeated matchup with two 100-percent finish rates on the line.

Rankings & Business

  • Record gate. UFC 329 set the highest gate in company history at $26.4 million.
  • Bonus outlay at UFC 329. Pimblett's $100,000 submission bonus sat alongside eight separate $25,000 Performance of the Night awards and a Fight of the Night for Royval–Kavanagh.
  • Divisional note. Tonight's main event pairs a former middleweight champion with a former welterweight champion moving up in weight — a result at 185 has direct implications for a division whose champion, Sean Strickland, has yet to defend the belt he won in May.
