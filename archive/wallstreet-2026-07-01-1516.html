The Lead

Stocks broaden higher into the afternoon even as chips lag — as of ~3:10 PM ET

Wall Street firmed as the session wore on, a day after the major indexes wrapped their strongest quarter since 2020. As of roughly 3:10 PM ET the Dow Jones Industrial Average was up about 0.6% and back near a fresh intraday record, the S&P 500 had turned higher (about +0.15%) and the small-cap Russell 2000 led with a gain near 0.6% — with communication-services and financial stocks doing the lifting and roughly two-thirds of NYSE issues advancing. The Nasdaq Composite remained the laggard, off about half a percent, as investors kept dumping semiconductors.

The marquee move belonged to Meta, which jumped as much as ~10% intraday on a Bloomberg report that it will sell excess AI computing capacity through a new cloud unit — internally "Meta Compute" — lifting fellow mega-cap tech such as Microsoft (~+3%) and Apple (~+2%) but pressuring rival "neocloud" providers such as CoreWeave (~−11%) and Nebius (~−12%). The drag came from chipmakers giving back some of a blistering first-half run. Traders also parsed a cooler June ADP payrolls report (+98,000 vs. ~113,000 expected), a softer ISM manufacturing read (PMI 53.3; prices-paid down to 73.0 from 82.1), falling oil, and remarks from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh at the ECB's Sintra forum, where he said inflation risks have eased in recent weeks while stressing the Fed's 2% goal.

Movers & Drivers

Meta ▲ ~10%

Led the tape, up as much as ~10% intraday, on a report it will launch a cloud business — "Meta Compute" — to sell surplus AI compute and model access, taking on AWS, Azure and Google Cloud; the halo lifted Microsoft (~+3%) and Apple (~+2%).

Mega-cap

Corning ▼ ~9%

Fell roughly 9% (−$22.66 to ~$232.78) to rank among the day's biggest S&P 500 decliners as AI-infrastructure and chip-adjacent names sold off.

Biggest loser

Memory & chips ▼

Semiconductors led the retreat as investors booked profits: Micron and SanDisk each sank about 8%, while Nvidia slipped roughly 2%.

Semis

Chip equipment ▼

Toolmakers were swept up in the rout: Teradyne fell about 6%, Lam Research roughly 6% and KLA near 6% as the AI trade rotated away from silicon.

Semis

Nike ▼ ~3%

Slipped despite beating on earnings (EPS 72¢, a 59¢ beat; revenue ~$11B) after executives warned that sales weakness — particularly in China — could persist.

Earnings

Software & staples ▲

Money rotated out of chipmakers: Palantir climbed about 5.5%, while defensive consumer-staples names outperformed — General Mills (~+6%), Conagra (~+5%) and Campbell's (~+4%).
