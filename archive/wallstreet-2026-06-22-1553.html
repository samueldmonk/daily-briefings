The Lead

Megacap tech and gold miners sink the S&P and Nasdaq while the Dow stands alone — as of ~3:40 PM ET

Wall Street split into two tapes Monday afternoon. As of about 3:40 PM ET, in the final stretch before the close, the Dow held a gain of roughly 0.4% (about +200 points), while the S&P 500 slipped about 0.3% — with 299 of its 503 members actually higher — and the Nasdaq Composite fell roughly 1%, dragged by a selloff in the megacap technology names. Alphabet tumbled about 7%, the single biggest drag on both indexes, amid AI-spending anxiety as combined 2026 hyperscaler capital expenditure is now seen topping ~$452 billion, stoking fears that AI monetization lags the buildout; reports of AI-talent departures (Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer to OpenAI, DeepMind Nobel laureate John Jumper to Anthropic) added pressure. Amazon (≈−4%) and other megacaps (Meta, Microsoft) were also lower, and Moderna fell about 9.5%, the S&P 500's steepest decliner. Newly public SpaceX dropped about 11% after confirming its first bond sale — a roughly $20 billion offering to repay a bridge loan and fund AI and data-center projects, dragging Virgin Galactic (≈−11%) and Rocket Lab (≈−8%) with it.

A sharp reversal in precious metals hammered the gold complex: with bullion retreating to just above $4,000 an ounce, Alamos Gold sank about 14% — the market's worst large-cap performer — and Gold Fields fell about 10%. Dealmaking and biotech supplied the upside. Apogee Therapeutics soared nearly 47% after AbbVie agreed to acquire it for $135.11 a share in cash, valuing the biotech at about $10.9 billion, while Definium Therapeutics rocketed about 55% — the day's biggest percentage gainer. Chip and hardware names bucked the tape: Super Micro Computer jumped about 15%, Sandisk rose about 7% and Micron added about 5% ahead of its earnings later this week, and big banks firmed (JPMorgan and Bank of America ≈+2%), while EV maker Lucid slipped about 2% after saying it would cut roughly 18% of its U.S. workforce. Oil eased — WTI down about 3% near $74, its lowest since early March — as U.S. and Iranian negotiators, with Qatari and Pakistani mediation, agreed a roadmap toward a final deal within 60 days. Investors look ahead to Thursday's May PCE inflation report, the key macro test after last week's hawkish Fed.

Movers & Drivers

The day's biggest percentage gainer among larger stocks. The biotech rocketed after positive late-stage trial data for its depression therapy, topping a leaderboard unusually dominated by biotech names and fueling takeover speculation.
