Top Story

UFC and Meta scrap the media panel for an Elo-based ranking system

The UFC has rolled out the Meta UFC Rankings, beginning June 20 — retiring the long-criticized media-voting panel in favor of a statistical, Elo-based model built with Meta that updates automatically after every event. Officials stress the active system is math, not AI (machine learning was used to design it, but the final model doesn't run AI): a fighter's rating is shaped chiefly by wins and the quality of opposition, with activity and decay factored in.

The launch arrives without a pound-for-pound list, which the UFC says needs a separate model to build. The first machine-calculated update reshuffled the divisions and knocked several big names down the order, drawing a polarized reaction from fans — the biggest structural change to how the promotion ranks its fighters in over a decade. For now the UFC says it will keep referencing the old media rankings for matchmaking while the new system beds in.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement: the Elo-based Meta UFC Rankings replaced the media panel beginning June 20, auto-updating after events; the system launched without a pound-for-pound list, and its first machine-calculated run reshuffled the divisions and slid several notable names down the order.

Business & broadcast: UFC Freedom 250 at the White House (June 14) became the most-watched live event ever on Paramount+ — roughly 8.2 million average viewers (about 7 million in the U.S. per ESPN) and around 17 million total reach across the U.S. and Latin America, topping the prior record (UFC 324). It came in year one of TKO's seven-year Paramount+ deal (reported at about $7.7 billion, ~$1.1B/yr average) that moved the UFC to streaming with no pay-per-view. Performance bonuses stand at $100,000 (four paid at UFC Vegas 119).
