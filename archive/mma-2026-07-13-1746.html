Top Story

Holloway–McGregor 2 ends in 69 heartbreaking seconds: McGregor's knee gives out, Holloway takes the TKO at UFC 329

Nearly thirteen years after their first meeting, the rematch lasted barely a minute. Conor McGregor opened UFC 329's main event Saturday at T-Mobile Arena with a jumping kick — and his knee gave way on the landing. The returning Irishman slipped to the canvas twice as Max Holloway measured him; when they stood, Holloway landed one kick, McGregor took an unsteady step back, and referee Mike Beltran waved it off at 1:09 of Round 1 — a TKO via knee injury (UFC.com). "A wild night of action was working to a perfect crescendo but instead ended in unexpected fashion," is how UFC.com's recap put it, with the arena shifting from raucous excitement to stunned disbelief.

The anticlimax capped an otherwise violent card: eleven of fourteen bouts ended inside the distance, and Dana White said the event's $25 million gate beat the Sphere's $22 million as the biggest in UFC history. As for what's next — UFC.com's E. Spencer Kyte writes that a matchup between Holloway and newly crowned lightweight champion Justin Gaethje "is top of mind right now," though nothing is booked.
