The Lead

Stocks edge higher as chips steady before Micron's after-the-bell earnings; Alphabet to join the Dow (as of ~12:05 PM ET, June 24)

U.S. stocks are trying to claw back ground Wednesday after a two-session, AI-driven selloff. As of about 12:05 PM ET, the S&P 500 is up about 0.35%, the Nasdaq Composite about 0.62% and the Russell 2000 about 0.41%, while the Dow slips about 0.17%. The steadier tone follows a rebound in battered memory and semiconductor names — Micron rose roughly 4% in pre-market after closing down about 13% Tuesday and traded little-changed-to-higher into midday; the memory maker, up more than 250% year to date, reports fiscal third-quarter results after today's close, the week's marquee corporate catalyst. The session leads off Tuesday's losses, when the Nasdaq Composite closed down 2.21% at 25,587.04, the S&P 500 fell 1.44% to 7,365.46, the Dow finished essentially flat (−0.09%) at 51,666.84 and the Russell 2000 slipped 0.78% to 2,980.82, dropping back below 3,000.

In a notable index shake-up, Alphabet (GOOGL) will join the Dow Jones Industrial Average on Monday, June 29, replacing Verizon — S&P Dow Jones Indices says the change broadens the price-weighted blue-chip gauge's exposure to AI, cloud and digital advertising, putting Google's parent alongside Nvidia, Amazon, Apple and Microsoft; Honeywell stays in the Dow as it spins off its aerospace arm. Alphabet shares rose about 0.5% on the news. The morning's biggest single-stock story is well away from the AI trade: Wendy's rocketed about 24% after appointing former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer (a role he held under current Wendy's CEO Bob Wright) and becoming the latest target of a retail "meme-stock" short squeeze — it traded more shares pre-market than Micron or Intel. Sunrun jumped about 19–22% and Builders FirstSource added about 10%, while on the downside Hertz tumbled more than 20% after launching a roughly $100 million stock offering, Cerebras Systems extended its post-earnings drop, and Casey's General Stores fell about 7%. Take-Two kept climbing — BTIG initiated a Buy with a $290 target as the stock rallied from $206 to about $247 ahead of Grand Theft Auto VI pre-sales. Macro and rates stay in the frame: the 10-year Treasury yield hovers near 4.5% and the Fed-funds target sits at 3.50–3.75% after last week's hold, with markets pricing at least one more 2026 hike (September odds around 50%) — Bank of America this week went further, calling for three quarter-point hikes in 2026 under new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh. Oil keeps sliding, with WTI below $71 — its lowest since before the U.S.–Iran conflict (Brent ~$77) — after Washington and Tehran agreed a 60-day roadmap toward a peace deal and the U.S. issued a 60-day license letting Iran sell oil internationally; gold tumbled more than 3% to below $4,000, a roughly seven-month low. Beyond Micron tonight, the week's key macro test is Thursday's May PCE inflation report.

Movers & Drivers

Wendy's — meme squeeze + new CFO ▲ ≈ +24%

The burger chain rocketed about 24% on a retail "meme-stock" short squeeze plus the appointment of former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer — a role he previously held under current Wendy's CEO Bob Wright. It traded more shares pre-market than Micron or Intel. The morning's biggest single-stock mover.

Micron — steadies before tonight's report ▲ ≈ +4% pre-mkt

Micron rose roughly 4% pre-market — recovering part of Tuesday's ~13% slide — and traded little-changed-to-higher into midday ahead of its fiscal-Q3 earnings after today's close. FactSet consensus is about $20.83 adjusted EPS on roughly $35.9B revenue — up sharply from $1.91 a year earlier — with the company's own guidance at $33.5B, a record gross margin near 81% and HBM capacity sold out through 2026. Wall Street keeps a "Strong Buy."

Alphabet — joining the Dow ▲ ≈ +0.5%New

S&P Dow Jones Indices will add Alphabet (GOOGL) to the 30-stock Dow before Monday's open, replacing Verizon — broadening the price-weighted index's tilt toward AI, cloud and digital advertising and seating Google's parent beside Nvidia, Amazon, Apple and Microsoft. Honeywell stays in the Dow as it spins off its aerospace unit. Shares ticked up about 0.5%.

Decliners — Hertz, Cerebras, Casey's ▼New

Hertz Global tumbled more than 20% after launching a roughly $100 million common-stock offering that dilutes holders. AI-chip maker Cerebras Systems extended its post-IPO, post-earnings slide on softer gross-margin guidance, and Casey's General Stores fell about 7% — the notable early laggards alongside the still-mixed chip names. Sunrun (+19–22%) and Builders FirstSource (+10%) led the gainers.
