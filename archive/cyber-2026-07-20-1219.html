Top Story

SonicWall SMA1000 zero-days chained into ransomware footholds

Two vulnerabilities in SonicWall's SMA1000 series secure-access gateways are under active exploitation, and the pairing is what makes them dangerous. CVE-2026-15409 is a server-side request forgery flaw in the SMA 1000 Work Place interface, rated CVSS 10.0, that lets a remote and unauthenticated attacker force the appliance to make network requests to locations of the attacker's choosing. CVE-2026-15410 is a code-injection flaw in the Appliance Management Console — the administrative interface used to configure the appliance, manage users, set access policies and monitor sessions — rated CVSS 7.2 and nominally requiring authentication.

The chaining is the new detail confirmed this run: analysis published since the initial disclosure states the two flaws may have been chained together, which makes the exploitation path possible without authentication despite 15410's own requirement. Affected models named in this run's coverage include the SMA6210, SMA7210 and SMA8200v. SonicWall has confirmed the zero-day exploitation and has released a hotfix for both.

Rapid7's MDR team discovered the activity, and its telemetry ties it to a threat actor connected to the Inc ransomware-as-a-service group, which used both flaws as zero-days to burrow into multiple enterprise networks, sweep credentials and stage ransomware. The reason patching alone is not sufficient: after gaining a foothold the actor extracts high-value credentials, active session databases and TOTP multi-factor authentication seed configurations, which preserve access even after the appliance is fixed. Anyone who ran an exposed SMA1000 before the hotfix should treat credential rotation and MFA re-enrolment as part of the remediation, not an optional extra.

Patch Priority

Overdue — act now

SonicWall SMA1000: CVE-2026-15409 & CVE-2026-15410 — CISA deadline was July 17

CISA added both CVEs to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on July 14, 2026 with a federal remediation deadline of Friday, July 17, 2026 — a three-day window, assigned per-CVE under the risk-based BOD 26-04 rather than the older flat three-week rule. That deadline is now —. SonicWall's hotfix is available. This is the single most urgent item on the board: maximum severity, unauthenticated, confirmed ransomware use.

Secondary but also past due: CVE-2026-58644, the CVSS 9.8 Microsoft SharePoint Server deserialization flaw exploited as a zero-day before patches existed, was added to KEV on July 16 with a July 19 deadline — now —.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Ransomware-as-a-serviceEdge devices

Inc ransomware

Inc operates a ransomware-as-a-service model, and the SonicWall campaign is a clean illustration of how its affiliates work. Rather than deploying an encryptor immediately, the actor uses the initial foothold to harvest three things: high-value credentials, active session databases, and TOTP MFA seed configurations. That combination survives patching — a defender who applies the hotfix and stops there has closed the door while the intruder still holds a key. The group has prior form on edge infrastructure: the FortiBleed incident, in which roughly 74,000 FortiGate credentials were exposed, has been linked to both Inc and the Lynx operation.

Breaches & Incidents

NewSupply chain

DigiCert certificate theft traced to CylindricalCanine

Check Point researchers analysed CylindricalCanine, a subgroup of the Chinese cybercrime collective GoldenEyeDog, and linked it to DigiCert's April 2026 support portal compromise. The actor stole code-signing certificates, leading to 60 revocations, including at least 27 associated with malware. Stolen signing certificates let malicious binaries inherit the trust of a legitimate publisher, which is why the revocation count matters more than the intrusion itself.

NewAI-assisted

China-linked campaign automated with AI coding tools

Check Point identified a China-linked campaign that used Claude Code and DeepSeek to automate attacks against government and financial organizations. Per the report, the tools generated scripts, adapted failed exploits, created credential-harvesting pages and executed commands — automating the iterative parts of an intrusion rather than replacing the operator.

Supply chain

Jscrambler npm packages backdoored

Five malicious releases of the Jscrambler JavaScript protection package — 8.14.0, 8.16.0, 8.17.0, 8.18.0 and 8.20.0 — were pushed on July 11 over roughly three hours. Jscrambler confirmed the cause as a compromised npm publishing credential. The payload is a cross-platform Rust infostealer harvesting cloud, crypto-wallet and AI-coding-assistant credentials. Critically, 8.18.0 and 8.20.0 moved the dropper into the package's main code and CLI, so npm install --ignore-scripts does not stop it. Socket flagged it six minutes after publication; 8.22.0 is the clean release. The package sees more than 15,000 weekly downloads.

Ransomware

Fairlife production halted

Coca-Cola's U.S. dairy subsidiary Fairlife confirmed a ransomware attack that temporarily halted production across the United States. Attackers accessed systems supporting manufacturing operations, prompting the company to activate incident response and business continuity procedures. Coca-Cola filed an SEC Form 8-K on 2026-07-16.

Ransomware

Spirals: under 24 hours from access to encryption

A Rust-based ransomware family used against a South Asian IT services company. The operators moved from initial access to full network encryption in less than 24 hours, using an IIS web shell, WMI and PsExec to spread, disabling security services and disrupting backups before encrypting. The dwell time is the story: a daily backup cadence would not have survived it.

Third party

Ernst & Young support platform breach

An unauthorized third party breached an IT-staff support-ticket platform. Anomalous activity was identified April 23, 2026, with an access window of March 28 – April 12, 2026. Tickets held client tax data plus personal information tied to investment holdings with EY's institutional clients, potentially including SSNs and financial account codes. California AG notification came July 15; individual notifications began July 13, with Experian IdentityWorks offered. No current evidence of misuse.
