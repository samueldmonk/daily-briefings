The Lead

Dow closes at a record high as jobs miss; a second chip selloff drags the Nasdaq lower

Wall Street split on Thursday. The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed at a record high, jumping 594.83 points (1.14%) to 52,900.07 — its first all-time closing high of the summer — while a second straight session of semiconductor selling dragged the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite down 0.80% (−207.36) to 25,832.67. The S&P 500 finished essentially flat at 7,483.24, as strength in financials and other blue-chip sectors offset the slide in technology. The divide told the story: information technology — semiconductors in particular — lagged for a second straight session, while non-tech blue chips powered the Dow to its record close.

The catalyst was a cooler-than-expected June jobs report that Wall Street read as easing pressure for another Fed rate hike. U.S. employers added just 57,000 positions in June — well short of the roughly 110,000 economists expected — and April and May were revised down by a combined 74,000. The unemployment rate came in at 4.2% (below the 4.3% consensus), average hourly earnings rose 0.3% on the month (3.5% year over year), and labor-force participation slipped to 61.5%. Under Fed Chair Kevin Warsh — who has urged investors to follow the data rather than Fed guidance — the soft print revived hopes that policy could stay on hold.

The drag again came from the AI-hardware trade: chip, memory and semiconductor-equipment names sold off for a second straight session on overseas weakness and fresh analyst caution, with Citi warning on semiconductor and hyperscaler stocks. Money kept rotating toward blue chips, financials and communication services — powering the Dow to its record close and capping its best first half since 2021. U.S. markets are closed Friday, July 3 for the Independence Day holiday.

The session's standout gainer: Rivian surged after the EV maker raised its 2026 delivery guidance to 65,000–70,000 vehicles (from 62,000–67,000), citing 12,194 second-quarter deliveries and the ramp of its R2 SUV. It is today's Chart of the Day.
