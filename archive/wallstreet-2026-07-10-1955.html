The Lead

Chips carry a choppy week home: S&P 500 +0.42% at the Friday close as SK Hynix pops in record debut

Stocks closed out a volatile week in the green. At Friday's closing bell the S&P 500 rose +0.42% to 7,575.39, the Nasdaq Composite added +0.29% to 26,281.61, and the Dow gained 149.60 points (+0.29%) to 52,637.01 — leaving the S&P up more than 1% for the week while the Dow, despite last week's record close, finished the week slightly lower.

Big Tech did the carrying: Nvidia rose roughly 3–4% and Meta jumped about 6%, capping a ~15% week — its best weekly performance since early 2024, per CNBC. The other story of the session was SK Hynix, whose American depositary shares opened at $170 — a 14% pop over the $149 pricing — and closed the day up 13% at $168.01 after the memory-chip maker raised $26.5 billion in the largest-ever U.S. listing by a foreign company. In the bond market, the 10-year Treasury yield finished the week little changed near 4.55–4.56% as stocks and oil prices drifted toward a quiet finish to the week (AP; ETF Trends).

Sources: CNBC, Yahoo Finance, AP via The Columbian (July 10 close)

Movers & Drivers

SK Hynix (SKHYV) +13% — record foreign debut

Priced at $149, opened at $170, closed its first Nasdaq session at $168.01. The $26.5B raise is the largest-ever U.S. listing by a foreign company; Chairman told CNBC memory "demand is enormous." The Nvidia supplier trades as SKHYV before switching to its permanent SKHY ticker.

Meta (META) ~+6% — best week since early 2024

Jumped about 6% Friday and roughly 15% on the week, its strongest weekly run since early 2024 per CNBC — one of the two Big Tech names that powered the S&P 500's Friday gain.

Nvidia (NVDA) ~+3–4% — AI trade stays hot

Rose roughly 3–4% Friday (sources' session marks ranged from about 3% to 4%), extending the chip-led rally that began Thursday on Micron's U.S. investment news and fresh AI optimism into the SK Hynix listing.

Delta (DAL) −0.6% — beat, but fuel bites

Q2 adjusted EPS of $1.56 beat the $1.48 consensus on adjusted revenue of $17.67B (vs $17.53B expected) despite what Delta called the highest quarterly fuel expense in its history; net income still fell 25% y/y to $1.6B. Q3 guide: $2.00–$2.50 EPS; full-year $6.50–$7.50 reaffirmed. Shares slipped about 0.6% to $88.50.
