Top Story

Ankalaev headlines UFC Abu Dhabi on Saturday against late-notice Guskov

The UFC returns to the Etihad Arena in Abu Dhabi on Saturday, July 25 with a 13-fight card topped by a light-heavyweight main event: No. 2-ranked Magomed Ankalaev, back for the first time since losing his 205-pound title, faces No. 9 Bogdan Guskov, who accepted on short notice for the injured Khalil Rountree Jr. Ankalaev is a heavy favorite at roughly −535, with Guskov around +400 (DraftKings). The six-bout main card is scheduled for approximately noon ET on Paramount+.

Around the Sport
  • Ankalaev eyes a heavyweight move: ahead of Saturday, the former light-heavyweight champ says a win over Guskov could tee up a heavyweight "money fight" trilogy against Alex Pereira.
  • Heavyweight unification looms: undisputed champ Tom Aspinall has agreed to a rematch with interim titleholder Ciryl Gane — a bout being targeted for later this year to settle the division.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement

ESPN's latest divisional rankings put newly crowned lightweight champ Justin Gaethje and interim heavyweight champ Ciryl Gane at the top of the conversation after their Freedom 250 wins. Ankalaev (No. 2 at 205) can reassert himself with a statement over No. 9 Guskov on Saturday.

Business & broadcast

UFC cards now stream on Paramount+ rather than traditional pay-per-view — Saturday's Abu Dhabi main card goes at approximately noon ET, and the August slate (Belgrade, Las Vegas and UFC 330 in Philadelphia) follows on the same platform.
