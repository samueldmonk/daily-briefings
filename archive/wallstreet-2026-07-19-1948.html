The Lead

A Chinese model release pushed chip stocks into a bear market — and U.S. markets are closed until Monday

U.S. markets are shut for the weekend, so everything below reflects Friday's session. It was not a quiet one. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell into a bear market on July 17, more than 20% below its June peak, after Chinese startup Moonshot AI unveiled Kimi K3 — an open-weight model the company says performs on par with leading systems from OpenAI and Anthropic. Reporting puts Kimi K3 at 2.8 trillion parameters, the largest open-weight model in the world as of its launch, and traders drew immediate comparisons to the DeepSeek shock of 2025.

The reversal is dramatic because the run-up was: the 30-stock chip benchmark had soared 105% between its March low and last month's peak. Coverage this run puts the value shed across the chip sector since June 22 at roughly $3.3 trillion, and calls it the sector's worst week since April 2025. Even after the rout, the index remains up about 63% year to date.

The damage was broad but not universal. Energy was the only gaining sector on Friday, helped by an oil spike, while the major indices all posted weekly losses. The market's next real test is not this week — there is no FOMC meeting during July 20–24 — but the earnings calendar is dense, and Alphabet and Tesla report into a tape that has just decided it is no longer sure about AI capital spending.

Travelers (TRV) — the day's standout

Shares jumped about 9% to close at $368.98, with CNBC's midday read framing it as “more than 8%.” Adjusted core earnings came in at $10.04 per diluted share against a consensus near $5.39 — an 88% beat. Net income was $2.21 billion, up 46% year over year, on revenue of $12.15 billion.

Catastrophe losses fell nearly by half and prior-year reserve releases added $578 million. Raymond James lifted its target from $350 to $400 with a Strong Buy; Truist began coverage at Buy with a $395 target.

Reporting this run puts the sector's lost value since June 22 at about $3.3 trillion and calls it the worst week since April 2025. The index is still up roughly 63% year to date.
