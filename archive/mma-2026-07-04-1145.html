McGregor returns at UFC 329 as a heavy underdog

Conor McGregor makes his first walk to the Octagon since breaking his leg at UFC 264 in July 2021, headlining UFC 329 on July 11 at T-Mobile Arena during International Fight Week — a welterweight rematch with Max Holloway — whom McGregor beat by unanimous decision at featherweight back in 2013. Sportsbooks have nonetheless installed Holloway as a clear favorite — around -210 with McGregor near +160 as of July 2, in from an opening -400 / -298 — making McGregor the longest-priced underdog of his UFC career.

Holloway has stayed active while McGregor sat out nearly five years, and the line has drifted from an opening -400 toward -210 as money pours in on McGregor. The card streams on Paramount+ with no pay-per-view; Paddy Pimblett vs. Benoit Saint-Denis is the lightweight co-main event. UFC 329 anchors International Fight Week (July 9-12). (Covers, OddsShark, Yahoo Sports)
