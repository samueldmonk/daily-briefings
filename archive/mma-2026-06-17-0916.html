Top Story

Gaethje shocks the White House lawn — dethrones Topuria to claim the undisputed lightweight title

At UFC Freedom 250 on June 14 — the promotion's first card on the South Lawn of the White House — Justin Gaethje pulled off a stunning upset of Ilia Topuria, battering and bloodying the previously unbeaten champion until his corner stopped the fight between the fourth and fifth rounds (TKO, corner stoppage). Gaethje, a two-time former interim lightweight champion, finally has the undisputed belt; it is Topuria's first professional loss.

The historic card delivered more drama in the co-main, where Ciryl Gane knocked out Alex Pereira in the second round for the interim heavyweight title — ending Pereira's bid to become the first three-division UFC champion. Sean O'Malley, Bo Nickal and Diego Lopes all scored finishes on a stacked main card.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement

In the UFC's post-White House update, new lightweight champion Justin Gaethje crashed into the men's pound-for-pound rankings, while Michael Chandler was dropped from the lightweight top 15 after his fourth straight loss. Ilia Topuria absorbs the first defeat of his career, falling to 17-1; Ciryl Gane claimed interim heavyweight gold, and Josh Hokit (10-0) vaulted into the heavyweight contender conversation.

Business & broadcast

UFC Freedom 250 was staged on the South Lawn of the White House as part of programming for America's 250th anniversary — a first for the promotion, with every fight on the card ending in a knockout. The UFC raised the purse for the occasion: a $400K Fight of the Night and $425K Performance of the Night. Justin Gaethje took home a record $825K for winning both awards, Ilia Topuria earned $400K for Fight of the Night, and Ciryl Gane collected the other $425K Performance bonus. The road show continues June 20 (Las Vegas) and June 27 in Baku, Azerbaijan, before UFC 329 (McGregor vs. Holloway 2) on July 11 — all streaming on Paramount+.
