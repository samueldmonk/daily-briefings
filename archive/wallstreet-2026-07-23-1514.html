The Lead

Big Tech tumbles and oil tops $100 — Wall Street sells off (as of ~3 PM ET)

U.S. stocks fell sharply Thursday as disappointing capital-spending outlooks from Alphabet and Tesla collided with a surge in oil prices tied to the widening U.S.–Iran war. As of about 3 PM ET the S&P 500 was down about 1.4%, the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite down about 2.3%, and the Dow off about 1% (roughly 541 points) — with a gauge of megacaps on track for its worst day since April 2025, per Bloomberg. Tesla tumbled about 14% after its profit fell despite record quarterly deliveries, and Alphabet sank about 6% after raising its 2026 capital-spending outlook to $195–$205 billion from prior guidance of as much as $190 billion, reviving worries over whether the AI build-out will pay off. Brent crude crossed $100 a barrel — its highest in about two months — after Iran-backed Houthi militants claimed attacks on two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea, sending the 10-year Treasury yield up 5 basis points to about 4.71%, its highest since January 15, 2025. Defense names bucked the slide, with Lockheed Martin up about 11% after earnings.

Movers & Drivers
▼ ~14%

Tesla (TSLA)

The day's biggest megacap decliner and the Nasdaq's largest drag. Q2 brought record deliveries of more than 480,000 vehicles but an earnings miss and Tesla's first negative free cash flow in more than two years as costs surged. CEO Elon Musk called 2026 a "massive capex year," pointing to Optimus robots, robotaxis and data centers.

▼ ~6%

Alphabet (GOOG)

Fell despite a strong quarter — revenue of $119.8B beat the ~$117B expected, EPS was $2.26, and Google Cloud revenue jumped 82% to $24.8B — after the company raised its 2026 capex outlook to $195–$205B, up from prior guidance of as much as $190B, intensifying scrutiny of AI's return on investment. CNBC's midday movers list had the drop at ~4.5% around noon.

▲ ~11%

Lockheed Martin (LMT)

Popped about 11% after a Q2 beat — EPS of $7.94 on revenue of $20.06B versus expectations of $7.19 and $19.34B — and a hiked full-year earnings outlook, as defense stocks rallied on the escalating Middle East conflict. A rare bright spot on the Dow.
