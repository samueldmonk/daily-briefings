Top Story

FortiBleed confirmed as a ransomware pipeline: mass FortiGate credential theft now feeds INC and Lynx

Researchers have, for the first time, tied the sprawling FortiBleed credential-theft campaign directly to ransomware deployment. An initial-access broker used a custom Golang tool — FortigateSniffer — to abuse FortiOS's native diagnose sniffer packet command and passively intercept authentication traffic across roughly two dozen protocols on internet-facing FortiGate firewalls. The operation is assessed to have targeted about 430,000 FortiGate devices worldwide and to have harvested more than 110 million credentials; SOCRadar counts 86,644 firewalls confirmed compromised.

What makes this week's finding different is the explicit link to extortion. SOCRadar observed an operator tied to FortiBleed's infrastructure working the victim-negotiation panels of both the INC and Lynx ransomware groups — the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been connected directly to ransomware deployment. The access has already produced at least 12 ransomware intrusions, encrypting hundreds of endpoints; investigators tracked scanning against roughly 11,250 FortiGate portals in more than 150 countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets and completed the full attack chain on 354. Files exposed on one of ~200 newly discovered servers point to a Russian-speaking initial-access broker running an organized ~20-person operation that has singled out manufacturing, technology and logistics targets across Latin America and Asia-Pacific — and is believed to hold a Nextcloud zero-day. The takeaway for defenders: rotate FortiGate credentials, enforce MFA, restrict management-interface exposure and hunt for the sniffer abuse.

Patch SimpleHelp now — CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0)

A max-severity flaw is being actively exploited, and CISA's federal remediation deadline is today, July 2, 2026. Upgrade to SimpleHelp's fixed build (5.5.16 / 6.0 RC2); if you can't patch immediately, disable the OIDC group-authenticated-login option the attack requires, then hunt for rogue Technician accounts and TaskWeaver / Djinn Stealer artifacts. Next up: patch the actively-exploited SharePoint RCE (CVE-2026-45659) ahead of its July 4 deadline.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Lynx — the INC rebrand now cashing in FortiBleed's stolen access

Lynx is a ransomware-as-a-service operation that emerged in mid-2024 as a rebrand of INC Ransom, whose source code was reportedly sold for $300,000 on the RAMP cybercrime forum — which is why the two families look nearly identical and now surface together in this week's FortiBleed findings. Since launching in July 2024, Lynx has amassed roughly 393 confirmed victims, with a heavy North-American tilt (about 208 in the United States and 20 in Canada) and reach into Europe, Asia and Oceania. The crew has kept a high operational tempo into 2026 — on January 5 alone it added 20 organizations to its leak site in a single day — and pairs data theft with double-extortion pressure. With FortiBleed's initial-access broker feeding both Lynx and its INC predecessor, the pipeline from stolen firewall credentials to encrypted networks is now explicit.

Breaches & Incidents

FortiBleed feeds INC & Lynx

An initial-access broker behind the FortiBleed campaign was found working the negotiation panels of both the INC and Lynx ransomware crews — access that has already produced at least 12 ransomware intrusions and hundreds of encrypted endpoints across affected organizations.

NewRansomware pipeline

SharePoint RCE added to CISA KEV

CISA added Microsoft SharePoint Server flaw CVE-2026-45659 (CVSS 8.8, deserialization of untrusted data) to its KEV catalog on July 1, citing active exploitation; federal agencies must patch by July 4. Microsoft fixed it in May 2026 and had rated exploitation "less likely" — CISA's listing shows it is now happening in the wild, though who is behind it is not yet known.

Exploited

Kemp LoadMaster under attack

Progress Kemp LoadMaster (CVE-2026-8037, vendor CVSS 9.6) lets an unauthenticated attacker run root commands via a crafted API request (an OS command-injection flaw). Researchers report active exploitation attempts began June 29; patched firmware (GA 7.2.63.2 / LTSF 7.2.54.18) is available.

Exploited

NetScaler exploited via public PoC

Attackers are actively targeting Citrix NetScaler ADC / Gateway appliances using public proof-of-concept code for CVE-2026-3055 (CVSS 9.3) — a pre-auth memory over-read that leaks session tokens from devices configured as a SAML identity provider (default configs are unaffected). A Metasploit module is now available; the flaw has been on CISA's KEV list since March. Fixed builds: 14.1-66.59 / 13.1-62.23 / 13.1-37.262.
