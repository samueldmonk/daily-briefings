Top Story

One phone call, 6.2 million records: Dutch police close in on the Odido hackers

Dutch police say they have "strong indications" that Dutch criminals were behind February's breach of telecom operator Odido, which exposed personal data of roughly 6.2 million people — about a third of the Netherlands. The pivotal evidence is a recorded phone call: before the attack, a Dutch-speaking man posing as an Odido IT employee called the company's customer service desk and helped trick staff into granting the access that let the hackers in. Investigators have analyzed the recording, believe the voice is genuine, and say they may release it publicly to identify the caller.

The ShinyHunters extortion group claimed the hack and published the stolen data on the dark web after Odido refused to pay. Police suspect the perpetrators have talked about the breach online or in their social circles and are urging tipsters to come forward — an unusually public turn in the hunt.

Sources: NL Times (Jul 9); The Record; DutchNews.nl; BleepingComputer

⚠ Patch Priority — deadline passed

Adobe ColdFusion — CVE-2026-48282 (CVSS 10.0): path traversal in the RDS FILEIO handler enabling unauthenticated remote code execution on ColdFusion 2025.9, 2023.20 and earlier. In-the-wild exploitation was captured within two hours of public details. Adobe's fix shipped June 30 in bulletin APSB26-68; CISA added the flaw to the KEV catalog July 7 with a federal remediation deadline of July 10 — that deadline has now passed. If you haven't patched, treat the system as exposed and hunt for compromise.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters (UNC6240) — from Odido's dark-web dump to PeopleSoft zero-days

The group at the center of the Odido investigation has had a busy 2026. Beyond claiming the 6.2M-record Dutch telecom breach and dumping the data when the ransom wasn't paid, ShinyHunters exploited Oracle PeopleSoft flaw CVE-2026-35273 (CVSS 9.8) as a zero-day between late May and early June — Google's Mandiant notified more than 100 exposed organizations, roughly 68% of them in higher education, and CISA added the flaw to the KEV catalog June 12. The common thread is data theft and extortion over encryption: help desks, HR systems and payroll PII are the targets of choice.

Breaches & Incidents

FortiBleed: 86,644 firewalls compromised across 194 countries Campaign

The credential-compromise campaign against Fortinet FortiGate devices counts 86,644 compromised firewalls across 194 countries — roughly half of all internet-facing FortiGates, per SOCRadar, which attributes the operation to the Lynx/INC ransomware group. Attackers scanned 59.3 million hosts, fingerprinted more than 437,000 FortiGate devices and harvested over 105 million credentials with automated tooling; victims span banks, telecoms, hospitals and government agencies. SOCRadar calls it a live, ongoing operation; CISA's June 18 alert urges credential resets, session termination and phishing-resistant MFA.

Accenture confirms breach; hacker "888" shops 35GB of source code New

Consulting giant Accenture confirmed a security incident after a threat actor using the handle "888" offered stolen company data for sale — reporting puts the haul at just over 35GB of source code. The company acknowledges the breach while the full scope, and whether client-related material is included, is still being assessed.

Ransomware negotiator gets 70 months for working with BlackCat

A 41-year-old former professional ransomware negotiator was sentenced to 70 months — nearly six years — for conspiring with operators of the now-defunct ALPHV/BlackCat group: he negotiated for victims while secretly leaking their confidential positions, including insurance limits, so attackers could maximize demands, and helped deploy BlackCat against U.S. victims in 2023.

"Hyadina" rebrand deploys GodDamn locker with a Microsoft-approved driver New

A rebranded ransomware crew tracked as Hyadina is hitting U.S. companies with a new locker called "GodDamn" that abuses a malicious — but officially Microsoft-signed — kernel driver to kill security tooling before encryption. Initial access reportedly comes through legitimate remote-access software such as AnyDesk, a reminder to inventory and restrict remote-admin tools.

Weekend leak-site roundup: healthcare and manufacturing hit Attacker claims

Leak-site postings logged July 10 include Krybit claiming Shanghai Xuerong Biotechnology, TheGentlemen claiming Chinese solar maker Xiamen Mibet, AiLock claiming Mexican paint manufacturer Pinturas Prisa, Doommageddon claiming Brazil's Hospital Di Camp, and CRPxO claiming both Creative Smiles Pediatric Dentistry (2.5GB) and biopharma firm AMHWA (37GB).
