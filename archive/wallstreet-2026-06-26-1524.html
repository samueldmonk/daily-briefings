The Lead
Markets open · as of ~3:05 PM ET

Stocks drift just below the flatline in the final hour as an AI-cost tech sell-off caps the Nasdaq — as of ~3:05 PM ET

U.S. stocks are hovering just under the flatline in the final hour of trade Friday after opening sharply lower and clawing nearly all the way back. As of roughly 3 PM ET the S&P 500 (≈ −0.2%), the Dow Jones Industrial Average (≈ −0.2%) and the Nasdaq Composite (≈ −0.3%) were each off a fraction — having pared an early drop of more than 1% — while the small-cap Russell 2000 outperformed, up about 0.7%. The session is a rotation out of technology and into cyclicals and defensives, with health care leading on a surge in biotech, joined by materials, industrials and energy, while technology and communication services lagged. Even so, the major indexes are still tracking a losing week, with the S&P down more than 1% and the Nasdaq off about 4% — its worst week in some time — though the Dow is pacing for a roughly 0.6% weekly gain. The trigger: mounting worries over the cost of building out artificial-intelligence infrastructure, amplified by a New York Times report that OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its IPO to next year — reportedly wary of the weak post-debut showing of newly public SpaceX and broad volatility in AI-related shares — which chilled AI-adjacent names and pressured chips.

The backdrop is a market on its back foot: on Thursday the Nasdaq Composite fell a fourth straight session — its first four-day losing streak since February — to 25,358.60, while the S&P 500 finished essentially flat at 7,357.49 and the Dow eked out a 0.14% gain to 51,920.62. Friday's single biggest mover is ON Semiconductor, down about 22.6% after announcing a roughly $7 billion deal to acquire Synaptics — its largest-ever acquisition — leading a broad chip pullback that also pulled AMD (≈ −2.6%), Arm (≈ −4%), Marvell (≈ −3.4%) and Intel (≈ −2%) lower, with Micron easing about 2% as it pared Thursday's blockbuster post-earnings surge. To the upside, Moderna jumped roughly 15% toward $70 — the S&P 500's top performer, at a 52-week high — after FDA advisers backed its mRNA flu shot and its Science Day showcased an expanded mRNA pipeline spanning oncology, autoimmune and rare disease, while SpaceX rose more than 1.5% ahead of a fast-tracked entry into the Russell 1000 after today's close. Crude kept sliding — WTI back below $69, off more than 3.5% as tankers keep transiting the Strait of Hormuz — while the 10-year Treasury yield held near 4.4%.
