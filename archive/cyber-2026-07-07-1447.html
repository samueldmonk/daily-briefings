Top Story

FortiBleed credential theft is now a confirmed pipeline into INC and Lynx ransomware

Researchers tracking the FortiBleed campaign say a mass harvest of Fortinet FortiGate credentials has become a direct route into ransomware. The operation collected more than 110 million credentials from roughly 430,000 internet-facing FortiGate firewalls, using a custom packet-sniffing tool dubbed "FortiGate Sniffer" to intercept VPN logins and other authentication data straight off the wire.

Investigators logged scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals in more than 150 countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets and completed the full attack chain on 354 of them. One operator was found logged into both the INC Ransom and Lynx negotiation panels, and at least 12 ransomware deployments — encrypting hundreds of endpoints — have been tied to the access.

An internal tracking document tied to the campaign points to an operation of roughly 20 people — some running high-impact intrusions, others providing technical support — marking the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been linked directly to ransomware deployment. Fortinet's tooling is under fire on more than one front: eSentire separately reports attackers exploiting a critical FortiClient EMS flaw (CVE-2026-35616, CVSS 9.1) to drop the EKZ Stealer against an energy-and-utilities customer. Defenders should treat any exposed FortiGate credentials as compromised: rotate all device and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, and apply Fortinet's latest FortiOS updates.

Patch Priority

Patch SimpleHelp now — CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0) is actively exploited and past its federal deadline

An authentication-bypass flaw in SimpleHelp's OIDC login accepts identity tokens without verifying their signature, letting an unauthenticated attacker forge a token, seize a full technician session and — in some setups — bypass MFA. It is being used to drop TaskWeaver and Djinn Stealer malware to steal credentials, SSH keys and crypto wallets. CISA set a July 2, 2026 KEV remediation deadline (now past), and because managed service providers rely on SimpleHelp, one compromised server can reach every downstream client. Fix: upgrade to 5.5.16 or 6.0 RC2 and rotate secrets.

Threat Actor Spotlight

The FortiBleed → INC / Lynx nexus

The operation behind the FortiBleed-to-ransomware pipeline is assessed at roughly 20 people, split between operators running high-impact intrusions and others providing technical support. The tell that links it directly to ransomware: a single operator was seen logged into both the INC Ransom and Lynx negotiation panels. INC and Lynx are human-operated, double-extortion ransomware brands; at least 12 deployments, encrypting hundreds of endpoints, have been traced to the stolen FortiGate access.

Breaches & Incidents

Adobe ColdFusion max-severity flaw exploited within hoursExploited

Adobe's June 30 update fixed a CVSS 10.0 path-traversal / arbitrary-file-write flaw in ColdFusion (CVE-2026-48282) that lets an unauthenticated attacker run code on unpatched servers. Threat-intel platforms observed exploitation begin within roughly two hours of public disclosure. Affected: ColdFusion 2025.9, 2023.20 and earlier — apply ColdFusion 2025 Update 10 / 2023 Update 21 now.

ShinyHunters breach 100+ orgs via an Oracle PeopleSoft zero-dayExploited

The ShinyHunters extortion crew (UNC6240) exploited CVE-2026-35273, an unauthenticated deserialization RCE in Oracle PeopleSoft's /PSEMHUB/hub endpoint (CVSS 9.8), as a zero-day from May 27 to June 9 — roughly two weeks before Oracle's out-of-band advisory — heavily hitting the higher-education sector. The gang claims 300+ PeopleSoft instances across 100+ organizations. On July 3, Nissan, Kubota North America and Aflac's Japan unit all confirmed data theft; Nissan says employee SSNs, banking and tax data may be exposed.

DHS investigates breach of its Homeland Security Information Network

The Department of Homeland Security is investigating a cyberattack that compromised the Homeland Security Information Network (HSIN) — a sensitive collaboration platform used by federal, state, local and private-sector partners. The intrusion is believed to have occurred between late May and early June.

JADEPUFFER: first agentic ransomwareAI-driven

Sysdig documented what it calls the first ransomware run end-to-end by an autonomous LLM agent — no human in the decision loop. The agent breached an internet-facing Langflow server via CVE-2025-3248, then independently ran reconnaissance, harvested cloud and API credentials, moved laterally, wiped a production MySQL database and locked 1,342 Nacos configuration items.
