Top Story

Du Plessis wants the title back — and is telling Strickland to fight someone else first

Dricus du Plessis returned to the win column at UFC Oklahoma City on Saturday, July 18, taking a five-round unanimous decision over Kamaru Usman on scores of 50–45, 49–46, 49–46. He wobbled Usman repeatedly in the first two rounds with clubbing strikes and well-timed knees up the middle, used improved takedown defense to shut down Usman's wrestling, and then outstruck him — though he showed signs of fatigue in the later rounds after an eleven-month layoff, as Usman found some confidence in the third.

What he did afterwards is the interesting part. At the post-fight press conference du Plessis said he "absolutely" deserves the next title shot, and then argued against himself: Sean Strickland, he said, has earned the right to defend the middleweight belt against Nassourdine Imavov first. "Fight Imavov, get a title defense. He earned that." Du Plessis also called a Strickland–Khamzat Chimaev rematch "senseless."

The queue behind Strickland is now genuinely crowded. Chimaev, ranked No. 1, is pushing for an immediate rematch after losing the belt to Strickland by split decision at UFC 328 in May. Imavov sits at No. 3 on five consecutive wins, having beaten Caio Borralho, Israel Adesanya and Brendan Allen in his last three. Du Plessis is No. 2 at 24-3, holds two wins over Strickland including a title defense, and has publicly deferred. Usman, No. 9, framed his own future simply: "It's either Sean Strickland or Islam" Makhachev. Dana White has still not named a challenger.
