Top Story

FortiBleed credential theft is now a confirmed pipeline into INC and Lynx ransomware

Researchers tracking the FortiBleed campaign say a mass harvest of Fortinet FortiGate credentials has become a direct route into ransomware. The operation collected more than 110 million credentials from roughly 430,000 internet-facing FortiGate firewalls, yielding a verified set of 86,644 working logins across 194 countries.

Investigators logged scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals in more than 150 countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets and completed the full attack chain on 354 of them. One operator was found logged into both the INC Ransom and Lynx negotiation panels, and at least 12 ransomware deployments — encrypting hundreds of endpoints — have been tied to the access.

Russian-speaking operators reportedly prioritized NATO-linked organizations, government bodies and critical-infrastructure providers, including a Turkish defense contractor. Defenders should treat any exposed FortiGate credentials as compromised: rotate all device and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, and apply Fortinet's latest FortiOS updates.

Patch Priority

Patch SimpleHelp now — CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0) is actively exploited and past its federal deadline

An authentication-bypass flaw in SimpleHelp's OIDC login accepts identity tokens without verifying their signature, letting an unauthenticated attacker forge a token, seize a full technician session and — in some setups — bypass MFA. It is being used to drop TaskWeaver and Djinn Stealer malware to steal credentials, SSH keys and crypto wallets. CISA set a July 2, 2026 KEV remediation deadline (now past). About 14,000 SimpleHelp servers are internet-exposed with an estimated 1,000 directly vulnerable, and because MSPs rely on it, one compromised server can reach every downstream client. Fix: upgrade to 5.5.16 or 6.0 RC2 and rotate secrets.

Threat Actor Spotlight

The FortiBleed → INC / Lynx nexus

A Russian-speaking crew — reported at roughly 20 operators — sits behind the FortiBleed-to-ransomware pipeline. The tell: a single operator was observed logged into both the INC Ransom and Lynx negotiation panels, and INC Ransom's victim list overlaps with organizations found in the FortiBleed data. The group has leaned toward NATO-aligned targets, government agencies and critical infrastructure. INC and Lynx are related human-operated, double-extortion ransomware brands.

Breaches & Incidents

Conduent breach hits 62.2MRansomwareNew

The attack on government-services contractor Conduent, claimed by the SafePay gang, now counts more than 62.2 million affected individuals — the third-largest U.S. healthcare data breach on record, behind Change Healthcare (2024) and Anthem (2015). Exposed data spans SSNs and medical and insurance details; Texas alone jumped to 15.4M residents, Oregon to about 10.5M.

JADEPUFFER: first agentic ransomwareAI-drivenNew

Sysdig documented what it calls the first ransomware run end-to-end by an autonomous AI agent (disclosed July 1). The agent breached an internet-facing Langflow server via CVE-2025-3248, dumped databases, hunted credentials and pivoted to a production MySQL/Alibaba Nacos server — going from a failed login to a working fix in 31 seconds — then encrypted 1,342 Nacos config items and left a README_RANSOM note with a Bitcoin address.

Djinn Stealer spreads via SimpleHelpExploited

Attackers are chaining the SimpleHelp auth-bypass (CVE-2026-48558) to deliver TaskWeaver and the Djinn Stealer across MSP-managed fleets, exfiltrating credentials, SSH keys, crypto wallets and developer tooling from downstream clients.

SharePoint RCE weaponized months after patchExploited

CISA confirmed active exploitation of a Microsoft SharePoint Server RCE (CVE-2026-45659) — fixed back in May but only now being abused — and added it to the KEV catalog. Who is behind the activity and how it is being exploited remain unclear.
