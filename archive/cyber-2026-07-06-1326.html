Top Story

FortiBleed becomes a ransomware pipeline: one operator ran both INC and Lynx panels

Researchers have connected the FortiBleed credential-theft campaign directly to the INC and Lynx ransomware operations — the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been tied straight to ransomware deployment. A custom packet-sniffing tool dubbed "FortiGate Sniffer", planted on compromised firewalls, helped harvest more than 110 million credentials across a campaign scoped to roughly 430,000 FortiGate devices.

The activity was exposed by an attacker operational-security slip — a server holding stolen credentials was left open on the internet. SOCRadar tracked scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals in more than 150 countries, confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets, completed the full attack chain on 354, and traced at least a dozen ransomware deployments to the access. A single operator was found logged in to negotiation panels for both INC Ransom and Lynx, and an internal document points to a structured crew of roughly 20 people. FortiBleed itself involves no new CVE — Fortinet has moved administrator password hashing to PBKDF2 in FortiOS 7.2.11, 7.4.8 and 7.6.1.

Patch Priority

⚠ Patch now: CVE-2026-45659 — Microsoft SharePoint Server (on-prem)

A CVSS 8.8 remote-code-execution flaw (deserialization of untrusted data) added to CISA's KEV catalog on evidence of active exploitation, and linked to Storm-2603's Warlock ransomware. CISA's federal remediation deadline was July 4, 2026 — now overdue. Any authenticated user with Site Member permissions can trigger it (no admin required); fixes shipped in Microsoft's May 2026 updates for SharePoint Subscription Edition, 2019 and 2016. On-prem SharePoint operators should patch and hunt immediately.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Storm-2603  Ransomware

Storm-2603 has been deploying Warlock ransomware by exploiting known vulnerabilities in on-premises SharePoint servers since mid-2025. The group is now tied to CVE-2026-45659, the SharePoint deserialization RCE that CISA added to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. With the federal remediation deadline already passed and a ransomware operator behind the flaw, any unpatched on-prem SharePoint farm should be treated as a live target and hunted, not just patched.

Breaches & Incidents

RansomwareFortiBleed → INC & Lynx

Stolen FortiGate credentials from the FortiBleed campaign have been linked to at least a dozen INC and Lynx ransomware deployments, with hundreds of endpoints encrypted across affected organizations.

NewIntrusionKubota North America

The equipment maker disclosed that hackers had access to some of its network systems for more than a month earlier this year before the intrusion was detected — the latest manufacturer to report a prolonged, undetected foothold.

NewFederalDHS — HSIN platform

The U.S. Department of Homeland Security is investigating a cyberattack that compromised the Homeland Security Information Network (HSIN), a sensitive information-sharing platform used by federal, state, local and private-sector partners.

MalwareSimpleHelp RMM exploited in the wild

Attackers are abusing the max-severity SimpleHelp flaw (CVE-2026-48558) to forge technician sessions and deploy TaskWeaver and Djinn Stealer across managed environments — a live MSP supply-chain risk. Roughly 14,000 servers are exposed, about 1,000 directly vulnerable.

Data TheftMedtronic — ShinyHunters

The medical-device maker is notifying customers after a breach exposed personal data to an unauthorized third party, in an intrusion linked to the ShinyHunters extortion crew. Exposed data may span personal and health information; customer notifications began in late June.

RansomwareJADEPUFFER — first "agentic" ransomware

Sysdig documented what it calls the first ransomware operation run end-to-end by an AI agent. Breaking in through an internet-facing Langflow server (CVE-2025-3248), the LLM handled recon, credential theft, lateral movement and encryption — once going "from a failed login to a working fix in 31 seconds."

MalwareAvalon framework / CrownX

A newly documented modular malware framework, Avalon, bundles credential theft, lateral movement, remote access and a ransomware payload dubbed CrownX. It arrives via a spoofed legal-document email pointing to a Proton Drive archive, hiding its payload inside an ISO image to slip past email defenses.

ExtortionFord Motor Company

Ford was listed by the Krybit ransomware group on a data-leak forum, tied to a June incident affecting its Mexican operations, with the crew threatening to publish stolen data absent negotiations.
