The Lead

Chips rebound lifts the Nasdaq past 1%; the Dow taps a 53,000 record before easing

U.S. markets came back from the long Independence Day weekend on the front foot. As of ~2:30 PM ET, the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite was up roughly 1.1% and the S&P 500 about 0.8%, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average — which briefly punched above 53,000 for the first time in its history earlier in the session — hovered just above the flat line (about +0.1%) as a handful of blue-chips (Nike, Amgen) dragged. Thursday's 52,900.07 close — itself a record — remains the last official finish.

The engine was a rebound in beaten-down memory and chip names: the PHLX Semiconductor Index jumped more than 4% and the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) rose about 2.7% as investors bet the AI trade has more room to run. Astera Labs surged about 10.5% early after Bank of America lifted its target to a Street-high $450 (from $240), Western Digital climbed about 9% on its own stack of target hikes (Cantor to $900, Bank of America to $732, Melius initiating at $1,050), and AMD rose about 8.5%. Weighing on the blue-chips, Microsoft slipped about 1.7% on reports it is cutting 4,800 jobs (~2.1% of staff), and retail names O'Reilly (−6.6%), AutoZone (−5.6%) and Insulet (−5.4%) were the notable laggards. Oil eased below $69 after OPEC+ approved another August output hike.

Movers & Drivers

Astera Labs (ALAB)

▲ ~+10.5% early

The day's biggest gainer: ALAB jumped about 10.5% early after Bank of America lifted its target to a Street-high $450 (up from $240), spiking toward a roughly $466 intraday high before profit-taking pared the pop. Its record $499.48 was set at quarter-end.

Western Digital (WDC)

▲ ~+9%

The memory-and-storage maker climbed about 9% as chips snapped back. Analysts stacked target hikes: Cantor to $900, Bank of America to $732, and Melius initiated at Buy with a $1,050 target, citing tight HDD supply and AI-storage demand.

Advanced Micro Devices (AMD)

▲ ~+8.5%

AMD jumped about 8.5% alongside the broad semiconductor bounce; the PHLX Semiconductor Index rose more than 4% and the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) gained about 2.7% as UBS, Citi and Bank of America reframed late-June's memory selloff as a buying opportunity.

Microsoft (MSFT)

▼ ~−1.7%

The software giant slipped about 1.7% after reports it is eliminating 4,800 jobs — roughly 2.1% of its workforce — in a fresh restructuring, leaving it among the weaker megacaps on an otherwise green tech tape.

Retail Laggards

▼ −5% to −7%

O'Reilly (−6.6%), AutoZone (−5.6%) and Insulet (−5.4%) were among the session's biggest S&P decliners — a reminder that Monday's rally is concentrated in semiconductors rather than broad-based.

WTI Crude Oil

▼ below $69

WTI slipped to about $68.60 a barrel — near its lowest since late February — and Brent held below $72 after OPEC+ agreed Sunday to raise August output by a combined +188,000 bpd, a fifth straight monthly increase, with Strait of Hormuz shipping normalizing.
