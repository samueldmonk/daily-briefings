Top Story

Fight week in Oklahoma City: du Plessis returns to face a middleweight Usman, with the winner in the title mix

The Octagon returns to Oklahoma City on Saturday for a 12-fight card headlined by two former champions. Dricus du Plessis fights for the first time since dropping the middleweight title to Khamzat Chimaev at UFC 319 last August — his first bout of 2026 — while Kamaru Usman makes his first appearance in 13 months in a full-time move up to 185 pounds, chasing a piece of two-division history. Du Plessis opened as a −340 favorite on DraftKings Sportsbook (Usman +270) and has stayed a heavy favorite (DraftKings Network/UFC.com/Forbes).

Ahead of the fight, du Plessis has been his usual blunt self — telling UFC.com "I hate being a loser" as he looks to reset after the Chimaev defeat, and waving off talk of a Sean Strickland–Chimaev rematch as "senseless" (UFC.com/Yahoo Sports, Jul 16). Meanwhile the biggest recent story in the sport still belongs to UFC 329: Max Holloway's 69-second TKO of Conor McGregor headlined a card that drew a record $26.4 million gate, and lightweight champion Justin Gaethje's next move remains an open question after his manager teased a first defense "against someone he's never fought before" (see Around the Sport).
