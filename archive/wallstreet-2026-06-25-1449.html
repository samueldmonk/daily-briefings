The Lead

Dow prints a fresh intraday record as money rotates out of Big Tech; Apple and Microsoft price hikes drag the Nasdaq while Micron's blowout lifts chips (as of ~2:30 PM ET)

Wall Street stayed divided on Thursday, June 25, as the Dow Jones Industrial Average traded at a fresh all-time high while the tech-heavy Nasdaq lagged. The blue-chip index held a gain of about 0.4% into the mid-afternoon — after printing a fresh all-time intraday high earlier that eclipsed its prior June 16 peak — with the strength coming from outside Big Tech: a rotation into health care, financials and industrials, led by names like Caterpillar (about +5%), JPMorgan Chase (about +2%) and Johnson & Johnson (more than +1%). The S&P 500 was little changed, while the Nasdaq Composite slipped about 0.6% after rising earlier — dragged lower by Apple, which fell roughly 6% after raising prices on MacBook and iPad, and Microsoft, down about 4% after announcing price increases of its own (including the Xbox console). Both companies cited surging memory and storage costs tied to the AI boom.

The spark for chips was still Micron, whose after-the-bell blowout the night before reinvigorated the AI trade. The memory maker posted record fiscal-third-quarter revenue of about $41.46 billion (roughly quadruple the $9.3 billion a year earlier) and adjusted EPS of $25.11 (well above the ~$20.78 consensus), and guided current-quarter revenue to about $50 billion — reiterating that its high-bandwidth memory is sold out through 2026. Micron shares jumped about 16% to a record high; SanDisk rode the same wave, up about 18%, and Qualcomm gained around 10% after raising its 2029 targets. The day's biggest single gainer was Bio-Techne, up about 19% after Germany's Merck KGaA agreed to buy it for roughly $11.3 billion. Tempering the mood, the Fed's preferred inflation gauge ran hot: the PCE price index rose at a 4.1% annual rate, the highest since April 2023. The session builds on Wednesday's mixed close (Dow +182.06 to a record 51,848.90, +0.35%; S&P 500 −0.10% to 7,358.22; Nasdaq Composite −0.43% to 25,476.64).

Movers & Drivers

Bio-Techne ▲ ~19%

The day's biggest single gainer after Germany's Merck KGaA agreed to acquire the biotech-tools and gene-therapy maker for about $11.3 billion ($73/share in cash, a ~36% premium), expanding Merck's life-science footprint.

Micron ▲ ~16%

Record fiscal-Q3: revenue ~$41.46B (roughly 4× a year ago) and adjusted EPS $25.11 both crushed estimates; guided current-quarter revenue to ~$50B, with HBM sold out through 2026. Shares hit an all-time high.

Apple ▼ ~6%

Dragged the Nasdaq lower after raising prices on MacBook and iPad, blaming surging memory and storage costs driven by AI demand. Other megacap chip-buyers slipped alongside it.

Microsoft ▼ ~4%

Joined Apple in pressuring the Mag7 after announcing price increases on consumer products including the Xbox console, with the same AI-driven component-cost rationale. Caterpillar (+5%), JPMorgan (+2%) and J&J (+1%) led the Dow's offsetting value rotation.
