Top Story

'Operation Endgame' guts the StealC and Amadey infostealer networks

Law enforcement and industry partners announced on June 24 the latest action in Operation Endgame, the long-running international campaign against the malware services that feed ransomware and data theft. Authorities took down 326 servers and 142 domains, recovered roughly 27 million stolen credentials, and froze about €41 million (~$46.5 million) in cryptocurrency, disrupting the StealC and Amadey operations along with SocGholish ("fake update") infrastructure.

StealC has run as a malware-as-a-service since 2023, letting customers harvest browser passwords, cookies, autofill data, card details, tokens and crypto-wallet data; Amadey is a modular Windows loader used to drop StealC, ransomware, miners and RATs. The pair were tied to more than 140,000 infected computers in the first two weeks of May 2026 alone. The takedown was coordinated by Europol and Eurojust with private partners including Microsoft's Digital Crimes Unit, Bitdefender, Bitsight, ESET, IBM X-Force and Proofpoint; investigators also dismantled SocGholish servers run by the actor tracked as TA569, helping clean roughly 14,971 compromised WordPress sites.

Patch Priority

⚠ Ubiquiti UniFi OS — CVE-2026-34910 (CVSS 10.0) · federal deadline June 26

An improper-input-validation command-injection flaw that chains with two access-control / path-traversal bugs (CVE-2026-34908 and CVE-2026-34909) to give a network-adjacent, unauthenticated attacker root-level remote code execution when the management interface is reachable. CISA reports active exploitation, including zero-day attacks that created rogue "John Sim" administrator accounts and pushed Mirai botnet malware onto exposed UniFi devices. CISA added the three flaws to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 23 with a remediation deadline of June 26, 2026 under BOD 26-04. Update to UniFi OS Server 5.0.8 now (fix released in May) and restrict management-interface exposure.

Threat Actor Spotlight

'KongTuke' — the access broker behind the new 'Mistic' backdoor

Researchers have tied an initial-access broker tracked as KongTuke to a stealthy new backdoor called Mistic (also tracked as MLTBackdoor), deployed alongside a Python remote-access trojan dubbed ModeloRAT. The suspected financially motivated campaign has hit organizations across insurance, education, IT and professional services since April 2026, with the toolset built for quiet, long-term access that can later be handed off to ransomware affiliates — a reminder of how brokered footholds increasingly seed the bigger extortion incidents downstream.

Breaches & Incidents

Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN zero-day zero-day

Mandiant says an unknown actor exploited CVE-2026-20245 in Catalyst SD-WAN Manager as a zero-day at least two months before it was publicly disclosed; the flaw lets an authenticated local attacker run arbitrary commands with elevated privileges.

Instructure / Canvas LMS breach education

Instructure has confirmed a breach of its Canvas learning platform that the ShinyHunters extortion crew claims exposed up to 275 million student and teacher records (about 3.65 TB) across thousands of institutions — described by researchers as the largest education-sector breach on record.

'Cordyceps' CI/CD supply-chain flaw supply chain

Researchers at Novee Security disclosed a new class of CI/CD workflow weakness dubbed Cordyceps that lets attackers hijack build pipelines and poison open-source supply chains; they say the technique was reproducible against workflows at major organizations including Microsoft, Google, Apache and Cloudflare.

Aflac data breach insurance

Insurer Aflac is notifying roughly 22.7 million people — customers, beneficiaries, employees and agents — after a breach of its U.S. network disclosed earlier this month.
