Top Story

"Icarus" threatens to publish Klue supply-chain loot today

The extortion group Icarus, which emerged in April 2026, has added competitive-intelligence platform Klue to its Tor-based leak site and threatened to release data stolen from Klue customers' Salesforce instances today, June 22, unless the company and affected organisations enter negotiations. The intrusion (around June 11–12) used compromised legacy credentials; attackers then abused OAuth tokens connecting Klue to third-party platforms — including Salesforce — to reach data inside connected customer environments.

The victim list is unusually security-heavy: HackerOne, Huntress, Jamf, OneTrust, Recorded Future, Snyk and Tanium are among the affected Klue customers. Stolen data is business CRM information — sales-account records and contact details (names, emails, job titles, phone numbers, addresses) — rather than credentials or source code. Huntress said it received extortion contact from an actor calling itself "mr bean," pointing to a Session Messenger ID tied to Icarus; Salesforce has disabled the Klue integration.

Splunk Enterprise — CVE-2026-20253 (CVSS 9.8)

A missing-authentication flaw in a PostgreSQL sidecar service lets an unauthenticated, remote attacker create or truncate files — chainable to pre-auth remote code execution. It is actively exploited (the first Splunk bug ever added to CISA's KEV) and the federal remediation deadline of Sunday, June 21 has now passed — patch immediately. Affected: Splunk Enterprise 10.0.0–10.0.6 and 10.2.0–10.2.3; fix to 10.0.7 / 10.2.4 / 10.4.0. The window from public proof-of-concept (June 12) to confirmed in-the-wild exploitation was under a week.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Icarus — OAuth-token theft, no malware required

Icarus is a data-theft and extortion crew that surfaced in April 2026 and operates a Tor-based leak site. Rather than deploying ransomware, it monetises stolen business data: in the Klue campaign it leveraged compromised legacy credentials and OAuth tokens from a trusted SaaS integration to siphon Salesforce CRM records from a chain of downstream customers — a clean supply-chain pivot that sidesteps endpoint defences entirely.

The group negotiates over Session Messenger (the Klue contact used the alias "mr bean") and pressures victims with public leak-site countdowns. Its targeting of security vendors — HackerOne, Huntress, Snyk, Tanium and others — makes the Klue incident a notable case of attackers turning a defender's own SaaS supply chain against it.

Breaches & Incidents

"FortiBleed" harvests Fortinet credentials at scale

Researchers tallied roughly 86,644 working Fortinet credentials (as of June 19) in a sweeping campaign attributed to Russian-speaking actors; a fresh report on June 22 described a database of about 30,000 working FortiGate logins. Generic admin accounts (35%) and built-in system accounts (28.3%) make up most of the haul. CISA issued a hardening advisory (~74,000 devices).

Check Point VPN zero-day tied to Qilin ransomware

CVE-2026-50751, an IKEv1 authentication-bypass in Check Point Remote Access / Mobile Access VPN, has been actively exploited since May 7. Check Point links at least one incident to a Qilin ransomware affiliate (medium confidence) and shipped a hotfix for the deprecated IKEv1 path.

ShinyHunters' PeopleSoft zero-day hits universities

The ShinyHunters extortion crew exploited an unpatched Oracle PeopleSoft flaw (CVE-2026-35273) to break into enterprise systems, steal data and demand payment — a campaign that has hit higher education hardest. CISA added the CVE to its KEV catalog with a July 3 federal deadline.

UK NCSC: AI-written code is a looming risk

Britain's National Cyber Security Centre warned on June 22 that AI-generated code could lead to security disasters if left unchecked — echoing wider concern that LLM-assisted development is shipping unreviewed flaws into production.
