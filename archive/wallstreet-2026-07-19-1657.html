The Lead

Semiconductors close the week in a bear market as a Chinese model rattles the AI trade

U.S. equity markets are shut for the weekend — there is no live session to report, and the most recent one was Friday, July 17. It was an ugly close to the week: the S&P 500 fell 1.01%, the Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.40% and the Dow slipped 0.77%, as a worsening semiconductor rout outweighed strong insurance-sector earnings.

The headline damage was in chips. The PHLX Semiconductor Index entered a bear market on Friday — Yahoo Finance flagged the threshold crossing as the sell-off deepened — after falling more than 13% over the past month. The Motley Fool notes the same index is still up almost 63% year to date, which is the other half of the story: this is a violent unwind of a very large run, not a collapse from a standing start.

Two catalysts got named repeatedly. Chinese start-up Moonshot AI released an advanced model that investors fear could undercut U.S. vendors' pricing power — TheStreet identified it as Kimi K3. Alongside that, a re-escalation of violence between the U.S. and Iran drove a broad risk-off tone and pushed crude sharply higher.

Breadth was narrow: by the close, energy (+1.82%) was the only S&P sector to finish higher, while information technology (−2.65%) was the weakest and communication services (−1.45%) also lagged. The week's damage: the S&P 500 off 1.6%, the Nasdaq down 2.9% and the Dow down 0.9%.

Movers & Drivers

+8%Earnings

Travelers jumps on a blowout Q2

The insurer surged more than 8% after posting earnings per share of $10.04 against a Wall Street consensus of $5.41, with roughly $2.2 billion of net income versus about $1.5 billion a year earlier. It was the day's biggest named single-stock move, and it kept a floor under the Dow.

NewMegacap

Apple briefly retakes the crown from Nvidia

Apple passed Nvidia in market value during Friday's session to become — for a stretch — the world's most valuable company, with both hovering around $4.9 trillion. The positions reversed before the bell and Nvidia closed slightly ahead. Apple is up almost 23% in 2026 as investors reward its lighter capital-spending approach to AI; Nvidia had held the title since June 2025.

−3.5%Semis

Nvidia leads the chip unwind

Nvidia slid about 3.5%, with its market capitalisation easing to roughly $4.86 trillion, per CNBC's midday movers wrap. AMD and Intel were also hit as the Moonshot AI headline reframed the pricing-power question for U.S. silicon.

LowerAI

Alphabet falls on a reported Gemini delay

Alphabet shares dropped on reports that the launch of its Gemini 3.5 Pro model would be pushed back — an unwelcome headline on a day when the market was already re-pricing who wins and loses in AI.

Sharply lowerStreaming

Netflix extends its post-earnings slide

Netflix plunged again on Friday in the wake of its Q2 report, where revenue of $12.56 billion and EPS of $0.80 came with a light Q3 outlook and a decision to move engagement reporting to an annual cadence. Sourced closing prints for the day conflict, so no single percentage is published here.

Only gainerEnergy

Crude does the heavy lifting

Energy was the sole sector in the green, supported by a sharp rally in crude as Middle East supply risk resurfaced. WTI closed up on the day and finished the week substantially higher — see the commodities table for the sourced figures and the discrepancy between them.
