The Lead

Dow taps a record and small caps hit new highs as chips drag the Nasdaq — Wall Street's first session of the second half

U.S. stocks closed a split session on Wednesday, the first trading day of the second half, a day after the major indexes wrapped their strongest quarter since 2020. The Dow Jones Industrial Average tapped a fresh intraday record early — trading above 52,700 — before easing back, the S&P 500 finished little changed, and the small-cap Russell 2000 pushed to a new all-time high, with communication-services and financial stocks doing the lifting. The Nasdaq Composite was the clear laggard, dragged lower as investors kept dumping semiconductors to lock in a blistering first-half run. Precise closing levels are in the Weekly Scorecard once finalized.

The marquee move belonged to Meta, which jumped as much as ~10% intraday on a Bloomberg report that it will sell excess AI computing capacity through a new cloud unit — internally "Meta Compute" — lifting fellow mega-cap tech such as Microsoft and Apple but pressuring rival "neocloud" providers such as CoreWeave (~−11%) and Nebius (~−12%). The drag came from chipmakers giving back some of a blistering first-half run. Traders also parsed a cooler June ADP payrolls report (+98,000 vs. ~113,000 expected), a softer ISM manufacturing read (PMI 53.3; prices-paid down to 73.0 from 82.1), oil holding near a four-month low, and remarks from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh at the ECB's Sintra forum on central banking.

Movers & Drivers

Meta ▲ ~6%

Led the tape, up about 6% (as much as ~10% intraday), on a report it will launch a cloud business — "Meta Compute" — to sell surplus AI compute and model access, taking on AWS, Azure and Google Cloud; the halo lifted Microsoft (~+3%) and Apple (~+2%).

Mega-cap

CoreWeave ▼ ~11%

The AI-cloud "neocloud" provider slid about 11% as Meta's plan to rent out its own excess compute rattled specialist rivals (Nebius fell ~12%).

AI cloud

Memory & chips ▼

Semiconductors led the retreat as traders locked in a huge first-half run: Micron, SanDisk and other memory names fell and Nvidia slipped, keeping the Nasdaq in the red even as the broad market advanced.

Semis

Corning & chip tools ▼

Corning was among the biggest S&P 500 decliners (−8.87% to ~$232.78); toolmakers Teradyne (−6.3%), Lam Research (−6.0%) and KLA (−5.9%) were swept up in the AI-hardware pullback.

Semis

Nike ▼ ~3%

Slipped despite beating on earnings (EPS 72¢, a 59¢ beat; revenue ~$11B, −0.9% YoY) after executives warned that sales weakness — particularly in China — could persist.

Earnings

Software & staples ▲

Money rotated out of chipmakers: Palantir climbed about 5.5%, while defensive consumer-staples names outperformed — General Mills (+5.8%), Conagra (+5.3%) and Campbell's (+4.2%).
