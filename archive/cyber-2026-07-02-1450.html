Top Story

FortiBleed confirmed as a ransomware pipeline: 430,000 FortiGate firewalls, harvested credentials, and a direct line to INC and Lynx

Researchers this week confirmed that the sprawling FortiBleed credential-theft campaign is feeding ransomware operations. Attackers deployed a custom packet-sniffing tool dubbed "FortiGate Sniffer" on compromised Fortinet firewalls to intercept VPN and other authentication traffic directly off the wire. The operation targeted more than 430,000 FortiGate firewalls worldwide and planted sniffers on roughly 19,000 devices; a single exposed server was found holding credentials pulled from more than 73,000 Fortinet appliances, alongside cracking infrastructure and configuration files.

The ransomware link is the new part. According to SOCRadar, an operator tied to the campaign's infrastructure was found working negotiation panels for both the INC and Lynx ransomware crews — the first time mass FortiGate credential theft has been tied directly to ransomware deployment. Trackers logged scanning against about 11,250 FortiGate portals in 150+ countries, admin-level access on 409 targets and a completed attack chain on 354, resulting in at least 12 ransomware intrusions and hundreds of encrypted endpoints. Defenders should rotate all FortiGate credentials, review VPN/admin logs for the sniffer, and treat exposed appliances as compromised.

Patch Priority

Patch now: SimpleHelp CVE-2026-48558 (CVSS 10.0) — CISA deadline is TODAY

A maximum-severity OIDC authentication-bypass in SimpleHelp remote-support software accepts unsigned identity tokens, letting a remote, unauthenticated attacker forge a fully authenticated technician session. It is being actively exploited to deploy the TaskWeaver backdoor and Djinn Stealer. Affected: SimpleHelp 5.5.15 and earlier and 6.0 pre-release builds. Fix: upgrade to 5.5.16 / 6.0 RC2 immediately — MSPs are a supply-chain amplifier here.

CISA KEV: added Jun 29, 2026 → remediation due July 2, 2026 (today). Risk-based BOD 26-04 assignment (3-day window).

Threat Actor Spotlight

Lynx Ransomware-as-a-Service

Lynx — a RaaS operation widely assessed as a rebrand of the earlier INC ransomware — is one of the two crews now being fed by the FortiBleed credential pipeline. Investigators found the same operator running victim-negotiation panels for both Lynx and INC, and tied the harvested FortiGate access to at least 12 ransomware intrusions with hundreds of endpoints encrypted. The wider operation is assessed to run roughly 20 members with defined roles, from access brokering to negotiation. Watch for Lynx follow-on activity anywhere FortiGate credentials were exposed.
