The Lead

Chips power stocks to a higher close as oil slides on Iran-diplomacy hint — Nasdaq +1.30%, S&P 500 +0.81%, Dow +0.27%

Markets closed, Thursday, July 9. A semiconductor surge carried the major averages higher, shrugging off a second day of U.S.–Iran tensions. The Nasdaq Composite rose 1.30% to 26,206.89, the S&P 500 added 0.81% to 7,543.65, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 139.02 points, or 0.27%, to 52,487.41 — as chip stocks jumped and crude eased.

The chip complex led broadly: a gauge of semiconductor firms climbed roughly 4–5.7%, the iShares Semiconductor ETF gained about 5.2% and the VanEck Semiconductor ETF rose 2.5%. Micron jumped about 7.5% after announcing a $3 billion investment in the U.S. semiconductor supply chain — part of a plan to spend "more than $250 billion" over the next decade — while AMD gained about 7.2% and Sandisk popped 7.6%. Smaller chip-equipment names ran hardest: Onto Innovation topped the group at roughly +12% and Allegro MicroSystems added nearly 11.7%.

Oil, the other big story, went the other way: crude slid almost 3% after President Trump hinted at possible diplomacy with Iran, easing some of the geopolitical premium built up over the prior two sessions — though fighting between U.S. and Iranian forces continued. The 10-year Treasury yield hovered near a two-month high (it touched about 4.59% Wednesday as firmer oil revived inflation worries), and investors leaned back into the AI trade ahead of SK Hynix's U.S. trading debut Friday.
