The Lead

Stocks edge higher but pare an early bounce as chips steady before Micron's after-the-bell earnings (as of ~10:35 AM ET, June 24)

U.S. stocks are trying to claw back ground Wednesday after a two-session, AI-driven selloff, though the morning's bounce has been fading. As of about 10:35 AM ET, the S&P 500 is up about 0.35%, the Nasdaq Composite about 0.62% and the Russell 2000 about 0.41%, while the Dow slips about 0.17%. The steadier tone follows a rebound in battered memory and semiconductor names — Micron rose roughly 4% in pre-market after closing down about 13% Tuesday; the memory maker, up more than 250% year to date, reports fiscal third-quarter results after today's close, the week's marquee corporate catalyst. The session leads off Tuesday's losses, when the Nasdaq Composite closed down 2.21% at 25,587.04, the S&P 500 fell 1.44% to 7,365.46, the Dow finished essentially flat (−0.09%) at 51,666.84 and the Russell 2000 slipped 0.78% to 2,980.82, dropping back below 3,000.

The morning's biggest single-stock story is well away from the AI trade: Wendy's rocketed about 24% after appointing former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer (a role he held under current Wendy's CEO Bob Wright) and becoming the latest target of a retail "meme-stock" short squeeze — it traded more shares pre-market than Micron or Intel. Sunrun jumped about 22% and Builders FirstSource added about 10%, while on the downside Hertz tumbled about 27% after launching a roughly $100 million stock offering, Cerebras Systems slid about 16%, extending its post-earnings drop, and Casey's General Stores fell about 7%. Take-Two kept climbing — BTIG initiated a Buy with a $290 target as the stock rallied from $206 to about $247 ahead of Grand Theft Auto VI pre-sales. Macro and rates stay in the frame: the 10-year Treasury yield hovers near 4.5% and the Fed-funds target sits at 3.50–3.75% after last week's hold, with markets pricing at least one more 2026 hike (September odds around 50%) — Bank of America this week went further, calling for three quarter-point hikes in 2026 under new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh. Oil keeps sliding, with WTI near $71 (Brent ~$77, a near three-month low) as tankers resume transit through the Strait of Hormuz amid reported U.S.–Iran progress. Beyond Micron tonight, the week's key macro test is Thursday's May PCE inflation report.

Movers & Drivers

Wendy's — meme squeeze + new CFO ▲ ≈ +24%

The burger chain rocketed about 24% on a retail "meme-stock" short squeeze plus the appointment of former Potbelly executive Steven Cirulis as CFO and chief strategy officer — a role he previously held under current Wendy's CEO Bob Wright. It traded more shares pre-market than Micron or Intel. The morning's biggest single-stock mover.

Micron — steadies before tonight's report ▲ ≈ +4% pre-mkt

Micron rose roughly 4% pre-market, recovering part of Tuesday's ~13% slide, ahead of its fiscal-Q3 earnings after today's close. FactSet consensus is near $35.75B revenue and about $20.83 adjusted EPS (estimates range ~$19.7–$20.8), with a record gross margin above 80% and HBM capacity sold out through 2026. TD Cowen lifted its target to $1,500; Goldman to $900 (neutral).

Hertz — stock offering ≈ −27%New

Hertz Global tumbled about 27% after launching a common-stock offering of roughly $100 million, diluting holders. Casey's General Stores (−7%) was the other notable early decliner alongside the chip names.

Cerebras — post-earnings hangover ≈ −16%New

The newly public AI-chip maker fell about 16%, extending losses after its first quarterly report since its May IPO — strong revenue growth and a raised outlook still undercut by softer gross-margin guidance and a stretched post-IPO valuation. Sunrun (+22%) and Builders FirstSource (+10%) led the early gainers.
