The Lead

Markets closed for the weekend — Friday ended with a semiconductor rout and a Netflix miss

U.S. markets are shut for the weekend, so the most recent session remains Friday, July 17. It was a broad decline: the S&P 500 lost 1.01%, the Nasdaq Composite shed 1.4% and the Dow fell about 0.7%, leaving all three benchmarks with weekly losses.

Semiconductors did the damage. The PHLX Semiconductor Index entered a bear market on Friday, capping the cohort’s worst week since April 2025, with a key industry gauge sinking 20% from its record. The catalyst was a surprise breakthrough from Chinese AI startup Moonshot, whose new model — which TheStreet identifies as Kimi K3 — raised concerns among investors that it could undercut U.S. chip and AI sales. Even after the drawdown, the semiconductor gauge is down more than 13% over the past month but still up roughly 63% year to date.

Netflix supplied the other headline. Shares fell more than 10% early Friday after quarterly earnings per share slightly topped expectations at $0.80 while revenue of $12.56 billion narrowly missed consensus of $12.6 billion, and the company’s third-quarter earnings and revenue guidance both came in below FactSet consensus. Energy was the one sector to gain on the day as oil prices spiked; communication services and consumer cyclicals fared worst.
