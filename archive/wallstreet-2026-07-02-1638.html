The Lead

Stocks close higher to cap the holiday-shortened week as tech shakes off a second chip selloff

Wall Street closed broadly higher Thursday, ending a holiday-shortened week on a firm note. The Dow Jones Industrial Average led the majors, finishing up roughly 0.5% and hovering near record territory after climbing about 0.8% to around 52,740 in late-morning trading. The S&P 500 and the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite each ended modestly higher — a notable late-day recovery for the Nasdaq, which had been dragged down through midday by a second straight session of semiconductor selling. Small caps outperformed, with the Russell 2000 up about half a percent. (Precise closing levels were still being confirmed shortly after the 4 PM bell; see the Weekly Scorecard.)

The catalyst was a cooler-than-expected June jobs report that Wall Street read as easing pressure for another Fed rate hike. U.S. employers added just 57,000 positions in June — well short of the roughly 115,000 economists expected — and April and May were revised down by a combined 74,000. The unemployment rate ticked down to 4.2% (from 4.3%), average hourly earnings rose 0.3% on the month (3.5% year over year), and labor-force participation slipped to 61.5%. Under Fed Chair Kevin Warsh — who has urged investors to follow the data rather than Fed guidance — the soft print revived hopes that policy could stay on hold.

The morning's pressure came from abroad: a 7.9% plunge in South Korea's Kospi — SK Hynix fell about 14% and Samsung Electronics about 9% — rippled into U.S. chip names before they pared losses into the close. Blue chips, consumer staples, materials and industrials led as money kept rotating out of the AI-hardware trade; the Dow's advance capped its best first half since 2021. U.S. markets are closed Friday, July 3 for the Independence Day holiday.

Movers & Drivers

Rivian ▲ ~13%

The session's standout gainer: Rivian jumped nearly 13% to about $19.38 after the EV maker raised its 2026 delivery guidance to 65,000–70,000 vehicles, citing stronger-than-expected second-quarter demand. It is today's Chart of the Day.

Palantir ▲ ~8%

Palantir rallied after D.A. Davidson upgraded the stock to Buy and lifted its price target to $175 (from $165), arguing rising geopolitical tension will push customers to use Palantir to orchestrate AI models. Shares had fallen roughly 29% in 2026 heading into the call.

Semiconductors ▼ 2nd day

The dominant drag: chip and AI-hardware names sold off for a second straight session after the Kospi's 7.9% plunge spilled over. Corning tumbled about 13%, with Micron, Applied Materials, KLA and Teradyne among the laggards, before U.S. tech trimmed losses into the close. Citi added caution with a warning on semiconductor and hyperscaler stocks.

Bitcoin ▲ ~5%

The world's largest cryptocurrency surged about 5% to roughly $61,275 as inflation fears eased, after Fed Chair Kevin Warsh said energy prices — and "inflation risks" — "have come down" since the U.S.–Iran de-escalation.
