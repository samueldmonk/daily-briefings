UFC 329 • Las Vegas • Jul 11

Top Story: Gaethje's Stunning Victory at the White House

In an historic setting on the South Lawn of the White House on June 14, interim lightweight champion Justin Gaethje achieved what many doubted—he dethroned unbeaten Ilia Topuria with a dominant fourth-round TKO to claim the undisputed UFC lightweight title. Gaethje controlled the pace with heavy striking, leaving Topuria a swollen, bloody mess by the mid-rounds. Topuria declined to continue into the fifth, handing Gaethje his lifelong dream in the sport's most iconic venue. All seven bouts on the Freedom 250 card ended inside the distance, including Ciryl Gane's impressive KO2 of Alex Pereira in the co-main event.

Around the Sport

  • UFC Freedom 250 historic: First professional sporting event ever held at the White House grounds (South Lawn); all seven main-card bouts ended in the distance.
  • Paramount+ exclusive streaming: The UFC's new seven-year media deal with Paramount+ shifts all events away from traditional PPV. Paramount+ subscription required to watch numbered events (no PPV surcharge).
  • Topuria's reign ends: The previously undefeated Ilia Topuria (17-0 entering the bout) now 17-1; Gaethje's ascent to undisputed status marks a generational shift in the lightweight division.

Rankings & Business

  • Movado Evloev: Confirmed as #1 featherweight contender; title shot vs. Volkanovski targeted for late summer 2026.
  • Light Heavyweight flux: Carlos Ulberg (champion since UFC 327, Apr 11 2026) remains the undisputed belt holder after acL surgery post-title win. Alex Pereira vacated to chase heavyweight and lost the interim belt to Gane.
  • Business: Paramount+ integration continues the UFC's shift toward streaming-first distribution; traditional PPV buyrate data now consolidated into subscription metrics.
