The Lead

A holiday pause after Thursday's chip-led rebound

With Wall Street dark for Juneteenth on Friday and closed this weekend, the standing read is Thursday's broad advance. The S&P 500 closed +1.08% at 7,500.58, the Nasdaq Composite +1.91% at 26,517.93, the Dow +0.14% (+72) at 51,564.70, and the small-cap Russell 2000 led, +2.12% at 2,979.77 — a rebound from Wednesday's slide after Kevin Warsh's first Fed meeting landed hawkish, with the dot plot now pointing to higher rates by year-end.

The session's headline was Intel, up roughly 10% to a record close near $134 (an intraday high around $135.48), after President Trump posted on Truth Social that Apple had agreed to design and build chips with Intel in the U.S. — a claim neither company has confirmed (Intel declined to comment). The note lifted the broader semiconductor complex and helped power the Nasdaq, and technology was the best-performing sector on the holiday-shortened week. Cutting the other way, Accenture cratered about 18% — its worst single-day drop in years — after cutting full-year revenue-growth guidance to 3–4%.

Movers & Drivers

Intel ≈ +10% · record close

Surged ~10% to a record $133.99 close (intraday high ~$135.48) after Trump claimed on Truth Social that Apple will design and build chips with Intel in the U.S. Neither Apple nor Intel confirmed the arrangement; the rally still lifted the broader chip complex.

Accenture ≈ −18%

Worst single-day drop in years (down ~17–18%, the day's worst-performing large-cap) after cutting FY26 revenue-growth guidance to 3–4% from 3–5% and guiding Q4 revenue ($17.75–18.40B) below the ~$18.47B consensus, citing weak discretionary demand and a U.S. federal slowdown.

Chips & small caps broad bid

The Russell 2000 (+2.12%) led the majors and semiconductors were broadly bid alongside Intel — a risk-on, chip-and-cyclical rebound from Wednesday's hawkish-Fed selloff.

Energy week's laggard

Energy was the soft spot as crude slid on the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz; oil fell roughly 9% on the week even after Friday's modest bounce, leaving the sector trailing the broad rally.

Chart of the Day — Accenture (NYSE:ACN)

Thursday's single biggest mover: Accenture's worst-in-years ~18% slide (it opened down ~19%) on a cut to full-year revenue-growth guidance to 3–4% and soft Q4 guidance ($17.75–18.40B vs ~$18.47B consensus) — even as the firm unveiled roughly $4.18B in cybersecurity acquisitions (three deals).
