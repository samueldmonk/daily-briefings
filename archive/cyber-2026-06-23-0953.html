Top Story

Klue OAuth supply-chain breach fuels "Icarus" Salesforce extortion as the deadline lapses

A compromise of competitive-intelligence platform Klue has cascaded into a wave of Salesforce data thefts. Attackers gained access around June 11 using a long-disused but still-active credential Klue had created to prototype an integration it later dropped, then stole the OAuth tokens Klue uses to connect to customers' Salesforce (and Gong) environments — reaching CRM data including business contacts, sales communications and pricing records. Klue says it discovered the unauthorized activity on June 12, and Salesforce has since disabled the Klue integration.

A newly emerged extortion crew calling itself Icarus claimed the campaign and set a June 22 deadline to negotiate or see the data published. That deadline has now passed — and, per the latest reporting, data allegedly stolen from multiple Klue customers began appearing on the "Icarus" leak site on June 22. Firms that have confirmed data was exfiltrated include HackerOne, Huntress, Jamf, Recorded Future, Tanium, Snyk, OneTrust, Sprout Social, Insurity and Gong, and LastPass has disclosed that customer contact and CRM data were exposed in the same breach (it stresses its password vaults and products were not affected).

Patch Priority

Patch Splunk Enterprise now — CVE-2026-20253 (CVSS 9.8) is exploited and past its federal deadline

The single most urgent action for defenders today is the unauthenticated remote-code-execution / arbitrary file create-or-truncate flaw in Splunk Enterprise (CVE-2026-20253, CVSS 9.8) — the first Splunk bug ever added to CISA's KEV catalog. It is actively exploited, and its federal remediation deadline of June 21 has already passed (overdue). Affected: Splunk Enterprise 10.2.0–10.2.3 and 10.0.0–10.0.6. Upgrade to 10.4.0, 10.2.4, 10.0.7 or later immediately. Splunk Cloud and Enterprise 9.4 and earlier are not affected.

Threat Actor Spotlight

Icarus — emerging data-theft & extortion crew

A new extortion group, Icarus, has claimed the Klue/Salesforce data-theft campaign. By its own account it has been active since April 28, 2026 and lists a small set of prior victims on its leak site. Its operators contacted targets by email under the alias "mr bean," directing victims to negotiate via a Session Messenger ID — a pure data-theft-and-leak model with no file-encrypting ransomware involved.

Breaches & Incidents

LastPass — exposed via Klue

Disclosed that customer contact and CRM data were exposed through the Klue supply-chain breach; LastPass revoked Klue access, rotated tokens and notified law enforcement, and says its products and password vaults were not affected.

10+ firms hit via Klue → Salesforce

HackerOne, Huntress, Jamf, Recorded Future, Tanium, Snyk, OneTrust, Sprout Social, Insurity and Gong have all confirmed business data was exfiltrated through the compromised Klue integration.

Splunk Enterprise exploited in days

Splunk shipped fixes for CVE-2026-20253 on June 10; CISA confirmed active exploitation on June 18 — about eight days later — and added it to the KEV catalog as the first-ever Splunk entry.

Microsoft June Patch Tuesday — record

Microsoft patched a record ~208 CVEs this month, including an actively-exploited Microsoft Defender elevation-of-privilege zero-day (CVE-2026-41091) and a wormable, unauthenticated Windows kernel RCE (CVE-2026-45657, CVSS 9.8).
