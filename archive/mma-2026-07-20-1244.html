Top Story

Du Plessis wants the trilogy — and says Imavov should go first

Dricus du Plessis bounced back at UFC Fight Night 281 in Oklahoma City on Saturday, July 18, beating former welterweight champion Kamaru Usman by unanimous decision, 50–45, 49–46, 49–46. It moves him to 24–3 and puts him squarely back in the middleweight title conversation.

What makes the aftermath interesting is that du Plessis argued both sides of it. In his post-fight interview he said "a performance like that would get me a title shot," and at the UFC OKC press conference he doubled down that he "absolutely" deserves the next one. Then he conceded the queue: "Fight Imavov, get a title defense. He earned that." Taken at his word, du Plessis would not meet the Strickland–Imavov winner until 2027.

He has the head-to-head case. Du Plessis holds two wins over Sean Strickland, including one title defense, which is what makes a third fight a trilogy rather than a rematch. Against that, Khamzat Chimaev has been demanding an immediate rematch since losing the belt to Strickland by split decision at UFC 328 in May — a fight du Plessis has called "senseless." Nassourdine Imavov comes in on five straight wins, over Caio Borralho, Israel Adesanya and Brendan Allen.

Against Usman, du Plessis wobbled him repeatedly in the first two rounds with clubbing strikes and knees up the middle, and improved takedown defense shut down Usman's wrestling. Usman found some confidence in the third, and du Plessis showed fatigue after an eleven-month layoff. Dana White has still not named a challenger.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement. On June 20, 2026 the UFC began replacing its traditional media-panel system with the Meta UFC Rankings, an Elo-style mathematical model built with Meta. It weights opponent quality and activity, decays a rating if a fighter does not compete within 18 months, progressively discounts wins older than five years, and removes pound-for-pound rankings entirely. The media panel continues only through a transition period. The UFC has been explicit that this is not AI at runtime: "We didn't build an A.I. to rank fighters… A mathematical model is doing the work on the back end."

That changeover is the reason this page reports ranking numbers with attribution rather than asserting them. Sources this run continue to disagree — UFC.com's July 25 event page shows Ankalaev #1 and Guskov #9 in both its bout markup and event art, while LowKick has carried Guskov at #10 and described Ankalaev as the "current No. 2 contender." Records conflict too: this run's sources give Ankalaev 21–2–1 and Guskov 18–3–1, while a carried LowKick preview said Ankalaev 20–2. Both are shown rather than silently reconciled.

Business & broadcast. No gate, viewership or TKO Group figures were sourced this run, so none are published. The July 25 card streams on Paramount+.
