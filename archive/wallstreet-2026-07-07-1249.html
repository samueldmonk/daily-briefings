The Lead

Chips tumble as Samsung's record quarter and a DeepSeek chip report rattle the AI trade — oil jumps on a Hormuz strike

As of midday ET, Tuesday, July 7. Two blows to the AI trade landed at once. Samsung Electronics reported record preliminary Q2 results that beat estimates — but the stock slid in Seoul on profit-taking after a huge run and fresh worries about AI-spending durability. Separately, a Reuters report that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI inference chip to cut its reliance on Nvidia sent a fresh chill through the complex, knocking Nvidia down about 2% (KeyBanc reiterated its overweight rating).

The result was a broad chip selloff, with a gauge of semiconductor stocks sinking about 6%. Intel dropped roughly 10% (to about $110) and Applied Materials — which counts Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron and Intel among its customers — fell about 10%, while AMD lost around 8% and Micron, SanDisk and Western Digital each slipped about 5–7%. That pulled the Nasdaq Composite down roughly 1% (the Nasdaq 100 about 2%) and eased the S&P 500 about 0.2%, though most S&P 500 stocks actually rose — a rotation out of this year's biggest winners — while the Dow hovered near flat, clinging to Monday's record close (up about 0.3% in one late-morning read).

Oil added a second worry: WTI crude climbed to a one-week high near $69 a barrel after Iranian Revolutionary Guard forces fired on two commercial vessels near the Strait of Hormuz — including a Qatari state-owned LNG carrier struck by a projectile while exiting the strait — reviving supply fears; Brent traded above $72, though it remains near four-month lows on ample supply. Traders also looked ahead to the June FOMC minutes, while SpaceX made its debut in the Nasdaq-100.

The hardest-hit large-caps. Intel fell about 10% (to roughly $110); Applied Materials dropped about 10% given its exposure to memory and AI-capex customers Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron and Intel. KLA, Marvell and Broadcom also fell.

AMD (AMD)

≈ −8%

AMD cratered about 8% (to roughly $508), reversing a Monday rally as the Samsung- and DeepSeek-driven reset bled across every corner of the chip space.

Micron · SanDisk · WDC

≈ −5% to −7%

The memory names each slipped about 5–7% (Micron near the $1,000 mark) as Samsung's record report reignited fears that elevated memory pricing and margins are already fully priced in.

Nvidia (NVDA)

Lower

A Reuters report that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI inference chip — to reduce reliance on Nvidia — pressured the shares; KeyBanc reiterated its overweight rating, calling Nvidia "uniquely positioned" in AI data-center growth.

First Solar (FSLR)

Higher

A bright spot: shares jumped after Deutsche Bank told investors to "buy the dip," calling First Solar a fundamentally strong U.S.-based panel maker with a solid balance sheet. Apple also firmed after JPMorgan lifted its target to $345 from $325.

SpaceX (SPCX)

Index add

SpaceX joined the Nasdaq-100 before Tuesday's open — a move expected to force billions in fund buying; Morgan Stanley initiated coverage at overweight, citing an "X of 1" position in space infrastructure.
