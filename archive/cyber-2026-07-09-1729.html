Top Story

FortiBleed credential theft is now a confirmed pipeline into INC and Lynx ransomware

Researchers tracking the FortiBleed campaign say a mass harvest of Fortinet FortiGate credentials has become a direct route into ransomware. Ongoing since at least February, the financially motivated operation collected more than 110 million credentials from hundreds of thousands of internet-facing FortiGate firewalls — silently compromising 73,932 unique Fortinet firewall URLs across 194 countries — by intercepting VPN logins and other authentication traffic directly from compromised appliances.

SOCRadar's Threat Research Unit confirmed admin-level access on 409 targets and completed the full attack chain on 354 of them. Through an operational-security slip, a single initial access broker was found simultaneously logged into the negotiation panels of both INC Ransom and Lynx, and at least 12 ransomware deployments — encrypting hundreds of endpoints — have already been tied to the harvested access.

Threat-intel teams (SOCRadar, SecurityWeek, The Hacker News, CybersecurityNews) call it among the first confirmed cases connecting mass FortiGate credential theft to actual ransomware deployment. Defenders should treat any exposed FortiGate credentials as compromised: rotate all device and VPN credentials, enforce MFA, and apply Fortinet's latest FortiOS updates.

Patch Priority

Patch Adobe ColdFusion now — CVE-2026-48282 (CVSS 10.0) is actively exploited and the federal deadline is July 10

A maximum-severity path-traversal flaw (CWE-22) in Adobe ColdFusion's Remote Development Services (RDS) FILEIO handler lets an unauthenticated attacker perform arbitrary file operations and achieve remote code execution in the context of the ColdFusion service account. It affects ColdFusion 2025.9, 2023.20 and earlier. Researchers logged in-the-wild exploitation within hours of the July 7 disclosure (an attempt from an IP geolocated to India), and CISA added it to the KEV catalog the same day with an FCEB remediation deadline of July 10, 2026 (one day out) under BOD 26-04. Fix: apply Adobe's ColdFusion security update immediately and hunt for webshells and anomalous RDS activity.

Threat Actor Spotlight

ShinyHunters (UNC6240)

The data-theft and extortion crew ShinyHunters — tracked by Google/Mandiant as UNC6240 and notorious for breaches of Microsoft, AT&T and Ticketmaster — is running a live campaign against Oracle PeopleSoft. Operators exploit the unauthenticated RCE zero-day CVE-2026-35273 (CVSS 9.8, in the Updates Environment Management component), reportedly combined with a gadget chain of older flaws, to gain access and exfiltrate HR, payroll and PII from PeopleSoft databases — with the education sector disproportionately hit. Oracle disclosed the flaw in an out-of-band alert on June 10; observed activity dates to May 27–June 9, 2026. ShinyHunters claims roughly 300 instances across 100+ organizations, and a July 3 notification cluster (Nissan, Kubota North America and Aflac's Japan unit) confirmed data theft. Internet-facing PeopleSoft should be treated as emergency-priority: apply Oracle's fixes and hunt for credential theft from psappsrv.cfg.

Breaches & Incidents

Conduent breach balloons past 62 million people

The business-services and healthcare-tech giant Conduent saw its breach tally expand sharply, with healthcare breach reporting now placing the affected population at more than 62.2 million individuals — one of the year's largest exposures. Affected people should watch for identity-theft and phishing follow-ons.

Adobe ColdFusion max-severity flaw exploited within hoursExploited

A CVSS 10.0 path-traversal flaw in ColdFusion (CVE-2026-48282) yields unauthenticated remote code execution. Attackers began exploiting it within hours of the July 7 disclosure; it is now in CISA KEV with a July 10 federal deadline. Patch immediately.

Accenture acknowledges an incident after a 35GB source-code theft claimNew

A threat actor using the handle "888" posted on a cybercrime forum claiming to have stolen "just over 35GB of source codes" from consulting giant Accenture, along with RSA and SSH keys, Azure access tokens and config files now offered for sale. Accenture has acknowledged a security incident; scope is still being assessed.

Sysdig documents the first ransomware run end-to-end by an AI agentRansomware

Security firm Sysdig reported what it believes is the first ransomware attack carried out start-to-finish by an AI agent — a large language model handling the break-in, credential theft, lateral movement and the final encrypt-and-wipe of a production database. It is an early marker of autonomous, AI-driven intrusion.
