Top Story

UFC and Meta scrap the media panel for an Elo-based ranking system

On June 20 the UFC unveiled the Meta UFC Rankings, retiring the long-criticized media-voting panel in favor of an Elo-based mathematical model that updates automatically the Monday after every event. Officials describe it as a statistical/Elo model: victory and opponent quality drive roughly 95% of a fighter's rating, finishes add a minor boost, raw fight stats such as significant strikes are excluded, and recency weighting plus inactivity penalties reward active, in-form fighters.

The launch arrives without a pound-for-pound list, which the UFC says needs a separate model, and the first automated update shuffled the divisions — several notable names slid or dropped out entirely, drawing a mixed reaction from fans. It is the biggest structural change to how the promotion ranks its fighters in over a decade.

Rankings & Business

Rankings movement: the Meta UFC Rankings replaced the media panel on June 20, auto-updating each Monday after events; the system launched without a pound-for-pound ranking, and its first run reshuffled several divisions.

Business & broadcast: UFC Freedom 250 at the White House (June 14) paid the largest bonuses in promotion history — Performance of the Night recipients earned $425,000 each ($100K from the UFC, $125K in USD1 from World Liberty Financial, and $200K in CRO from Crypto.com), while Fight of the Night winners took home $400,000 each ($100K UFC plus $300K in CRO). Recipients included Justin Gaethje, Josh Hokit, Mauricio Ruffy, Bo Nickal and Diego Lopes. The UFC's standard post-fight bonus was permanently raised from $50K to $100K in the Paramount+ era.
