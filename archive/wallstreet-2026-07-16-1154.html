The Lead

Chips slide a second day on AI-spending jitters as TSMC's record quarter fails to lift the tape — UnitedHealth's beat keeps the Dow green (as of late morning ET)

For the second time in three days, blockbuster earnings from a dominant chipmaker preceded a pullback in the sector. Taiwan Semiconductor reported record second-quarter results — profit up roughly 77% year over year — and lifted its capital-spending outlook for the year, but the stock slipped in Thursday trading on skepticism that AI hyperscalers could eventually slow their infrastructure build-out, dragging the whole complex lower (Yahoo Finance/TheStreet). Micron, AMD, Intel and Broadcom each fell around 3%, and SanDisk dropped more than 5%. As of late morning ET the S&P 500 was off about 0.4% and the Nasdaq roughly 0.75%, while the Dow edged higher — the lone major index in the green.

What was holding the Dow up: UnitedHealth, which jumped about 6% after a strong second quarter and raised full-year guidance, and Abbott, up around 4% on its own beat and lifted outlook. United Airlines cut the other way, falling roughly 3% after pairing an earnings beat with soft third-quarter guidance tied to rising fuel costs. The backdrop stayed geopolitical: the U.S. launched another wave of airstrikes on Iran on Wednesday, and The Wall Street Journal reported Trump was briefed on options to widen the conflict, keeping oil near one-month highs and Treasury yields in focus. Netflix headlines the earnings calendar after the close (Yahoo Finance/CNBC).
