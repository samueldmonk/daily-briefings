The Lead

A global chip rout grips Wall Street — Nasdaq Composite down ~2% off its lows, Micron off ~11%, while the Dow holds near flat on bank strength (as of ~11:45 AM ET)

U.S. stocks stayed under heavy pressure late Tuesday morning as a worldwide technology selloff that began Monday deepened, with memory-chip shares routed on fresh doubts about stretched AI and semiconductor valuations. The Nasdaq Composite was off about 2% — bouncing back from an early plunge of nearly 3% — and the S&P 500 around 1.2%, but the Dow Jones Industrial Average held up far better, hovering near the flat line, as the damage stayed concentrated in chips and banks rallied. Micron sank about 11.4% to $1,074.60 ahead of its fiscal-Q3 earnings Wednesday, Qualcomm fell about 8.6%, Arm Holdings about 8.4% and Taiwan Semiconductor 5.2% to $443.35, while the VanEck Semiconductor ETF dropped about 6.5% to $625.62 and even Nvidia slid about 3.2% to $201.97. The selling spread from Asia, where South Korea's Kospi plunged 9.99% — its steepest drop in more than three months — triggering a 20-minute trading halt as Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix each tumbled more than 12%, and Japan's Nikkei 225 fell 3.55%.

Beneath the chip carnage, breadth held up far better than the headline tech damage suggested. Banks led the gainers, with Bank of America and JPMorgan each up about 2%, and some megacaps bucked the rotation — Microsoft and Amazon traded higher — while Super Micro jumped about 15% and Coherent rose roughly 9% as the day's standout large-cap gainers. Super Micro's surge followed NVIDIA's unveiling of its Vera Rubin platform at the ISC High Performance 2026 conference, where Super Micro was named alongside Dell to build custom Vera Rubin NVL4 racks, plus a GF Securities upgrade to Buy. Oracle slipped about 2.6% to $170.85 after disclosing it cut roughly 21,000 jobs over the past year as it retools around AI. Oil stayed soft — WTI settled down about 2.3% near $74.82 on Monday and held lower on reported U.S.–Iran progress — while the 10-year Treasury yield held near 4.5% (it touched 4.509% Monday, a high since June 12) after last week's more hawkish Fed under new Chair Kevin Warsh. Investors are looking ahead to FedEx earnings after Tuesday's close and Thursday's May PCE inflation report, the week's key macro test.

Movers & Drivers

The AI-chip leader slid about 3.2% to $201.97 as the rout broadened beyond memory; Qualcomm fell ~8.6%, Arm ~8.4% and Taiwan Semiconductor ~5.2% to $443.35. Nvidia hosts its shareholder meeting Wednesday.

Fell to about $170.85 after disclosing it cut roughly 21,000 jobs over the past year — to ~141,000 workers from ~162,000 — as AI adoption reshapes its workforce.
